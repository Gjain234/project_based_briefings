# Sudan FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Active Multi-Front War and Its Direct Disruption of Project Operations

Sudan's ongoing war between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) has entered its fourth year with no resolution in sight, with Kordofan and Darfur emerging as the most intense epicentres of hostilities. Drone strikes on hospitals—including the March 2026 attack on Dilling General Hospital—and sustained combat across Blue Nile and Sennar states have created an operating environment of extreme volatility. Three projects in the portfolio carry direct exposure to this risk. The health system strengthening project explicitly identifies Darfur and Greater Kordofan as priority implementation areas, with the PAD acknowledging that 75% of health facilities in conflict-affected areas are already non-functional and that project materials face ongoing risks of looting, vandalism, and armed attack <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The emergency safety net project targets West and South Kordofan—identified in the PAD as high-security-risk locations where grand robbery and looting of life-saving aid by soldiers have been documented—and acknowledges that security is the central implementation challenge <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The resilience and livelihoods project similarly flags that approximately 30% of Sudan's counties are directly affected by conflict, with project areas in Kassala, Northern, and River Nile states selected precisely because they are currently neutral, though the PAD recognizes the risk of escalation into these zones <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. These risks are now materializing across the portfolio. The health project's ISR documents that shifting frontlines are forcing continuous adaptation of facility targeting and supply chain approaches, with territorial changes creating unpredictable access constraints that prevent reliable service delivery <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The education project's Aide Memoire confirms that school closures driven by active conflict are preventing grant distribution, with the project's Security Management Framework still pending clearance—a direct signal that unresolved security threats are blocking operational normalization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Multiple ISRs across the portfolio rate overall implementation risk as high, with active warfare cited as a primary driver of procurement delays, low fund utilization, and staff deployment constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>.

## 2. De Facto State Fragmentation and Governance Vacuum

The RSF's proclamation of a parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" in Darfur and parts of Kordofan has created a de facto partition of Sudan, with the SAF-aligned government operating from Khartoum while the RSF consolidates control over large swaths of territory where several portfolio projects operate. This legitimacy vacuum—compounded by the African Union's refusal to recognize SAF chief al-Burhan as head of state—has rendered federal institutions non-functional and created competing administrative structures that fundamentally complicate project coordination. The health project's PAD identifies this fragmentation directly, noting that federal institutions are non-functional and that the project must rely on state and locality-level institutions with limited capacity, while explicitly avoiding channeling funds through federal or parastatal entities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The safety net project operates under OP 7.30 (Dealings with De Facto Governments) precisely because of this political instability, with the PAD acknowledging that the government apparatus has relocated to Port Sudan and that significant civil servant displacement has occurred, yet without specifying how the project will navigate RSF-controlled areas in Darfur <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The resilience project similarly identifies high political and governance risk, noting that state and locality-level authorities are operational but resource-constrained, and that the project will not move forward without consensus from all stakeholders—a condition that becomes nearly impossible to satisfy when multiple governance authorities claim legitimacy over the same territory <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. These governance risks are actively materializing. ISRs for the health project rate political and governance risk as high, with the RSF's parallel administration creating unclear counterparts, conflicting policy directives, and institutional paralysis that undermine health system preservation efforts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The energy and digital infrastructure project's ISR documents that sector policy instability is directly rooted in the absence of a unified national authority capable of establishing coherent policies, with competing power centers issuing conflicting directives <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The safety net project's ISR confirms that political and governance instability is manifesting as fragmented institutional legitimacy, unclear jurisdictional boundaries, and competing administrative claims that prevent coherent safety net delivery across conflict-affected regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>.

## 3. Mass Displacement and Famine as Compounding Operational Threats

Sudan hosts the world's largest displacement crisis—14 million forcibly displaced since April 2023—while confirmed famine (IPC Phase 5) in El Fasher and Kadugli, with 20 additional areas at risk across Greater Darfur and Greater Kordofan, is creating a compounding humanitarian emergency that directly undermines project design assumptions. The health project's PAD explicitly targets 11.16 million internally displaced persons as primary beneficiaries but acknowledges that security challenges and population mobility may compromise beneficiary identification, and that frequent population movements fleeing conflict areas represent a core technical design risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The project's nutrition component—including severe acute malnutrition management—will face severe resource constraints in famine conditions where food insecurity is the primary driver of malnutrition, yet the PAD does not address famine-specific operational constraints in confirmed Phase 5 areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The safety net project targets IPC Phase 3 and 4 areas in Northern West Darfur, East Darfur, and West and South Kordofan, with a cash transfer design providing US$10 per person per month to cover 50% of daily caloric needs—a transfer value that may be wholly inadequate if famine conditions worsen or if hyperinflation (recorded at 211% in October 2024) erodes purchasing power faster than anticipated <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The resilience project's food security component targets agricultural production in states adjacent to confirmed famine zones, with the PAD acknowledging that food prices have increased 50% since the war started and that the project cannot fully mitigate the macroeconomic crisis, yet without assessing the scenario where famine spreads to target states <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the safety net project's ISR confirms that macroeconomic instability is eroding the real value of cash transfers, with famine conditions in project-relevant regions creating collapsed markets and non-functional supply chains that render planned transfer amounts insufficient <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The resilience project's ISR documents that IDP and host community social cohesion—a core project objective—is deteriorating as resource scarcity driven by displacement and food insecurity intensifies competition between groups <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The education project's Aide Memoire and ISRs confirm that school closures and limited access to schools are preventing grant distribution, with famine-driven survival pressures reducing school attendance and engagement in Darfur and Kordofan <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>.

## 4. Deliberate Obstruction of Humanitarian Access

Both the SAF and RSF are wilfully obstructing aid movement, attacking humanitarian workers, and restricting access to conflict-affected populations in violation of international humanitarian law. Drone strikes on WFP operations since early February 2026 and the UN Security Council's condemnation of the weaponization of starvation underscore that obstruction is systematic and deliberate rather than incidental to conflict. This risk cuts across the entire portfolio. The health project's PAD identifies a three-tiered service delivery model—distinguishing fully accessible, partially accessible, and severely restricted access areas—as the primary adaptation mechanism, but this model assumes some level of humanitarian access that current conditions indicate is being deliberately denied <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The project relies on high-level UN coordination and OCHA humanitarian corridor facilitation for supply movement, creating a dependency on access that warring parties can unilaterally revoke <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The safety net project's PAD acknowledges high security risk from military operations and violence toward civilians and aid workers, as well as bureaucratic impediments and politicization of aid, but does not assess whether current obstruction levels in Kordofan and Darfur permit meaningful implementation for the 552,000 beneficiaries in Component 1 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The resilience project similarly relies on UN humanitarian supply routes and operates under the UN security umbrella, yet the PAD does not detail contingencies for sustained access obstruction <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. These risks are now fully realized. The health project's ISR documents that constrained access to health facilities and beneficiaries is forcing ongoing adaptation of facility targeting, with the project unable to maintain consistent service delivery as warring parties restrict movement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The education project's ISRs confirm that deliberate obstruction is preventing school grant distribution, with procurement delays of 98.3% underspend ($281k of $16.8m signed) directly attributable to access restrictions and supply route insecurity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The safety net project's ISR rates environment and social risks as high, with access obstruction in Kordofan and Darfur creating implementation bottlenecks that prevent cash delivery to vulnerable populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>.

## 5. Collapsed Health System, Civil Society Suppression, and Fiduciary Integrity

Sudan's health system has been driven to near-total collapse—37% of facilities non-functional, 201 verified attacks on healthcare facilities since April 2023, and renewed disease outbreak risks from cholera, dengue, malaria, and measles—while both warring parties engage in systematic arbitrary detention, torture, and suppression of civil society that degrades the institutional and community-level infrastructure on which projects depend. These two risks are deeply intertwined: the same governance vacuum that enables impunity for war crimes also eliminates the independent oversight mechanisms necessary for fiduciary accountability. The health project's PAD acknowledges that 75% of health facilities in conflict-affected areas are non-functional and that the project aims to restore basic health services, but its disease surveillance component can identify outbreaks without having the resources to contain them at scale, and the underlying drivers of disease transmission—overcrowded displacement sites, disrupted WASH services, collapsed immunization—remain largely outside project scope <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. On civil society suppression, the PAD identifies high stakeholder engagement risk stemming from Sudan's long history of conflict affecting engagement with authorities and communities, and relies on over 1,200 community health workers and 25 mobile teams for service delivery—a community engagement model that creates direct exposure to the arbitrary detention and retaliatory attacks documented against local volunteers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The safety net project's PAD identifies political pressure on NGOs and implementing partners during community targeting as a governance risk, yet does not assess whether current detention of activists and volunteers affects partner availability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The education project's PAD similarly relies on community-based targeting and civil society partners without detailing security protocols for protecting them from political repression [P504621 | PAD]. In implementation, the health project's ISR rates institutional capacity as high risk, with the collapsed health system directly degrading the infrastructure required to implement health system strengthening activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The education project's ISRs document high fiduciary risk with only 7.3% fund utilization, with civil society suppression undermining the independent monitoring and verification mechanisms necessary for financial accountability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The energy and digital infrastructure project's ISR confirms that fiduciary risk is rated high, with the suppression of civil society watchdogs and independent media removing the checks and balances required for effective fund management oversight <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The safety net project's ISR documents that institutional capacity constraints are compounded by the funding crisis—with only 5.5% of the $2.9 billion 2026 humanitarian response plan funded—forcing WFP and UNICEF to reduce services and close facilities, directly translating into implementation deficits that no project-level mitigation can fully offset <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>.
