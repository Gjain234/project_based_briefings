The monopolization of power by the Taliban and the adoption of exclusionary and discriminatory policies:

The Taliban's consolidation of power and systematic exclusion of women from public life represents the most direct governance-level threat to portfolio implementation. Projects designed with women as primary beneficiaries or requiring female staff face structural incompatibility with Taliban edicts. The Afghanistan Cities Inclusivity Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086bee5fe&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166127 – PAD</a> explicitly targets women through 30 Gozar/Business Gozar investments and female-only passport windows, while the Women's Economic Empowerment National Priority Program <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0861210e1&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164443 – PAD</a> directs 78 percent of its 450,000 beneficiaries to female recipients and relies on female social organizers and village facilitators for community mobilization. The TAGHIR civil service project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0868776cf&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166978 – PAD</a> mandates a 10 percent gender quota for new civil service positions across 16 priority ministries, while the Afghanistan Education Quality Reform Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0861210e2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P159378 – PAD</a> centers female teacher recruitment and girl-friendly school infrastructure as core implementation pillars. These design assumptions are now fundamentally undermined: the Taliban's January 2026 Criminal Procedural Regulations criminalize women's independent mobility, the September 2025 ban on women entering UN offices remains in force as of late January 2026, and women civil servants were formally terminated in January 2026 without due process. Implementation evidence confirms these risks are materializing. The education project records a worsening trajectory, with out-of-school girls rising from 1,387,717 to 1,628,133 and female school attendance declining from 1,616,799 to 1,468,720, while teacher training indicators show zero progress <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087139b61&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P159378 – ISR</a>. The Herat Electrification Project documents that none of the Grievance Redress Committees established across four districts include a female member, and recruitment of a female safeguard officer in Herat remains incomplete due to limited applications from female staff <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0859386e1&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162022 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TAGHIR project records that 169 of 838 appointed civil servants—20 percent—have left the civil service for personal, professional, and potentially political reasons, with the government collapse eliminating the primary implementing agency IARCSC entirely <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870ff8d0&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166978 – ISR</a>.

Mounting intra-Taliban tensions:

Intra-Taliban tensions and the fragility of the movement's internal cohesion create compounding governance risks for portfolio projects that depend on sustained political commitment, policy continuity, and institutional reliability. The BTI 2026 assessment cited across multiple project risk assessments identifies lack of professionalism, declining institutional capacity, and shortage of funds for soldiers' salaries as serious threats to Taliban authority, while the National Resistance Front continues guerrilla operations in northern Afghanistan. Projects requiring multi-ministry coordination and sustained government ownership are particularly exposed. The Afghanistan Gas Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08728c957&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172109 – PAD</a> depends on the Ministry of Mines and Petroleum as primary implementing agency, with the Deputy Minister serving as Senior Project Director, and explicitly identifies cyclical structural and staffing changes weakening institutional capacity and frequent changes to core leadership as high institutional risks. The TAGHIR project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0868776cf&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166978 – PAD</a> relies on an Inter-Ministerial Steering Committee and requires the government to finance 1,500 new civil service positions from core budget by project exit—a sustainability assumption directly threatened by political instability and leadership turnover. The Citizens' Charter Afghanistan Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0846160e5&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P160567 – PAD</a> depends on strong commitment from the Office of the President to monitor the project as a flagship program, a mitigation measure that presupposes political stability and coherent executive authority. Implementation evidence confirms that political instability is actively disrupting project execution. The gas project records ARTF cancellation and IDA grant suspension due to the extraordinary political situation, with only 8.1 percent of the USD 52.5 million IDA credit disbursed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172109-a3b62cd8-729c-46e2-aa59-f40bff9ee7b8.pdf" target="_blank">P172109 – ISR</a>. The TAGHIR project documents government collapse eliminating IARCSC and approximately USD 1 million in unpaid government liabilities for salaries and services as of August 2021, with political and governance risk rated High across all assessment periods <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870ff8d0&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166978 – ISR</a>. The Afghanistan Cities Inclusivity Project records stakeholder risk escalating from Substantial at approval to High currently, indicating deteriorating relationships with government authorities and local assemblies <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a1e841&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166127 – ISR</a>.

Economic hardship and vulnerability to economic shocks, leading to intensified contestation over resources:

Afghanistan's catastrophic economic contraction—over 20 percent since 2021—combined with the mass returnee crisis and trade disruption from the Pakistan-Afghanistan conflict creates compounding resource contestation risks that undermine project implementation across the portfolio. The Hindukush Connectivity Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08311647b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P145347 – PAD</a> identifies macroeconomic risk as Low at approval but acknowledges that 40 percent of Afghanistan's poor reside in the northeast and central regions separated by the Hindukush, with 60 percent of households in the project area surviving on less than USD 1 per capita per day. The Afghanistan Cities Inclusivity Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086bee5fe&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166127 – PAD</a> identifies macroeconomic risk as High, noting weak labor demand despite an increasing labor force, and targets 80,000 vulnerable households for short-term employment in a context where 17.4 million people face crisis-level food insecurity. The Women's Economic Empowerment project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0861210e1&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164443 – PAD</a> targets poor and ultra-poor women for enterprise development and market linkages, but the project's assumption that beneficiaries can invest in productive activities is severely constrained when households face emergency food needs. The Afghanistan Connectivity and Digital Economy Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08585693d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P156894 – PAD</a> identifies poor and low-income citizens as vulnerable groups and notes that retail internet prices of USD 37 per megabit per month are unaffordable, a barrier that deepens as household incomes collapse. Implementation evidence confirms economic shocks are materializing across the portfolio. The Hindukush Connectivity Project records macroeconomic risk escalating from Low at approval to High currently, with IDA grant disbursements suspended and project performance rated Highly Unsatisfactory with zero roads constructed and zero fiber optic installation completed <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d739fc&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P145347 – ISR</a>. The Afghanistan Cities Inclusivity Project records zero progress on passport processing for Afghan refugees in Pakistan and zero IDP/returnee households receiving employment support against targets of 1.5 million passports and 10,000 households <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a1e841&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166127 – ISR</a>. The Citizens' Charter project records macroeconomic risk escalating from Substantial to High, with fiduciary risk also escalating, while the project's emergency support to 3,094 high IDP/returnee communities demonstrates the scale of resource contestation pressures <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0880c0f18&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P160567 – ISR</a>.

Fragmented aid provision and inequitable access to services and humanitarian relief:

The collapse of humanitarian funding—with Afghanistan projected to lose approximately USD 859 million in ODA in 2026 compared to 2023 baseline levels, equivalent to 5 percent of GNI—has created severe fragmentation in service delivery that directly undermines project design assumptions and implementation capacity. The Afghanistan COVID-19 Emergency Response Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0877a5a12&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173775 – PAD</a> relies on partnerships with WHO and UNICEF as essential implementation partners, with essential goods and supplies procured through MOUs with UN agencies, but the UN has been forced to reprioritize its response from 16.8 million to 12.5 million people assisted. The Afghanistan Water Supply, Sanitation and Hygiene Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088006cec&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P169970 – PAD</a> is financed 75 percent through ARTF and identifies unpredictable and variable sector funding as a sector strategy risk, while the project's health and sanitation objectives are undermined by the closure of 153 Family Health Houses and loss of 1,700 female health workers. The Afghanistan Education Quality Reform Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0861210e2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P159378 – PAD</a> is financed through IDA, ARTF, and GPE grants totaling USD 298 million and explicitly identifies macroeconomic risk as Substantial due to declining aid and fiscal pressures, with development budget execution at only 50 percent of budgeted amounts. The Afghanistan Infrastructure and Institutional Development Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b085d7cd68&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P158768 – PAD</a> is financed entirely through donor grants and acknowledges that project success depends on availability of donor funding for guarantees, insurance, and capital subsidies. Implementation evidence confirms that aid fragmentation is causing direct project suspension across the portfolio. The COVID-19 project records ARTF and ESMP trust fund cancellation, IDA grant suspension, project commitment reduction from USD 113 million to USD 60 million, and institutional capacity risk escalating from Substantial to High <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173775-dbe2bb5c-bf77-42a4-ad8c-ba234b33ea99.pdf" target="_blank">P173775 – ISR</a>. The water project records ARTF cancellation, IDA grant suspension, and zero progress on all PDO and intermediate results indicators since December 2020, with no water supply network constructed and no beneficiaries reached <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0883ba442&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P169970 – ISR</a>. The education project records ARTF and GPE fund cancellation and IDA grant suspension, with overall risk rating elevated to High <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087139b61&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P159378 – ISR</a>.

Tensions with neighboring countries:

The escalation of Pakistan-Afghanistan armed conflict in February 2026—with Pakistani airstrikes striking Kabul, Kandahar, Nangarhar, and Paktika, and Taliban retaliatory operations along the Durand Line—creates direct implementation threats for projects operating in border provinces and dependent on cross-border trade and logistics. The Afghanistan Cities Inclusivity Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086bee5fe&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166127 – PAD</a> targets Jalalabad in Nangarhar Province, Khost in Khost Province, and Asadabad in Kunar Province as project cities, with Component 1 operating through Afghan missions in Pakistan including Islamabad, Karachi, Peshawar, and Quetta. The Hindukush Connectivity Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08311647b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P145347 – PAD</a> covers 11 provinces including Badakhshan, Kunduz, Takhar, and Nangarhar, with significant portions of the B2B road passing through Taliban-controlled areas and areas outside effective government control, and security-related cost premiums of up to 30 percent included in estimates. The Afghanistan Gas Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08728c957&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172109 – PAD</a> requires procurement of specialized equipment not available in Afghanistan, with the PAD identifying timely procurement as a high technical design risk, while the Torkham border closure for nearly a month caused USD 90 million in private sector losses and stranded thousands of trucks. The Afghanistan Connectivity and Digital Economy Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08585693d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P156894 – PAD</a> explicitly identifies cross-border connections with Pakistan as a project location and acknowledges that rising insecurity could increase component costs and delay infrastructure rollout. Implementation evidence confirms that conflict-driven trade disruption and security deterioration are materializing as implementation constraints. The Afghanistan Cities Inclusivity Project records zero progress on passport processing for Afghan refugees in Pakistan and zero IDP/returnee households reached, with stakeholder risk escalating to High <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a1e841&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166127 – ISR</a>. The Hindukush Connectivity Project records macroeconomic risk escalating from Low to High, IDA grant disbursements suspended, and project performance rated Highly Unsatisfactory with zero physical progress <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d739fc&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P145347 – ISR</a>. The Herat Electrification Project records construction and installation of four substations halted from August 15, 2021 due to security reasons, with political and governance risk rated High and IDA grants suspended <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0850b57b9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162022 – ISR</a>.

Weaknesses in technical institutional capacity:

Across the portfolio, weak technical institutional capacity in implementing agencies represents a persistent and worsening constraint that compounds all other FCV risks. The Afghanistan Gas Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08728c957&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172109 – PAD</a> identifies institutional capacity as a High risk, noting that the Ministry of Mines and Petroleum undergoes cyclical structural and staffing changes weakening institutional capacity, with frequent changes to core leadership and limited capacity of institutions managing the hydrocarbon sector. The Afghanistan Water Supply project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088006cec&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P169970 – PAD</a> identifies AUWSSC as vulnerable to political capture, insecurity, and instability, with high staff turnover and weak institutional capacity, while the project's reliance on a locally-based PIU with autonomy to operate efficiently in three cities assumes institutional stability that does not exist. The TAGHIR project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0868776cf&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166978 – PAD</a> identifies institutional capacity as a High risk, noting limited capacity of public administration to conduct ambitious civil service reforms and risk that government commitment to finance project-supported positions by project exit is undermined by macroeconomic constraints. The Afghanistan Infrastructure and Institutional Development Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b085d7cd68&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P158768 – PAD</a> identifies institutional capacity as High risk throughout, noting highly uneven and thinly spread technical capacity in the public sector, long-term aid dependency creating vulnerability to delays, and the Central PPP Authority as a new agency with limited experience. Implementation evidence confirms that institutional capacity is collapsing across implementing agencies. The DABS utility implementing the Herat Electrification Project records persistent delays in processing contractual obligations including advance payments and technical document reviews, procurement system fragility requiring external training in Sri Lanka, weak fiduciary systems requiring strengthening measures, and failure to fill key PIU positions in a timely manner <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0859386e1&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162022 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TAGHIR project records government collapse eliminating IARCSC, 20 percent staff attrition among appointed civil servants, sector strategies and policies risk escalating from Substantial to High, and stakeholder risk escalating from Substantial to High <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870ff8d0&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166978 – ISR</a>. The Afghanistan Infrastructure and Institutional Development Project records ARTF cancellation, IDA grant suspension, PPP regulatory framework destabilized by October 2020 law amendments, and compliance review requirements triggered by governance changes, with institutional capacity rated High across all review periods <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08829ed4f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P158768 – ISR</a>.
