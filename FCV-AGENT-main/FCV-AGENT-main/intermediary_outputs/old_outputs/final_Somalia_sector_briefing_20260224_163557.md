## Social Protection and Humanitarian Response

The social protection and humanitarian response cluster—anchored by the Somalia Safety Net Project [P507443 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-00f4f571-1823-453b-9c9d-8aeb7c1ee72e.pdf) and the Shock-Responsive Safety Net and Emergency Preparedness project [P509224 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509224-f9db880c-5abe-48a3-9915-400e96d1dcb7.pdf)—operates at the intersection of Somalia's most acute FCV pressures. Country-level risks are severe: 6.5 million people face crisis-level food insecurity (SOM-001), the 2025 Humanitarian Response Plan is only 24% funded (SOM-002), and nearly 4 million people are displaced (SOM-003), with displacement directly disrupting agricultural production, market access, and aid delivery. Both PADs explicitly anticipate these dynamics: P507443 acknowledges that "delivery capacity is constrained by insecurity, population mobility, and limited infrastructure, especially in contested and climate-vulnerable areas" [P507443 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-00f4f571-1823-453b-9c9d-8aeb7c1ee72e.pdf), while P509224 documents the sharp collapse in cash assistance from US$592 million in 2023 to US$262 million in 2024, signaling the very funding environment in which these projects must operate [P509224 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509224-f9db880c-5abe-48a3-9915-400e96d1dcb7.pdf). Critically, P507443's implementation modality is institutionally tied to WFP via a required output agreement [P507443 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-00f4f571-1823-453b-9c9d-8aeb7c1ee72e.pdf), meaning WFP's own resource depletion risk (SOM-002) directly threatens delivery and supervision. Realized implementation risks confirm these vulnerabilities: the Somalia Shock-Responsive Community Resilience Project (P173315) required a 12-month closing date extension after security, flooding, and logistical constraints prevented completion of four infrastructure works [P173315 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a396a8&stream=Yes), and the Somalia Urban Resilience Project (P170922) recorded large inflows of drought-induced IDPs in Mogadishu, Baidoa, and Garowe that overshot targeting plans, while simultaneously activating its Contingent Emergency Response Component for drought response [P170922 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087783da4&stream=Yes) [P170922 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170922-8ebda4aa-5070-41fa-b865-72e4172bb0b8.pdf). Insecurity at delivery points—including a terror attack near the Kismayo project site [P170922 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170922-8ebda4aa-5070-41fa-b865-72e4172bb0b8.pdf) and the need for armed police deployment at an IDP settlement in Garowe [P170922 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170922-8ebda4aa-5070-41fa-b865-72e4172bb0b8.pdf)—illustrates how al-Shabaab's territorial resurgence (SOM-006) and ongoing kinetic operations (SOM-008) translate directly into operational disruption for cash and service delivery.

## Governance, Public Financial Management, and Intergovernmental Systems

The governance cluster—spanning the Recurrent Cost and Reform Financing project [P173731 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0879e0e01&stream=Yes), the Public Financial Management project [P177298 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0187c8e3-e606-4407-b94f-d27e9320d601.pdf), and the digital identity and financial inclusion project [P168115 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086a0284d&stream=Yes)—faces compounding risks from Somalia's contested federal architecture. Country risks SOM-004 (electoral and constitutional reform disputes), SOM-005 (federal–state armed confrontation), and SOM-007 (federal fragmentation enabling al-Shabaab) are directly material: the PAD for P173731 warns that "risk of political instability remains high and could slow or derail reforms" and that elections imply leadership change and constitutional friction [P173731 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0879e0e01&stream=Yes), while P177298 flags that "FGS and FMSs dialogue and inter-governmental collaboration could also be disrupted because of upcoming FMSs election processes" [P177298 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0187c8e3-e606-4407-b94f-d27e9320d601.pdf). P168115 identifies that "unclear mandates and structures, overlap of responsibilities, and lack of a shared strategy remain as key challenges, creating tensions, and delaying the decision-making process" [P168115 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086a0284d&stream=Yes). These PAD-level risks have materialized extensively. The Federal–Member State Fiscal Framework (FMFF) was not convened in 2025, blocking tabling of agreed policy items [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf); fiscal transfers to Jubaland State have been withheld for two years [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf); the 2024 budget did not apply the agreed intergovernmental transfer formula [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf); and Puntland has not signed education functional assignment agreements, leaving teachers on project rather than government payroll [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf). Political tensions are already delaying adoption of a policy brief on functional and revenue assignment [P173731 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-2e907ab5-ba94-464c-ad53-4736db5c7867.pdf), and a security incident in Kismayo required a formal corrective action plan [P173731 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-2e907ab5-ba94-464c-ad53-4736db5c7867.pdf). In Somaliland, parliamentary review delays are holding up publication of audited financial statements [P177298 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177298-c0e08772-796a-485b-90c6-334b7bf77f30.pdf), and budget approval delays are cascading into citizen budget preparation [P177298 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177298-c0e08772-796a-485b-90c6-334b7bf77f30.pdf). Across the cluster, persistent audit recommendation backlogs signal that accountability reforms are not keeping pace with governance fragility [P177298 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177298-c0e08772-796a-485b-90c6-334b7bf77f30.pdf).

## Infrastructure, Energy, and Natural Resource Management

The infrastructure, energy, and natural resource management cluster—encompassing the Somalia Urban Resilience Project [P170922 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08735b2ef&stream=Yes), the Energy Sector Recovery Project [P173088 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088c01d81&stream=Yes), the Distributed Renewable Energy project [P181341 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2ca62b03-e1a7-47d1-9c72-b59a2fa65b89.pdf), the Water and Agri-Food project [P177627 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-63dd48bb-2de2-45f6-884c-8c9a581a5515.pdf), and the Fisheries project [P178032 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-08b97878-d848-4270-8d6d-e665f996841d.pdf)—confronts the full spectrum of Somalia's FCV operating environment. Al-Shabaab's territorial resurgence (SOM-006) is the most direct threat: P177627's PAD states that "implementation will not be possible in al-Shabaab–controlled areas and security issues need to be carefully assessed and managed elsewhere" [P177627 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-63dd48bb-2de2-45f6-884c-8c9a581a5515.pdf), while P173088 conditions key disbursements on adoption of a Security Management Plan [P173088 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088c01d81&stream=Yes) and P181341 builds in explicit site-substitution flexibility for security-constrained locations [P181341 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2ca62b03-e1a7-47d1-9c72-b59a2fa65b89.pdf). Federal–state confrontation (SOM-005) and fragmentation (SOM-007) create structural implementation barriers: P177627 requires subsidiary agreements with each Federal Member State before disbursement, and P178032's governance model depends on a Director-General Technical Working Group spanning FMS fisheries directors [P178032 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-08b97878-d848-4270-8d6d-e665f996841d.pdf). Large-scale displacement (SOM-003) adds social risk, with P170922's PAD explicitly warning that "urban investments will result in increased land values, which can trigger displacement and further impoverish the already vulnerable IDPs" [P170922 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08735b2ef&stream=Yes). Realized risks are severe and persistent: in P177627, the Puntland Subsidiary Agreement remains unsigned, halting implementation in that state and negatively affecting disbursement rates [P177627 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177627-fbbfa6e1-4f42-4b2f-985f-98a91aaca38e.pdf) [P177627 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177627-b162b511-6e2c-44e2-b3a2-f44c382d7f6b.pdf), while unclear administrative control in Sool and Sanaag required third-party implementation arrangements and ongoing clarifications [P177627 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177627-b162b511-6e2c-44e2-b3a2-f44c382d7f6b.pdf). In P170922, insecurity prevented road transport of heavy equipment to Baidoa, requiring costly airlifts [P170922 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170922-8ebda4aa-5070-41fa-b865-72e4172bb0b8.pdf), and Security Management Plan operationalization delays caused further schedule slippage [P170922 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170922-8ebda4aa-5070-41fa-b865-72e4172bb0b8.pdf). In P173088, delays in electricity service provider merger discussions are holding back grid sub-components [P173088 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173088-742a6279-5b21-4709-b718-c401ed1d615b.pdf), and in P178032, Puntland's absence from Fisheries Management and Development Council meetings is undermining sector governance coordination [P178032 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178032-1b6ba979-3fa4-456a-8f0a-e5136d58776f.pdf). UXO and explosive device screening requirements on infrastructure sites [P177627 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177627-b162b511-6e2c-44e2-b3a2-f44c382d7f6b.pdf) underscore that conflict intensity (SOM-008) is not a background condition but an active constraint on safe construction operations across the cluster.
