## Social Protection and Humanitarian Response

Somalia's social protection and humanitarian response cluster—anchored by projects P507443 and P509224—operates under acute and compounding country-level risks. Nearly 6.5 million people face crisis-level food insecurity driven by drought, conflict, and global aid cuts (SOM-001), while the 2025 Humanitarian Response Plan is only 24% funded and WFP warns of imminent assistance stoppage (SOM-002). Displacement of nearly 4 million people further strains delivery systems (SOM-003). PAD risks across both projects are correspondingly severe: insecurity and population mobility explicitly constrain cash transfer delivery, field verification, and the large-scale household survey [P507443 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-00f4f571-1823-453b-9c9d-8aeb7c1ee72e.pdf); WFP's role as a mandated implementation partner creates direct exposure to humanitarian financing shortfalls [P507443 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-00f4f571-1823-453b-9c9d-8aeb7c1ee72e.pdf); and the sharp drop in humanitarian cash assistance—from USD 592 million in 2023 to USD 262 million in 2024—threatens the emergency response instrument's core modality [P509224 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509224-f9db880c-5abe-48a3-9915-400e96d1dcb7.pdf). Realized implementation risks confirm these vulnerabilities are not theoretical: a drought shock already triggered CERC activation for crisis response, diverting planned investments [P170922 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087783da4&stream=Yes); nearly 300,000 drought-induced IDPs were reached across Mogadishu, Baidoa, and Garowe, straining urban service capacity [P170922 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087783da4&stream=Yes); and a security incident in Kismayo required a formal corrective action plan [P173731 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-2e907ab5-ba94-464c-ad53-4736db5c7867.pdf). The combination of worsening food insecurity, collapsing humanitarian financing, and active insecurity at delivery points creates a high-probability scenario for outcome disruption across this cluster.

## Governance, Public Financial Management, and Intergovernmental Coordination

The governance and PFM cluster—spanning projects P173731, P177298, P172434, P176898, and P168115—faces structural fragility rooted in Somalia's contested federal architecture. Electoral and constitutional reform disputes (SOM-004) and federal–state confrontation (SOM-005) are the dominant country-level risks, with al-Shabaab's fragmentation of the federal system (SOM-007) compounding governance dysfunction. PAD risks are pervasive: incomplete constitutional review and FGS–FMS contestation over resource distribution are explicitly flagged as threats to intergovernmental fiscal frameworks and reform traction [P173731 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0879e0e01&stream=Yes); political instability around elections is identified as likely to exacerbate fiduciary risks through leadership turnover in key PFM institutions [P177627 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-63dd48bb-2de2-45f6-884c-8c9a581a5515.pdf); and fragmented public finance administrations across FGS and FMSs create inefficiencies that directly impede state-building objectives [P177298 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0187c8e3-e606-4407-b94f-d27e9320d601.pdf). Realized risks are extensive and persistent: the Federal–Member State Fiscal Forum (FMFF) was not convened in 2025, blocking advancement of agreed intergovernmental policy items [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf); Jubaland State transfers have been withheld for two years [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf); the 2024 budget did not apply the agreed fiscal transfer formula in full, requiring renewed negotiations for 2025 [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf); Puntland has not signed education functional assignment agreements, leaving teachers on project rather than government payroll [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf); and elections are already delaying parliamentary approval of harmonized tax reforms [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf). Political tensions are simultaneously delaying adoption of functional and revenue assignment policy briefs [P173731 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-2e907ab5-ba94-464c-ad53-4736db5c7867.pdf), and Somaliland's audited financial statements remain pending parliamentary review [P177298 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177298-c0e08772-796a-485b-90c6-334b7bf77f30.pdf). Across this cluster, the realized pattern of intergovernmental non-compliance and coordination breakdown is directly undermining the reform trajectory the portfolio is designed to support.

## Infrastructure, Energy, and Urban Service Delivery

The infrastructure, energy, and urban services cluster—encompassing projects P170922, P173315, P173088, P177627, P178032, and P181341—is acutely exposed to Somalia's security environment and federal fragmentation. Al-Shabaab's territorial resurgence and increased attack tempo (SOM-006), ongoing kinetic operations in the south (SOM-008), and large-scale displacement (SOM-003) constitute the primary country-level threats. PAD risks are operationally concrete: implementation is explicitly impossible in al-Shabaab-controlled areas, requiring site substitution and contingency planning [P177627 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-63dd48bb-2de2-45f6-884c-8c9a581a5515.pdf); disbursements under energy projects are conditioned on adoption of Security Management Plans [P508408 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1c1564c9-a880-45d6-9b95-a7f1b4568685.pdf); forced IDP displacement is described as rampant in urban centers such as Mogadishu, Hargeisa, and Garowe, creating land acquisition and social conflict risks around infrastructure investments [P173088 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088c01d81&stream=Yes); and inaccessibility of coastal locations requires third-party monitoring and GEMS-based supervision [P178032 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-08b97878-d848-4270-8d6d-e665f996841d.pdf). Realized risks confirm severe and persistent disruption: security and flooding prevented completion of four infrastructure works—two roads and two canals—within the original project timeframe, necessitating a 12-month closing date extension [P173315 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a396a8&stream=Yes); road rehabilitation under Component 2 is projected to miss closure due to security-related delays [P173315 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a396a8&stream=Yes); insecurity made road transport of heavy equipment impossible in Baidoa, requiring costly airlifting [P170922 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170922-8ebda4aa-5070-41fa-b865-72e4172bb0b8.pdf); a terror attack occurred near the Kismayo project site [P170922 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170922-8ebda4aa-5070-41fa-b865-72e4172bb0b8.pdf); armed police were deployed to the Garowe IDP settlement site following civil works disruptions [P170922 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170922-8ebda4aa-5070-41fa-b865-72e4172bb0b8.pdf); and the Puntland Subsidiary Agreement for water infrastructure remains unsigned, halting implementation in that state and negatively affecting disbursement rates [P177627 | Implementation Status and Results Report; P177627 | Aide Memoire]. Contested administrative responsibility in Sool and Sanaag has required third-party implementation arrangements with ongoing clarification needs [P177627 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177627-b162b511-6e2c-44e2-b3a2-f44c382d7f6b.pdf), while ESP merger delays are slowing grid network investments in the energy portfolio [P173088 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173088-742a6279-5b21-4709-b718-c401ed1d615b.pdf).
