# Sierra Leone FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Deep Ethnic and Political Divisions Threatening National Cohesion

Sierra Leone's political landscape remains defined by a volatile North-South ethno-regional divide and entrenched partisan polarization between the ruling SLPP and the opposition APC. The 2023 elections exposed these fault lines acutely, requiring a fragile national unity agreement in 2024 to defuse tensions that had generated inflammatory rhetoric and episodes of political instability. This structural fragility poses a persistent forward-looking risk to the entire World Bank portfolio, which spans projects requiring sustained inter-institutional cooperation across party lines. Multiple project appraisal documents explicitly flag this dynamic: one WASH project involves opposition-led implementing agencies (FCC and WARDC), creating governance arrangements that span the ruling party-opposition divide and are vulnerable to politically motivated obstruction [P507588 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf). A transport project notes that the opposition APC holds a parliamentary majority, raising the prospect of legislative gridlock blocking business environment and land reforms [P164212 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087bc19f1&stream=Yes). A decentralization project identifies the North-South ethno-regional divide as a historic fragility directly linked to the project's objective of rebalancing political power through local government reform [P172492 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes). A social protection project flags that election-related incidents could delay the establishment of the Project Implementation Unit during a critical startup window [P176789 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088cebb9f&stream=Yes). These risks are materializing: ISR evidence across the local governance portfolio shows that political and governance risk ratings have remained persistently Substantial, with post-election political tensions contributing to an unstable counterpart environment [P172492 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-1884d056-da29-49f2-ac93-9d9026ba75ad.pdf) [P177947 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177947-30baebee-e248-4fc7-a119-db12dcc76430.pdf) [P168608 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf). Aide Memoire evidence from the local governance project documents that parliamentary policy hearings disrupted project activity scheduling, reflecting the broader pattern of unpredictable institutional behavior in a polarized political environment [P172492 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf). The fragility of the 2024 unity agreement means these risks remain live and could re-escalate as political competition intensifies ahead of future electoral cycles.

## 2. Executive Overreach and Concentration of Power Undermining Democratic Institutions

Despite constitutional provisions for separation of powers, Sierra Leone's executive exercises pervasive control over state institutions through presidential appointments, patronage networks, and the instrumentalization of public bodies for political ends. This structural governance deficit creates systemic vulnerability across the portfolio. PAD evidence is extensive and consistent: a WASH project flags that the limited independence of the Auditor General and Audit Service Sierra Leone from the executive branch constitutes a high financial management risk [P507588 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf); a transport project documents that a prior World Bank operation was compromised when the government deviated from agreed design without due process, with the ICR explicitly calling for stronger measures against political interference in future operations [P177077 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf); a decentralization project identifies the risk that government review of legal mandates could roll back rather than advance decentralization, given historical recentralization decisions [P172492 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes); an urban project notes that five of seven beneficiary city mayors are from the opposition, creating a structural tension between central government and project implementing authorities that political risk remains Substantial despite mitigation measures [P168608 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes); and a financial inclusion project identifies that state-owned banks—eligible for the project's line of credit—failed nearly 60 percent of corporate governance benchmarks, with governance risk rated Substantial [P177947 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf). These risks are actively materializing across the portfolio. ISR evidence shows governance risk ratings have been upgraded or sustained at Substantial to High levels across multiple projects [P168608 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf) [P172102 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf) [P171059 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-f1fe9968-181d-40e3-a806-9d201fda93f3.pdf). Aide Memoire evidence documents concrete manifestations: fuel pilferage from public generators in the energy project [P171059 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf), misappropriation of a $141,000 grant advance by a project grantee with slow government enforcement response [P153437 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf), persistent counterpart fund non-disbursement [P153437 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf) [P153437 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf), and EDSA's resistance to transparent fiduciary arrangements required for private sector engagement in the energy sector [P171059 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf). Chiefdom-District Council revenue sharing disputes requiring ministerial-level intervention further illustrate how patronage dynamics permeate sub-national governance [P172492 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf). The cumulative picture is of a governance environment where executive overreach is not merely a background risk but an active implementation constraint across the portfolio.

## 3. Suppression of Civil Society and Erosion of Anti-Corruption Accountability

Sierra Leone's accountability ecosystem is being systematically weakened: civil society organizations face incentives through state contracts and board appointments to remain silent on corruption and governance failures, while those critical of the government are labeled as political opponents. The Anti-Corruption Commission collects asset declarations but keeps their content confidential, eliminating a key instrument of public scrutiny. This erosion of accountability infrastructure creates compounding fiduciary and oversight risks across the portfolio. PAD evidence documents the vulnerability: a social protection project records a confirmed fraud incident in which NaCSA staff extorted cash from potential beneficiaries during COVID-19 cash transfer enrollment, with fiduciary system weaknesses described as persistent and slow to improve [P176789 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088cebb9f&stream=Yes); a local governance project identifies weak complaint redress systems and fraud and corruption risks including collusion and outside interference in contracting as explicit procurement risk factors, while requiring public hearings as a minimum condition for grant access [P172492 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes); a land administration project flags that local elites and private sector actors benefiting from the status quo may attempt to undermine reforms, with limited civil society capacity to check them [P177031 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088db0cd6&stream=Yes); and an agriculture project acknowledges that the bulk of external assistance is channeled off-budget with insufficient transparency, undermining the integrity of the budgetary system [P153437 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08412c2b7&stream=Yes). Realized risks are pervasive. Aide Memoire evidence from the local governance project documents citizen audit workshop findings of fake receipts, opaque revenue management, and lack of transparency in remote communities [P172492 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf). The health project documents a non-functional grievance redress mechanism, with the Aide Memoire noting that communities lack effective channels to surface complaints [P172102 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-3e2bda14-bed5-45b3-bd7c-580e61a39b3d.pdf). The education project records the withdrawal of Save the Children from an implementing consortium, with responsibilities transferred to a government ministry—a pattern consistent with contracting civic space [P167897 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167897-f4984073-1dc6-4a44-a2fa-8b6ab0ccc16b.pdf). ISR evidence across the local governance, financial inclusion, and urban projects shows that governance risk ratings remain elevated and that accountability reform progress is slow [P172492 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-1884d056-da29-49f2-ac93-9d9026ba75ad.pdf) [P177947 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177947-30baebee-e248-4fc7-a119-db12dcc76430.pdf) [P168608 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf). The systemic suppression of independent oversight creates an environment in which project-level accountability mechanisms—GRMs, citizen audits, third-party monitoring—operate against a structural headwind.

## 4. Political Violence Including State Security Force Abuses and Election-Related Violence

Sierra Leone exhibits four documented forms of political violence—communal violence, state security force abuses against civilians, riots over national issues, and election-linked violence—that collectively create a high-risk operating environment for field-level project implementation. Demonstrators and opposition parties have faced police violence and restrictions on assembly, reflecting systemic vulnerabilities in the security and political environment. PAD evidence across the portfolio identifies multiple channels of susceptibility: a WASH project rates SEA/SH risk as Substantial, noting that 62 percent of women in Sierra Leone experience violence, and acknowledges that labor influx from civil works could heighten GBV risks in an already volatile security environment [P507588 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf); a transport project identifies local conflicts among project beneficiaries related to disrupted navigation and reduced fish catch downstream of bridge construction sites as a social risk in an environment with weak dispute resolution [P178683 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c83ae70a-5a33-45ba-a77a-95ccca1d4fda.pdf); a financial inclusion project flags that improving women's financial inclusion can upset gender relations and increase intimate partner violence risk, with project staff potentially demanding sexual favors in exchange for enrollment [P177947 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf); a land administration project acknowledges SEA/SH risks arising from project actors engaging directly with beneficiaries in field settings with limited supervision [P177031 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088db0cd6&stream=Yes); and an energy project notes that election-related incidents could disrupt construction activities spanning multiple districts [P171059 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08819710d&stream=Yes). These risks are materializing. Aide Memoire evidence from the education project documents persistent SRGBV cases, with teachers accused of abuse absconding from legal accountability—a pattern consistent with broader impunity norms [P167897 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167897-f4984073-1dc6-4a44-a2fa-8b6ab0ccc16b.pdf) [P167897 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167897-f4984073-1dc6-4a44-a2fa-8b6ab0ccc16b.pdf). Sexual harassment and drug abuse incidents on school buses are documented as recurring protection risks [P167897 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167897-f4984073-1dc6-4a44-a2fa-8b6ab0ccc16b.pdf). ISR evidence from the education project flags school-related gender-based violence as a persistent implementation risk [P167897 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087b90655&stream=Yes). The energy project's Aide Memoire documents theft and vandalism of solar infrastructure in remote areas, reflecting the weak rule-of-law environment in peripheral locations [P171059 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf). The combination of systemic political violence, weak security sector accountability, and impunity for perpetrators creates a structural protection risk that field-intensive projects across the portfolio must navigate throughout implementation.

## 5. Border Conflict with Guinea Following Military Incursion

In February 2026, Guinean military forces invaded Sierra Leonean territory, attacking border guards with knives and wounding personnel in an escalating dispute over control of a border point. This incident marks a significant deterioration in bilateral relations and introduces a new and acute security risk for portfolio projects with geographic or thematic exposure to the Sierra Leone-Guinea border corridor. Two projects carry direct susceptibility. A transport project finances the Kabba Bridge in Karene/Northwest Region on the Makeni-Kamakwei-Madina Oula-Guinea Border route, where the PAD explicitly identifies regional integration with Guinea as a project benefit [P178683 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c83ae70a-5a33-45ba-a77a-95ccca1d4fda.pdf). An escalating border dispute could disrupt contractor access, logistics, and the anticipated cross-border connectivity gains from this specific investment. A WASH project has applied the World Bank's policy on international waterways because its National Water Resources Master Plan may survey transboundary aquifers shared with Guinea and could recommend drawing on those aquifers for future water supply operations [P507588 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf). Deteriorating bilateral relations could complicate the diplomatic and technical dimensions of transboundary water governance that this component depends on. A land administration project plans to establish a national geodetic network including Continuously Operating Reference Stations across the country, with field operations extending to provincial areas proximate to the Guinea border [P177031 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088db0cd6&stream=Yes). Survey teams operating near contested boundary areas could face security risks and access constraints if the border dispute escalates further. No ISR or Aide Memoire evidence yet documents realized impacts from this specific incident, as the February 2026 incursion postdates most available implementation records. However, the forward-looking risk is material: the dispute remains unresolved, bilateral tensions are intensifying, and the portfolio contains investments whose geographic footprint and thematic scope place them directly in the path of potential escalation.
