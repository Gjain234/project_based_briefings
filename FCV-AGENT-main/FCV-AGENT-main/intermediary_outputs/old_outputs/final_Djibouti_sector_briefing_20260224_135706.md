**Governance, public sector management, and digital/data systems** operations face elevated disruption and legitimacy risks in a context of high political tension around constitutional change and the run-up to the April 2026 elections, alongside constrained civic space that can weaken accountability and reform coalitions. PADs anticipate substantial governance/fiduciary vulnerabilities, including weak oversight and limited non-state participation that can undermine transparency and controls, and risks that political-economy dynamics erode public trust and slow reform execution [P171777 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes), as well as high residual risk that cabinet/reform-priority shifts can stall governance reforms that require sustained high-level support [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf). These risks have already materialized as delays in required legal instruments and governance structures: the PFM reform decree/order was adopted but not signed/published, delaying project start, and governance structures for the PFM strategy have been slow to operationalize, affecting related donor-funded coordination [P180696 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf); similarly, the Digital Code remains unadopted—weakening the telecom regulatory framework and delaying dependent activities [P174461 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-a6aeb6a6-887c-41f9-8eb7-fb31c9e7d652.pdf)—while fragmented aid coordination is explicitly being “rebooted,” underscoring persistent coordination weaknesses [P171777 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-4b12f487-05cf-4472-bbf3-012c7cb41ce1.pdf).

**Human capital (education, TVET, and refugee-inclusive skills)** interventions operate under heightened security and social-cohesion stress (including violence risks in the capital and broader fragility), which can disrupt centralized training, verification, and delivery to FCV-affected groups. PADs flag structural delivery constraints—especially limited internet/physical access in remote refugee-hosting locations that impede ICT-related training [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf)—and governance/coordination failures across ministries that can delay curriculum and system reforms [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf), compounded by results-based and audit/verification dependencies that are sensitive to administrative disruptions in periods of political instability [P166059 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes). Implementation evidence shows acute under-delivery to refugees: refugee enrollment in selected TVET areas is reported at zero and refugee participation in work-based learning is also zero [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf), with refugee outcome data for completion/employment not yet available, limiting results management for this target group [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf). Governance and safeguards execution gaps have also surfaced, including an inactive steering committee arrangement [P175483 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf) and delayed operationalization of GBV prevention/response measures (action plan not transmitted despite training) [P175483 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf).

**Social protection, food security, and community resilience/urban inclusion** activities are especially exposed to displacement pressures, drought-driven mobility, and potential unrest linked to price spikes—risks that could intensify if sectarian violence in Djibouti City and election-period instability further erode social cohesion and state responsiveness. PADs anticipate substantial social risks in safety nets and community-facing delivery, including elite capture, exclusion, and social conflict arising from beneficiary targeting and selection processes [P506969 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf), while emergency food and market-stabilization designs explicitly aim to avert demonstrations associated with shortages, implying that failure or perceived inequity in response could itself become a disruption channel [P178988 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf). These risks are compounded by constraints on inclusive engagement (early consultations limited to central entities, with local governments/communities not consulted during preparation) that can weaken local buy-in and feedback loops [P178988 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf). Realized implementation bottlenecks are consistent with these governance and inclusion challenges in urban-focused work: land regularization has been slowed with case files blocked at the Land Directorate due to internal data-migration prioritization, delaying delivery of land certificates and, by extension, weakening credibility of inclusion and tenure-security commitments in fragile settings [P162901 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes).

**Infrastructure, trade connectivity, housing/urban services, and productive sector (agri-food) development** are highly sensitive to Djibouti’s security environment (including insurgent activity in northern zones and terrorism/regional conflict exposure) and to election-linked political instability that can interrupt cross-border collaboration, contractor access, and private investment appetite. PADs highlight that some corridor/trade facilitation activities are conditional on a satisfactory mutual understanding between Djibouti and Ethiopia customs authorities—making implementation vulnerable to political instability that disrupts bilateral operational cooperation [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes)—and that procurement/execution of large works packages is exposed to the “regional security context,” affecting bid response, timelines, and cost overruns [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes). In agri-food growth poles, PAD assumptions explicitly rely on the absence of major political crises, and social risks around beneficiary selection (elite capture, transparency, and exclusion) are flagged for investment financing and technical assistance [P178836 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf). Portfolio-wide, realized delays in adopting and operationalizing key legal/regulatory instruments and steering arrangements in other sectors (e.g., Digital Code and PFM reform instruments) signal a concrete, cross-cutting risk to time-sensitive approvals and institutional coordination needed for long-term contracts, evolving guarantee-governance arrangements, and multi-agency steering structures in infrastructure and housing reforms [P174461 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-a6aeb6a6-887c-41f9-8eb7-fb31c9e7d652.pdf) [P180696 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf).
