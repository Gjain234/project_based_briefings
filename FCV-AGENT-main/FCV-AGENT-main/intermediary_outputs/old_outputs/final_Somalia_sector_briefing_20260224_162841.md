Social protection and shock response (including urban recovery) is central in a context of acute food insecurity, large-scale displacement, and volatile security that can rapidly overwhelm delivery systems. Portfolio design anticipates that insecurity, mobility, and limited infrastructure will constrain delivery capacity for cash and systems-building, and that access restrictions can limit supervision and monitoring [P507443 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-00f4f571-1823-453b-9c9d-8aeb7c1ee72e.pdf); it also flags dependence on partners for field implementation support, creating exposure if humanitarian pipelines tighten [P507443 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-00f4f571-1823-453b-9c9d-8aeb7c1ee72e.pdf), and notes that declining ODA and cash assistance could undercut the scale-up model for emergency response [P509224 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509224-f9db880c-5abe-48a3-9915-400e96d1dcb7.pdf). These PAD risks are materializing through repeated climate shocks and displacement pressures: the urban resilience operation activated its CERC for drought response, and implementation has had to operate amid large inflows of drought-induced IDPs and unevenly inclusive intergovernmental/municipal coordination forums [P170922 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087783da4&stream=Yes), alongside security-management frictions and logistics constraints that slow works and service delivery (e.g., SMP operationalization and equipment transport constraints) [P170922 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170922-8ebda4aa-5070-41fa-b865-72e4172bb0b8.pdf).  

Human capital (health, vaccines, and education) operations are being implemented amid conflict intensity, election-related uncertainty, and climate shocks that disrupt access to services and outreach. Health PADs explicitly warn that floods/extreme weather and insecurity can make areas inaccessible and suspend outreach campaigns, while political impasse and fragmented stakeholder environments can weaken coordination (including conditionality for Somaliland arrangements) [P172031 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886c3194&stream=Yes); vaccine deployment similarly faces high risks of attacks and constrained state delivery capacity amid tensions across federal and state actors [P176956 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a76c27&stream=Yes). On education, PADs anticipate fragile federal–state roles, displacement-driven access constraints, and election-related legitimacy risks that can delay commencement and complicate community engagement and grievance handling [P172434 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08863e060&stream=Yes). Implementation experience aligns: education delivery is advancing but at a slower-than-planned pace due to security and access constraints in project areas, directly validating the access-and-supervision risks identified at design [P172434 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172434-92591eed-4886-40e9-916c-13eb17066b3d.pdf).  

Infrastructure and basic services (water/agriculture/environment, energy, and transport) face compounded risks from insecurity, contested governance, and displacement-driven social tensions, which can affect siting, land access, and continuity of works. PADs highlight that implementation may be impossible in some armed-group-controlled areas and that intergovernmental arrangements (subsidiary agreements and funds-flow) can be gating factors for activity rollout across FMS and Somaliland [P177627 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-63dd48bb-2de2-45f6-884c-8c9a581a5515.pdf); they also anticipate that security deterioration can force flexibility in site selection and contingency planning for works, while fiduciary and supervision challenges are heightened in insecure, decentralized settings [P173088 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088c01d81&stream=Yes). These risks have already translated into delays and sequencing changes: road and canal works were not completed by the original closing date due to security, flooding, and logistical constraints—requiring a closing-date extension [P173315 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a396a8&stream=Yes)—and in the water/services portfolio, Puntland activities remain on hold pending a signed Subsidiary Agreement, while unclear administrative responsibility in Sool/Sanaag is driving reliance on third-party modalities and ongoing clarification needs [P177627 | Implementation Status and Results Report; P177627 | Aide Memoire]. Energy implementation is also experiencing stakeholder-coordination bottlenecks, with delayed electricity service provider merger discussions slowing network investments [P173088 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173088-742a6279-5b21-4709-b718-c401ed1d615b.pdf).  

Governance, fiscal federalism, and core public sector systems (PFM/DRM, intergovernmental transfers, and statistics) are being delivered in an environment of high political polarization, federal–state confrontation, and insecurity that undermines coordination, decision-making, and accountability. PADs emphasize that fragmented public finance systems across FGS/FMS can slow harmonization, that election-related turnover can exacerbate fiduciary risks, and that insecurity constrains access for implementation and verification—necessitating heavy reliance on third-party monitoring [P177298 | PAD; P173731 | PAD]. Implementation results show these risks are binding: elections are already delaying approval of harmonized tax reforms [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf), key fiscal policy decisions stalled because the FMFF was not convened in 2025 [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf), and transfers to Jubaland State have been withheld for two years [P173731 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf); in Somaliland, parliamentary/budget processes are delaying publication of audited financial statements and citizen budget outputs, with a persistent backlog of audit recommendations weakening accountability traction [P177298 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177298-c0e08772-796a-485b-90c6-334b7bf77f30.pdf). Even foundational data collection reflects FCV constraints, requiring dedicated security arrangements for enumerators during fieldwork [P171160 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171160-7b3abb42-b599-42e9-9f1f-f13c1d093706.pdf), consistent with PAD expectations about restricted access and safety-driven survey design [P171160 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087b0a0f9&stream=Yes).  

Jobs and economic transformation (fisheries and digital economy/financial inclusion enablers) is exposed to the same FCV drivers—al‑Shabaab threat, displacement, and federal fragmentation—that can deter private investment, reduce inclusion, and stall sector governance reforms. Fisheries PADs warn that weak demarcation of FGS–FMS roles and political pressure may limit FMS participation, while insecurity can make some locations inaccessible and elevate GBV/SEA/SH and inclusion risks among displaced and vulnerable groups engaged in value chains [P178032 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-08b97878-d848-4270-8d6d-e665f996841d.pdf). Digital economy and financial inclusion interventions similarly anticipate that fragile security conditions and unclear mandates across institutions can delay decision-making, raise logistics and duty-of-care costs, and that political instability can slow digital ID rollout and related reforms needed for service access [P168115 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086a0284d&stream=Yes). These governance risks are already visible in sector coordination: Puntland has not yet been able to participate in Fisheries Management and Development Council meetings, constraining inclusive federal–state coordination for sector policy and implementation [P178032 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178032-1b6ba979-3fa4-456a-8f0a-e5136d58776f.pdf).
