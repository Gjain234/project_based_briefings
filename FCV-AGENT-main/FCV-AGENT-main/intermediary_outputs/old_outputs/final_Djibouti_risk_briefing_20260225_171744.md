# FCV Portfolio Briefing: Djibouti

## Sectarian Violence and Social Cohesion Risks

Escalating sectarian tensions between Issa and Afar communities have shifted from peripheral military confrontation to organized violence in Djibouti City, with coordinated attacks on neighborhoods and destruction of homes. This represents a fundamental threat to social cohesion and creates direct implementation vulnerabilities across the portfolio. Projects targeting vulnerable populations—including cash transfers, community-driven infrastructure, and livelihood support—face heightened risk of disruption through social conflict, elite capture in beneficiary selection, and disputes over resource allocation that can exacerbate local tensions. [P506969 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf) The risk is not merely forward-looking: social tensions are already constraining project activities in target areas where community-driven processes depend on inclusive participation and trust. [P506969 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf) Additionally, projects anchored in Djibouti City itself—including central coordination functions and media-based sensitization—face direct operational disruption if violence constrains mobility or communications in the capital. [P178836 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf) The portfolio's ability to deliver benefits equitably and prevent social conflict through targeting mechanisms remains substantially at risk in this environment.

## Political Instability and Electoral Fragility

The April 2026 elections are occurring amid unprecedented domestic instability, compounded by a constitutional revision in October 2025 that removed presidential age limits and eliminated referendum requirements for constitutional adoption. This political environment creates acute implementation risks across multiple projects. Government reform momentum and inter-ministerial coordination—critical for projects spanning public financial management, governance, and sector reforms—are vulnerable to cabinet reshuffles and shifting political priorities during the electoral period. [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf) Projects dependent on sustained high-level political support for demanding reforms face heightened risk of deprioritization or reversal. [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf) The electoral period also threatens cross-border collaboration: activities requiring functional coordination with Ethiopia customs authorities are explicitly conditioned on satisfactory mutual understanding, which could be disrupted by regional instability or political distraction. [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes) Realized evidence confirms these risks are materializing: the non-adoption of the Code du Numérique (the foundational telecom regulatory framework) as of October 2025 reflects governance bottlenecks plausibly linked to heightened political focus on constitutional matters and electoral positioning. [P174461 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-9e33bd55-6107-428e-bde8-2dc02b8b55cf.pdf) Implementation timelines for emergency food stabilization, housing finance, and trade facilitation are all explicitly conditioned on the absence of major political crises; the current trajectory suggests this assumption is increasingly fragile.

## Institutional Erosion and Governance Constraints

Persistent erosion of democratic institutions, constrained civic space, and weakened checks and balances create a third-order fragility risk affecting implementation across the portfolio. The fiduciary environment is weak, with limited participation from non-state oversight actors and constrained capacity in supreme audit institutions. [P171777 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes) Projects relying on transparent beneficiary selection, citizen feedback mechanisms, and participatory governance—including community-driven investments, social registries, and policy consultations—face disruption when civic space is restricted and accountability mechanisms are underfunded. [P506969 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf) [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf) Realized implementation evidence shows this is not theoretical: a non-operational project steering committee reflects weak inter-ministerial coordination capacity, [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf) and delayed operationalization of PFM reform governance structures points to persistent institutional coordination failures. [P180696 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf) Additionally, the project steering committee for a major social protection operation has not been established despite being contractually required, indicating that governance coordination mechanisms are not functioning reliably. [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf) The portfolio also faces specific vulnerabilities from line ministry interference in project autonomy and from constrained space for civil society partnerships needed to operationalize safeguards (such as gender-based violence prevention) and reach marginalized beneficiaries including refugees. [P506969 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf) [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf) Without functional governance structures and meaningful participation channels, projects cannot effectively manage targeting, accountability, and social cohesion objectives in an increasingly fragile political context.
