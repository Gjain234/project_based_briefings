**Risk Overview:** Deep ethnic and political divisions persist in Sierra Leone despite a veneer of national cohesion, evident during the 2023 contested elections, which required a national unity agreement to defuse tensions between the government and the opposition All People's Congress (APC). ([ISS Africa](https://issafrica.org/iss-today/political-agreements-alone-won-t-heal-sierra-leone-s-social-divide)) Since the Agreement for National Unity in 2024, the country has experienced a decrease in tensions, marked by reductions in inflammatory rhetoric and political violence. ([Afrobarometer](https://www.afrobarometer.org/publication/ad1029-large-majorities-of-sierra-leoneans-favour-cross-party-collaboration-over-divisive-politics/)) However, the executive wields significant control over state institutions despite constitutional provisions for the separation of powers, with presidential appointments to key government positions concentrating power in the executive and turning public institutions into vehicles for patronage. ([ISS Africa](https://issafrica.org/iss-today/political-agreements-alone-won-t-heal-sierra-leone-s-social-divide)) Regionally, the Sahel accounted for 51 percent of global terrorism-related deaths in 2024, up from 48 percent in 2023. ([Security Council Report](https://www.securitycouncilreport.org/whatsinblue/2025/07/informal-interactive-dialogue-on-enhancing-regional-counter-terrorism-cooperation-in-west-africa-and-the-sahel.php)) Terrorist activity is increasing in coastal West Africa, with the increase in terrorist attacks in Benin and Togo raising alarm about potential spillover into previously unaffected regions. ([Security Council Report](https://www.securitycouncilreport.org/whatsinblue/2025/07/informal-interactive-dialogue-on-enhancing-regional-counter-terrorism-cooperation-in-west-africa-and-the-sahel.php)) In February 2026, Guinean military invaded Sierra Leone territory, attacking border guards with knives and wounding personnel, after intensification of a dispute over control of a border point. ([Pravda Guinea](https://guinea.news-pravda.com/en/world/2026/02/24/1649.html)) ([Pravda Guinea](https://guinea.news-pravda.com/en/world/2026/02/24/1649.html))

**Institutional fragility, political tensions, and social unrest:** June 2023 general elections saw President Julius Maada Bio reelected for a second term and his Sierra Leone People's Party (SLPP) win a majority of parliamentary seats, but the election outcomes were rejected by the opposition APC, with domestic and international observers raising concerns about the credibility of the official results. ([Freedom House](https://freedomhouse.org/country/sierra-leone/freedom-world/2024)) In January 2025, former President Ernest Bai Koroma was charged with treason for his alleged role in an unsuccessful November 2023 coup d'état, but was released later that month to seek medical treatment in Nigeria under an ECOWAS-brokered agreement. ([Freedom House](https://freedomhouse.org/country/sierra-leone/freedom-world/2025)) Institute for Security Studies interviews with political and civil society actors in June 2024 highlighted concerns over the politicisation of public appointments and deployment of state institutions against political opponents. ([ISS Africa](https://issafrica.org/iss-today/political-agreements-alone-won-t-heal-sierra-leone-s-social-divide)) The Anti-Corruption Commission sought to collect legally required asset declarations from all public officials, but the content of the declarations remained confidential and not subject to public scrutiny. ([Freedom House](https://freedomhouse.org/country/sierra-leone/freedom-world/2025)) Civil society organizations are incentivized through state contracts and board appointments to remain silent on state injustices and corruption, while organizations critical of the government are regularly challenged as acting as agents of political opposition. ([International IDEA](https://www.idea.int/publications/catalogue/html/state-democracy-sierra-leone-assessment-2025-report)) ([International IDEA](https://www.idea.int/publications/catalogue/html/state-democracy-sierra-leone-assessment-2025-report))

**Conflict and violence dynamics:** Sierra Leone, along with several other UN Security Council members, has emphasized the need for the Council to pay close attention to the West Africa and Sahel region, including exploring ways to support regional efforts to counter terrorism and violent extremism. ([Security Council Report](https://www.securitycouncilreport.org/monthly-forecast/2025-11/west-africa-and-the-sahel-15.php)) ([Security Council Report](https://www.securitycouncilreport.org/monthly-forecast/2025-11/west-africa-and-the-sahel-15.php)) Beyond the Sahel, terrorist activity is increasing in coastal West Africa, with militant groups having expanded financial and logistical networks that now stretch towards the Gulf of Guinea and facilitated transnational organized crime. ([Security Council Report](https://www.securitycouncilreport.org/whatsinblue/2025/07/informal-interactive-dialogue-on-enhancing-regional-counter-terrorism-cooperation-in-west-africa-and-the-sahel.php)) ([Security Council Report](https://www.securitycouncilreport.org/whatsinblue/2025/07/informal-interactive-dialogue-on-enhancing-regional-counter-terrorism-cooperation-in-west-africa-and-the-sahel.php)) Sierra Leone has four dominant forms of political violence: communal violence; violence targeting civilians by state security forces; riots and protests over national issues; and violence accompanying political competition, including violence around national elections and inter- and intra-party violence. ([ACLED](https://acleddata.com/2020/12/16/when-emerging-democracies-breed-violence-sierra-leone-20-years-after-the-civil-war/)) Demonstrators and opposition parties have faced police violence and restrictions on assembly. ([Freedom House](https://freedomhouse.org/country/sierra-leone/freedom-world/2025))

**Displacement and refugee dynamics:** As of 30 June 2025, a total of 20,575 forcibly displaced persons were registered in the Multi-Country Office in Senegal covering Sierra Leone and five other countries. ([UNHCR](https://data.unhcr.org/en/country/sle)) Sierra Leone's asylum system has faced challenges including a lack of resources, limited infrastructure and the need to strengthen institutional capacities, and the country has been identified as a transit point for people on their way to North Africa or Europe. ([UNHCR](https://www.unhcr.org/where-we-work/countries/sierra-leone)) Sierra Leone faces challenges in encouraging refugees to opt for durable solutions such as local integration or voluntary repatriation, with many refugees remaining hesitant to take up these options. ([Permanent Mission Of Sierra Leone](https://missionsierraleone.ch/236-post-sierra-leone-strengthens-commitment-to-refugee-rights-amp-protection-407))

**Sensitivities between government and development actors:** The World Bank's Country Partnership Framework focuses on sustainable growth and accountable governance, human capital acceleration, and economic diversification, with the Sierra Leone portfolio comprising 18 projects totaling almost $1 billion. ([World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/sierraleone/overview)) ([World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/sierraleone/overview)) The government proposes amendments to the Refugee Protection Act of 2007 to bring legislation in line with international standards, creating a more robust legal framework for refugee protection and establishing a roadmap for the sustainable phase-out of refugee programs. ([Permanent Mission Of Sierra Leone](https://missionsierraleone.ch/236-post-sierra-leone-strengthens-commitment-to-refugee-rights-amp-protection-407)) The UN High Commissioner for Refugees commended the government of Sierra Leone for safeguarding the fundamental human rights of refugees through the implementation of national and international laws and programs. ([Permanent Mission Of Sierra Leone](https://missionsierraleone.ch/236-post-sierra-leone-strengthens-commitment-to-refugee-rights-amp-protection-407))
