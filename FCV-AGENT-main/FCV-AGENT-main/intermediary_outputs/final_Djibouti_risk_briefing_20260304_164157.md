# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

**Paragraph 1: Democratic Backsliding and Executive Power Consolidation**

Djibouti's political landscape is defined by deepening authoritarian consolidation. In October 2025, the government removed the presidential age limit through constitutional revision, enabling President Guelleh—in power since 1999—to seek a sixth term in April 2026. This democratic backsliding is compounded by the near-total collapse of independent civil society and media freedom: Freedom House classifies Djibouti as "Not Free," journalists and activists face regular harassment and arrest, and virtually no independent oversight mechanisms remain. These conditions create a structurally hostile environment for development project governance. Across the portfolio, projects relying on government-led steering committees, multi-ministry coordination, and civil society engagement face acute vulnerability. The Public Financial Management Modernization Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a> explicitly identifies political risk as "High," noting that vested interests could delay reforms and that mitigation depends on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that weakens as executive power concentrates. The TVET and Skills Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> similarly relies on a high-level steering committee under the Economic Development Council without independent oversight or civil society safeguards. These risks are materializing. Across multiple projects, ISR and Aide Memoire evidence documents inactive steering committees <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>, delayed publication of governance decrees <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>, stalled regulatory board appointments at the telecommunications regulator since 2022 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>, and persistent "High" or "Substantial" political and governance risk ratings [P180696 | ISR; P174300 | ISR; P174461 | ISR]. The absence of independent civil society is directly enabling these failures: project-level grievance mechanisms, M&E systems, and stakeholder engagement plans are either non-functional or structurally compromised by the restricted civic space [P175483 | Aide Memoire; P178988 | Aide Memoire; P178332 | Aide Memoire]. Looking forward, the April 2026 election cycle and succession uncertainty surrounding the president's health [P178033 | ISR; P174300 | ISR] introduce additional institutional unpredictability that could further divert government attention from development commitments and disrupt ministerial continuity across the portfolio.

**Paragraph 2: Severe Youth Unemployment, Economic Inequality, and Structural Labor Market Fragility**

Youth unemployment in Djibouti exceeds 75%, compounded by endemic corruption, high public debt, and limited private sector absorptive capacity. These structural economic conditions create a dual risk for the portfolio: they undermine the demand-side viability of employment-oriented project components while simultaneously constraining the institutional capacity and human capital available for implementation. The TVET and Skills Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> is acutely exposed—it targets 23,775 trained graduates across diploma, short-term, and work-based pathways, yet the PAD acknowledges "macroeconomic uncertainties" without conducting a labor market demand assessment or documenting private sector absorption capacity. The bilateral labor mobility agreements intended to create Gulf employment pathways remain entirely unimplemented <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>, and the online gig work component has stalled due to technical and coordination failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>—suggesting the project underestimated the severity of structural economic barriers. The Djibouti-Ethiopia Corridor Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> similarly lacks youth employment targets or skills training provisions despite identifying road transport and trade beneficiaries. These risks are actively materializing. Refugee employment outcomes in the TVET project remain stuck at 5% against targets <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>, and the Social Safety Net Project documents persistent difficulty identifying viable income-generating activities in remote localities due to weak market access and limited economic diversification [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire]. Rural savings groups (CREC) face governance and financial management capacity constraints that undermine their function as economic inclusion mechanisms for marginalized youth [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire]. The Food Security Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a> has achieved minimal financial execution, with critical shortfalls in food stock acquisition and zero achievement on water infrastructure, directly undermining livelihood resilience for vulnerable households. The GBV Prevention Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-4908093d-8bfd-4f36-a29c-d9575d229601.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a> documents that high economic inequality and limited resources correlate with elevated GBV risk while simultaneously constraining the institutional capacity to respond—a compounding dynamic that the portfolio has not adequately addressed.

**Paragraph 3: Ethnic Tensions, Political Exclusion, and Risks to Inclusive Implementation**

Djibouti's political economy is structurally shaped by ethnic imbalance: the Issa Somali group (approximately 60% of the population) dominates governance, while the Afar minority (approximately 35%) remains politically marginalized. This structural exclusion creates persistent risks of elite capture, inequitable benefit distribution, and community resistance across projects operating in ethnically mixed or Afar-proximate regions. Multiple PADs acknowledge this risk in general terms but fail to operationalize ethnic safeguards. The TVET Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> notes "political and governance challenges" as substantial but provides no ethnic representation safeguards in its steering committee or beneficiary selection processes. The Agri-Food and Rural Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a> operates across three geographically dispersed zones without ethnic composition analysis or inclusivity criteria in its Sub-Project Selection Committee. The Education Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a> targets inclusive enrollment across five regions but does not address how ethnic power imbalances may affect resource allocation to minority communities. The Corridor Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> lists "populations living in corridor area" as beneficiaries without disaggregation by ethnicity or targeted inclusion mechanisms for the Afar minority. These risks are beginning to materialize in implementation. ISR evidence from the Corridor Project documents persistent "Substantial" stakeholder engagement risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>, which may reflect underlying ethnic tensions and trust deficits with marginalized communities. The Social Safety Net Project documents opaque youth training program selection without pre-defined eligibility criteria or accessibility safeguards [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire]—a gap that, in Djibouti's ethnic context, creates direct vulnerability to favoritism along ethnic lines. The Urban Upgrading Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a> documents zero implementation by refugee-led civil society organizations, a pattern consistent with the structural exclusion of non-dominant groups from meaningful participation in development activities. School Management Committees are functional in only 62% of schools <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>, with non-functionality potentially reflecting ethnic power imbalances in local governance structures. Forward-looking, the absence of ethnic-disaggregated monitoring across the portfolio means these dynamics will remain invisible until they generate visible conflict or implementation breakdown.

**Paragraph 4: Insurgent Activity, Regional Security Threats, and Operational Disruption**

Djibouti faces active domestic security threats from the FRUD-armé insurgent group, which conducted a raid on an army post in October 2023 killing seven soldiers, as well as proximity to al-Shabaab and other terrorist organizations operating in Somalia. Simultaneously, the country is navigating an escalating diplomatic crisis with Somaliland—marked by ambassador recalls and Djibouti's military alignment with Mogadishu—that threatens cross-border operational stability. Eight foreign military powers maintain compressed presence in Djibouti, creating a hyper-congested strategic environment with deconfliction risks. These security dynamics create direct operational exposure for projects with field operations in remote or border-proximate areas. The TVET Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> operates in Ali Sabieh and Obock regions, where 3,165 refugee beneficiaries are concentrated in camps geographically proximate to documented insurgent activity, yet security risk is assessed as only "moderate" with mitigation limited to "institutional protocols" and "coordination with security agencies" without FRUD-armé-specific threat assessment. The Corridor Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> acknowledges "security/fragility (substantial)" risk from regional instability but relies solely on "regular security risk assessments" without specific protocols for insurgent threats to road rehabilitation sites or border operations at Guelileh. The Statistics Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a> explicitly identifies security risks for census field teams and acknowledges the project operates in conflict-affected areas, yet assesses Djibouti as "politically stable"—an assessment that appears to underweight the demonstrated insurgent threat. These risks are partially materializing. The Statistics Project Aide Memoire documents security concerns in refugee settlements as a concrete implementation barrier for field enumeration teams <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Corridor Project documents coordination failures between implementing agencies and contractors <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the ISR records persistent "Substantial" political and governance risk driven partly by regional fragility <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis adds a forward-looking dimension: the Corridor Project's cross-border coordination with Ethiopia at the Guelileh border post <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> and the Food Security Project's reliance on stable regional trade routes <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a> are both exposed to escalation scenarios that the PADs do not adequately assess.

**Paragraph 5: Refugee and Displacement Pressures Straining Service Delivery and Project Implementation**

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers, primarily from Somalia, Ethiopia, and Yemen—with 85% residing in refugee villages, alongside an estimated 150,000 non-registered migrants. This hosting burden, maintained through an open-door policy despite recurrent droughts and severely limited public resources, creates systemic strain on public services and institutional capacity that directly affects portfolio implementation. Multiple projects explicitly target refugee populations as beneficiaries, creating a structural dependency on a fragile hosting environment. The TVET Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> targets 3,165 refugees for skills training and sets a 220-refugee enrollment target, while the Emergency Resilience Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a> serves 32,000 displaced persons through agencies (SEGRC and MEFI) identified as having "limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff." The Food Security Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a> designates emergency food stocks for refugees without specifying allocation protocols between refugee villages and host communities. The Urban Upgrading Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a> targets 115,000 slum inhabitants including 10–12% non-Djiboutian populations. These risks are extensively materializing across the portfolio. Refugee TVET enrollment remains at zero <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>, refugee employment outcomes are stuck at 5% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>, and the online gig work component targeting refugees has stalled entirely <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. Refugee student enrollment in the Education Project has declined from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>, and refugee schools remain unintegrated into the national education management information system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The Food Security Project has achieved critical shortfalls in food stock acquisition and zero progress on water infrastructure rehabilitation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>, directly undermining humanitarian capacity for the displaced population. The June 2025 drought declaration has triggered emergency resource reallocation, forcing the project to shift from preparedness to crisis response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>—compounding the resource strain on an already fragile hosting system. The Urban Upgrading Project documents zero implementation by refugee-led civil society organizations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>, zero recommendations implemented from the 2017 National Refugee Law <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>, and stalled property rights documentation in refugee villages <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The Statistics Project requires boosted sampling of migrant and refugee households in both the national census and household budget survey, reflecting the complexity of data collection in a displacement-affected environment [P171777 | ISR; P171777 | Aide Memoire]. Collectively, these realized risks indicate that the portfolio's refugee-facing components are systematically underperforming, with resource constraints, institutional capacity gaps, and governance fragility converging to prevent meaningful service delivery to one of Djibouti's most vulnerable populations.
