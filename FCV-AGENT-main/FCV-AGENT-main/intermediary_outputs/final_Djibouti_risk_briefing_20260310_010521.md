# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Political Consolidation and Governance Fragility Under Indefinite Presidential Rule

Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms that poses systemic risk across the portfolio. Projects relying on multi-ministerial coordination and sustained political commitment are particularly exposed. The Public Finance Management project depends on a PFM Reform Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance, with mitigation resting on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that becomes fragile when political attention shifts toward consolidating executive power and managing succession dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The TVET and skills development project similarly relies on a high-level steering committee under the Economic Development Council, but the PAD does not address how to insulate project governance from executive pressure or ensure institutional autonomy in an increasingly centralized political environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project explicitly identifies that "ability to influence private sector finance mobilization depends on political will" and that "line ministries may lack incentives or resist change"—vulnerabilities that intensify as executive concentration weakens inter-ministerial accountability <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing. The PFM project carries a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with ISR evidence linking this directly to the institutional fragility created by constitutional manipulation that removes democratic constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The corridor project similarly maintains a "Substantial" political and governance risk rating in implementation, with ISR evidence confirming that constitutional concentration of presidential power correlates with weakened institutional autonomy across government bodies <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's steering committee has been inactive, with governance coordination between government and development partners stalled—a pattern consistent with executive consolidation superseding institutional development <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the pattern is consistent: formal governance structures exist on paper but lack operational effectiveness in an environment where institutional independence is subordinated to regime stability objectives.

## 2. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Accountability Deficits

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House, with virtually no independent civil society remaining and journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested, creates a structural accountability deficit that undermines project monitoring, grievance mechanisms, and stakeholder engagement across every operation in the portfolio. The PFM project's Component 3 explicitly targets civil society engagement and oversight of public finances, yet the PAD does not acknowledge that the country context makes meaningful independent civil society participation structurally impossible <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The education project establishes a grievance redress mechanism "for all stakeholders," but in an environment where activists face arrest for criticism, vulnerable groups including refugees, rural students, and girls face severe barriers to voicing concerns without fear of retaliation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. The corridor project includes a Stakeholder Engagement Plan and project-level grievance mechanisms, but does not address how these will function when independent civil society cannot monitor or amplify community concerns <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing across the portfolio. The corridor project has documented systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards affecting 150 daily workers—violations enabled precisely because workers lack recourse to independent unions, labor advocates, or media scrutiny <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The same project has recorded critical construction site safety violations, with government monitoring reports omitting non-conformities—a direct consequence of the absence of independent oversight mechanisms <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's internal audit function has been compromised, with the organizational independence necessary for objective accountability absent <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The food security project's complaint management mechanism remains incomplete, with the same governance constraints that eliminated independent civil society also impeding the creation of trustworthy grievance channels <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The GBV prevention project has failed to establish accounting systems at project launch and faces delayed financial reporting, with limited external accountability enabling these fundamental gaps to persist unchecked <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. The digital connectivity project carries a persistent "High" stakeholder engagement risk, with the severely restricted civic space creating institutional pressure and self-censorship that impedes transparent collaboration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>. Across the portfolio, the collapse of independent civil society is not a background condition—it is an active implementation constraint with measurable consequences for fiduciary integrity, safeguards compliance, and beneficiary protection.

## 3. Severe Youth Unemployment, Economic Inequality, and Structural Labor Market Constraints

Youth unemployment exceeding 75%, compounded by endemic corruption, high inequality, and limited economic diversification, creates both demand-side and supply-side constraints that undermine project effectiveness across skills development, livelihoods, and infrastructure operations. The TVET project targets 23,775 trained graduates across diploma, short-term, and work-based pathways, but the PAD acknowledges macroeconomic risk as "substantial" without conducting a labor market demand assessment or documenting private sector absorption capacity—a critical gap given that training cohorts may have no viable employment pathways <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food project's economic opportunity component allocates US$14 million to income-generating activities, but identifies macroeconomic risk as substantial without addressing how structural economic conditions beyond project scope will constrain meaningful job creation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – PAD</a>. The corridor project lists road transport service providers and local traders as beneficiaries but provides no youth employment targets, skills training provisions, or analysis of how corridor improvements translate to jobs for the 75%+ unemployed youth population <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing with measurable impact. The TVET project's refugee employment outcome indicator is stuck at 5% against an 80% target, with ISR evidence confirming that the labor market lacks sufficient formal sector jobs for either citizens or refugees—making the employment PDO unachievable within the current economic context <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The online gig work component has stalled entirely, with ISR evidence indicating that supply-side training interventions cannot overcome demand-side constraints including limited digital infrastructure and lack of formal employment pathways <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The social protection project has been unable to identify viable income-generating activities in remote localities, with Aide Memoire evidence confirming that weak market access and limited economic diversification are systemic issues extending to the project's implementation areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The food security project's drought-driven reallocation of resources has exacerbated economic inequality and food access disparities, with ISR evidence noting that emergency prioritization reflects the country's limited fiscal space to address multiple concurrent crises <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>. The corridor project has documented exploitation of 150 daily workers through non-compliant contracts and lack of social security registration—directly enabled by the severe youth unemployment context where workers facing 75%+ joblessness have minimal bargaining power <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 4. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Under Acute Resource Constraints

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers, with 85% concentrated in refugee villages—alongside 150,000 non-registered migrants, placing acute strain on already fragile public services and institutional capacity. This hosting burden intersects with virtually every project in the portfolio, creating compounded vulnerabilities for both refugee beneficiaries and host community populations. The TVET project directly targets 3,165 refugees concentrated in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps in Ali Sabieh region, but does not address sustainability of training delivery if humanitarian conditions deteriorate or if competing priorities—food security, shelter—undermine training participation and completion rates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The emergency preparedness project targets the full refugee population but identifies implementing institutions SEGRC and MEFI as having "limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff," creating a structural mismatch between the scale of refugee hosting and institutional delivery capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The education project directly targets 29,214 refugees across three villages for integration into the national education system, but acknowledges that external shocks including recurrent droughts could disrupt refugee education programming <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing with documented severity. The health project shows refugee immunization rates at 50% against an 80% target, with ISR evidence confirming that the persistent gap reflects systemic service delivery challenges inherent to hosting large refugee populations amid constrained infrastructure <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. Vitamin A supplementation coverage among refugee children has declined from 10% to 5%, directly reflecting resource constraints and deteriorating health service utilization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The TVET project has enrolled zero refugees against a target of 220, with ISR evidence confirming that resource constraints and institutional capacity limitations prevent operationalization of refugee training programs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The education project shows declining refugee student enrollment from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025, with ISR evidence linking this to systemic resource scarcity undermining basic service provision <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The slum upgrading project has stalled on refugee village restructuring plans in Ali Addeh and Holl Holl, with ISR evidence confirming that administrative bottlenecks are symptomatic of the broader institutional fragility and resource scarcity characterizing the country's refugee management system <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The food security project has achieved only 50% of its emergency food stock target and zero achievement on water infrastructure, directly affecting the 32,000 displaced persons the project is designed to serve <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Ethnic Tensions, Political Exclusion, and Inclusive Implementation Risks

The structural imbalance between Issa Somali dominance (approximately 60% of the population) in governance and the political marginalization of the Afar minority (approximately 35%) creates persistent risks of exclusionary implementation, elite capture, and contested resource allocation across projects operating in ethnically mixed or geographically dispersed areas. The TVET project's inclusive targeting of women, persons with disabilities, and refugees may create implementation friction if ethnic representation in beneficiary selection or institutional governance is perceived as favoring particular groups, yet the PAD does not address how ethnic balance will be maintained in steering committees or implementation units <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food project's geographic targeting across three zones—Northern (Tadjourah/Obock), Southern (Dikhil/Ali Sabieh), and Central (Arta/peri-urban)—does not address the ethnic composition of target areas or mechanisms to ensure equitable benefit distribution across Issa and Afar populations, with the Sub-Project Selection Committee lacking explicit ethnic inclusivity criteria <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The corridor project lists "populations living in corridor area" as beneficiaries but provides no disaggregation by ethnicity or targeted inclusion mechanisms for the Afar minority despite their structural political exclusion <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing in implementation. The corridor project's stakeholder engagement risk has remained at "Substantial" throughout implementation, with ISR evidence confirming that ethnic tensions and political exclusion dynamics create trust deficits and resistance from marginalized populations, resulting in persistent engagement difficulties <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The social protection project has documented opaque youth training program selection without pre-defined eligibility criteria or accessibility safeguards, creating a direct mechanism through which ethnic favoritism could operate—with Aide Memoire evidence confirming that without transparent selection, the program risks reproducing existing ethnic inequities in economic opportunity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The slum upgrading project's refugee-led CSO participation has achieved zero implementation, with ISR evidence noting that refugees—predominantly Somali and Ethiopian—face compounded barriers to organizing as non-citizens from groups that overlap with existing ethnic fault lines <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The education project shows that private Arabic schools—often associated with minority communities—have not adopted the national EMIS system, with ISR evidence suggesting this institutional fragmentation may reflect community-based resistance to centralized government oversight along ethnic lines <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The food security project's water distribution has documented non-compliance across six regions, with Aide Memoire evidence raising the possibility that regional actors may be redirecting resources in ways that reflect underlying ethnic tensions in resource allocation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.
