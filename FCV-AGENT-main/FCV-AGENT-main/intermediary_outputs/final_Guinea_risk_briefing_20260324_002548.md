# Guinea FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Authoritarian Consolidation and Institutional Fragility

Guinea's junta has systematically dismantled democratic institutions since the September 2021 coup, culminating in the March 2026 dissolution of 40 political parties—including the three main opposition groups—by late-night decree, stripping them of legal status and ordering asset confiscation. This follows a December 2025 presidential election in which all major opposition leaders were barred, yielding an 86.72% reported victory for President Doumbouya, and a September 2025 constitutional referendum extending presidential terms and permitting junta members to stand. Legislative elections scheduled for May 24, 2026 will proceed with meaningful competition structurally absent. Across the portfolio, PADs anticipated this trajectory: the energy access project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7b1cb57-9078-490e-8988-30bc9fe608c0.pdf" target="_blank">P511453 – PAD</a>, emergency response project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509503-9e19c665-633a-4b5d-9057-1897309bb41a.pdf" target="_blank">P509503 – PAD</a>, water project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a>, fisheries project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ff8aaae6-4241-49f6-b294-04cfff550aaf.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – PAD</a>, local governance project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-dbb6f3f7-06bb-4d73-a6df-8c71eb7f7711.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – PAD</a>, mining governance project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08859ec01&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168613 – PAD</a>, social protection project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08852e437&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168777 – PAD</a>, and agribusiness project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087d59368&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – PAD</a> all identify high political and governance risk, with mitigation strategies relying on "continued high-level policy dialogue" and "transitional government assurances"—mechanisms that become less credible as the government eliminates institutional checks. These risks are now materializing concretely. The local governance project documents that municipal councils were dissolved and replaced with appointed special delegations, directly disrupting project implementation and cascading into delayed planning cycles and frozen commune accounts since 2022 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-0d85cb88-e8fe-4bb3-b9b1-81c8c0178a90.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – ISR</a>. The mining governance project records inter-ministerial coordination failures between the Mines and Infrastructure ministries on Simandou rail and port oversight, and persistent delays in critical regulatory reforms including the Protected Area Code and Water Code <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-8608b6ee-d8be-4d24-a9b0-3883596421e7.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – ISR</a>. The agribusiness project carries a persistent high political and governance risk rating, with contractor non-performance in the Mambia region and technical support firm demobilization without replacement planning <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08872333c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – ISR</a>. The fisheries project similarly rates political and governance fragility as high, with a newly established implementation team operating in a governance environment where institutional autonomy is eroding <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180906-af1ea935-d30a-4433-ab02-ba43568d4457.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – ISR</a>. The social protection project documents escalating fiduciary risk and a high governance risk rating, with the fuel depot explosion in Conakry triggering CERC activation and a financing gap requiring additional mobilization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168777-23f25466-2716-4d90-b731-347460542b3c.pdf" target="_blank">P168777 – ISR</a>. Forward-looking, the May 2026 legislative elections—occurring with all major opposition dissolved—will further entrench the party-state model, reducing the political space for sector dialogue, reform advocacy, and the performance-based conditions totaling US$60 million in the energy project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7b1cb57-9078-490e-8988-30bc9fe608c0.pdf" target="_blank">P511453 – PAD</a> and the decentralization commitments underpinning the local governance project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-dbb6f3f7-06bb-4d73-a6df-8c71eb7f7711.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – PAD</a>.

## 2. Security Force Violence, Protest Suppression, and Civic Space Closure

Guinea's junta has maintained a ban on public protests since May 2022, and security forces have responded to violations with live ammunition, killing at least 59 protesters and bystanders since 2022—including at least 20 in 2024 and at least eight people including three children during Conakry protests documented by Human Rights Watch. Simultaneously, enforced disappearances have been used as a systematic tool of repression: prominent FNDC activists Oumar Sylla and Mamadou Billo Bah have been missing since July 2024, civil society leader Abdoul Sacko was abducted and tortured in February 2025, and investigative journalist Habib Marouane Camara disappeared in December 2024. Amnesty International and 25 organizations documented a "climate of terror" with lawyers and politicians in hiding. These conditions create compounding implementation risks across the portfolio. The energy project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7b1cb57-9078-490e-8988-30bc9fe608c0.pdf" target="_blank">P511453 – PAD</a> targets peri-urban Conakry and 26 rural localities, with resettlement action plans budgeting US$5.5 million for compensation—previous GNEAP-1 had Unsatisfactory E&S ratings due to RAP delays, and in a context where protests are banned and security forces use lethal force, community grievances over displacement or tariff increases could escalate into confrontations. The water project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a> similarly budgets US$13 million for resettlement compensation in Greater Conakry, with PAD lessons noting the "importance of timely compensation to avoid delays"—delayed compensation in this security environment risks triggering protests met with violent crackdowns. The local governance project's conflict early warning system (SAPR) covers only 169 of 337 communes, leaving blind spots precisely where protest activity and security force responses are most likely <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>. The fisheries project documents high political and governance fragility, with the project's reliance on security forces for marine protected area patrols creating direct exposure to documented patterns of excessive force <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180906-af1ea935-d30a-4433-ab02-ba43568d4457.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – ISR</a>. The mining governance project identifies that weak institutionalized channels for citizen input on mining infrastructure, combined with the protest ban, creates a structural vulnerability where affected communities lack legitimate avenues for grievance expression—increasing the likelihood that concerns manifest as informal protests triggering security force violence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-8608b6ee-d8be-4d24-a9b0-3883596421e7.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – ISR</a>. The health project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-aa26d346-cc66-4708-a538-60e0b30cdda4.pdf" target="_blank">P506072 – PAD</a> deploys 300 community health workers and 2,700 community relays across seven regions, and the PAD's moderate political risk rating predates the March 2026 party dissolutions and does not reflect the current elevated risk of violent crackdowns as the junta entrenches power ahead of May 2026 elections. The social protection project's high governance risk rating explicitly reflects the security force violence and protest ban environment, with implementation in Conakry requiring beneficiary identification and payment point access in areas where lethal force has been documented <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168777-23f25466-2716-4d90-b731-347460542b3c.pdf" target="_blank">P168777 – ISR</a>.

## 3. Simandou Mining Boom: Revenue Management Failure and Social Disruption

Guinea shipped its first iron ore from the $20 billion Simandou mega-project in December 2025, with the IMF projecting a 26% GDP lift by 2030. However, employment peaked at over 60,000 construction jobs in 2024–2025 and will fall to under 15,000 operational workers, creating a mass layoff dynamic threatening significant social disruption in mining communities. The World Bank's July 2025 Guinea Economic Update warned of a "narrow window to ensure benefits of growth are widely shared," noting that more than half the population lives in poverty despite Guinea being the world's top bauxite exporter. Guinea holds only a 15% stake in Simandou, with Chinese interests dominant, and the IMF has called for windfall revenues to be channeled into education and infrastructure. The portfolio's exposure to this risk is both direct and severe. The mining governance project's Environmental Management Agency (AGEE) lacks sustainable financing for independent monitoring missions of Simandou operations, creating a governance gap where environmental safeguards lack institutional capacity precisely as the $20 billion project operationalizes <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>. Resettlement and compensation frameworks remain non-operationalized, with Resettlement Action Plan procedures unfinalized, leaving displaced mining communities without procedural protections during the critical employment transition <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>. Institutional capacity for revenue collection is weak, with diagnostic work revealing poor inter-agency coordination across customs, tax, and central bank authorities—directly undermining the government's ability to capture and redistribute Simandou revenues for poverty reduction <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>. Environmental and social safeguard implementation stands at less than 20%, and the E&S assessment database was not modernized by the June 2025 target <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-8608b6ee-d8be-4d24-a9b0-3883596421e7.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – ISR</a>. The agribusiness project explicitly targets Boké and Kindia prefectures—mining regions where the PAD identifies "social tensions due to mining activities and lack of inclusive development opportunities"—and the project's capacity to absorb 24,000 jobs will be overwhelmed by the tens of thousands of Simandou construction workers facing layoffs <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087d59368&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – PAD</a>. The local governance project's suspended recruitment of Local Development Planning Service Chiefs (CSPDL since April 2024) directly undermines local planning capacity at the moment when Guinea faces massive employment transitions, with temporary community agents substituting for permanent civil service positions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>. The water project identifies "incomplete information on Simandou iron operations revenue" as a macroeconomic risk, with challenges in revenue mobilization from mining companies potentially undermining project financial sustainability and tariff affordability for poor households <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a>.

## 4. Chronic Food Insecurity and Malnutrition Among Vulnerable Populations

Guinea is among 31 African countries requiring external food assistance as of the FAO March 2026 assessment. The Cadre Harmonisé October–December 2025 analysis classified Guinea among countries contributing to 1.4 million people in Emergency (Phase 4) food insecurity in West Africa, with FAO warning the regional figure could rise to 52.8 million during the June–August 2026 lean season. WFP data indicates over 21.8% of Guinea's households struggle with food insecurity, with 6.1% of children under five suffering global acute malnutrition and 24.4% stunted; more than half the population experiences poverty. Guinea remains heavily dependent on rice imports representing 40% of all food imports, exposing it to global price shocks. The emergency response project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509503-9e19c665-633a-4b5d-9057-1897309bb41a.pdf" target="_blank">P509503 – PAD</a> is designed to address food insecurity affecting 17% of the population, targeting 225,000 households with food assistance, 75,000 with agricultural inputs, and 300,000 with cash transfers—but the scale of the crisis, with Guinea among countries requiring external assistance and facing a projected lean season surge, may overwhelm project capacity and create social tensions over aid distribution. The health project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-aa26d346-cc66-4708-a538-60e0b30cdda4.pdf" target="_blank">P506072 – PAD</a> identifies chronic food insecurity as a fragility driver and includes nutrition and WASH support groups, but the PAD's mitigation strategy does not address how the project will function during the projected June–August 2026 lean season; the assumption that free health care will stimulate demand for services may not hold if households lack food security to support pregnant women's nutrition or child health outcomes. The fisheries project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ff8aaae6-4241-49f6-b294-04cfff550aaf.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – PAD</a> targets 65,000 people including small-scale fishers, women, youth, and processors—among Guinea's most food-insecure groups—and acute food insecurity could undermine community participation and reduce beneficiary engagement in value chain development. The social protection project's emergency cash transfer of 250,000 GNF (~$26 USD) per month may prove insufficient if food prices spike during the lean season, and the project covers only 150,000 of an estimated 840,000 eligible households <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08852e437&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168777 – PAD</a>. The agribusiness project's 145,000 target farmers and rural households face the risk that deepening food insecurity during the June–August 2026 lean season will force beneficiaries to consume project inputs for immediate survival rather than invest in commercial agriculture <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087d59368&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – PAD</a>. The mining governance project's artisanal mining expansion—63 tonnes in 2022—reflects deepening poverty driving people into informal extraction as a survival strategy, with artisanal mining serving as a critical informal safety net for food-insecure populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Development Partner Tensions: Governance Conditionalities Versus Mining Engagement

The governance environment—including the dissolution of political parties, protest bans, enforced disappearances, and media suppression (six major private outlets banned since May 2024, RSF documenting a "profound media vacuum" and Guinea dropping 25 places in the 2025 Press Freedom Index)—creates fundamental friction between multilateral development partners committed to governance conditionalities and the Doumbouya government, which is increasingly aligned with Chinese and Russian investors who operate without political conditions. The Doumbouya junta has facilitated Russian arms shipments to Sahelian juntas, and Russia maintains significant bauxite mining interests. USAID-funded democratic governance support runs through November 2026 in a context severely constrained by the political trajectory. This tension is materializing across the portfolio in concrete implementation failures. The local governance project documents that frozen commune accounts since 2022, dissolved municipal councils, suspended local public service reform, and delayed deployment of municipal treasurers and public accountants collectively reflect a state apparatus being repurposed for political control rather than functional governance—with treasury approval delays blocking 45% of FNDL fund transfers to communes [P177095 | Implementation Status and Results Report; Aide Memoire]. The mining governance project records that inter-ministerial divergence on mining infrastructure oversight, weak E&S governance implementation at less than 20%, and inadequate AGEE financing for Simandou monitoring all exemplify the governance fragility that development partners have flagged as requiring reform—while the government's alignment with Chinese interests in the Simandou arrangement (Guinea holding only 15%) signals reduced incentives to strengthen institutional oversight [P168613 | Implementation Status and Results Report; Aide Memoire]. The energy project's four performance-based conditions totaling US$60 million linked to governance reforms and tariff restructuring face the risk that the government deprioritizes these in favor of mining-related priorities or resists World Bank conditionalities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7b1cb57-9078-490e-8988-30bc9fe608c0.pdf" target="_blank">P511453 – PAD</a>. The agribusiness project documents UN agency implementation delays (FAO, WFP) in agricultural value chains, with governance deterioration—including the climate of terror from enforced disappearances and media suppression—disrupting local partner networks and community engagement channels essential for multi-stakeholder agricultural programming <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08872333c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – ISR</a>. The water project's pipeline Development Policy Financing with prior actions for water sector reforms depends on "constant dialogue with authorities" that may be strained if the government perceives governance conditionalities as interference <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a>. The health project's PAD rates political risk as only "moderate" and relies on "strong political commitment evidenced by health sector budget increases"—an assessment that predates the March 2026 political consolidation and does not account for the risk that the government's deepening alignment with Russia could create pressure to reduce World Bank influence over project implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-aa26d346-cc66-4708-a538-60e0b30cdda4.pdf" target="_blank">P506072 – PAD</a>.
