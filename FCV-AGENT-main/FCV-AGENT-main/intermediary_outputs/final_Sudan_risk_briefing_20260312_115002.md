# Sudan FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Active Multi-Front War and Escalating Insecurity Across Kordofan and Darfur

Sudan's war between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) has entered its fourth year with no resolution in sight, with Kordofan emerging as a key epicentre of hostilities alongside persistent fighting in North Darfur, Sennar, and Blue Nile states. Drone strikes on civilian infrastructure — including a documented strike on Dilling General Hospital — and sustained attacks on El Obeid over five consecutive days in early March 2026 illustrate the conflict's intensifying reach into areas that host active World Bank operations. All three projects operating in Sudan explicitly identify Kordofan and Darfur as priority implementation zones, and their design documents acknowledge that health facilities in these areas have previously been attacked and looted, that security risks from armed groups, extortion, and theft are ongoing, and that approximately 30% of Sudan's counties are directly affected by conflict <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. These risks are not merely prospective: implementation status reporting confirms that shifting frontlines are continuously disrupting facility targeting and supply chain approaches, forcing projects to adapt their operational footprints in real time <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The education project reports that schools remain closed in conflict-affected target states, directly preventing grant distribution and learning material delivery, with the Security Management Framework still pending clearance — a procedural gap that itself signals unresolved operational security concerns <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. Across the portfolio, the active war is the primary driver of low disbursement rates, constrained field presence, and the inability to sustain consistent service delivery to intended beneficiaries.

## 2. Deliberate Obstruction of Humanitarian Access and Attacks on Aid Operations

Both the SAF and RSF continue to wilfully obstruct aid movement, attack humanitarian workers and local volunteers, and restrict access to conflict-affected populations in violation of international humanitarian law. Drone strikes on WFP operations since early February 2026 and the UN Security Council's condemnation of repeated attacks on humanitarian workers underscore that obstruction is systematic and deliberate rather than incidental to combat. All projects in the portfolio are structurally dependent on humanitarian access corridors: the health system strengthening project relies on UN humanitarian supply routes and UNOCHA corridor facilitation; the emergency safety net project depends on WFP and UNICEF's ability to reach beneficiaries across multiple states; and the education project requires physical access to schools and communities for grant distribution <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The PAD for the health project explicitly acknowledges a three-tiered service delivery model designed to adapt to changing accessibility, but this model presupposes some minimum level of access that current obstruction patterns are eroding <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. In practice, implementation status reports confirm that constrained access to health facilities and beneficiaries is an active implementation challenge, with the project continuously recalibrating its operational approach <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. For the education project, the combination of access obstruction and school closures has produced a 98.3% underspend rate on signed contracts as of April 2025, with only $281,000 of $16.8 million disbursed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The safety net project similarly faces documented bureaucratic impediments and politicization of aid that compound physical access restrictions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>.

## 3. De Facto State Fragmentation, Governance Vacuum, and Institutional Collapse

The RSF's proclamation of a parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" (Tasis State) in August 2025, based in Darfur and allied with the SPLM-N, has deepened Sudan's political fragmentation to the point where the country operates under competing governance authorities with no unified institutional framework. The SAF-aligned government returned national institutions to Khartoum in January 2026, but the RSF consolidates control over most of Darfur and parts of Kordofan — precisely the regions where the portfolio's largest operations are concentrated. All three projects identify political and governance fragmentation as a high-rated risk, with PADs noting that federal institutions are non-functional, that reliance on state and locality-level institutions is necessary but constrained, and that coordination with federal-level security apparatus is challenging <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The safety net project explicitly operates under OP 7.30 (Dealings with De Facto Governments) in recognition of this fragmentation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio: implementation status reports for all four projects rate political and governance risk as High, with fragmented institutional authority creating unclear counterparts, conflicting policy directives, and dual administrative structures that undermine project coordination, financial management, and stakeholder engagement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The energy and digital infrastructure project faces sector policy instability directly attributable to the absence of a unified national authority capable of establishing coherent sector frameworks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. Stalled peace diplomacy — with the Quad, AU Quintet, and bilateral Egyptian efforts all failing to produce a ceasefire — means there is no near-term prospect for governance consolidation, and the non-constitution of the UN Panel of Experts supporting the 1591 Sudan Sanctions Committee further removes accountability mechanisms that could constrain the fragmentation dynamic.

## 4. Mass Displacement, Famine, and Collapsed Health System

Sudan hosts the world's largest displacement crisis, with approximately 14 million people forcibly displaced since April 2023, including 7.3 million internally displaced persons and 4.2 million refugees in neighbouring countries. Famine (IPC Phase 5) has been confirmed in El Fasher (North Darfur) and Kadugli (South Kordofan) — both project priority locations — with 20 additional areas projected at risk across Greater Darfur and Greater Kordofan through 2026. Sudan's health system has been driven to near-collapse, with 37% of health facilities non-functional as of January 2026 and 201 verified attacks on healthcare facilities since April 2023. These three intersecting crises create compounding operational risks for the portfolio. The health system strengthening project explicitly targets restoration of basic health and nutrition services in conflict-affected areas, but its PAD acknowledges that 75% of health facilities in these areas are non-functional and that the project's disease surveillance component may identify outbreaks without having resources to contain them at scale <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The safety net project targets IPC Phase 3 and 4 areas in Northern West Darfur, East Darfur, and West and South Kordofan, but its cash transfer design — providing US$10 per person per month — may be insufficient as famine conditions worsen and hyperinflation erodes purchasing power <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The project linking cash transfers to health service utilization for 30,000 pregnant and lactating women depends on "relatively stable areas with functioning primary healthcare facilities," a condition that the health system collapse renders increasingly untenable <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. Implementation status reports confirm that macroeconomic instability is a realized high risk across the safety net portfolio, with currency depreciation and inflation directly undermining the real value of transfers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The education project's Aide Memoire documents that disease outbreaks and health system failure intersect with learning continuity through school closures and the socio-emotional wellness component <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Displacement-driven beneficiary mobility is also materializing as an implementation constraint, with rapid population movements rendering targeting mechanisms and biometric registration systems difficult to maintain <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>.

## 5. Systematic Violence Against Civilians, Sexual Exploitation Risks, and Suppression of Civil Society

Both the SAF and RSF continue to commit war crimes, including mass killings, sexual violence, and unlawful detention, with the UN Fact-Finding Mission identifying hallmarks of genocide following the RSF seizure of El Fasher. The UN has documented at least 330 cases of conflict-related sexual violence, with actual numbers believed to be far higher due to access restrictions and stigma. Simultaneously, both warring parties are detaining over 3,000 people in Gezira state — many of them political activists — conducting retaliatory attacks against local emergency response volunteers, and suppressing civilian political space. These dynamics create direct risks for project staff, implementing partners, community health workers, and beneficiaries across the portfolio. The health system strengthening project and the emergency safety net project both explicitly identify sexual exploitation and abuse as a high risk, noting that health services, community engagement, and cash transfers create SEA/SH exposure in a context of displaced populations, weak governance, and humanitarian crisis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The community-led governance model of the livelihoods project — which relies on Community Development Committees with minimum quotas for women (40%) and IDPs (30%) — is directly threatened by the documented pattern of arbitrary detention and retaliation against local volunteers and activists <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. Implementation status reports confirm that stakeholder engagement is rated High risk across the portfolio, with civil society suppression and political repression narrowing the pool of legitimate, accessible partners and creating fear-based barriers to meaningful community participation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The education project's delayed recruitment of a Third-Party Monitor — attributed to budgetary constraints — leaves a verification vacuum precisely when independent oversight is most critical to detect coercion, misappropriation, and exclusion of beneficiaries in an environment of pervasive impunity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The severe humanitarian funding gap, with Sudan's 2026 humanitarian response plan receiving only 5.5% of required funds as of early February 2026, compounds all of these risks by forcing closure of safe spaces for women and girls, withdrawal of UNFPA from health facilities, and reduction of food rations to survival minimums — degrading the complementary services on which project health and protection outcomes depend <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>.
