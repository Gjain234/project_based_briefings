# Ethiopia FCV Portfolio Briefing: Sector-Aligned Risk Assessment

---

**1. Social Protection and Livelihoods**

The social protection and livelihoods cluster—anchored by the Productive Safety Net Program (P172479) and the Urban Productive Safety Net (P169943)—operates across Ethiopia's most conflict-saturated regions, where the country risk environment is defined by simultaneous armed insurgencies in Amhara and Oromia, mass internal displacement affecting 3.3–4.7 million people, and a severe humanitarian funding crisis that has forced WFP to suspend treatment for 650,000 malnourished women and children. The PAD for P172479 explicitly acknowledges "ongoing internal displacements due to conflicts" and identifies political risk as high due to "ongoing conflicts, internal displacements, ethnic tensions, political instability affecting implementation" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087f8ffa9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172479 – PAD</a>. Similarly, P169943 targets 816,000 urban poor across 83 cities, including conflict-affected areas, with the PAD identifying "security (substantial)" risk and "potential conflicts in implementation areas" requiring "gradual rollout" and "protection monitoring" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087e0afcb&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P169943 – PAD</a>. These design-stage acknowledgments have proven prescient: implementation evidence confirms that PSNP operations are being conducted "amidst protracted conflicts in Amhara and Oromia regions, persistent shocks, the cumulative effects of frequent droughts, and a challenging macro-economic situation" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. Security challenges are directly preventing adequate follow-up and monitoring of public works, with linkages between public works and livelihoods sub-components described as "insufficient in some of the regions due to security challenges and limited follow-up of public works implementation in conflict-affected areas" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. Fiduciary integrity is also compromised: the risk of improper use of livelihoods capacity development budgets has been flagged given a sixfold increase in funds (from ETB 7.3 billion to ETB 80.5 billion) and compressed implementation timelines of only seven months <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. In the urban safety net, refugee beneficiaries in Pugnido, Gambella experienced insecurity and relocation, causing a large number to drop out of the program, requiring re-targeting of 3,175 new beneficiaries <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>. Macroeconomic risk ratings have remained persistently "High" across all review periods for both projects <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a> <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0881185c3&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P169943 – ISR</a>, and five contested woredas are excluded from beneficiary estimates due to territorial disputes <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>, reflecting the degree to which conflict geography is actively reshaping program coverage.

**2. Health, Nutrition, and Education**

The health, nutrition, and education cluster—comprising the Ethiopia Primary Health Care (P175167) and the Accelerating Inclusive Human Capital Development (P172284) operations—faces a country risk environment characterized by multi-front armed conflict closing schools and health facilities, mass displacement generating acute service demand, and a humanitarian funding collapse that is directly competing with development financing for the same vulnerable populations. The PAD for P175167 targets conflict-affected populations with mobile health and nutrition teams (MHNTs) and temporary clinics for IDPs, acknowledging the "devastating internal conflict since November 2020" and the need for "special training programs for conflict-affected regions" [P175167 | PAD]. P172284 is explicitly "designed to work in conflict-affected areas with flexible implementation arrangements, focus on social cohesion building, peace-building through equitable service delivery" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a>. Realized implementation risks have been severe and worsening. Mobile Health and Nutrition Teams intended to serve conflict-affected areas remain largely unestablished, with only 4 of 20 targets achieved, and health facilities in conflict-affected areas have resumed services to pre-conflict status in only 2 of a target of 262 facilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>. Postnatal care coverage in the four lowest-performing regions has declined from a baseline of 64% to 52%, attributed to "budget shortages and high staff turnover" in conflict-affected areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>. The security situation is preventing completion of routine data quality assessments, meaning the RDQA report has yet to be prepared <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>. For P172284, the selection of 23 target woredas (10 conflict-affected, 10 drought-affected, 3 refugee-hosting) remains pending finalization, blocking implementation activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172284-c3e2849b-c5b6-4a37-b729-d72e2d877d90.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – Aide Memoire</a>, and data from Amhara and Tigray regions is absent from results framework reporting, creating blind spots in measuring progress <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172284-c3e2849b-c5b6-4a37-b729-d72e2d877d90.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – Aide Memoire</a>. Declining humanitarian assistance is described as "likely to have major impacts on refugee services—particularly education" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>, compounding the structural service delivery deficits that these projects are designed to address.

**3. Infrastructure and Energy**

The infrastructure and energy cluster—spanning the Ethiopia Rural Roads (P176303), the Electrification Program (P171742), and the Modjo-Hawassa Highway (P148850)—confronts a country risk environment in which active armed conflict in Amhara and Oromia directly threatens construction access, supply chains, and staff safety, while macroeconomic instability driven by foreign exchange shortages and currency depreciation erodes project financing. The PAD for P176303 identifies "security (substantial): Internal conflicts affecting project sites, staff safety, material transportation" and requires "conflict-sensitive site selection and implementation procedures" across all 13 regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a>. P171742 acknowledges "political disruption and social unrest" as a high political risk and notes the project operates in "conflict-affected and internal displacement areas, with over 920,000 refugees and 2.8 million internally displaced persons" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08839e83a&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171742 – PAD</a>. P148850 identifies "political and governance (moderate)" risk and relies on a multi-donor coordination framework with AfDB, China EXIM Bank, and Korea EXIM Bank <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b082ea6308&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P148850 – PAD</a>. Realized implementation risks have materialized acutely. For P176303, basic financial management arrangements have not yet been established in conflict areas before resource transfers, with payments not yet transferred in conflict areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-7b125857-00f3-4f1b-a25e-a0ad429dba62.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – ISR</a>. Political and governance risk ratings have remained persistently "High" across all review periods <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-7b125857-00f3-4f1b-a25e-a0ad429dba62.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – ISR</a>. For P171742, overall implementation progress has declined from Moderately Satisfactory to Moderately Unsatisfactory, with security concerns requiring site replacements for mini-grid installations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-a6dffcd4-0989-414f-875d-c153cf49eb19.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-45fbd416-22c6-4fe2-a001-21a06b2bddd9.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – Aide Memoire</a>. The political and governance risk rating for P148850 has escalated from Moderate at approval to High in the most recent rating <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0848e86aa&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P148850 – ISR</a>, and the Lot 4 contractor faces USD 71.67 million in unpaid foreign currency payments, causing partial suspension of asphalt pavement activities <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08588ff6c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P148850 – Aide Memoire</a>. A newly assigned contractor is on the AML sanction list, preventing direct World Bank payments <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08588ff6c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P148850 – Aide Memoire</a>, compounding the governance and fiduciary fragility that characterizes this cluster.

**4. Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene (WASH) and Urban Services**

The WASH and urban services cluster—anchored by the One WASH Program (P167794) and the Ethiopia Urban Water Supply and Sanitation (P156433)—operates in a country risk environment defined by conflict-driven displacement pressuring localized water sources, governance fragility in newly established regions, and a humanitarian funding crisis straining government counterpart financing commitments. The PAD for P167794 explicitly identifies "displacement adds pressure to localized water sources" and lists "internally displaced persons" as a vulnerable group requiring targeted approaches, with the project depending on 8,260 community-based WASHCOMs and woreda-level coordination structures that assume stable community organization <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d2b493&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – PAD</a>. P156433 identifies "political (high)" risk from "state of emergency and political unrest" and relies on a Federal Project Management Unit and Regional Water Bureaus for coordination across 23 cities including Mekelle, Bahir Dar, Gondar, and Gambella <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b084a10035&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P156433 – PAD</a>. Realized implementation risks have been extensive and in some cases irreversible. A special financial audit of the Tigray region component revealed failure to provide financial documents from woredas, inability to verify asset existence and ownership, and absence of assessment of lost or damaged assets <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. Active conflicts in Amhara and Oromia have impacted project resources, with documents destroyed or misplaced in three woredas, preventing settlement of expenditures <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. Internal conflict is flagged as directly impeding movement and slowing WASH implementation in Amhara <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. Newly established regions show implementation rates of only 27% (Southwest Ethiopia), 46% (South Ethiopia), and 51% (Central Ethiopia), with Southwest Ethiopia at only 26% staffing <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167794-f8ff51f0-6541-4d6a-944c-966e7779455e.pdf" target="_blank">P167794 – ISR</a>. Government matching fund delays have become "a bottleneck dragging implementation of some large value contracts and causing payment delays for completed activities" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. For P156433, a pattern of contract extensions across all ongoing Fecal Sludge Treatment Plants, combined with discrepancies between physical and financial progress, has been described as posing "serious concerns" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P156433-02d58b01-e7da-4021-85ac-39e6c17c89f0.pdf" target="_blank">P156433 – Aide Memoire</a>, and construction in Dessie has been pending since 2023 awaiting safeguards compliance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P156433-02d58b01-e7da-4021-85ac-39e6c17c89f0.pdf" target="_blank">P156433 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**5. Governance, Digital Economy, and Refugee-Host Integration**

The governance, digital economy, and refugee-host integration cluster—comprising the Digital Ethiopia (P171034), the Lowland Livelihood Resilience Project (P180076), the Durable Solutions for Displaced Communities (P177233), and the Development Response to Displacement Impacts (P178047)—faces a country risk environment defined by systematic crackdowns on media and civil society ahead of the June 2026 elections, cross-border spillover from Sudan and South Sudan conflicts, fragile peace process implementation in northern regions, and legitimacy concerns that threaten the governance structures these projects depend upon. The PAD for P171034 identifies "political (high)" risk including "upcoming elections" and acknowledges the Tigray region is excluded due to "ongoing conflict and communication blackout since November 2020," directly contradicting the stated objective to increase digital inclusiveness for all citizens <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08847bca4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171034 – PAD</a>. P177233 identifies "political and governance volatility" as a high risk and relies on Federal Steering Committees, Regional Steering Committees, and Woreda Project Coordination Teams for implementation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088d466ef&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P177233 – PAD</a>. P178047 notes that "all Tigray Region woredas" are designated as High Risk of Ongoing Conflict (HROC) areas requiring differentiated implementation arrangements <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088ddd75f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P178047 – PAD</a>. Realized implementation risks are pervasive. For P171034, Ethio Telecom cancelled meetings three times during a supervision mission, actively opposing regulatory reforms that are critical project prior actions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the government requested cancellation of USD 30 million in project funds for reallocation, signaling governance tensions over project priorities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>. For P178047, security restrictions prevented any field travel during a supervision mission <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>, a woreda head in Afar reportedly left the country with a project vehicle <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>, and more than 50% of pending activities in Tigray are delayed due to slow UNOPS performance and transition delays back to the Regional Project Coordination Unit <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. Refugee inclusion in livelihood activities stands at only 12% against a 30% target <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>, and internal control gaps in the Somali Region have led to suspension of recommended disbursements <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. For P180076, resource-related conflict management activities are significantly behind schedule, with only 49% of conflict-prone woredas assessed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>, and regions including Gambella and Benishangul-Gumuz have become inaccessible to World Bank supervision <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>.
