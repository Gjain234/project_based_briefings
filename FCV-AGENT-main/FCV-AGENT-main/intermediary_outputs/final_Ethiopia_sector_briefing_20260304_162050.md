# Ethiopia Portfolio FCV Risk Briefing

## 1. Active Armed Conflict in Amhara and Oromia Regions

Ethiopia's two most populous regions remain engulfed in active armed conflict that poses the most direct and pervasive threat to the portfolio. In Amhara, multiple Fano militia factions continue armed resistance against government forces despite a partial peace agreement signed with one faction in December 2025, with insurgents maintaining significant territorial control, over 4,000 schools closed, and more than 4 million children out of school. In Oromia, violence persists despite a December 2024 peace agreement with one OLA faction, with over 25 civilians killed in East Arsi Zone in October 2025 and 288,000 people displaced since July 2025 from intercommunal violence along the Oromia-Somali border. Across the portfolio, projects operating in these regions are explicitly susceptible: the rural roads project acknowledges "internal conflicts affecting project sites, staff safety, material transportation" as a substantial security risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a>; the lowland pastoral development project identifies "localized conflicts in Amhara and Oromia regions" as a high political risk and notes operations in "lowland pastoral areas affected by resource-based conflicts" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-334703ce-834f-4cd4-bf49-4e941b00bb0a.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – PAD</a>; the TVET project requires PTCs in each region including Amhara and Oromia while acknowledging that "security risks may hinder smooth implementation especially for PTCs in conflict regions" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a>; and the education project identifies both regions as conflict-affected areas requiring flexible implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing. The PSNP reports "protracted conflicts in Amhara and Oromia regions" directly impeding operations, with "security challenges limiting public works implementation in conflict-affected areas" and insufficient linkages between public works and livelihoods sub-components <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. The One WASH project documents "internal conflict which impedes movement" as a key implementation challenge in Amhara, with conflict-related damages to project resources in Amhara and Oromia unassessed and unreported to development partners <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The energy access project has seen its overall implementation progress rating decline from Moderately Satisfactory to Moderately Unsatisfactory, with security concerns forcing site replacements for mini-grid installations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-a6dffcd4-0989-414f-875d-c153cf49eb19.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – ISR</a>. The livestock and fisheries project reports security issues "prevalent in all DRDIP II implementing regions" and conflict-affected areas in Gambella and Benishangul-Gumuz becoming inaccessible to World Bank supervision <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. Forward-looking, the persistence of multiple armed Fano factions with popular support and the absence of a comprehensive peace process in Amhara, combined with continued OLA operations in Oromia, indicate these disruptions will intensify rather than abate through the 2026 election period.

## 2. Political Crisis in Tigray and Fragile Peace Process in Northern Regions

Tigray faces a compounding political and security crisis that threatens to unravel the fragile 2022 Pretoria Agreement. The TPLF's deregistration from the June 2026 elections—with Tigrayan leaders labeling it a "clear declaration of war"—has escalated tensions between the TPLF and Tigray Defense Forces, while federal authority is rapidly shrinking in Mekelle and other key cities. Simultaneously, Eritrean troops continue to commit sexual violence in occupied areas of Tigray, Tigrayans continue to be forcibly displaced from Western Tigray, and the referendum on Western Tigray's fate remains pending, sustaining territorial disputes that undermine stabilization. Projects across the portfolio are directly exposed. The rural roads project relies on Federal and Regional Steering Committees for coordination and acknowledges "political (high): ongoing conflicts, internal displacements, ethnic tensions, political instability affecting implementation" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a>. The TVET project explicitly targets Tigray as a conflict-affected region requiring PTC coverage and notes that "volatile security situation affects service delivery" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a>. The IDP recovery project designates "all Tigray Region woredas" as High Risk of Ongoing Conflict areas requiring differentiated implementation arrangements <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088ddd75f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P178047 – PAD</a>. The digital government project explicitly excludes Tigray due to "ongoing conflict and communication blackout since November 2020," directly contradicting its stated objective of inclusive digital services for all citizens <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08847bca4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171034 – PAD</a>. The education project excludes Tigray from project locations, itself reflecting recognition of implementation risks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing and worsening. The PSNP documents "delays in finalizing account opening in the 23 woredas in Tigray region" attributed to "limited coordination, accountability and collaboration among implementing entities," alongside unexploded ordnance risks requiring specialized training in Tigray and Amhara hotspot woredas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. The One WASH project's special financial audit for Tigray revealed "failure to provide financial documents of Woredas, inability to verify existence, condition, and ownership of assets, and absence of assessment of lost or damaged assets" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The refugee and host community project reports that over 50% of pending activities are in Tigray due to slow UNOPS performance and delays caused by the transition back to the Regional Project Coordination Unit <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban water and sanitation project lists Mekelle as an implementation city and was designed during a state of emergency, with political risk rated High <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b084a10035&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P156433 – PAD</a>. The convergence of electoral exclusion, armed faction tensions, and unresolved territorial disputes creates a high probability of renewed armed conflict in Tigray that would further collapse the already fragile implementation environment across the northern portfolio.

## 3. Mass Internal Displacement and Deteriorating Humanitarian Conditions

Ethiopia's displacement crisis has reached catastrophic proportions, with 3.3 to 4.7 million people internally displaced and an additional one million refugees hosted from neighboring countries, all against a backdrop of a severe humanitarian funding collapse. WFP has been forced to suspend treatment for 650,000 malnourished women and children, 3.6 million vulnerable people face loss of food assistance, and humanitarian operations in Amhara have been disrupted by access challenges—all exacerbated by the Trump administration's foreign aid freeze and reduced international donor support. The portfolio's susceptibility is pervasive. The rural roads project acknowledges "ongoing internal displacements due to conflicts" and implements a voluntary land donation protocol to avoid involuntary resettlement, but its targeting of 11.3 million beneficiaries assumes stable populations that displacement dynamics fundamentally undermine <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a>. The lowland pastoral project explicitly identifies "displaced populations" as a vulnerable group and addresses "climate-induced displacement and cross-border mixed migration," but its baseline of 3 million direct beneficiaries across 600,000 households is vulnerable to displacement-driven targeting failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-334703ce-834f-4cd4-bf49-4e941b00bb0a.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – PAD</a>. The TVET project mandates that at least 15% of 200,000 direct beneficiaries be IDPs, acknowledging 4.39 million IDPs in its rationale, but deteriorating humanitarian conditions threaten the stability and retention of IDP beneficiaries in training programs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a>. The education project identifies 512,000 drought-induced IDPs as a target group but faces a displacement reality of 3.3 to 4.7 million total IDPs with dire camp conditions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a>. The PSNP includes explicit provisions for internally displaced beneficiaries and protocols for hosting displaced families, but the scale of displacement vastly exceeds the project's 2.5 million household beneficiary target <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087f8ffa9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172479 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing across the portfolio. The PSNP reports "disruption in implementation in conflict-affected areas" affecting program delivery and beneficiary access, with five contested woredas excluded from beneficiary estimates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. Mobile health and nutrition teams intended for conflict-affected areas remain largely unestablished—only 4 of 20 targets—and temporary clinics for IDPs are similarly non-operational, with health service restoration in conflict-affected areas achieving only 2 of 262 facilities against program closing targets <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>. The refugee and host community project reports refugee inclusion in livelihood activities at only 12% against a 30% target, with declining humanitarian assistance described as likely to have "major impacts on refugee services—particularly education" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban safety net project documents refugee beneficiaries in Pugnido, Gambella dropping out due to insecurity and relocation, requiring re-targeting of 3,175 new beneficiaries <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>. The humanitarian funding crisis simultaneously threatens government counterpart funding commitments across multiple projects—the rural roads project requires US$107.3 million in government counterpart funding <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a>, the One WASH project requires US$50 million <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d2b493&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – PAD</a>, and the urban water project requires US$60 million <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b084a10035&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P156433 – PAD</a>—at precisely the moment when fiscal space is most constrained.

## 4. Governance Fragility, Institutional Capacity Collapse, and Election-Related Political Instability

Ethiopia's governance environment is deteriorating on multiple fronts simultaneously: a systematic crackdown on media freedom with 44 journalists detained in 2025, the country dropping to 145th in the World Press Freedom Index, and the June 2026 elections carrying high risk of triggering wider instability through opposition boycotts, TPLF exclusion, and government crackdowns on civil society. Underlying these acute pressures is a structural governance deficit—weak multisectoral coordination, limited state capacity at subnational levels, high staff turnover driven by low remuneration, and fragmented accountability systems—that pervades the entire portfolio. Projects are broadly susceptible to these compounding governance risks. The rural roads project relies on Federal and Regional Steering Committees and Technical Committees at all levels, with its monitoring framework dependent on an Independent Verification Agent and third-party performance evaluation that could be compromised by civil society contraction <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a>. The TVET project's governance structure depends on a Project Steering Committee chaired by the MoLS Minister and Regional Skills Bureaus coordination, with political risk rated Substantial <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a>. The education project's Steering Committee is chaired by the State Minister of MOF and requires coordination across MOE, MOH, MOA, MOWE, MOWSA, and MOPD—a multisectoral coordination architecture vulnerable to election-driven political turmoil <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a>. The digital government project identifies "upcoming elections" as a political risk and relies on the Ministry of Innovation and Technology for implementation, with its Contingent Emergency Response Component valued at zero, limiting flexibility <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08847bca4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171034 – PAD</a>. These governance risks are extensively materialized across the portfolio. Political and governance risk ratings are sustained at High across the PSNP <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>, rural roads <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-7b125857-00f3-4f1b-a25e-a0ad429dba62.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – ISR</a>, IDP recovery <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177233-294d0424-a5ca-4223-89a1-57418a1c9fbc.pdf" target="_blank">P177233 – ISR</a>, energy access <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-a6dffcd4-0989-414f-875d-c153cf49eb19.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – ISR</a>, lowland pastoral <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-2646766e-1d32-49db-8d05-cc6efb166efc.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – ISR</a>, and One WASH <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167794-f8ff51f0-6541-4d6a-944c-966e7779455e.pdf" target="_blank">P167794 – ISR</a> projects. The PSNP documents shock response disbursement delays caused by the lead government institution failing to confirm beneficiary numbers in a timely manner, and an Early Warning dashboard not available to all institutions in the prioritization committee <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's Project Steering Committee has held only one meeting to date, with critical decisions on Skills for Jobs focus areas lacking PSC guidance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-a2d54917-aedd-4d6b-bdb7-01bbf1399378.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – Aide Memoire</a>. The One WASH project documents matching fund contribution delays becoming "a bottleneck dragging implementation," governance fragility in newly established regions with staffing at 26% in Southwest Ethiopia, and political instability affecting staff retention at woreda level <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban water project reveals a pattern of contract extensions across all FSTP contracts, discrepancies between physical and financial progress, and construction pending since 2023 in Dessie awaiting safeguards compliance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P156433-02d58b01-e7da-4021-85ac-39e6c17c89f0.pdf" target="_blank">P156433 – Aide Memoire</a>. The digital government project faces state-owned incumbent Ethio Telecom actively opposing regulatory reforms and cancelling meetings three times during a supervision mission, while a government request to cancel US$30 million in project funds for reallocation signals competing priorities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>. The livestock and fisheries project reports resource-related conflict management activities at only 49% completion in conflict-prone woredas, with planned partnerships with international research institutions failing to materialize due to cost disagreements <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>. Looking forward, the June 2026 elections represent a critical inflection point: if insecurity prevents voting in conflict-affected regions, if the TPLF exclusion triggers renewed armed conflict in Tigray, or if opposition boycotts delegitimize the government, the political consensus underpinning multi-agency project coordination could collapse precisely when implementation momentum is most needed.

## 5. Cross-Border Spillover, Regional Instability, and Macroeconomic Stress

Ethiopia faces compounding external pressures from multiple directions: escalating tensions with Eritrea over Red Sea access that Prime Minister Abiy has described as an "existential matter," with both countries reportedly mobilizing forces to their border and Ethiopian officials accusing Eritrea of supplying weapons to Fano rebels; cross-border spillover from the Sudan conflict driving over 163,000 individuals into Ethiopia with 50,000 new arrivals in 2025; and cross-border incursions by South Sudanese opposition forces in Gambella protecting Nuer communities. These external security pressures interact with a severe macroeconomic crisis—high inflation, foreign exchange shortages, significant Birr depreciation since July 2024, and debt sustainability concerns—to create a compounding stress environment for the portfolio. Projects in border regions are directly susceptible. The lowland pastoral project lists Afar, Gambella, and other border regions as implementation locations and explicitly notes "consideration of geopolitical tensions in Horn of Africa" in its Security Risk Assessment and Management Plan, with cross-border tensions and cross-border mixed migration identified as contextual risks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-334703ce-834f-4cd4-bf49-4e941b00bb0a.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – PAD</a>. The refugee and host community project targets Gambella as one of six main refugee-hosting regions and acknowledges cross-border security threats, with the project's water resources and market facilities components in border areas vulnerable to refugee influx and security operations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088ddd75f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P178047 – PAD</a>. The education project commits to supporting 800,000 refugees and integrating them into national systems, but current dynamics show 50,000 new arrivals in 2025 and cross-border incursions in Gambella creating access and security challenges <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a>. The energy access project operates in Gambella and Somali regions with over 920,000 refugees and 2.8 million IDPs in its operating environment, requiring stable beneficiary identification for mini-grid and solar home system deployment <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08839e83a&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171742 – PAD</a>. The rural roads project faces macroeconomic risk rated High, with high inflation, foreign exchange shortages, and currency distortions affecting project costs, and government counterpart funding of US$107.3 million vulnerable to defense spending diversion if Ethiopia-Eritrea tensions escalate <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing. The refugee and host community project documents conflict-affected areas in Gambella and Benishangul-Gumuz becoming inaccessible to World Bank supervision, with internal audit scope required to be widened <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban safety net project reports refugee beneficiaries in Pugnido, Gambella dropping out due to insecurity and relocation, with Commercial Bank of Ethiopia branch capacity in refugee-hosting locations "considerably low" causing payment delays <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>. The digital government project faces customs duties that "can almost double the contract price" and a significant Birr weakening since July 2024 requiring thorough analysis of implementation impacts, with over US$16 million in outstanding customs duty issues <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>. The rural roads project documents basic financial management arrangements not yet established in conflict areas before resource transfers, with currency depreciation threatening the government's 30% matching fund commitment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-f8f4167e-72a4-4049-a5c0-0b3f0fd456c6.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – Aide Memoire</a>. The highway project faces a contractor on the AML sanction list preventing direct World Bank payments, outstanding foreign currency payments of USD 53.58 million from Chinese EXIM Bank unpaid to the contractor, and political and governance risk escalating from Moderate at approval to High in the most recent rating <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0848e86aa&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P148850 – ISR</a>. Forward-looking, the combination of Ethiopia-Eritrea military mobilization, continued Sudan conflict-driven refugee flows, and macroeconomic deterioration creates a risk of cascading shocks that could simultaneously strain government fiscal capacity, disrupt border-region project operations, and divert political attention from development priorities at a moment when the portfolio requires sustained institutional engagement.
