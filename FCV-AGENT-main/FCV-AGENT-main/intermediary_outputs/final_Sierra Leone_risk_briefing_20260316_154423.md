# Sierra Leone FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Governance and Corruption Vulnerabilities

Sierra Leone's governance landscape is characterized by systemic institutional weaknesses documented in the IMF's 2025–2026 Governance and Corruption Diagnostic, which identified procurement vulnerabilities, limited rule of law enforcement, selective anti-corruption application, and weakened fiscal oversight—including the removal of the respected Auditor-General in late 2024. These structural deficits create a high-risk operating environment for the entire portfolio. Projects are broadly susceptible: the WASH project flags "limited independence of auditor general and Audit Service Sierra Leone from executive branch" as a high FM risk alongside procurement challenges across a US$40 million envelope <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>; the roads project identifies "weak institutional capacity" and "procurement delays and higher-than-expected bid prices" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c83ae70a-5a33-45ba-a77a-95ccca1d4fda.pdf" target="_blank">P178683 – PAD</a>; the digital transformation project explicitly rates fiduciary risk as Substantial, noting "limited fiduciary capacity in MIC" and a "weak complaint redress system" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>; and the local governance project cites a 2019 ASSL audit identifying Le5.3 billion in cash losses across 22 local councils <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172492 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing. The agriculture project's aide memoire documents US$141,000 in unresolved grant advances and contract overpayments persisting since March 2024, contractor non-compliance with environmental reporting, and government counterpart funding gaps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. The energy project records land disputes requiring ministerial intervention, delayed RAP preparation before contractor engagement, and EDSA recruitment failures for critical management positions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf" target="_blank">P171059 – Aide Memoire</a>. The local governance project's aide memoire confirms taxpayer non-compliance and low enforcement capacity at local government level, with revenue-sharing disputes between chiefdoms and district councils reflecting the same institutional fragility <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – Aide Memoire</a>. The health project's ISR documents a non-functional grievance mechanism and persistent DHMT capacity gaps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. Across the portfolio, governance vulnerabilities are not merely forward-looking risks but are actively constraining implementation quality, fiduciary integrity, and beneficiary outcomes.

## 2. Macro-Economic Fragility, Debt Vulnerability, and Structural Reform Gaps

Sierra Leone's economy exhibits structural fragility rooted in high public debt, heavy dependence on extractive industries, limited diversification, and exposure to commodity price shocks. The IMF's Extended Credit Facility provides some fiscal anchor, but the World Bank's November 2025 economic update signals continued structural weaknesses, and the supplementary 2025 budget was criticized as austerity-oriented for citizens while preserving favourable terms for political insiders. Projects across the portfolio are susceptible through multiple channels: the financial inclusion project rates macroeconomic risk as High, noting "high inflation (37% in January 2023), currency depreciation (66% in 2022), fiscal deficit nearly 10% of GDP, public debt-to-GDP ratio increased to 94%," with MSME loan repayment capacity directly threatened <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>; the social protection project identifies "inflation reducing real value of transfers" and "tight fiscal situation limiting government budget allocation to social protection" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088cebb9f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176789 – PAD</a>; the energy project flags EDSA's financial viability and government O&M funding sustainability as critical risks <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08819710d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171059 – PAD</a>; and the local governance project explicitly warns that "despite investment in better systems, LCs are not provided meaningful additional funding to deliver basic services role, undermining systems investments" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172492 – PAD</a>. Materialization is evident. The agriculture project's aide memoire documents a US$10.944 million financing gap with unclear government commitment to bridge it, counterpart funding delays of US$6.944 million, and a ferry grounding due to climate-related flooding that isolated communities dependent on subsistence agriculture <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban resilience project's ISR records an escalation of macroeconomic risk from Substantial to High, with reduced government MDA engagement attributed partly to fiscal constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf" target="_blank">P168608 – ISR</a>. The health project's ISR confirms "very limited government health spending (less than 5% of government budget)" and "constrained fiscal space for resource mobilization" as active implementation barriers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. Structural economic fragility is thus both a forward-looking sustainability threat and a present-day implementation constraint across infrastructure, social protection, and service delivery projects.

## 3. USAID Dismantlement and Global Aid Cuts Undermining Health and Civil Society

The dismantlement of USAID and sharp reduction in US foreign assistance since January 2025 has created acute resource gaps across Sierra Leone's health, civil society, and governance sectors. Sierra Leone's health budget is approximately half-funded by donors, and field reports from March 2026 describe children arriving at hospitals with untreated severe malaria due to the absence of USAID-funded diagnostic tests. A Freetown-based democracy advocacy organization lost US$1 million in funding for civic education and female political participation. Projects are susceptible through their reliance on civil society partners for community engagement, monitoring, and grievance redress, and through complementary health and education service delivery. The WASH project depends on a "donor coordination platform" and community engagement through the Wan Fambul Framework, with health outcomes (waterborne disease reduction) contingent on a functioning health system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>; the education project targets vulnerable girls and includes GBV response components that depend on civil society and health system support <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087a46502&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – PAD</a>; the digital transformation project relies on "close collaboration with UN Foundation DIAL" and civil society participation in stakeholder engagement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>; and the land administration project depends on CSO involvement in design, implementation, and monitoring as a core conflict-mitigation strategy <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088db0cd6&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P177031 – PAD</a>. Materialization is documented across multiple projects. The education project's aide memoire records an NGO consortium withdrawal disrupting support for 120,000 out-of-school children, with MBSSE unable to absorb the expanded mandate, and the ISR confirms fund unavailability for back-to-school incentives and accelerated curriculum delivery [P167897 | Aide Memoire; P167897 | Implementation Status and Results Report]. The health project's ISR confirms that institutional capacity constraints at MoH level are directly exacerbated by donor withdrawal, with the project's decentralization reforms undermined by reduced technical assistance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. The agriculture project's aide memoire notes E&S safeguard staffing gaps potentially linked to reduced donor-funded technical support <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. The aid withdrawal shock is thus actively degrading implementation capacity, safeguarding quality, and beneficiary outcomes across the portfolio.

## 4. Political Deadlock, Criminalisation of Opposition Speech, and Electoral Institution Credibility

Sierra Leone faces compounding political risks from the deep SLPP-APC confrontation rooted in the disputed 2023 elections and the credibility crisis affecting electoral institutions ahead of 2028. The February 2026 arrest and remand of APC Secretary-General Lansana Dumbuya on incitement charges, the PPRC's politically-motivated one-day suspension of the APC, and the stalled Tripartite Committee dialogue signal that political polarization is intensifying rather than resolving. Simultaneously, the contested appointment of ECSL Chief Electoral Commissioner Edmond Sylvester Alpha—opposed by civil society and the APC as procedurally unlawful—and the pending Constitutional Amendment Bill 2025 have eroded institutional trust. Projects are susceptible through their dependence on multi-party coordination mechanisms and institutional legitimacy. The WASH project was "prepared in close collaboration with all IAs including opposition-led FCC and WARDC," with the PSC requiring cross-party cooperation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>; the urban resilience project explicitly flags "upcoming elections with mayors in five of seven beneficiary cities from opposition party, including Freetown where bulk of investments focused" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168608 – PAD</a>; the digital transformation project identifies "difficulty organizing timely PSC/TC meetings" and "potential policy reversal or political interference" as substantial risks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>; and the social protection project's Inter-Agency Forum is chaired by the Vice President, creating potential for executive political pressure <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088cebb9f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176789 – PAD</a>. Materialization is confirmed across multiple ISRs. The health project's ISR documents an escalation from Moderate to Substantial political risk, directly citing the deepening SLPP-APC confrontation, the February 2026 Dumbuya remand, and the contested ECSL appointment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. The urban resilience project's ISR records an escalation of political and governance risk to High <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf" target="_blank">P168608 – ISR</a>. The local governance project's ISR confirms Substantial political risk sustained by the same dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-1884d056-da29-49f2-ac93-9d9026ba75ad.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – ISR</a>. The education project's ISR identifies political deadlock as a direct driver of implementation constraints <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087b90655&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – ISR</a>. The education project's aide memoire further documents delayed GRM follow-up monitoring and stalled social audits, with the political environment reducing institutional responsiveness to grievances <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167897-f4984073-1dc6-4a44-a2fa-8b6ab0ccc16b.pdf" target="_blank">P167897 – Aide Memoire</a>. These risks are thus actively materializing across the portfolio, with political polarization constraining institutional coordination, procurement neutrality, and civil society engagement.

## 5. Chronic Food Insecurity, Climate-Driven Agricultural Shocks, and Ethno-Regional Polarisation

Two structurally linked risks—chronic food insecurity exacerbated by climate shocks, and deep ethno-regional political polarization—create compounding fragility across the portfolio. Over one million Sierra Leoneans are severely food insecure per WFP data, agriculture employs over 60 percent of the population at subsistence level, and repeated climate shocks in 2022, 2023, and 2024 displaced thousands and damaged crops. Simultaneously, Sierra Leone's two-party system remains structured along Mende-dominated south/east (SLPP) and Temne-dominated north (APC) lines, with patrimonialism deepening exclusion of youth who are economically and socially marginalized and have previously led violent protests. Projects are susceptible through beneficiary vulnerability, implementation logistics, and benefit distribution equity. The agriculture project targets 50,000 smallholder farmers across all four provinces, with project outcomes dependent on farmers having sufficient surplus production to commercialize—directly threatened by food insecurity and climate vulnerability <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08412c2b7&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P153437 – PAD</a>; the roads project's agricultural market infrastructure component depends on stable value chains in Bo, Moyamba, Tonkolili, and Karene districts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c83ae70a-5a33-45ba-a77a-95ccca1d4fda.pdf" target="_blank">P178683 – PAD</a>; the financial inclusion project's MSME credit line faces loan repayment risk from agricultural shocks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>; and the social protection project acknowledges "food insecurity rising from 48% to 63% between January–June 2020" with a zero-allocation CERC that may be insufficient for major shocks <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088cebb9f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176789 – PAD</a>. On ethno-regional polarization, the roads project identifies "elite capture in market infrastructure" and "social exclusion of vulnerable groups" as substantial risks across ethnically diverse districts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c83ae70a-5a33-45ba-a77a-95ccca1d4fda.pdf" target="_blank">P178683 – PAD</a>, while the education project notes that performance-based school grants and teacher deployment across 16 districts depend on District Education Offices operating within polarized governance structures <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087a46502&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – PAD</a>. Materialization is documented. The agriculture project's aide memoire records a ferry grounding due to heavy rainfall and rising water levels that isolated communities and forced residents to use unsafe wooden canoes, directly manifesting climate-driven infrastructure disruption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>; contractor delays are attributed to "extreme weather conditions" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>; and land acquisition tensions at bridge sites carry elevated risk of mobilization along ethno-regional lines <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. The local governance project's aide memoire documents revenue-sharing disputes between chiefdoms and district councils that reflect ethno-regional political fragmentation and patronage dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – Aide Memoire</a>. The education project's aide memoire records delayed SRGBV reporting and GRM monitoring gaps that are particularly consequential given youth marginalization and the risk that unaddressed grievances channel into localized political violence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167897-f4984073-1dc6-4a44-a2fa-8b6ab0ccc16b.pdf" target="_blank">P167897 – Aide Memoire</a>. These twin risks are thus both structurally embedded in project design vulnerabilities and actively manifesting in implementation disruptions across the portfolio.
