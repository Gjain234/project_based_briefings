# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## Djibouti City and National Governance Environment

Djibouti's political landscape is defined by deepening executive consolidation. In October 2025, the government removed the presidential age limit through constitutional revision, enabling President Guelleh—in power since 1999—to seek a sixth term in April 2026. This democratic backsliding is compounded by the near-total collapse of independent civil society and media freedom, with Freedom House classifying the country as "Not Free" and journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested for criticism. The international community has largely tolerated these trends, prioritizing Djibouti's strategic stability over governance accountability. Across the portfolio, these conditions are not merely forward-looking risks—they are actively materializing. Projects dependent on government-led steering committees, multi-ministry coordination, and civil society engagement face structural vulnerabilities: the Public Finance Management Reform Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a> identifies political risk as "High," noting that vested interests could delay reforms and that mitigation relies on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption increasingly fragile under indefinite presidential rule. ISR evidence confirms this risk is realized: the project's steering committee governance structures remain contested, administrative decrees have not been published despite ministerial approval, and the overall political and governance risk rating has remained persistently "High" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. Similarly, the Digital Economy Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088b5f99a&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174461 – PAD</a> flags substantial political and governance risk, and aide-mémoire evidence confirms that the telecommunications regulatory authority (ARMD) has been unable to appoint board members since 2022 and has failed to adopt the Digital Code's telecommunications regulation book despite repeated mission recommendations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-a6aeb6a6-887c-41f9-8eb7-fb31c9e7d652.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET and Skills Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> relies on a high-level steering committee under the Economic Development Council—an executive body—without independent oversight safeguards; ISR evidence confirms the steering committee has been inactive <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across all projects, the absence of functioning civil society means grievance mechanisms, M&E systems, and stakeholder engagement plans operate in a structural vacuum, with beneficiaries unable to safely report concerns and independent verification of outcomes impossible.

## Ali Sabieh Region and Refugee Village Settlements

The Ali Sabieh region—home to the Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl refugee camps, which concentrate the majority of Djibouti's approximately 32,000 displaced persons—represents the portfolio's most acute convergence of humanitarian strain and security risk. Djibouti's open-door refugee policy, maintained despite recurrent droughts and severely limited public resources, creates structural pressure on service delivery that directly undermines project implementation. The FRUD-armé insurgent group, classified as a terrorist organization by the National Assembly, conducted a raid on an army post in October 2023 killing seven soldiers—demonstrating active domestic security threats in areas geographically proximate to refugee settlements and project sites. Multiple projects are exposed to these compounding risks. The Inclusive Education Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a> directly targets 29,214 refugees across Ali-Addeh, Holl-Holl, and Markazi villages, acknowledging external shocks as a moderate refugee protection risk; ISR evidence confirms that refugee student enrollment has declined from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025, refugee schools remain unintegrated into the national education management information system, and refugee-led CSO sub-projects have achieved zero implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The TVET Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> targets 3,165 refugees in these same settlements with security risk assessed as only "moderate," without specific protocols for FRUD-armé threats; ISR evidence confirms zero refugee enrollment in training programs and zero employment outcomes for refugees, with the project unable to track refugee-specific data <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The Urban Resilience and Land Use Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a> identifies host-refugee tensions as a substantial risk; ISR evidence confirms that land regularization in refugee villages has stalled since October 2023, property rights documentation for refugees is delayed, and the refugee integration and social cohesion assessment remains incomplete <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The Food Security Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a> explicitly targets emergency food stocks for refugee populations; ISR evidence shows only 50% of food stock targets achieved, zero water infrastructure completed, and the project restructured following a June 2025 drought declaration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>.

## Obock and Northern Zone

The northern regions of Obock and Tadjourah present a distinct risk profile shaped by geographic remoteness, insurgent proximity, and the intersection of pastoral livelihood fragility with refugee hosting. FRUD-armé activity has historically been concentrated in northern Djibouti, and the October 2023 raid underscores the active threat to government infrastructure and personnel in this zone. Severe youth unemployment—exceeding 75% nationally and likely higher in remote northern areas—compounds the challenge of sustaining project implementation and community engagement. The Agri-Food and Rural Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a> operates across a Northern Zone covering Tadjourah and Obock, including climate-proof public infrastructure such as boreholes, solar pumping systems, and rural roads; the PAD asserts "no security risks identified for project implementation" while acknowledging FRUD-armé's demonstrated capability, a significant assessment gap. The Social Safety Net Project [P508560 | PAD] and its successor [P180999 | PAD] operate in remote rural localities across Djibouti, including northern zones; aide-mémoire evidence from both projects confirms that poor road accessibility creates persistent logistical delays in cash and in-kind transfer distributions, that viable income-generating activities cannot be identified in remote localities due to weak market access, and that rural savings groups (CREC) suffer from weak governance and financial management capacity [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire]. Youth training programs under these projects lack transparent selection criteria and accessibility safeguards, creating documented risks of ethnic favoritism and exclusion of the most vulnerable youth—a risk that is particularly acute in northern areas where the Afar minority is concentrated [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire]. The Statistics and Economic Management Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a> requires field enumeration teams to conduct population census and household survey operations across national territory, including northern regions; ISR evidence confirms security concerns in refugee settlements and active field access challenges, with INSTAD's institutional capacity strained by concurrent survey operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## Djibouti-Ethiopia Corridor and Border Infrastructure

The Djibouti-Addis Ababa corridor—encompassing road networks RN1, RN5, and RN19 and the Guelileh border crossing—is the country's primary economic artery and the focus of the Regional Trade Facilitation and Transport Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. This project faces a compounding set of realized risks that span security, governance, labor standards, and regional diplomatic instability. The escalating Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis—marked by ambassador recalls and Djibouti's military alignment with Mogadishu—directly threatens cross-border coordination capacity; the PAD acknowledges coordination dependency with Ethiopia but does not assess how Somaliland tensions could destabilize the Guelileh border crossing or complicate One-Stop Border Post operations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. ISR evidence confirms that the project's political and governance risk remains "Substantial," with institutional capacity for cross-border coordination persistently weak <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. Aide-mémoire evidence reveals a cascade of realized implementation failures: 150 daily workers are employed under non-compliant contracts without social security registration, exploiting a labor pool with minimal bargaining power in a context of 75% youth unemployment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>; construction sites lack dedicated health, safety, and environment agents with critical violations omitted from official government reports <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>; road safety risks remain unmitigated with high accident rates on project corridors <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>; and informal traders at Arta face resettlement without adequate consultation or alternative livelihood provisions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. Stakeholder engagement risk remains "Substantial" in ISR ratings, consistent with the absence of independent civil society to facilitate authentic community consultation along the corridor <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The macroeconomic risk rating has been downgraded to "Moderate" in the most recent ISR, but structural vulnerabilities—including the fiscal burden of refugee hosting and potential trade disruption from regional diplomatic tensions—remain underlying concerns <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>.

## Dikhil and Southern Zone

The southern regions of Dikhil and Ali Sabieh, bordering Ethiopia and Somalia, represent a zone of overlapping vulnerability where ethnic tensions, economic marginalization, and proximity to regional conflict dynamics converge. The Afar minority—approximately 35% of Djibouti's population—is structurally excluded from political participation dominated by the Issa Somali majority, and the southern zones include ethnically mixed communities where resource allocation decisions carry heightened political sensitivity. The Refugee and Host Community Support Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – PAD</a> targets 12,860 refugees and 15,900 host community residents across southern settlements; ISR evidence confirms that the project failed to achieve legal effectiveness by its original deadline, requiring an extension to August 2025, and that implementing agencies MASS and ADDS face high institutional risk ratings with limited capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506969-c23fc0d1-df16-470e-9ddf-fdd831536a8b.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – ISR</a>. The project's dual beneficiary structure—serving both refugees (predominantly Somali) and host communities in ethnically mixed areas—creates documented tension risks that the PAD acknowledges but does not adequately mitigate <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – PAD</a>. The GBV Prevention and Response Project [P178332 | PAD] operates in vulnerable communities across Djibouti, including southern zones; aide-mémoire evidence confirms that NGO contract negotiations for GBV response services are stalled, psychosocial counseling capacity is insufficient, accounting systems were absent at project launch, financial reporting is delayed, and the Ministry of Women and Family faces critical fiduciary management gaps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-4908093d-8bfd-4f36-a29c-d9575d229601.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Corridor Project's southern operations in Dikhil and Ali Sabieh face additional exposure: the Djibouti-Somaliland crisis could affect refugee populations from Somalia concentrated in this zone, and the absence of ethnic-disaggregated targeting criteria in project design creates vulnerability to elite capture along ethnic lines <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. Presidential succession uncertainty—with recurring health concerns about President Guelleh fueling speculation about a pre-election transition—adds a layer of institutional unpredictability that could disrupt ministerial leadership and government commitment to development priorities across all southern zone projects in the period leading to the April 2026 elections.
