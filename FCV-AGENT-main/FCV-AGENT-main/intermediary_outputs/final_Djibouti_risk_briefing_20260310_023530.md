# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Political Consolidation and Governance Fragility Under Indefinite Presidential Rule

Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms. This deepening political consolidation creates an environment of governance unpredictability that permeates the entire portfolio. The TVET and skills development project is exposed because its governance relies on a multi-ministry steering committee under the Economic Development Council, an executive body with no independent oversight safeguards; political interference in beneficiary selection or resource allocation becomes structurally plausible as executive power concentrates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The contingency emergency response project faces analogous risk, as its activation architecture spans multiple ministries with "unclear mandates," making interministerial consensus-building vulnerable to executive prioritization shifts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The public finance management reform project is particularly exposed because its entire theory of change rests on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—a mitigation strategy that assumes stable political conditions that the constitutional revision directly undermines <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. In implementation, these risks are materializing. The PFM project's political and governance risk has been rated "High" and is sustained, with ISR evidence linking this directly to the institutional fragility created by constitutional manipulation and weakened rule-of-law predictability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The digital connectivity project similarly carries a persistent "High" political and governance risk, with ISR evidence noting that concentration of executive power creates uncertainty around policy continuity and government commitment to multi-year development initiatives <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's steering committee has been inactive, with governance coordination between ministries and development partners stalled, reflecting the broader pattern of institutional subordination to executive authority <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the constitutional revision is not merely a political backdrop—it is actively degrading the institutional architecture on which project governance depends.

## 2. Severe Youth Unemployment and Structural Economic Exclusion

With youth unemployment exceeding 75% and endemic corruption compressing economic opportunity, Djibouti's structural economic fragility creates pervasive implementation risk across projects that depend on labor market absorption, community participation, and institutional capacity. The TVET project's core employment objective is directly threatened: the PAD acknowledges macroeconomic risk as "substantial" but documents no labor market demand assessment or private sector absorption analysis, leaving 23,775 trained graduates potentially without viable employment pathways <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food development project similarly targets youth as direct beneficiaries but provides no macro-level analysis of how training converts to employment in an economy structurally unable to generate sufficient jobs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project identifies "road transport service providers and local traders" as beneficiaries but includes no youth employment targets or mechanisms to translate infrastructure improvements into jobs for the 75%+ unemployed youth population <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The TVET project's online gig work component—explicitly designed to address youth unemployment—has stalled entirely, with ISR evidence indicating that supply-side training interventions cannot overcome demand-side constraints including limited digital infrastructure and lack of formal employment pathways <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The refugee employment indicator for the TVET project remains stuck at 5% against target, with ISR evidence confirming that the labor market lacks sufficient formal sector jobs for either citizens or refugees <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The social protection project has been unable to identify viable income-generating activities in remote localities, with Aide Memoire evidence confirming that weak market access and limited economic diversification are structural barriers beyond project scope <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project's labor compliance monitoring has revealed 150 daily workers employed under non-compliant contracts without social security registration—a direct consequence of workers' minimal bargaining power in a 75%+ unemployment environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. Economic exclusion is not merely a background condition; it is actively undermining project results across the portfolio.

## 3. Ethnic Tensions and Structural Political Exclusion of the Afar Minority

The structural imbalance between Issa Somali dominance (approximately 60% of the population) in governance and the political marginalization of the Afar minority (approximately 35%) creates persistent risks of exclusionary implementation, elite capture, and contested resource allocation across the portfolio. The TVET project targets beneficiaries across multiple regions but includes no ethnic representation safeguards in its steering committee or implementation units, creating vulnerability to political interference or community resistance if beneficiary selection is perceived as ethnically skewed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The emergency response project explicitly targets "entire population including refugees, migrants, IDPs, poor and vulnerable households" but provides no mechanisms to ensure impartial distribution across ethnically diverse populations during emergency activation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The education project targets 29,214 refugees across five regions and refugee villages, and the PAD acknowledges that resource allocation to refugee education could become politically contested if ethnic tensions intensify <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. The corridor project lists "populations living in corridor area" as beneficiaries but provides no ethnic disaggregation or targeted inclusion mechanisms for the Afar minority despite their structural political exclusion <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. In implementation, these risks are manifesting. The corridor project's ISR documents persistent "Substantial" stakeholder engagement risk, with evidence linking this to ethnic tensions and political exclusion dynamics that create trust deficits and coordination failures if project representation is skewed toward dominant groups <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The social protection project's Aide Memoire documents weak grievance mechanisms and inadequate beneficiary participation, with evidence noting that structural ethnic marginalization of the Afar minority creates barriers to inclusive engagement and risks exacerbating perceptions of unequal treatment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's ISR documents zero enrollment data for persons with disabilities, with evidence noting that structural patterns of political exclusion and marginalization extend to other vulnerable populations who lack institutional champions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The education project's ISR documents non-functional School Management Committees (62% functionality), with evidence linking this to ethnic power imbalances in local governance structures where marginalized groups lack meaningful participation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>.

## 4. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Media Freedom

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House, with virtually no independent civil society remaining and journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested, creates a systemic implementation risk that undermines accountability, monitoring, grievance redress, and stakeholder engagement across the entire portfolio. The PFM reform project is most directly exposed: Component 3 explicitly targets "civil society engagement" and "citizen access to digital services," yet the PAD does not acknowledge that the country context makes meaningful civil society participation structurally impossible <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The TVET project's M&E system relies on "independent academic evaluation" and "confidential reporting," but in an environment where independent oversight is absent, these mechanisms are vulnerable to government pressure and self-censorship <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The statistics project's mitigation strategy for political risk explicitly relies on "systematic citizen engagement interventions," yet the country has virtually no independent civil society to engage <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a>. The urban development project's community facilitators and participatory processes operate in an environment where authentic community voice is structurally constrained <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the consequences are pervasive and severe. The corridor project's Aide Memoire documents systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards affecting 150 workers, with evidence directly linking this to the absence of independent unions, labor advocates, or media scrutiny that would otherwise expose violations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project's Aide Memoire also documents inadequate government oversight and weak enforcement of safety standards, with evidence noting that critical violations are omitted from official submissions precisely because there are no independent monitoring mechanisms to demand transparency <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's Aide Memoire documents a compromised internal audit function lacking organizational independence, mirroring the broader erosion of institutional checks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban development project's ISR documents zero implementation by refugee-led CSOs, with evidence confirming that the restrictive governance environment that has dismantled domestic civil society extends to refugee communities, limiting their ability to organize and implement autonomous sub-projects <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The GBV prevention project's Aide Memoire documents stalled NGO contract negotiations, capacity gaps in psychosocial counseling, and missing accounting systems, all linked to the institutional hollowing that follows from the collapse of independent civil society <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Amid Severe Resource Constraints

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers, with 85% residing in refugee villages—alongside 150,000 non-registered migrants, placing acute strain on already fragile public services and infrastructure. This hosting burden, compounded by recurrent droughts, creates direct implementation risks for projects targeting refugee populations and indirect risks for all projects dependent on government institutional capacity. The TVET project targets 3,165 refugees concentrated in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps but includes no contingency for how humanitarian deterioration or resource scarcity would affect training participation and completion rates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The emergency response project faces a structural mismatch: it targets refugee and migrant populations but implementing institutions (SEGRC, MEFI) are rated "high" institutional risk due to "lack of funding and staff" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The food security project explicitly targets refugees for emergency food stock distribution but specifies no allocation protocols between refugee villages and host populations, nor contingency plans for competing demands under resource scarcity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. In implementation, these risks are materializing with measurable severity. The health project's ISR documents refugee immunization rates at 50% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5%—concrete manifestations of the systemic service delivery constraints inherent to hosting large refugee populations amid constrained infrastructure <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The food security project's ISR documents a June 2025 drought declaration that triggered project suspension and resource reallocation, with the project achieving only 50% of food stock targets and zero achievement on water infrastructure rehabilitation for 26 facilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The education project's ISR documents declining refugee student enrollment from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025, with refugee school data still not integrated into the national EMIS system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The urban development project's ISR documents zero implementation of refugee-led CSO sub-projects, zero delivery of vocational training certificates to refugees, and pending restructuring plans for Ali Addeh and Holl Holl villages <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's ISR documents zero refugee enrollment in training programs against a target of 220, with evidence confirming that fragile public service infrastructure is unable to operationalize refugee training delivery <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>.

## 6. Active Insurgent Threat and Regional Security Instability

The FRUD-armé splinter group's October 2023 raid killing seven soldiers, combined with Djibouti's proximity to al-Shabaab in Somalia and the escalating diplomatic crisis with Somaliland—including ambassador recalls and alleged logistical support for anti-Somaliland militias—creates a compounding security environment that threatens field operations, supply chains, and cross-border coordination across the portfolio. The TVET project operates across six regions including Ali Sabieh and Obock, geographically proximate to areas of FRUD-armé activity, yet security risk mitigation is limited to "institutional protocols" without any specific threat assessment for these regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The corridor project's infrastructure along RN1, RN5, and RN19 and the One-Stop Border Post at Guelileh face documented "security/fragility (substantial)" risk from regional instability, but mitigation relies only on "regular security risk assessments" without specific protocols for domestic insurgent threats <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The emergency response project acknowledges operating in the "restive Horn of Africa region" and notes that FRUD-armé activity could impede staff mobility, supply transport, and access to remote beneficiary populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis adds a distinct layer of risk: the corridor project's cross-border operations at Guelileh depend on bilateral customs cooperation with Ethiopia, yet the PAD does not assess how Djibouti's military alignment with Mogadishu could destabilize border operations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The TVET project's labor mobility component—dependent on bilateral negotiations with Kuwait and Qatar—faces risk from Djibouti's deteriorating regional diplomatic standing, which could reduce negotiating leverage with Gulf partners <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. In implementation, the corridor project's ISR documents a persistent "Substantial" political and governance risk with evidence directly linking the Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis to threats against cross-border coordination capacity essential for corridor functionality <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The statistics project's Aide Memoire documents security concerns in refugee settlements as a concrete barrier to field enumeration, with evidence noting that refugee villages as civilian concentrations in a fragile security environment face spillover risks from insurgent activity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>. The food security project's ISR documents the June 2025 drought declaration triggering project suspension, compounding the security-driven operational constraints already affecting implementation in remote regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>. The convergence of domestic insurgency, regional diplomatic escalation, and proxy theatre dynamics involving eight foreign military powers creates a security environment that is both underassessed in project design and increasingly consequential for implementation.
