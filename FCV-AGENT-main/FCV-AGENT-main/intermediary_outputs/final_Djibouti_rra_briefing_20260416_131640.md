**Social unrest and protests:** Djibouti's FCV landscape under this heading is shaped primarily by structural socioeconomic grievances rather than active protest movements, given the government's near-total suppression of dissent. Youth unemployment stands at approximately 73–75%, poverty affects roughly 35% of the population, and extreme inequality persists despite GDP growth of 6.6% in 2024 (DJI-2026-08). The constitutional removal of the presidential age limit in October–November 2025 and President Guelleh's 97.81% electoral result in April 2026 have foreclosed formal political competition, while the LDDH warns of preparations for a lifetime presidency (DJI-2026-01). Analysts warn that perpetuation in power risks popular unrest and clan-based fragmentation reminiscent of neighboring Somalia (DJI-2026-02). Media repression is acute: Djibouti ranked 168th of 180 in the 2025 RSF Press Freedom Index, with journalist arrests in November–December 2025 and documented torture of a detained activist in April–May 2025 (DJI-2026-03). These conditions create latent social instability that directly threatens portfolio projects reliant on community engagement and participatory mechanisms. The Skills for Employment project is susceptible because its employment outcomes depend on private sector absorption of over 23,000 trained graduates in a labor market structurally unable to accommodate them, and its M&E system relies on independent evaluation in an environment where civil society is absent <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. In implementation, zero refugees have been enrolled or completed TVET programs, zero bilateral labor agreements have been signed, and the online gig work component is being deleted due to coordination failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The Urban Poverty Reduction project targets 115,000 slum inhabitants through participatory processes, but youth unemployment and the absence of independent civil society undermine authentic community engagement <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a>; the socioeconomic inclusion and social cohesion assessment for refugees in target areas remained incomplete as of May 2025, and refugee-led CSO sub-projects stand at zero of a 60-unit target <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The Social Safety Nets projects face analogous constraints, with weak grievance mechanisms, inadequate stakeholder mobilization documentation, and risks of unequal access in youth training programs flagged across both <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Violence between refugees, host communities, and the state:** Djibouti hosts approximately 33,360 refugees and asylum seekers—over 3% of its population—primarily from Somalia, Ethiopia, and Yemen, with 85% concentrated in refugee villages such as Ali Addeh, Holl-Holl, and Markazi (DJI-2026-07). IOM recorded 506,600 migratory movements through Djibouti in 2025, an 18% increase over 2024, with over 900 migrant deaths on the Eastern Route making it the deadliest year on record; smuggler tensions in Djibouti were expected to continue into early 2026 (DJI-2026-06). US funding cuts have created humanitarian gaps, with IOM warning that shelters could close, cutting off medical care for trafficking survivors and unaccompanied children (DJI-2026-07). Despite a progressive 2017 Refugee Law, implementation gaps persist, and recurrent droughts, flooding, and disease outbreaks compound pressures on both refugees and host communities. Multiple portfolio projects are directly exposed. The Inclusive Urban Development and Land Management project targets 12,860 refugees and 15,900 host community residents, with the PAD identifying social conflict as a substantial risk and noting that resource allocation between the two groups could become contested <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – PAD</a>; the project had not yet achieved legal effectiveness as of June 2025, and refugee-host integration data remain at zero <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506969-c23fc0d1-df16-470e-9ddf-fdd831536a8b.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – ISR</a>. The Skills for Employment project targets 3,165 refugees concentrated in Ali Addeh, Holl-Holl, and Markazi, but refugee enrollment, completion, work-based learning participation, and employment outcomes are all at or near zero <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Education project directly targets 29,214 refugees for integration into the national system, but refugee enrollment has declined from 596 in 2021–22 to 384 in 2024–25, refugee schools remain outside the EMIS, and zero recommendations from sectoral action plans for operationalizing the 2017 Refugee Law have been implemented <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The Food Security project explicitly includes refugees as food distribution beneficiaries but lacks documented coordination mechanisms to manage host-refugee tensions during distribution <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>; a June 2025 drought declaration triggered project suspension and restructuring, with only 15.2% disbursement as of October 2024 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Organized violence between political and sectarian groups:** Djibouti does not face active inter-sectarian conflict, but the portfolio is exposed to latent ethnic tensions between the Issa Somali majority (approximately 60%) and the Afar minority (approximately 35%), as well as to the residual threat from FRUD-armé, which conducted a raid killing seven soldiers in October 2023. The ruling coalition shows signs of internal strain, including the September 2025 resignation of senior advisor Alexis Mohamed and a pattern of politically motivated arrests of senior political and security figures (DJI-2026-02). Ethnic imbalances in governance create risks that project targeting and resource allocation will be perceived as favoring dominant groups, potentially triggering grievances. The Djibouti-Addis Ababa Regional Integration project operates along RN1, RN5, and RN19 corridors and at the Guelileh border crossing, with the PAD identifying security/fragility as a substantial risk from regional instability and noting the project is in conflict-affected areas, yet mitigation relies only on regular security risk assessments without specific protocols for domestic insurgent threats <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>; political and governance risk has remained Substantial across all rating periods, and the July 2025 Aide Memoire documented systemic labor rights violations affecting 150 daily workers, critical safety failures, and weak government oversight with non-conformities unreported in official submissions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The Statistics and Economic Management project requires field teams for census and household surveys across national territory, with the PAD acknowledging security risks and road safety concerns during census activities <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a>; INSTAD's institutional capacity is being strained by multiple concurrent surveys, with the migrants and refugees survey requiring UNHCR facilitation for field access to refugee villages <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Emergency Resilience project operates across six regions including rural and pastoral areas, with the PAD noting regional instability affecting trade routes but lacking specific security protocols for insurgent threats <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>; water tanker trucks deployed to regions were found not following predefined routes, requiring urgent engagement with regional prefects <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Political instability:** The constitutional manipulation of October–November 2025 removing the presidential age limit, combined with succession uncertainty around the 78-year-old President Guelleh and speculation about his stepson as preferred successor, creates a medium-term horizon of elite fragmentation risk (DJI-2026-01, DJI-2026-02). BTI 2026 notes that a carefully managed transition rather than open competition is anticipated, but that elite fragmentation could occur if expectations are not met. The government's opaque policymaking, absence of a freedom of information law, and limited transparency on investment deals with Chinese entities further constrain independent oversight of World Bank-supported reforms (DJI-2026-11). The PFM Modernization project is most directly exposed: the PAD identifies political risk as High, relying on government commitment demonstrated through past successes, but the project cannot become effective because the government has not signed and published required governance decrees despite Council of Ministers confirmation on 25 February 2025, and delays in operationalizing PFM Reform Strategy governance structures are affecting all related donor projects <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The Economic Diversification PforR links disbursements to reform milestones in SOE governance and business climate, but the absence of independent civil society creates structural constraints on participatory implementation and social accountability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>; political and governance risk has been rated High across all assessment periods <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The Skills for Employment project's steering committee was not yet active as of February 2025, with the mission in discussion with institutions on an alternative governance arrangement, and EFTP governance modernization regulatory texts were signed but not yet effectively applied <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Statistics project faces persistent Substantial political and governance risk, with staffing constraints slowing Medium-Term Debt Strategy development and longstanding fragmentation in aid coordination requiring a consultant review to redesign the development cooperation framework <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0884c1bc2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>.

**External risks and intra-state conflict:** Djibouti's strategic location at the Bab el-Mandeb Strait exposes it to acute external risks. Iranian officials and Houthi leaders signaled readiness to disrupt shipping in connection with the US-Iran conflict that escalated in February 2026, with a senior Iranian source telling Reuters on 7 April 2026 that allies could close the strait, creating an existential threat to Djibouti's port-based economy (DJI-2026-04). The country hosts the densest cluster of foreign military bases in the world, with US Camp Lemonnier and China's first overseas military base only seven miles apart; Chinese state-owned entities hold over half of Djibouti's estimated $2.6 billion external debt, and the country suspended debt repayments to China in late 2022 before reaching a moratorium (DJI-2026-05). Over 90% of Ethiopia's trade transits through Djiboutian ports, exposing the economy to shocks from any Ethiopian trade diversion or Red Sea disruption (DJI-2026-09). The Djibouti-Addis Ababa Regional Integration project is most directly exposed: the PAD acknowledges coordination dependency with Ethiopia and identifies macroeconomic risk, which has been upgraded from Moderate at approval to Substantial in the most recent rating, reflecting worsening economic conditions; the project also faces persistent Substantial political and governance risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The PAD notes that activities requiring Ethiopia collaboration are conditional on mutual understanding between customs authorities, but does not assess how Houthi-related Red Sea disruption or US-China rivalry could affect corridor viability or border operations at Guelileh <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The Food Security project's Market Analysis Unit and Food Shortage Preparedness Fund depend on stable trade access, but the June 2025 drought declaration triggered suspension of main activities and a restructuring request, with reallocation of SDR 3,925,000 from preparedness to emergency food stock, reducing long-term resilience <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>. The DJIRESA rural water project, approved March 2026, acknowledges that a cross-border pipeline from Ethiopia supplies roughly one-quarter of urban water use, representing a transboundary water security risk whose long-term reliability depends on climate conditions in Ethiopia—a risk that is not mitigated in the current project design <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>.
