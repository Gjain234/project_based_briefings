# Lebanon FCV Portfolio Briefing

## Paragraph 1: Active Israel-Hezbollah Military Escalation and Direct Security Threats to Project Implementation

Lebanon is experiencing a severe armed conflict that erupted on 2 March 2026, when Hezbollah fired rockets and drones into northern Israel following a US-Israeli operation against Iran, triggering sweeping Israeli airstrikes and a ground offensive across southern Lebanon, Beirut's southern suburbs, and the Bekaa Valley. As of late March 2026, at least 1,238 people have been killed and more than 3,500 injured, with no ceasefire imminent. This risk cuts directly across the entire World Bank portfolio in Lebanon. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> explicitly targets southern Lebanon, the Bekaa Valley, and Beirut's southern suburbs—the exact zones of active military operations—and its PAD acknowledges that "geopolitical risks from Middle East conflict may impede implementation in border areas" and that "security conditions may restrict WB team visits for physical verification." The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a> states categorically that "no works will commence until conflict has ended and sites are safe for contractors and workers to resume operations," with works already suspended since the 2024 escalation. The Solid Waste Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> operates in Union of Municipalities of Tyre and Abbasiyeh—primary conflict zones—requiring on-site field teams for dumpsite rehabilitation that cannot safely deploy. The Beqaa Valley Water and Wastewater Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a> identifies that "ongoing Syrian conflict creates security concerns in the Beqaa region, potentially limiting mobility of consultants and contractors," a risk now acutely realized. These risks are materializing across the portfolio: aide memoire evidence for <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a> confirms that contractors suspended all sewage network works in the Beqaa due to military escalation beginning September 2024, that project teams are conducting virtual-only missions, and that UXO clearance is required before any work can resume. Similarly, <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a> documents that Component 2 activities in Nabatieh and Tyre were put on hold due to unstable security conditions, forcing a shift to remote data collection. The Beirut Critical Environmental Recovery Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176577-6c3b4a27-9cf9-4050-9a10-ed310209e452.pdf" target="_blank">P176577 – Aide Memoire</a> faces a tight October 2025 completion deadline with zero slippage tolerance, directly threatened by the conflict's unpredictability. Across the portfolio, the forward-looking risk of conflict-driven implementation suspension has become a realized constraint, with the UN Special Coordinator confirming in March 2026 that "a major World Bank loan was set to jump-start reconstruction but that progress has now collapsed due to the renewed hostilities" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>.

## Paragraph 2: Mass Internal Displacement, Humanitarian Crisis, and Collapse of Public Service Delivery

The March 2026 escalation has displaced approximately one quarter of Lebanon's population—over 1.2 million people internally displaced, with more than 136,000 sheltering in 660 collective sites at full capacity. Israeli displacement orders now cover approximately 14 percent of Lebanese territory. This displacement surge, compounding prior waves from 2024, is overwhelming public services including healthcare, water, sanitation, and electricity systems, and is directly disrupting project beneficiary targeting, service delivery, and institutional capacity across the portfolio. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> identifies 1.2 million affected people as its target beneficiaries and includes decommissioning IDP centers as a core activity, but the March 2026 escalation has created 667,000+ new displacements in a single week, rendering prioritization criteria and targeting mechanisms obsolete. The Social Recovery Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a> targets 15,475 beneficiaries across GBV survivors, mental health cases, and persons with disabilities—populations now scattered across collective shelters and host communities—while its mobile medical units and safe shelters require stable, accessible locations that active hostilities cannot guarantee. The Beqaa Valley Water Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a> identifies 344,000 direct beneficiaries and 800,000 indirect beneficiaries in Beqaa Governorate, but displacement from southern Lebanon and the Bekaa Valley has fundamentally altered the geographic distribution of the population the project was designed to serve. The COVID-19 Health System Strengthening Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a> targets 4.7 million people including 1.5 million Syrian and Palestinian refugees in high-concentration areas, but mass displacement disrupts geographic targeting and strains the public hospital infrastructure the project depends on. These risks are materializing: ISR evidence for <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a> confirms that service delivery to vulnerable populations in conflict-affected areas faces severe access and security challenges, with NGO partners losing core funding precisely when humanitarian demand has surged. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a> documents that the health system is under severe pressure from refugee and host community utilization, with hospital equipment availability at only 56 percent against a 70 percent target. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a> confirms that security delays in Nabatieh and Tyre have forced project teams to rely on virtual tools rather than field-based implementation. The collapse of public services—electricity providing minimal service, water and sanitation systems strained, healthcare overwhelmed—is simultaneously a realized risk and a forward-looking threat to every project in the portfolio that depends on functional infrastructure to deliver results <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 3: Sectarian Political Fragmentation, Institutional Paralysis, and Democratic Backsliding

Lebanon's confessional political system has produced chronic institutional paralysis that is now deepening under renewed conflict. On 9 March 2026, parliament voted to extend its term by two years—the fourth such extension in a decade—postponing elections originally scheduled for May 2026. Simultaneously, Hezbollah has flatly refused to cooperate with disarmament north of the Litani River, with senior officials walking out of cabinet sessions over disarmament discussions and warning that government pressure would lead to "instability, chaos, and perhaps even civil war." Analysts warn that the Lebanese state faces an acute structural dilemma: forcing disarmament risks civil war, while failing to do so invites further Israeli strikes and delays international reconstruction financing. This governance environment permeates every project in the portfolio. The Fiscal Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-5067fa60-78e4-4f5e-b6c1-5a428d852b01.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – PAD</a> is designed to "operate despite ongoing political deadlock" through technical-level implementation, but its Project Steering Committee—chaired by the Minister of Finance and comprising heads of relevant agencies—requires consensus-building in an environment where Hezbollah officials walk out of cabinet sessions. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> requires Lebanese Army clearance for ERW and coordination across multiple ministries, but the LAF cannot confront Hezbollah without risking sectarian splits within its own ranks. The Solid Waste Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> requires a Project Steering Committee including nine ministries and agencies, while the Beqaa Valley Water Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a> depends on the Qaraoun Committee for multi-agency oversight in areas where Hezbollah maintains significant presence. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. ISR evidence for <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a> confirms that parliamentary ratification of the IBRD loan remains pending, with the project not yet effective, directly reflecting the institutional paralysis that has blocked legislative action on development financing. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-f112aa75-2e9c-4c03-ac0e-46311eb3089b.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – ISR</a> documents that the project cannot obtain necessary approvals from Beirut Governorate and Municipality, with procurement delays attributed to "external coordination challenges among key government stakeholders." The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-4727dec5-5d09-4ce2-8055-7dcb271aae12.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – ISR</a> shows fiduciary risk escalating from Substantial to High, with governance dysfunction undermining financial controls. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176577-4f8e4069-1a4c-4b78-b5bb-344e2c6a59af.pdf" target="_blank">P176577 – ISR</a> records persistent "High" stakeholder tensions and political and governance instability ratings, while <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a> confirms that institutional capacity constraints and fiduciary risks remain elevated across 11 sub-projects. The forward-looking risk is that the parliamentary term extension removes electoral accountability precisely when governance capacity is most needed, while the Hezbollah disarmament deadlock signals that the state's monopoly on legitimate force remains contested—conditions that will continue to impair every project requiring coordinated inter-ministerial action <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 4: Economic Recovery Reversal and Macroeconomic Collapse

Lebanon had achieved a fragile 3.5 percent real GDP growth in 2025—its first positive expansion in years—driven by tourism, remittances, and reform progress including passage of a revised banking secrecy law. The World Bank had projected 4 percent growth for 2026 contingent on political stability and reform momentum. The March 2026 escalation has reversed this trajectory entirely. Real GDP has declined more than 38 percent since 2019, public debt stands at an estimated 357 percent of GDP, the banking sector carries over $72 billion in accumulated losses, and depositors remain locked out of savings above minimal monthly withdrawal limits. IMF negotiations remain stalled as Lebanon has not yet passed the "financial gap law," leaving the country outside international capital markets. This macroeconomic collapse is a binding constraint on every project in the portfolio. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a> identifies macroeconomic risk as "high," noting that "Lebanon experienced severe economic contraction of nearly 35% since 2019" and that "rising construction costs reduce bidder interest and increase procurement risks," with the government's "inability to finance contracts" cited as a cause of implementation delays. The Lebanon Electricity Transmission and Distribution Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c260299b-4517-48c7-a0c1-b95eb42d17a7.pdf" target="_blank">P180501 – PAD</a> identifies a structural currency mismatch between revenues in Lebanese pounds and costs in US dollars, with the Central Bank able to provide only approximately $1 billion per year in hard currency against sector needs—a constraint that has prevented operationalization of the Cash Waterfall Mechanism by the June 2025 target. The Beqaa Valley Water Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a> requires government counterpart funding of $5 million and assumes tariff increases and cost recovery improvements, but with 70 percent of the population in need of humanitarian assistance, tariff increases are politically and economically infeasible. The Agricultural and Rural Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-c14c98c6-d426-4177-9a31-f1acaae2fb97.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – PAD</a> targets SME lending and farmer investment, but with the banking sector collapsed and depositors locked out of savings, the enabling environment for agricultural credit has effectively disappeared. These risks are materializing: ISR evidence for <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180334-4899e0cd-2b9b-432c-88d6-4a6b7c577697.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – ISR</a> shows zero farmers reached, zero SME loans approved, and zero rural infrastructure completed as of January 2025, with the project stalled entirely due to "political instability and governance fragility." The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504170-acef5b66-7711-4d27-aa64-52a05ecd1751.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – ISR</a> confirms macroeconomic risk remains "High," with currency devaluation and inflation pressures undermining project viability. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180501-1bd1cf0b-8078-42e6-a4bc-c43a782224ca.pdf" target="_blank">P180501 – ISR</a> documents persistent "High" macroeconomic risk with EDL and LRA institutional capacity rated "High" risk due to resource depletion. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a> confirms macroeconomic crisis is impacting disbursements and operational costs. The forward-looking risk is that the March 2026 conflict has halted the nascent recovery momentum that was the precondition for all project sustainability assumptions, with the UN Special Coordinator warning that "progress has now come to a screeching halt" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 5: Destruction of Critical Infrastructure and Collapse of World Bank Reconstruction Framework

Israeli airstrikes have systematically destroyed roads, bridges, water pipelines, and fuel stations across southern Lebanon, with satellite imagery confirming at least seven bridges over the Litani River struck, isolating over 150,000 people. WHO documented 64 attacks on healthcare facilities between 2 and 28 March 2026, killing at least 53 healthcare workers and closing 47 primary health care centers and five hospitals. The UN and partners have appealed for $308.3 million to fund a three-month emergency response, while humanitarian actors report access to conflict-affected areas remains "critical." This infrastructure destruction directly threatens the World Bank's $250 million Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project (LEAP) and the broader $1 billion scalable reconstruction framework approved in June 2025—the programmatic umbrella under which most portfolio projects operate. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> was explicitly designed as part of this framework, targeting restoration of lifeline services in conflict-affected areas including water, energy, health, education, transport, and municipal services. The Lebanon Electricity Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c260299b-4517-48c7-a0c1-b95eb42d17a7.pdf" target="_blank">P180501 – PAD</a> targets hydropower plants in the Litani River basin (Qaraoun Dam, Joun, Awali, Markabi) and transmission infrastructure in the Bekaa Valley—areas now subject to active Israeli military operations and documented targeting of reconstruction equipment by Israeli forces. The Beirut Critical Environmental Recovery Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088d3d75f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176577 – PAD</a> depends on functioning electricity, water, and transport infrastructure for construction operations, with a tight October 2025 completion deadline. The Solid Waste Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> requires on-site access to dumpsites in southern Lebanon for rehabilitation and closure activities that cannot proceed under active hostilities. These risks are materializing comprehensively. Aide memoire evidence for <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176577-6c3b4a27-9cf9-4050-9a10-ed310209e452.pdf" target="_blank">P176577 – Aide Memoire</a> documents that owner access denial is blocking structural rehabilitation works, utility reconnection barriers are preventing housing access for beneficiaries, and building cancellations are creating reputational damage as reconstruction commitments cannot be honored. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a> confirms that asbestos-contaminated rubble management at the Bakalian site cannot be completed within project timelines, that OHS violations at the Karantina Sorting Facility reflect institutional fragility in post-conflict reconstruction, and that fund transfer delays between the Ministry of Finance and Ministry of Energy are preventing project execution. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a> documents that the project is conducting virtual-only missions due to ongoing regional conflict, that UXO clearance is required before work can resume, and that CDR/PMU government capacity is severely constrained by the security situation. ISR evidence for <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-f112aa75-2e9c-4c03-ac0e-46311eb3089b.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – ISR</a> confirms that contaminated rubble management activities are pending transfer to the LEAP, which itself is pending parliamentary ratification—a direct manifestation of how governance paralysis and conflict have compounded to halt the reconstruction agenda. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a> records persistent "High" institutional capacity risk and macroeconomic instability, with stakeholder engagement severely compromised by active conflict. The forward-looking risk is that the development-to-humanitarian aid pivot risks entrenching Lebanon in crisis-response mode rather than enabling the structural reforms needed for long-term recovery, while UNIFIL's scheduled closure by end of 2026 will remove the only systematic documentation of violations and monitoring mechanism in southern Lebanon—further increasing uncontrolled escalation risk in the exact areas where portfolio projects are concentrated <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a>.
