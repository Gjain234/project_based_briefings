**Social unrest and protests:** Djibouti's structural conditions for social unrest are acute and worsening. Youth unemployment exceeds 70–73%, poverty affects 35.5% of the population, and the 2023 Global Hunger Index rated the country at "serious" severity, with acute malnutrition rising to 14.7% in 2024. The April 2026 presidential election—conducted in a tightly controlled environment following constitutional amendments removing the age limit and tightening candidate eligibility—has deepened public grievances, with analysts warning that indefinite rule "paves the way for popular uprisings." Intercommunal clashes in Balbala's Warabaley neighbourhood in December 2025 left at least five dead and hundreds displaced, illustrating how electoral politics can ignite latent communal tensions. The dissolution of the sovereign wealth fund in April 2025 and an unsustainable public debt of 64% of GDP further constrain the government's capacity for social investment. Projects targeting economically marginalised populations are directly exposed. The Djibouti Skills Development and Employment Project is susceptible because its employment outcomes depend on private sector absorption of over 23,000 trained graduates in a labour market where structural unemployment is endemic <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The Inclusive Urban Development and Land Management Project targets 115,000 slum inhabitants and relies on participatory processes that high youth unemployment and economic desperation may undermine <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a>. The Agri-Food System Resilience Project faces similar constraints, with its youth and rural beneficiary targeting potentially overwhelmed by structural economic barriers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the Skills project shows zero refugee and disability enrollment across multiple TVET indicators, with bilateral labour agreements and gig-work components entirely stalled—the online gig-work indicator is being deleted at restructuring due to "significant delays" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The Urban Development project has not yet conducted its beneficiary perception survey and shows zero refugee-led CSO sub-projects implemented against a target of 60 <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The Productive Social Safety Net projects report that remote rural communities face difficulty identifying viable income-generating activities due to weak market access, with youth training equity risks flagged as requiring proactive management <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Violence between refugees, host communities, and the state:** Djibouti hosts approximately 33,360 refugees and asylum seekers—over 3% of its population—primarily from Somalia, Ethiopia, and Yemen, with 85% concentrated in the Ali Addeh, Holl-Holl, and Markazi refugee villages. The Eastern Migration Route recorded its deadliest year in 2025, with over 900 migrants killed or missing, and IOM warned in February 2026 that underfunding could force shelter closures cutting medical care for trafficking survivors and unaccompanied children. Resource competition between refugee and host communities is a persistent structural risk, compounded by the government's failure to operationalise the 2017 National Refugee Law. Multiple portfolio projects are directly exposed to this dynamic. The Contingency Emergency Response Project explicitly targets 32,000 refugees and asylum seekers alongside host communities, with the PAD acknowledging that implementing agencies SEGRC and MEFI have "limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The Inclusive Urban Development project targets refugees as direct beneficiaries but has not completed its socio-economic inclusion and social cohesion assessment, and refugee village restructuring plans for Ali Addeh and Holl-Holl remain pending approval <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a>. The Djibouti Inclusive Education project directly targets 29,214 refugees across five regions for integration into the national education system <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the Urban Development project shows zero property access certificates delivered to refugees, zero recommendations from refugee law sectoral action plans implemented, and zero civil servants trained on the 2017 Refugee Law <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The Education project records declining refugee enrollment—from 596 in 2021–22 to 384 in 2024–25—with refugee schools not yet integrated into the national EMIS <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The Skills project shows zero refugee enrollment in TVET diploma programs, zero completion, and zero employment outcomes for refugees, with a consultant only recently beginning profiling in Ali Addeh, Holhol, and Markazi camps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Health System Strengthening project reports that disaggregated immunisation data for refugees remains unavailable and vitamin A supplementation coverage among refugee children has declined from 10% to 5% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>.

**Organised violence between political and sectarian groups:** The FRUD-Armé insurgency represents the most acute organised violence risk in the portfolio. In January 2025, the Djiboutian army conducted drone strikes in Syarou targeting Afar civilians, with the LDDH claiming 14 killed including women and children. FRUD-A has reportedly seized military posts in Tadjoura, Obock, and Dikhil, and the government classified the group as a terrorist organisation in October 2024. Intercommunal reprisal violence between Issa and Afar communities—including home-burning and looting in Djibouti City—erupted in the wake of these incidents. Cross-border dynamics with Ethiopia's Afar Triangle and al-Shabaab's continued activity in Somalia compound the threat. Several projects operate in or near conflict-affected zones. The Djibouti-Ethiopia Transport Corridor project runs infrastructure along RN1, RN5, and RN19 and includes a One-Stop Border Post at Guelileh, with the PAD acknowledging "security/fragility (substantial)" risk from regional instability but without specific FRUD-A protocols <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The Skills project operates in Ali Sabieh and Obock—geographically proximate to FRUD-A activity—with 3,165 refugee beneficiaries concentrated in camps in these regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The Agri-Food Resilience project includes infrastructure in the Northern Zone (Tadjoura and Obock) where the PAD asserts "no security risks identified" despite active insurgent presence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the Transport Corridor project records persistent "Substantial" political and governance risk ratings, with the July 2025 Aide Memoire documenting critical safety failures on construction sites—absent HSE agents, unsecured trenches, 150 daily labourers without compliant contracts or social security registration—and 13 road accidents since project start <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The Statistics and Economic Management project required UNHCR support to facilitate field access to refugee villages for survey enumeration, reflecting security constraints on data collection in displacement-affected areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Political instability:** Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision—removing the presidential age limit, tightening candidate eligibility to bar exiled rivals, and eliminating the referendum requirement for new constitutions—entrenched President Guelleh's sixth term following his April 2026 election with 97.81% of the vote. Analysts warn that succession uncertainty around the 78-year-old president, speculation over dynastic succession through his stepson, and intra-elite tensions illustrated by the September 2025 resignation of senior advisor Alexis Mohamed create structural fragility within the ruling coalition. The BTI 2026 Country Report finds that democratic institutions lack independence due to executive dominance. This governance environment directly threatens projects dependent on sustained political commitment and multi-ministerial coordination. The Economic and Financial Governance Project faces the most acute exposure: the government adopted the required PFM Reform Decree and Order in the Council of Ministers on 25 February 2025 but has not signed or published them in the Official Gazette, blocking project effectiveness and affecting all donor-funded PFM projects in Djibouti <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The political and governance risk rating for this project is rated "High" across all assessment periods <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The Statistics project maintains a "Substantial" political and governance risk rating throughout implementation, with staffing constraints slowing Medium-Term Debt Strategy formulation and longstanding aid coordination fragmentation requiring a consultant redesign of the development cooperation framework <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0884c1bc2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>. The Skills project's steering committee remains inactive, with the February 2025 Aide Memoire noting the team is "in discussion with concerned institutions on a definitive alternative" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Digital Foundations for Djibouti project faces a persistent governance gap: the Digital Code (Code du Numérique), particularly Book Two on telecommunications regulation, remains unadopted despite repeated mission recommendations, blocking multiple project activities and leaving the regulatory authority ARMD without its primary operational tool; ARMD board appointments have been pending since 2022 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-9e33bd55-6107-428e-bde8-2dc02b8b55cf.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>.

**External risks and intra-state conflict:** Djibouti's economy is structurally dependent on port and logistics activities accounting for nearly 70% of GDP and handling over 90% of Ethiopia's trade. Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping since late 2023 drove major carriers to reroute around the Cape of Good Hope; port traffic declined 7% in Q1 2025 and 10.5% at mid-year. By March–April 2026, Iranian threats to close the Bab el-Mandeb following Operation Epic Fury have renewed maritime uncertainty, with US assets at Camp Lemonnier explicitly referenced as potential targets. Djibouti's hosting of eight foreign military bases in a territory smaller than 25,000 km² creates grey-zone incident risk and great-power friction. Ethiopia's efforts to diversify port dependency and the Afar Triangle's cross-border dynamics with Ethiopia and Eritrea add further external pressure. The Transport Corridor project is the most directly exposed, as its macroeconomic risk rating has been upgraded from "Moderate" at approval to "Substantial" in the most recent ISR, reflecting worsening economic conditions and fiscal pressures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The PAD acknowledges that "job losses from decreased goods volume through Djibouti ports" could offset project benefits, and stakeholder risk remains "Substantial" across all assessment periods <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The Food Security and Resilience project (PURCSA) has been severely disrupted: the government declared a drought emergency in June 2025, triggering project suspension and a restructuring request that reallocates SDR 3.925 million from the Food Shortage Preparedness Fund to emergency food stocks, while rehabilitation of 26 of 37 targeted water infrastructure facilities has been discontinued <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>. Procurement delays of up to 10 weeks in the signature circuit—requiring ministerial intervention to prioritise emergency files—have left disbursement at only 15.2% one year before project closure <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Statistics project faces institutional capacity strain from multiple concurrent data collection activities overwhelming INSTAD, with the EDAM5 household survey already delayed from October to December 2024 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>.
