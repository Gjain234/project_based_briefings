# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Political Consolidation and Governance Fragility Under Indefinite Presidential Rule

Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—has deepened an already fragile governance environment characterized by weakened institutional checks, reduced policy predictability, and growing executive capture of state functions. Across the portfolio, projects are structurally exposed to this risk through their dependence on multi-ministerial coordination and sustained political commitment. The TVET and skills development project relies on a high-level steering committee under the Economic Development Council, but the PAD acknowledges substantial political risk from governance challenges without providing safeguards against executive interference in beneficiary selection or resource allocation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The public finance management reform project identifies political risk as "high," with mitigation resting almost entirely on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that becomes increasingly fragile as constitutional manipulation signals a shift away from rules-based governance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project similarly flags that "ability to influence private sector finance mobilization depends on political will," with mitigation contingent on securing "commitment at the highest state level"—a strategy that is inherently vulnerable when executive power is concentrated and institutional autonomy is eroded <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The PFM reform project is experiencing a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with administrative delays in publishing governance decrees blocking project operationalization despite ministerial approval—a pattern consistent with institutional prioritization of political consolidation over development commitments <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The digital economy project has seen ARMD board appointments stalled since 2022, with the regulatory authority unable to adopt the Digital Code Book Two on telecommunications regulation, reflecting institutional subordination to executive authority rather than autonomous regulatory function <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-9e33bd55-6107-428e-bde8-2dc02b8b55cf.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's steering committee has been inactive, with governance coordination between government ministries and development partners (World Bank, USAID) failing to achieve effective formalization—a dynamic consistent with inter-ministerial coordination becoming increasingly dependent on executive discretion <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. Taken together, these patterns suggest that political consolidation is not merely a forward-looking risk but an active constraint on institutional performance across the portfolio.

## 2. Severe Youth Unemployment, Economic Inequality, and Structural Labor Market Fragility

With youth unemployment exceeding 75% and endemic corruption compressing economic opportunity, Djibouti's structural economic fragility creates compounding risks for projects that depend on private sector absorption of trained graduates, community economic participation, or institutional capacity sustained by a functional labor market. The TVET project is acutely exposed: the PAD targets 23,775 trained graduates across diploma, short-term, and work-based pathways, but acknowledges macroeconomic risk as "substantial" without conducting a labor market demand assessment or documenting employer absorption capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The food security project's livelihood restoration component targets farmers and livestock herders but provides no youth employment pathways, wage specifications, or income sustainability mechanisms within its drought mitigation activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project lists road transport service providers and local traders as beneficiaries but provides no youth employment targets or analysis of how corridor improvements translate to jobs for the 75%+ unemployed youth population <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing. The TVET project's online gig work component—designed specifically to address youth unemployment—has stalled entirely, with zero results achieved; the evidence suggests that supply-side training interventions cannot overcome demand-side constraints including limited digital infrastructure and the absence of formal employment pathways <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The project's refugee employment outcome indicator remains stuck at 5% against an 80% target, reflecting the structural inability of the host country labor market to absorb trained graduates even with targeted interventions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The social protection project has been unable to identify viable income-generating activities in remote localities due to weak market access and limited economic diversification—a localized manifestation of the economy-wide failure to create sustainable employment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project is experiencing persistent stakeholder engagement difficulties partly driven by community skepticism about whether project benefits will translate to meaningful local employment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. Across the portfolio, the evidence confirms that project-level skills and livelihood interventions are insufficient to overcome the structural economic constraints that drive Djibouti's youth unemployment crisis.

## 3. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Media Freedom as a Cross-Cutting Implementation Constraint

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House—with virtually no independent civil society remaining and journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested—creates a structural governance deficit that undermines accountability, monitoring, stakeholder engagement, and grievance mechanisms across every project in the portfolio. The PFM reform project's Component 3 explicitly targets civil society engagement and citizen oversight of public finances, but the PAD does not acknowledge that the country context makes meaningful independent civil society participation structurally impossible <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The statistics modernization project relies on "systematic citizen engagement interventions" as a mitigation strategy for political risk, but this approach depends on civil society that does not exist in practice <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a>. The TVET project's M&E system includes "independent academic evaluation" and confidential GRM reporting, but both mechanisms are vulnerable to government pressure and self-censorship in an environment where independent verification is structurally compromised <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing pervasively. The corridor project is experiencing systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards—150 daily workers operating under non-compliant contracts without social security registration—with no independent actors available to monitor, report, or enforce labor compliance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The same project's government oversight reports lack detail on non-conformities, with critical safety violations omitted from official documentation, reflecting the absence of independent scrutiny that would normally expose such gaps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban upgrading project's refugee-led CSO sub-projects have achieved zero implementation, consistent with the hostile environment for autonomous civil society that extends to refugee communities attempting to organize <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The GBV prevention project launched without accounting software in place, with delayed financial reporting and stalled NGO contract negotiations—patterns enabled by the absence of independent oversight mechanisms that would otherwise flag and pressure remediation of basic fiduciary failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the collapse of independent civil society is not a background condition but an active implementation constraint that is degrading accountability, safeguards compliance, and community voice in real time.

## 4. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Amid Severe Resource Constraints

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers, with 85% concentrated in refugee villages—while simultaneously managing recurrent droughts and severely constrained public finances. This creates a structural mismatch between humanitarian hosting obligations and institutional delivery capacity that is directly manifesting in project implementation failures across the portfolio. The emergency contingency response project explicitly identifies SEGRC and MEFI as having "limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff," while simultaneously targeting refugee and migrant populations (totaling approximately 5% of the population) for emergency response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The TVET project targets 3,165 refugees as direct beneficiaries but does not assess how recurrent droughts or resource scarcity would affect training delivery or beneficiary retention <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The food security project targets emergency food stocks for refugees but specifies no allocation percentages or distribution protocols between refugee villages and host communities, creating risk of competing claims in resource-scarce settings <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing with measurable severity. The health project serving refugee populations reports immunization coverage of only 12% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5%—concrete manifestations of the institutional fragility and resource limitations that prevent sustained preventive health delivery to displaced populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The TVET project has enrolled zero refugees against a target of 220, with zero employment outcomes tracked for refugee graduates, reflecting the systemic inability of resource-constrained institutions to operationalize refugee-focused programming <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The urban upgrading project has stalled on refugee village restructuring plans for Ali Addeh and Holl Holl, with pending validation and approval reflecting administrative bottlenecks rooted in the same institutional fragility that characterizes the country's refugee management system <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The food security project has achieved only 50% of its emergency food stock target and zero water infrastructure rehabilitation, directly reducing drought response capacity for the 32,000 displaced persons the country hosts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The statistics project requires boosted sampling of migrant and refugee households in both the national census and household budget survey, with field enumeration facing security concerns in refugee settlements—demonstrating how displacement dynamics complicate even basic data collection <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0884c1bc2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Ethnic Tensions, Political Exclusion, and Risks to Equitable Project Delivery

Djibouti's structural ethnic imbalance—with the Issa Somali group (approximately 60% of the population) dominating governance while the Afar minority (approximately 35%) remains politically marginalized—creates persistent risks of elite capture, exclusionary benefit distribution, and community resistance that cut across the portfolio's targeting and implementation mechanisms. The TVET project's multi-ministry coordination structure does not address how ethnic balance will be maintained in steering committees or implementation units, creating vulnerability to political interference in beneficiary selection <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project lists "populations living in corridor area" as beneficiaries without any ethnic disaggregation or targeted inclusion mechanisms for the Afar minority despite their structural political exclusion <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The education project targets 29,214 refugees across five regions and refugee villages, but does not detail how resource allocation to refugee education will navigate ethnic sensitivities in a context where Issa dominance shapes institutional priorities <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing in ways that are difficult to detect precisely because monitoring systems lack ethnic disaggregation and grievance mechanisms are structurally weak. The social protection project's youth training program has been implemented without pre-defined eligibility criteria or transparent selection procedures, creating a direct mechanism through which ethnic favoritism could operate in a context where Issa dominance shapes access to state resources <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project is experiencing persistent stakeholder engagement difficulties rated "Substantial," with evidence suggesting that ethnic marginalization of Afar communities may be contributing to trust deficits and coordination failures in project areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The urban upgrading project's delayed refugee integration and social cohesion assessment—still incomplete—means the project cannot identify whether refugee settlement patterns are exacerbating existing ethnic fault lines or creating new inter-group friction in target slum areas <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The education project shows private Arabic schools—often associated with minority communities—refusing to integrate into the national EMIS system, suggesting institutional fragmentation along ethnic lines that mirrors the broader pattern of community distrust of state institutions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. Without ethnic-disaggregated monitoring and explicit inclusion safeguards, the portfolio risks reproducing and deepening structural inequalities rather than mitigating them.
