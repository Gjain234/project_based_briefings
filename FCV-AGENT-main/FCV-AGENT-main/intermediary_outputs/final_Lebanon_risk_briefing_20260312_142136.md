# Lebanon FCV Portfolio Briefing

## Paragraph 1: Renewed Armed Conflict and Direct Project Disruption

The most acute risk facing the portfolio is the renewed Israel-Hezbollah armed conflict that erupted on 2 March 2026, when Hezbollah fired rockets and drones into northern Israel following the killing of Iranian Supreme Leader Khamenei, triggering large-scale Israeli military operations across southern Lebanon, the Bekaa Valley, and Beirut's southern suburbs—killing at least 294 people and injuring over 1,000 in the first eight days. Unlike the 2024 round, this escalation is more asymmetric and unpredictable, with no active diplomatic mediation capable of containing it. Projects across the portfolio are acutely susceptible because their implementation geographies map directly onto active conflict zones. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project explicitly targets southern Lebanon, the Bekaa Valley, and Beirut's southern suburbs for reconstruction of energy, transport, water, schools, and hospitals, and its PAD acknowledges that "no works will commence until conflict has ended and sites are safe" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>; the Greater Beirut Water Supply Project similarly suspended all civil works as of November 2024 and requires security risk assessments before any resumption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a>; the Integrated Solid Waste Management Project operates in Tyre, Abbasiyeh, and Sahel el Zahrani—municipalities in the heart of the conflict zone <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a>; and the Lake Qaraoun Catchment project covers the Bekaa Valley districts of Baalbeck, Hermel, and Rachaya where contractors suspended all sewage network works following the September 2024 escalation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a>. These risks are fully materializing: aide memoire evidence confirms that the LEAP project is conducting only virtual missions and prohibiting work near active shelling, with UXO clearance required before any resumption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>; Component 2 activities across multiple projects have been placed on hold <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a>; and the UN Special Coordinator stated in early March 2026 that "a major World Bank loan was set to jump-start reconstruction" but "progress has now come to a screeching halt" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a>. The conflict has rendered the portfolio's core reconstruction objectives operationally impossible in the near term.

## Paragraph 2: Mass Internal Displacement and Humanitarian Crisis Overwhelming Project Baselines

A second distinct risk is the mass internal displacement and acute humanitarian crisis that has fundamentally altered the operating environment for every project in the portfolio. As of 10 March 2026, more than 667,000 people had registered on Lebanon's government displacement platform—an increase of over 100,000 in a single day—with many displaced for the second time since 2024, and over 4.1 million people (more than 70% of Lebanon's population) already in need of humanitarian assistance before the March 2026 attacks. Projects were designed around beneficiary populations and geographic targeting assumptions that this displacement wave has rendered obsolete. The LEAP project identified 1.2 million affected people in priority conflict-affected districts as beneficiaries, including 900,000+ displaced during the 2023–24 conflict, but the March 2026 escalation has created a new displacement surge exceeding the original baseline, making prioritization criteria and targeting mechanisms inoperable <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project explicitly noted that displacement from southern Lebanon to Beirut was "expected to further strain BMLWE's capacity to meet growing water demand," a risk now dramatically amplified <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a>. The Social Recovery Project targets 15,475 beneficiaries across GBV survivors, mental health cases, and persons with disabilities—populations that are now scattered across collective shelters and host communities, making stable identification and service delivery impossible <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a>. The Fiscal Management Project's core mitigation strategy of paying allowances to ensure staff attendance becomes ineffective when displacement forces staff to relocate or prioritize family safety <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-5067fa60-78e4-4f5e-b6c1-5a428d852b01.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – PAD</a>. Realized evidence confirms these dynamics: ISR data for the Social Recovery Project documents persistent vulnerability to service delivery disruption in conflict-affected areas, with the project's target populations facing acute humanitarian needs that overwhelm planned social recovery interventions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a>; and aide memoire evidence for the Beirut housing project records that absentee owners who have left Lebanon are blocking property rehabilitation, directly reflecting displacement dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176577-6c3b4a27-9cf9-4050-9a10-ed310209e452.pdf" target="_blank">P176577 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## Paragraph 3: Sectarian Political Fragmentation, Institutional Paralysis, and Democratic Backsliding

A third systemic risk is the combination of sectarian political fragmentation, institutional paralysis, and democratic backsliding that structurally undermines the governance environment for all projects. On 9 March 2026, Lebanon's parliament voted 76 to 41 to extend its term by two years—the fourth such extension in roughly a decade—postponing elections originally scheduled for May 2026. Simultaneously, tensions between the reform-oriented government of President Aoun and Prime Minister Salam on one side, and the Hezbollah-Amal bloc and Parliament Speaker Nabih Berri on the other, have persisted over disarmament, electoral law, and fiscal policy, with senior Hezbollah officials walking out of cabinet sessions. Projects are susceptible because they depend on inter-ministerial coordination, parliamentary ratification of loan agreements, and sustained political commitment to reform. The LEAP project's PAD identifies "political and governance (high)" risk and notes that "past challenges with loan agreement signing and parliamentary ratification have caused delays" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>; the Fiscal Management Project requires a Project Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance and comprising heads of relevant agencies, a mechanism directly undermined by sectarian walkouts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-5067fa60-78e4-4f5e-b6c1-5a428d852b01.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – PAD</a>; the Energy Sector Project depends on ERA commissioner appointments and implementation of the Letter of Electricity Sector Policy as disbursement conditions, both requiring political consensus that the confessional system cannot reliably deliver <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c260299b-4517-48c7-a0c1-b95eb42d17a7.pdf" target="_blank">P180501 – PAD</a>; and the Lake Qaraoun Catchment project requires coordination across five ministries and agencies through the Qaraoun Committee <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio: ISR evidence shows the Fiscal Management Project has not achieved effectiveness due to pending parliamentary ratification of the IBRD Financing Agreement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a>; the GATE agricultural project achieved effectiveness only in August 2025 after a 14-month delay caused by parliamentary gridlock, with disbursement at 0.25% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180334-4899e0cd-2b9b-432c-88d6-4a6b7c577697.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – ISR</a>; the Beirut Critical Environmental Recovery Project cannot obtain necessary approvals from Beirut Governorate and Municipality <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-f112aa75-2e9c-4c03-ac0e-46311eb3089b.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – ISR</a>; and the Waste Management Project faces UNDP procurement delays attributed to "external coordination challenges among key government stakeholders" with USD 2.2 million in budget cancelled <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## Paragraph 4: Economic Collapse, IMF Reform Stalling, and Financial Sector Fragility

A fourth cross-cutting risk is Lebanon's severe and worsening economic crisis, which has seen real GDP decline more than 38% since 2019 (contracting 6.6% in 2024 alone), public debt estimated at over 357% of GDP, and the financial sector carrying over $72 billion in accumulated losses. The government committed to eight reform prior actions to unlock a $3 billion IMF program, but parliament passed only a bank secrecy amendment in April 2025, with persistent political paralysis blocking all other prior actions. Lebanese depositors continue to face monthly withdrawal restrictions of around $400–500. The March 2026 conflict escalation has halted nascent recovery momentum entirely. Projects are susceptible through multiple channels: the LEAP project's PAD identifies "macroeconomic (high)" risk including "triple-digit inflation, Lebanese pound lost 98% of value, dollarized cash-based economy" and notes that "reconstruction activity may drive up construction material and labor prices" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>; the Energy Sector Project's financial model depends on operationalizing a Cash Waterfall Mechanism by June 2025 to ensure EDL's access to foreign exchange for operational continuity—a mechanism whose viability is now in serious doubt <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c260299b-4517-48c7-a0c1-b95eb42d17a7.pdf" target="_blank">P180501 – PAD</a>; the Social Safety Net Project relies on USD-denominated cash transfers through money transfer agents, but banking sector dysfunction and capital controls threaten the liquidity of these networks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-07e5fa4b-e782-4ffd-ab74-21191c4b8c78.pdf" target="_blank">P178075 – PAD</a>; and the COVID-19 Health Project faces high procurement risks in the vaccine and treatment market compounded by currency collapse <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a>. Realized evidence confirms widespread materialization: ISR data shows macroeconomic risk ratings have escalated from Moderate to High across multiple projects including the Fiscal Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a>, the Beirut housing project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176577-4f8e4069-1a4c-4b78-b5bb-344e2c6a59af.pdf" target="_blank">P176577 – ISR</a>, the Health Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0867b7011&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163476 – ISR</a>, and the Sewage Treatment Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-740ffba4-1843-48ae-936f-b5ec49d1902a.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – ISR</a>; aide memoire evidence documents that Lebanese lira depreciation has reduced land compensation amounts to USD 27–728 per plot, making 52% of landowners unwilling to collect compensation and blocking project land acquisition <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a>; and the Zahle sewer network project faces the same compensation non-collection problem driven by currency devaluation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## Paragraph 5: Israeli Continued Occupation, UNIFIL Mandate Erosion, and Reconstruction Program Collapse

A fifth distinct risk cluster combines Israeli continued occupation and sovereignty violations in southern Lebanon with the erosion of UNIFIL's mandate and the resulting collapse of the World Bank's broader reconstruction program. Since the November 2024 ceasefire, Israel has maintained military presence at five positions inside Lebanese territory, conducted near-daily airstrikes, and recorded over 10,000 violations of Lebanese airspace between November 2024 and February 2026. Human Rights Watch documented Israeli forces targeting reconstruction equipment in December 2025. Simultaneously, the UN Security Council in August 2025 adopted a resolution granting UNIFIL its final mandate renewal, requesting cessation of operations by end of 2026 and full closure by 2027—removing the only systematic documentation of violations in southern Lebanon. Three Ghanaian peacekeepers were injured on 6 March 2026. These dynamics directly threaten projects with southern Lebanon footprints: the LEAP project targets southern Lebanon for reconstruction of critical infrastructure, but Israeli forces have been documented targeting reconstruction equipment in these exact areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>; the Energy Sector Project's hydropower plants (Joun, Awali, Markabi) and transmission infrastructure lie in the Litani basin subject to Israeli military activity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c260299b-4517-48c7-a0c1-b95eb42d17a7.pdf" target="_blank">P180501 – PAD</a>; the Waste Management Project's dumpsite rehabilitation in Tyre and Abbasiyeh requires on-site access in areas under Israeli military operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a>; and the Lake Qaraoun Catchment project's sewage network construction in Bekaa districts has been directly suspended due to conflict <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a>. Realized evidence confirms these risks are materializing: aide memoire documentation shows the ESIA for the Nabatieh Sanitary Landfill was delayed in early 2025 due to security issues in southern Lebanon [P176635 | Aide Memoire; P172770 | Aide Memoire]; the LEAP project is conducting only virtual missions and requiring UXO clearance before any work resumption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>; and ISR evidence confirms that the Waste Management Project's South Lebanon interventions remain on hold due to security conditions, with the project's political and governance risk rating at "High" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-4727dec5-5d09-4ce2-8055-7dcb271aae12.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – ISR</a>. The impending UNIFIL closure by 2027 removes the last systematic international monitoring mechanism, increasing the probability of uncontrolled escalation that would render reconstruction programming in southern Lebanon impossible for the foreseeable future.
