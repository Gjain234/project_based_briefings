**Paragraph 1: Renewed Full-Scale Hostilities and Direct Project Disruption**

Lebanon's collapse of the November 2024 ceasefire on 2 March 2026—when Hezbollah launched rockets at Israel following the killing of Iran's Supreme Leader, prompting massive Israeli retaliation—has created the most acute FCV risk facing the portfolio. By late March 2026, over 1,240 people had been killed, Israeli ground incursions were expanding a buffer zone toward the Litani River, and the ceasefire monitoring mechanism had dissolved. Every project in the portfolio is exposed to this risk through geographic overlap with active conflict zones. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project (LEAP) explicitly targets southern Lebanon, the Bekaa Valley, and Beirut's southern suburbs—the exact theaters of Israeli military operations—and its PAD acknowledges that "geopolitical risks from Middle East conflict may impede implementation in border areas" and that "security conditions may restrict WB team visits for physical verification" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project similarly suspended all civil works, with its PAD stating "no works will commence until conflict has ended and sites are safe" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a>. The Lake Qaraoun Pollution Control Project operates in Hermel, Ras Baalbek, and the Litani basin—areas now under active military pressure—and its PAD acknowledges that "military operations along Lebanon's southern border escalated from low to high intensity fighting since October 2023" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c260299b-4517-48c7-a0c1-b95eb42d17a7.pdf" target="_blank">P180501 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing: aide memoire evidence for the Beqaa wastewater project confirms that "contractors suspended all sewage network works due to escalating military activities" in the Bekaa region beginning September 2024 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>, and ISR evidence for the LEAP confirms that the project cannot access sites due to "ongoing regional conflict" requiring UXO clearance before work can resume <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-740ffba4-1843-48ae-936f-b5ec49d1902a.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – ISR</a>. The UN Special Coordinator's March 2026 warning that "a major World Bank loan was set to jump-start reconstruction but progress has now collapsed" captures the portfolio-wide consequence of this risk materializing at scale.

**Paragraph 2: Mass Displacement and Humanitarian Crisis Overwhelming Project Targeting**

Since 2 March 2026, more than one million people—approximately one in five Lebanese residents—have been displaced, with over 136,000 sheltering in 660 collective sites, mostly schools filled beyond capacity, and destruction of key bridges isolating over 150,000 people in southern Lebanon. This displacement crisis fundamentally undermines the beneficiary identification, targeting, and service delivery mechanisms on which the entire portfolio depends. The LEAP's PAD identifies 900,000+ people displaced during the 2023–24 conflict as a core vulnerable group and includes "decommissioning IDP centers" as a project component, but the March 2026 escalation has created 667,000+ new displacements in a single week, rendering prioritization criteria and geographic targeting obsolete <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Social Protection project targets 147,000 families through a newly launched DAEM registration platform requiring home visits to verify applicants, but displacement makes beneficiary lists inaccurate and prevents cash distribution through money transfer agents when beneficiaries are sheltering in collective sites or have fled across borders <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08813b443&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173367 – PAD</a>. The Social Recovery project explicitly targets Syrian refugees (almost 90% living in extreme poverty) and displaced persons across Beirut, Mount Lebanon, and the Bekaa Valley, but mass displacement disrupts both identification of beneficiaries and delivery of services requiring stable locations and community trust <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing: ISR evidence for the Social Recovery project confirms "challenges to accessing vulnerable populations (GBV survivors, mental health cases, displaced persons) due to security and access constraints" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a>, and aide memoire evidence for the Solid Waste Management project documents that security instability in Nabatieh and Tyre has forced a shift to remote data collection, preventing in-person project activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a>. The UNHCR operation being only 14% funded as of mid-March 2026 signals that the humanitarian response itself is underfunded, compounding the pressure on project-supported services.

**Paragraph 3: Institutional Paralysis, Democratic Backsliding, and Governance Fragmentation**

Lebanon's parliament voted 76–41 on 9 March 2026 to extend its term by two years—the fourth such extension in a decade—citing security conditions and mass displacement, while critics condemned the move as a power grab that avoids electoral accountability at a moment when Hezbollah's influence is weakened. This democratic backsliding compounds the deeper structural problem of Hezbollah's explicit rejection of the government's March 2 weapons ban and its continued military operations despite cabinet orders, exposing the state's inability to enforce sovereign decisions and creating a governance vacuum across project implementation areas. The Fiscal Management Project's PAD identifies "past challenges with loan agreement signing and parliamentary ratification" as a core risk and requires "close follow-up with borrower at all levels," while noting that Lebanon's confessional governance system creates "political uncertainty and ongoing political deadlock limiting government's ability to take expedient policy actions" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-5067fa60-78e4-4f5e-b6c1-5a428d852b01.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – PAD</a>. The LEAP's PAD identifies "stakeholder (high)" risk from "multiple stakeholders requiring effective coordination" across ministries including MoE, MoPWT, CDR, and rubble removal contractors, and notes that "inefficient decision-making processes (procurement approval takes 9 months, bid evaluation 2–8 months)" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio: ISR evidence for the Fiscal Management Project confirms the IBRD loan has not achieved effectiveness due to pending parliamentary ratification, with the document noting the project is "critical for the post-war recovery phase" yet parliament's demonstrated dysfunction is the binding constraint <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a>. ISR evidence for the LEAP confirms "High" political and governance risk with "sectarian tensions between reform-oriented government and Hezbollah-aligned factions" and "cabinet walkouts over disarmament" directly impeding inter-ministerial coordination <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a>. Aide memoire evidence for the Beirut Critical Environmental Recovery project documents procurement delays and budget cancellations rooted in "external coordination challenges among key government stakeholders" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a>, while the Beqaa wastewater project's ISR confirms a persistent "High" governance risk rating driven by the same confessional system dysfunction <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-740ffba4-1843-48ae-936f-b5ec49d1902a.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – ISR</a>.

**Paragraph 4: Economic Collapse, IMF Reform Stagnation, and Financial Sector Fragility**

An IMF staff mission visiting Beirut in February 2026 found Lebanon's bank restructuring strategy still misaligned with international principles, with the $3 billion IMF program stalled after Lebanon repeatedly failed to achieve required prior actions; depositors continue to face monthly withdrawal restrictions of around $400–500, and the banking sector carries over $72 billion in accumulated losses. Lebanon's real GDP has declined more than 38% since 2019, contracting 6.6% in 2024 alone, with public debt estimated at over 357% of GDP. The March 2026 conflict has effectively frozen the nascent recovery momentum that the World Bank's January 2026 Lebanon Economic Monitor had cautiously projected. The LEAP's PAD identifies "macroeconomic (high)" risk including "triple-digit inflation (now easing to double digits); Lebanese pound lost 98% of value; dollarized cash-based economy; reconstruction activity may drive up construction material and labor prices" and mitigates through "project contracts denominated in US dollars" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project's PAD identifies macroeconomic risk as "high," noting that "Lebanon experienced severe economic contraction of nearly 35% since 2019" and that "rising construction costs reduce bidder interest and increase procurement risks," while acknowledging the government's "inability to finance contracts" as a cause of prior implementation delays <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a>. The Social Recovery project's PAD identifies "Macroeconomic (substantial)" risk with "Lebanon's severe economic crisis with currency depreciation (LBP devalued >90%), triple-digit inflation, balance of payments deficit, and shrinking employment sector" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio: ISR evidence for the Beqaa wastewater project confirms escalation from "Moderate" to "High" macroeconomic risk, with the March 2026 conflict explicitly cited as halting nascent recovery momentum <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-740ffba4-1843-48ae-936f-b5ec49d1902a.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – ISR</a>. Aide memoire evidence for the Solid Waste Management project documents that Lebanese lira depreciation has reduced landowner compensation to USD 27–728 per plot in real terms, making compensation mechanisms economically meaningless and blocking project land acquisition <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a>. ISR evidence for the COVID-19 Health project confirms persistent "High" macroeconomic risk affecting disbursements and operational costs, with currency instability directly impairing the implementing agency's financial management capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a>.

**Paragraph 5: Development Program Disruption and the Humanitarian-Development Pivot**

The World Bank approved a $250 million Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project in June 2025 as part of a $1 billion scalable reconstruction framework, projecting 4% GDP growth for 2026 contingent on reform persistence and political stability—conditions now severely undermined by the March 2026 conflict resumption. UNHCR's Lebanon operation was only 14% funded as of mid-March 2026, and the $308 million OCHA Flash Appeal faces a massive financing gap as humanitarian needs grow faster than response capacity. An international conference in support of the Lebanese Armed Forces planned for Paris on 5 March 2026 was postponed due to escalating tensions, disrupting coordinated international engagement with Lebanon's security sector reform. The European Council on Foreign Relations warned in late March 2026 that Israel's strikes appear unconstrained while the US appears to disengage diplomatically, creating a gap that only France is actively attempting to fill. The UN Special Coordinator for Lebanon warned in March 2026 that progress achieved since the 2024 ceasefire "has now come to a screeching halt." The LEAP's PAD explicitly states it is "part of a $1 billion scalable reconstruction framework" and notes that "a World Bank Rapid Damage and Needs Assessment estimated total direct damages from the 2023–2024 conflict at $7.2 billion, with reconstruction and recovery needs of $11 billion" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Beirut Port Explosion Recovery project's PAD identifies World Bank financing as part of this same reconstruction framework and notes coordination through the Inter-Ministerial and Municipal Platform <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088d3d75f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176577 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing comprehensively: ISR evidence for the LEAP confirms zero meaningful disbursement (0.25%) as of August 2025, with the project unable to execute despite effectiveness due to governance constraints and security deterioration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180334-4899e0cd-2b9b-432c-88d6-4a6b7c577697.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – ISR</a>. ISR evidence for the Beirut Critical Environmental Recovery project documents that contaminated rubble management activities cannot proceed because Parliament has not ratified the follow-on LEAP loan, directly linking parliamentary dysfunction to the collapse of the reconstruction agenda <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-f112aa75-2e9c-4c03-ac0e-46311eb3089b.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – ISR</a>. Aide memoire evidence for the Social Recovery project confirms that NGO partners are experiencing core funding losses as international development financing contracts and donors pivot to emergency humanitarian response, reducing the institutional capacity to execute the social recovery programming that the reconstruction framework depends upon <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-bde1794c-f97c-48b9-a56e-1949644a584e.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – Aide Memoire</a>.
