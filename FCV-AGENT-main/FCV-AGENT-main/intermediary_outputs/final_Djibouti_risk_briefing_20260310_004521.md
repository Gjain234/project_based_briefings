# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Governance Consolidation and Democratic Backsliding

Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms. This political consolidation creates direct exposure for projects that depend on multi-ministerial coordination, sustained government commitment, and institutional autonomy. The TVET and skills development project relies on a Project Steering Committee housed within the executive Economic Development Council, with no independent oversight safeguards to insulate it from political capture <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The emergency contingency response project depends on interministerial consensus across MEFI, SEGRC, and regional committees, a coordination architecture that becomes fragile when executive power concentrates and institutional mandates blur <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The public finance management reform project faces a structurally similar vulnerability: its governance relies on a PFM Reform Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance, and the PAD explicitly acknowledges that vested interests could delay reforms—a risk that intensifies as political attention shifts toward consolidating power ahead of the 2026 election cycle <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project similarly flags that the ability to mobilize private sector finance depends on political will, and that line ministries may resist change—vulnerabilities that deepen under indefinite presidential rule <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing. The PFM project is experiencing a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with administrative delays in publishing governance decrees blocking project operationalization despite ministerial approval <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The statistics modernization project carries a sustained "Substantial" governance risk, with institutional fragility directly linked to the removal of democratic constraints <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0884c1bc2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>. The digital economy project has seen ARMD board appointments stalled since 2022, suggesting deliberate institutional subordination to executive authority rather than administrative oversight <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>. Across the portfolio, the pattern is consistent: constitutional manipulation at the apex is translating into institutional stagnation, procurement delays, and governance structures that exist on paper but lack operational independence.

## 2. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Accountability Mechanisms

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House—with journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested, and virtually no independent civil society remaining—creates a systemic accountability deficit that pervades the entire portfolio. Projects across sectors rely on grievance redress mechanisms, stakeholder engagement, community mobilization, and independent monitoring, all of which are structurally compromised in this environment. The TVET project's M&E system depends on independent academic evaluation and confidential reporting, but the PAD does not address how to protect beneficiaries or staff who raise concerns in a context of political repression <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The urban upgrading project establishes community facilitators and participatory priority-setting processes, but does not acknowledge that the absence of independent civil society may render these mechanisms performative rather than genuine <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a>. The PFM reform project's Component 3 explicitly targets civil society engagement for public finance oversight—yet the PAD fails to acknowledge that the country has no functioning independent civil society to perform this role <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project includes a Stakeholder Engagement Plan and grievance redress mechanisms, but does not address how communities along the corridor—particularly women traders and vulnerable groups—can voice concerns without fear of government retaliation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing across the portfolio. The corridor project is experiencing persistent "Substantial" stakeholder engagement risk, with community consultation constrained by the restricted civic space <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's inactive steering committee and compromised internal audit function reflect the broader pattern of institutional accountability erosion <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The digital economy project has seen the ARMD unable to adopt the Digital Code Book Two, with no independent civil society or media pressure to compel regulatory action <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-9e33bd55-6107-428e-bde8-2dc02b8b55cf.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project has documented systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards—150 daily workers operating without proper contracts or social security registration—enabled by the absence of independent labor monitoring or worker advocacy mechanisms <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The GBV prevention project launched without accounting software in place, with financial reporting delays persisting in an environment where weak institutional checks go unchallenged <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. The education project's School Management Committees are only 62% functional, reflecting the broader collapse of community-level governance structures that depend on civic autonomy <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>.

## 3. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Under Severe Resource Constraints

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers, with 85% concentrated in refugee villages—while simultaneously managing recurrent droughts and a fragile fiscal position. This creates a structural mismatch between humanitarian obligations and institutional capacity that runs through the entire portfolio. The health services project targets refugee populations directly, but the PAD does not address how training delivery or service continuity will be sustained if humanitarian conditions deteriorate or if competing demands on government capacity intensify [P178033 | PAD]. The TVET project enrolls 3,165 refugees as direct beneficiaries concentrated in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps, but provides no contingency for how resource scarcity or drought conditions would affect training participation and completion <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The emergency food security project explicitly targets refugees for food stock distribution but specifies no allocation protocols between refugee villages and host communities, nor contingency plans for managing competing demands under resource scarcity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. The education project targets 29,214 refugees across five regions for integration into the national system, but acknowledges that external shocks—including recurrent droughts—could disrupt the government's capacity to sustain refugee education programming <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. These risks are concretely materializing. The health project shows refugee immunization rates stuck at 50% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation among refugee children declining from 10% to 5%—direct manifestations of resource-constrained service delivery <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The TVET project reports zero refugee enrollment and zero refugee employment outcomes, with the 5% employment rate for refugees far below targets, reflecting both institutional incapacity and the structural inability of Djibouti's labor market to absorb refugee workers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The education project shows declining refugee student enrollment—from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025—with refugee schools still not integrated into the national EMIS data system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The urban upgrading project has stalled on refugee village restructuring plans for Ali Addeh and Holl Holl, with construction plan validation pending due to institutional bottlenecks <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The food security project has achieved only 50% of its emergency food stock target and zero progress on water infrastructure rehabilitation, directly reducing drought response capacity for the 32,000 displaced persons the country hosts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 4. Ethnic Tensions and Structural Political Exclusion

Djibouti's political landscape is defined by a structural imbalance in which the Issa Somali ethnic group—approximately 60% of the population—dominates governance, while the Afar minority (approximately 35%) remains systematically marginalized. This creates latent risks of elite capture, inequitable benefit distribution, and community resistance that cut across the portfolio. The TVET project's multi-ministry coordination structure does not include explicit ethnic representation safeguards in steering committees or implementation units, creating vulnerability to political interference in beneficiary selection in a context where institutional composition could become contested <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The emergency contingency response project targets the entire population including refugees and vulnerable households across multiple locations, but ethnic-based political exclusion may result in unequal resource allocation during emergency response activation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The agri-food development project operates across three geographically dispersed zones but provides no ethnic composition analysis of target areas or mechanisms to ensure equitable benefit distribution across Issa and Afar populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project lists corridor-area populations as beneficiaries but provides no disaggregation by ethnicity or targeted inclusion mechanisms for the Afar minority despite their structural political exclusion <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are manifesting in implementation. The corridor project is experiencing persistent "Substantial" stakeholder engagement risk, with evidence suggesting that ethnic tensions and political exclusion dynamics are contributing to coordination failures and trust deficits with communities along the corridor <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The social protection project shows weak beneficiary participation documentation, with structural ethnic marginalization creating barriers to inclusive engagement—particularly for Afar minority communities—that are not being adequately tracked or addressed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's non-implementation of GBV action plans and delayed stakeholder mobilization may reflect the same underlying dynamics, where marginalized groups face institutional barriers to accessing project safeguards <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban upgrading project's delayed refugee-led CSO participation—with zero implementation by refugee-led organizations—reflects compounded exclusion dynamics, as refugees from Somalia and Ethiopia face barriers similar to those confronting the Afar minority in accessing institutional participation channels <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. Youth training programs across multiple projects lack transparent selection criteria, creating conditions under which ethnic favoritism in resource allocation can operate without accountability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Severe Youth Unemployment and Structural Economic Fragility

Youth unemployment exceeding 75%, endemic corruption, high inequality, and rising debt create a deeply constrained economic environment that undermines both the demand-side conditions for project outcomes and the supply-side institutional capacity for implementation. The TVET project's employment objectives depend on private sector absorption of nearly 24,000 trained graduates, but no labor market demand assessment or private sector capacity analysis was conducted—a critical gap given that the project's bilateral labor mobility agreements remain aspirational without evidence of employer demand <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food project targets youth as direct beneficiaries but relies on technical assistance and business plan development without addressing the macro-level structural unemployment context that constrains whether training converts to actual income <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The PFM reform project acknowledges that extreme poverty increased to 23–30% due to multiple crises and that unsustainable debt limits government capacity, but does not address how economic desperation may drive social unrest or divert government attention from reform implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project identifies job losses from decreased goods volume through Djibouti's ports as a macroeconomic risk, suggesting that corridor improvements may be offset by broader economic contraction rather than generating net employment gains <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These structural constraints are actively undermining project results. The TVET project's online gig work component—designed specifically to address youth unemployment—has stalled entirely, with technical and coordination failures suggesting that supply-side training interventions cannot overcome demand-side constraints including limited digital infrastructure and low internet penetration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The social protection project is struggling to identify viable income-generating activities in remote localities, with weak market access and limited economic diversification reflecting the same systemic fragility that drives the national unemployment crisis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The food security project has failed to deliver agricultural inputs, livestock, and water infrastructure to vulnerable households, directly undermining livelihood pathways for populations already facing severe economic exclusion <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The education project's declining refugee enrollment is partially driven by household economic pressures forcing families to withdraw children for income-generating activities—a direct manifestation of poverty and inequality at the household level <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The corridor project's documentation of 150 daily workers in non-compliant informal arrangements reflects how extreme unemployment eliminates worker bargaining power, enabling systematic labor exploitation in World Bank-financed infrastructure <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>.
