Lebanon's portfolio of World Bank projects faces five distinct and compounding country-level FCV risks that are simultaneously forward-looking threats and actively materializing implementation crises. The following briefing synthesizes PAD susceptibility evidence with realized risk signals from ISR and Aide Memoire documentation.

---

**1. Renewed Full-Scale Conflict and Active Military Operations**

The collapse of the November 2024 ceasefire on 2 March 2026, following Hezbollah rocket and drone attacks into northern Israel and subsequent massive Israeli retaliation, has created an active military environment across southern Lebanon, Beirut's southern suburbs, and the Bekaa Valley. Israeli ground operations have expanded beyond five previously held positions, over 1,000 people have been killed and 2,584 injured, and displacement orders now cover approximately 14 percent of Lebanon's territory. This risk was explicitly anticipated in project design documents across the portfolio. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a> acknowledged that "works remain suspended and BMLWE operations severely disrupted" due to the 2024 escalation and stipulated that "no works will commence until conflict has ended and sites are safe," while the Lebanon Recovery and Reconstruction Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> identified "geopolitical risks from Middle East conflict may impede implementation in border areas" with project locations explicitly in southern Lebanon and the Bekaa Valley. The Solid Waste Management and Circular Economy Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> targets municipalities in Tyre, Abbasiyeh, Sahel el Zahrani, and Nabatieh—all now within or adjacent to active conflict zones. The Beirut Urban Resilience Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088d3d75f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176577 – PAD</a> operates in Beirut neighborhoods within five kilometers of the port explosion epicenter, now also subject to displacement orders. Risks are materializing across the portfolio: ISR and Aide Memoire evidence for <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a> documents that "contractors suspended all sewage network works due to escalating military activities in the Beqaa region," with UXO clearance required before resumption; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a> records that Component 2 activities were "put on hold" due to security conditions in Nabatieh and Tyre; and <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-bde1794c-f97c-48b9-a56e-1949644a584e.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – Aide Memoire</a> documents "sporadic armed strikes on South Lebanon and Beirut's southern suburbs" directly disrupting project operations. The <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a> confirms persistent "High" political and governance risk ratings, with stakeholder engagement impossible in active conflict zones. The absence of any active diplomatic mediation framework and Israel's declared intent to continue operations until Hezbollah is eliminated means this risk has no near-term resolution pathway, rendering the portfolio's conflict-contingent implementation assumptions structurally invalid.

**2. Mass Internal Displacement and Collapse of Beneficiary Targeting**

Over 1.2 million people have been newly displaced since 2 March 2026, with approximately 134,439 IDPs sheltering in 636 overstretched collective shelters, and displacement orders covering approximately 1,470 square kilometers. This compounds a pre-existing caseload of over 64,000 people who remained displaced from the 2024 escalation. The portfolio's susceptibility to this risk is pervasive. The Lebanon Recovery and Reconstruction Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> was designed around an area-based approach targeting 1.2 million affected people in priority conflict-affected districts, with Component 1 including decommissioning of IDP centers—an activity now rendered premature or impossible. The Social Protection project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-07e5fa4b-e782-4ffd-ab74-21191c4b8c78.pdf" target="_blank">P178075 – PAD</a> targets 147,000 families through household-based verification requiring home visits, a methodology that collapses when beneficiary populations are in collective shelters or have fled. The Lebanon Social Protection Enhancement Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08813b443&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173367 – PAD</a> similarly relies on home visits to verify all applicants and targets Syrian refugees alongside Lebanese nationals. The Health Resilience Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a> targets approximately 4.7 million people including 1.5 million Syrian and Palestinian refugees, with mobile vaccination units designed for areas now under evacuation orders. The Beqaa Wastewater Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a> identifies 525,000 Syrian refugees in the Beqaa as indirect beneficiaries, with 7,300 planned household connections now inaccessible. Materialization is documented extensively: [P178075 | Aide Memoire] records that cash transfer delivery to Bekaa beneficiaries is impossible due to destroyed bridges and access constraints; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173367-72c123cf-9083-4eb3-a51a-8b251cf4b86f.pdf" target="_blank">P173367 – ISR</a> shows zero enrollment of displaced Syrian beneficiaries against a target of 180,000; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a> documents that 86 percent of GBV incident beneficiaries are displaced Syrians, a population now experiencing secondary displacement; and <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176577-6c3b4a27-9cf9-4050-9a10-ed310209e452.pdf" target="_blank">P176577 – Aide Memoire</a> records that owner access denial blocking structural rehabilitation works is "plausibly connected to displacement dynamics" as apartment owners are absent or unreachable. The secondary displacement of over 200,000 people into Syria—including 180,000 Syrians and 28,000 Lebanese—has rendered beneficiary registries across the portfolio obsolete, with UNHCR Lebanon critically underfunded at 14 percent, further constraining the coordination capacity needed to track and serve mobile populations.

**3. Healthcare System Near-Collapse and Cascading Service Delivery Failure**

Since 2 March 2026, WHO has documented 64 attacks on healthcare infrastructure in Lebanon, killing at least 53 health workers and injuring 91 others, with four hospitals and 51 primary healthcare centers now closed and nine hospitals damaged. Hospitals are operating on critically low inventories of medical supplies, and mass displacement of healthcare workers has left facilities severely understaffed. This risk intersects with the portfolio at multiple levels. The Lebanon Recovery and Reconstruction Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> includes Component 2 for multi-sectoral restoration of critical infrastructure including health, with mobile clinics and equipment replacement as delivery mechanisms—all dependent on a functional health sector for coordination. The Health Resilience Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a> explicitly depends on operational hospitals and primary healthcare centers for case management, hospitalization bill payment, and medical equipment procurement, with the PAD acknowledging the system "suffers from lack of resources and limited capacities" even before the current crisis. The Social Protection Enhancement Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a> relies on primary healthcare centers and mobile medical units for specialized health service delivery, with the PAD noting that "51 percent of surveyed refugees access healthcare via PHCCs"—facilities now closed in conflict zones. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a> identifies "medium probability of serious adverse effects to worker/community health and safety" with mitigation measures that assume functional healthcare infrastructure for emergency response. Materialization is documented across the portfolio: <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a> shows hospital equipment availability at only 56 percent against a 70 percent target, with the gap widening under conflict pressure; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a> records that the national mental health hotline (Lifeline) and specialized health services through PHCCs face acute disruption as facilities close; <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0867b7011&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163476 – ISR</a> documents zero beneficiaries served under hospitalization cost financing despite the project being designed precisely for conflict-injured populations; and [P173367 | Aide Memoire] records that GBV referral pathways to medical services are non-functional as healthcare facilities are closed or overwhelmed. The crisis compounds Lebanon's pre-existing healthcare vulnerabilities stemming from the economic collapse, the 2020 Beirut port explosion, and chronic underfunding, creating a system that was already at the margin of functionality before the March 2026 escalation.

**4. Economic Recovery Reversal and Macroeconomic Implementation Collapse**

The World Bank's January 2026 Lebanon Economic Monitor reported 3.5 percent real GDP growth in 2025 and projected 4 percent growth in 2026, contingent on sustained reforms, modest reconstruction inflows, and political stability—conditions the March 2026 escalation has now fundamentally undermined. Lebanon remains in sovereign default, blocking access to international capital markets, and the cumulative GDP decline has exceeded 38 percent since 2019. The portfolio's susceptibility to this risk is structural. The Lebanon Recovery and Reconstruction Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> identified a US$750 million financing gap between appraised needs and available IBRD financing, with project contracts denominated in US dollars as the primary mitigation—a measure insufficient if economic crisis deepens beyond current parameters. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a> identified "Lebanon experienced severe economic contraction of nearly 35 percent since 2019" with "rising construction costs reduce bidder interest and increase procurement risks." The Solid Waste Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> relies on US$56 million in leveraged government budget contributions that cannot be maintained during renewed conflict. The Green and Inclusive Growth Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-c14c98c6-d426-4177-9a31-f1acaae2fb97.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – PAD</a> targets SME lending and farmer investment in an environment where the banking sector carries $72 billion in accumulated losses and depositors remain locked out of savings. The Energy Sector Reforms Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c260299b-4517-48c7-a0c1-b95eb42d17a7.pdf" target="_blank">P180501 – PAD</a> depends on a Cash Waterfall Mechanism operationalization requiring EDL access to foreign exchange—a mechanism contingent on macroeconomic stabilization now reversed. Materialization is pervasive: <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180334-4899e0cd-2b9b-432c-88d6-4a6b7c577697.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – ISR</a> shows zero SME loans approved and zero farmers reached, with disbursement at 0.25 percent; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-4727dec5-5d09-4ce2-8055-7dcb271aae12.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – ISR</a> documents that local authorities cannot sustain waste management operations post-project due to fiscal collapse; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176577-6c3b4a27-9cf9-4050-9a10-ed310209e452.pdf" target="_blank">P176577 – Aide Memoire</a> records that accumulated utility bills create barriers to housing rehabilitation as service providers demand high-level negotiations rather than standard reconnection procedures; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-740ffba4-1843-48ae-936f-b5ec49d1902a.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – ISR</a> shows macroeconomic risk escalated from Moderate at approval to High, with government counterpart funding capacity collapsed; and <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a> documents that parliamentary ratification of the IBRD loan remains pending, with the fiscal management project itself unable to achieve effectiveness in the environment it was designed to reform. The World Bank's January 2026 financing package of US$350 million and the US$250 million reconstruction project approved in mid-2025 both face serious implementation risks, with the UN Special Coordinator confirming in March 2026 that reconstruction progress "has come to a screeching halt."

**5. Institutional Fragility, State Sovereignty Deficit, and Governance Collapse**

Lebanon's institutional fragility predates the March 2026 escalation but has been dramatically accelerated by it. The Lebanese government took the unprecedented step of banning all Hezbollah military and security activities and ordering weapons surrender, but the Lebanese Armed Forces commander objected to direct confrontation, and the decrees have not been enforced. Parliamentary elections constitutionally required by May 2026 face severe jeopardy, with the UN Special Coordinator warning that "progress including election preparations has come to a screeching halt," and parliament extended its own term by two years on 9 March 2026—the fourth such extension in a decade. The portfolio's susceptibility to this compound governance risk is foundational. The Lebanon Recovery and Reconstruction Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> identified "institutional capacity (high)" risk, noting CDR's "inefficient decision-making processes (procurement approval takes 9 months, bid evaluation 2-8 months)" and "limited human resources," with the project depending on multi-ministerial coordination across Ministries of Energy and Water, Education, Social Affairs, Public Health, and Environment. The Public Finance Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-5067fa60-78e4-4f5e-b6c1-5a428d852b01.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – PAD</a> is designed to "restore the basic functions of domestic revenue mobilization and accountable allocation and use of public resources" and to "renew social contract between State and citizens through improved governance"—objectives that presuppose a state capable of enforcing its own authority. The Solid Waste Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> requires a Project Steering Committee including seven ministries and coordination with local authorities, municipalities, and unions of municipalities in areas where state authority is absent. The Social Protection Enhancement Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a> acknowledges "project design works through non-governmental sector given weak government systems" while simultaneously requiring technical partnerships with three ministries and participation in ten programmatic working groups. Materialization is documented across the portfolio: <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a> shows the IBRD loan has not achieved effectiveness due to pending parliamentary ratification, with the fiscal management project unable to begin operations; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-f112aa75-2e9c-4c03-ac0e-46311eb3089b.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – ISR</a> documents that the project cannot obtain necessary approvals from Beirut Governorate and Municipality for waste management infrastructure, with Parliament ratification of follow-on financing also pending; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a> records that fund transfers between the Ministry of Finance and Ministry of Environment face governance delays, with inter-ministerial coordination failures blocking project execution; <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-bde1794c-f97c-48b9-a56e-1949644a584e.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – Aide Memoire</a> documents that NGO partner funding losses reflect the broader donor financing environment disruption as international development financing contracts; and <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180334-4899e0cd-2b9b-432c-88d6-4a6b7c577697.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – ISR</a> shows the Green and Inclusive Growth project was not effective seven months after its planned date, with the ISR explicitly citing "Political and Governance Instability" as the binding constraint. The Hezbollah disarmament impasse, the parliamentary elections crisis, and the LAF's structural constraints collectively represent a state sovereignty deficit that renders the portfolio's governance assumptions—built on a functioning Lebanese state capable of coordinating, enforcing, and sustaining development activities—fundamentally misaligned with current ground realities.
