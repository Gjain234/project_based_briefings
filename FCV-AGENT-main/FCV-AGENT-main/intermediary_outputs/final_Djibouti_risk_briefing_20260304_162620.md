## Sector-Aligned FCV Portfolio Briefing: Djibouti

**Governance and Public Financial Management**

The governance and public financial management (PFM) cluster represents the most pervasive cross-cutting risk in the Djibouti portfolio. Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits (DJ-001), the near-total collapse of independent civil society and media freedom (DJ-004), and the international community's permissive tolerance of democratic backsliding (DJ-010) collectively create a governance environment in which institutional checks are systematically eroded. The PFM Modernization Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a> explicitly rates political risk as "High," noting that "vested interests within public administration and business community could delay reforms," while relying on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes" as a primary mitigation—an assumption that is structurally undermined by the constitutional consolidation of executive power. The Statistics and Economic Management Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a> similarly identifies "erosion of public trust in state institutions" as a substantial political risk and depends on "policy dialogue" and "leveraging broad consensus around Vision 2035," both of which are compromised by the tightening political environment. In realized implementation, the PFM project has experienced persistent failure to publish governance decrees despite ministerial approval <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>, inactive steering committees <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>, and a sustained "High" political and governance risk rating driven by constitutional manipulation, collapsed civil society oversight, and succession uncertainty <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The Digital Economy Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a> has seen ARMD board appointments stalled since 2022, the Digital Code Book Two unadopted despite repeated mission recommendations, and a "High" stakeholder engagement risk—all consistent with an institutional environment where executive consolidation supersedes regulatory modernization and where the absence of independent oversight removes corrective pressure.

**Infrastructure, Trade Corridors, and Connectivity**

The infrastructure and trade corridor cluster faces compounding risks from domestic security threats, regional diplomatic instability, and weak institutional accountability. The Djibouti-Ethiopia Transport Corridor Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> identifies "security/fragility (substantial)" risk from regional instability and confirms the project operates in conflict-affected areas, yet mitigation relies only on "regular security risk assessments" without specific protocols for FRUD-armé threats to road rehabilitation sites or border operations at Guelileh <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The escalating Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis (DJ-006), including ambassador recalls and military alignment with Mogadishu, directly threatens cross-border coordination capacity essential for corridor functionality, yet the PAD addresses only Ethiopia coordination risk without assessing how regional tensions could destabilize the Guelileh One-Stop Border Post <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. In realized implementation, the corridor project has experienced systemic labor non-compliance affecting 150 daily workers operating without social security registration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>—a direct manifestation of DJ-002's severe youth unemployment context, where workers facing 75%+ joblessness lack bargaining power to resist exploitative arrangements. Critical safety violations including absence of dedicated HSE agents, inadequate worker shelter, and non-compliant personal protective equipment have been documented <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>, enabled by the absence of independent civil society and media scrutiny (DJ-004) that would otherwise generate external accountability pressure. Resettlement of informal traders at Arta has stalled <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>, with ethnic tension dynamics (DJ-003) creating heightened political sensitivity around land use decisions in a context where Issa-Afar imbalances shape resource allocation perceptions. The project's political and governance risk has remained at "Substantial" throughout implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>, sustained by constitutional manipulation, succession uncertainty, collapsed civil society oversight, and the permissive international environment.

**Social Protection, Humanitarian Response, and Food Security**

The social protection and humanitarian cluster is the most directly exposed to Djibouti's refugee hosting burden (DJ-009) and the structural economic fragility driving youth unemployment and inequality (DJ-002). The Contingency Emergency Response Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a> rates institutional risk as "High" due to SEGRC's "limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff," while simultaneously targeting 32,000 displaced persons plus 150,000 non-registered migrants—creating a structural mismatch between implementation capacity and beneficiary scope <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The Food Security and Resilience Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a> acknowledges "high volatility of food commodity markets" and regional instability affecting trade routes but does not assess how the Djibouti-Somaliland crisis (DJ-006) specifically affects food commodity supply chains or port access critical to emergency stock procurement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. In realized implementation, the food security project has achieved only 50% of emergency food stock targets and zero progress on water infrastructure rehabilitation for 26 facilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>, with a June 2025 drought declaration triggering project suspension and resource reallocation from preparedness to emergency response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>—a direct materialization of DJ-009's resource constraint dynamics compounded by climate stress. The Social Safety Net Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> has documented poor rural road accessibility causing logistical delays in cash and in-kind transfer distributions, weak grievance mechanisms with inadequate complaint management, opaque youth training selection procedures without pre-defined eligibility criteria, and persistent governance and management capacity constraints in rural savings groups—all reflecting the intersection of DJ-002's structural economic fragility, DJ-003's ethnic exclusion risks, and DJ-004's collapsed civil society oversight. The GBV Prevention Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-4908093d-8bfd-4f36-a29c-d9575d229601.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a> has experienced stalled NGO contract negotiations, missing accounting systems at project launch, delayed financial reporting, and critical gaps in psychosocial counseling capacity—institutional failures rooted in the same governance environment that has eliminated independent civil society and constrained organizational autonomy.

**Education, Skills Development, and Labor Market Integration**

The education and skills cluster faces a distinctive convergence of refugee integration challenges, youth unemployment structural barriers, and governance constraints on monitoring and accountability. The TVET and Skills Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> acknowledges "macroeconomic (substantial)" risk and proposes "regular engagement with private sector stakeholders" as mitigation for labor market absorption, but documents no labor market demand assessment or contingency for limited job creation in a context where youth unemployment exceeds 75% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The project's refugee component targets 3,165 direct beneficiaries concentrated in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps in Ali Sabieh—a region with documented FRUD-armé insurgent activity (DJ-005)—yet security risk mitigation is limited to "institutional protocols" without specific threat assessment for this geography <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. In realized implementation, the TVET project has achieved zero refugee enrollment against a target of 220 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>, zero refugee employment outcomes against a 5% target <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>, a stalled online gig work component with no results <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>, and no progress on bilateral labor mobility agreements <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>—collectively demonstrating that supply-side training interventions cannot overcome demand-side structural barriers in Djibouti's constrained economy. The Education System Strengthening Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a> has seen refugee student enrollment decline from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025, refugee schools remain unintegrated into the national EMIS/GOSE system, School Management Committees functioning at only 62% capacity, and refugee-led CSO sub-projects achieving zero implementation—the last directly connected to DJ-004's hostile environment for autonomous civil society organizations. The TVET project's inactive steering committee <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a> and compromised internal audit function <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a> further reflect the governance fragility that pervades implementation across this cluster.

**Displacement, Refugee Integration, and Urban Development**

The displacement and urban development cluster is structurally shaped by Djibouti's hosting of approximately 32,000 displaced persons (DJ-009) in a resource-constrained environment, with implementation risks compounded by ethnic tensions (DJ-003) and the near-total absence of independent civil society (DJ-004). The Refugee and Host Community Support Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – PAD</a> targets 12,860 refugees alongside 35,900 host community residents, identifying "social conflict" as a substantial environmental and social risk, but does not detail how targeting will navigate ethnic sensitivities in a context where Somali-origin refugees interact with both Issa and Afar host communities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – PAD</a>. The Urban Slum Upgrading Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a> acknowledges "tensions communautés hôtes-réfugiés" as a substantial risk but relies on participatory processes without acknowledging that Djibouti's "virtually no independent civil society" fundamentally constrains the authenticity of such mechanisms <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a>. In realized implementation, the refugee integration project has experienced a delayed social cohesion assessment preventing early identification of integration tensions <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>, stalled property rights documentation in refugee villages <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>, zero implementation by refugee-led CSOs <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>, zero operationalization of recommendations from the 2017 National Refugee Law <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>, and land regularization stalled since October 2023 due to Land Directorate reprioritization without external accountability pressure <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The Health and Immunization Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a> has documented refugee immunization rates at 50% against an 80% target, vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5% among refugee children, and persistent coordination and outreach planning difficulties—all direct manifestations of DJ-009's resource constraint dynamics in a hosting environment where fragile public services cannot sustain basic preventive health interventions for displaced populations. The Statistics Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a> has encountered security concerns in refugee settlements during household survey enumeration, INSTAD institutional overload from concurrent survey operations degrading data quality, and persistent governance risk driven by constitutional manipulation and collapsed civil society oversight <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-4b12f487-05cf-4472-bbf3-012c7cb41ce1.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>—reflecting how displacement pressures and governance fragility jointly undermine the evidence base needed for evidence-driven development programming.
