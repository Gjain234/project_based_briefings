**Social unrest and protests:** Pakistan's political landscape is defined by a deepening civil-military hybrid governance model in which the PTI of jailed former PM Imran Khan has been effectively sidelined, democratic backsliding has intensified, and the Economist Intelligence Unit has reclassified the country as authoritarian (PAK-005). Sectarian protests following the killing of Iran's Supreme Leader Khamenei killed at least twenty people nationwide in March 2026, including eight in Skardu after demonstrators attacked UN Military Observer Group offices (PAK-004). The Merged Areas Development Project is susceptible because its implementation depends on coordination among five line departments under a KP provincial government in direct political conflict with the federal government and military, creating risk that political tensions disrupt inter-departmental coordination and resource allocation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-107c8a45-eb40-4cbd-85f2-4e94744ec5dd.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – PAD</a>. The Sindh Social Protection Project faces analogous exposure, as the nascent Sindh Social Protection Authority requires sustained inter-departmental collaboration with federal BISP that political instability could fracture <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-b7f2a541-1d57-46f3-b205-f65c1433ab11.pdf" target="_blank">P178532 – PAD</a>. The SPEED program in KP is similarly vulnerable, given that its Finance Department implementing agency depends on a provincial government politically isolated from federal authorities, threatening sustained commitment to PFM and education spending <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088486e7b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P175727 – PAD</a>. In implementation, political and governance risk ratings have worsened or remained persistently high across the portfolio: P176780 saw its rating escalate from Substantial to High, with staffing changes at the PCMU causing cascading delays <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176780-319537c9-6ae2-4eee-8dbf-6bb4120b5e55.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – ISR</a>; P178532 recorded a further escalation from Substantial to High in political and governance risk, compounded by sector policy deterioration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178532-6d993aa7-8a16-4819-90c3-4bf6693bb1bf.pdf" target="_blank">P178532 – ISR</a>; and P175727 documented that multi-year budget integration was not completed due to an interim government, directly impeding fiscal reform <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175727-191b6395-e711-43ac-a0c3-a0e4aa213f35.pdf" target="_blank">P175727 – ISR</a>.

**Violence between refugees, host communities, and the state:** Pakistan has expelled over 146,000 Afghan nationals in 2026 alone under its opaque Illegal Foreigners Repatriation Plan, with deportations raising serious non-refoulement concerns and UNHCR reporting only fifteen percent of required funding received as of February 2026 (PAK-006). Tens of thousands of civilians have been displaced from KP's Tirah Valley and border areas since January 2026 due to military counterterrorism operations, with displaced communities sheltering in improvised conditions including a British-era railway tunnel (PAK-010). The Refugees and Host Communities Project is directly exposed, as it is funded through IDA's Regional Sub-Window for Refugees and Host Communities and targets 519,303 refugees in KP districts; the accelerating deportation campaign reduces the beneficiary population and undermines the protection framework the project depends on <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0879fa728&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166309 – PAD</a>. The Afghan Refugees Management Project faces existential risk to its core objective, as it directly targets approximately 15,000 PoR card holders for visa policy benefits while Pakistan's deportation plan now encompasses 1.4 million PoR holders <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0875fcabb&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P165542 – PAD</a>. The Balochistan Flood Emergency Project targets 2.7 million flood-affected people in a province hosting significant Afghan refugee populations, yet the PAD does not address how forced deportations could affect beneficiary composition or create community instability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-cada74cb-95f5-4ca2-a46a-9560206ebad9.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the Afghan Refugees Management Project shows CCAR lacking adequate organizational capacity to undertake its mandate in a changing refugee protection context, with no automated system for monitoring refugee data and staff capacity misaligned to functions since 2019 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P165542-2226f908-40b4-4cb7-a8b5-21f422b5b997.pdf" target="_blank">P165542 – ISR</a>. The Refugees and Host Communities Project records 7,835 refugee children benefiting from direct learning interventions against a target of 12,000, with flood-related reconstruction works ongoing in 867 schools across twelve districts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166309-e6c7abf6-7829-4ecf-b963-bf4cfa1c931c.pdf" target="_blank">P166309 – ISR</a>.

**Organized violence between political and sectarian groups:** Pakistan ranked first on the Global Terrorism Index 2026, with 1,139 terrorism-related deaths and 1,045 incidents in 2025; TTP violence surged a further 76 percent in March 2026 alone, with militants deploying IEDs, targeted killings, and quadcopter-delivered explosives concentrated in KPK and Balochistan (PAK-001). The BLA carried out its largest-ever coordinated campaign in January–February 2026, striking across at least twelve locations in Balochistan, and in April 2026 claimed its first maritime attack on a Pakistan Coast Guard vessel (PAK-003, PAK-009). IS-Khorasan claimed the February 2026 Islamabad mosque bombing killing 31 Shia worshippers, and analysts document growing convergence between TTP, BLA, and ISKP (PAK-004, PAK-011). The Merged Areas Development Project operates directly in Bajaur, Khyber, Kurram, Mohmand, North Waziristan, and South Waziristan—all high-intensity TTP conflict zones—requiring sustained field presence and community mobilization in active insurgency areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-107c8a45-eb40-4cbd-85f2-4e94744ec5dd.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – PAD</a>. The Balochistan Flood Emergency Project is marked Fragile within a non-fragile Country with security rated High; the PAD acknowledges the current security situation may restrict or prevent travel to project areas and relies on remote supervision tools to mitigate access constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-cada74cb-95f5-4ca2-a46a-9560206ebad9.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – PAD</a>. The National Health Support Program operates in KP where TTP violence accounted for 311 terror-related deaths in Q1 2026, with the PAD explicitly acknowledging insecurity affects staff recruitment, retention, and mobility, particularly for female health workers <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088dba71e&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172615 – PAD</a>. The Dasu Hydropower Project involves a 48 km construction corridor and 5,000-plus worker influx in Upper Kohistan, KP, with the PAD identifying potential for theft or sabotage of equipment and broader security challenges in the province <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0828b816d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P121507 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the Balochistan Flood Emergency Project records fiduciary risk escalating from Substantial to High and stakeholder risk escalating from Substantial to High, with physical progress remaining slow owing to procedural delays <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180323-aca41980-8e57-40b5-96a1-1f120e049689.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – ISR</a>. The Dasu Hydropower Project maintains a persistently High overall risk rating with macroeconomic risk escalating to High, and only 33–35 percent of affected persons in consultations reporting their suggestions have been taken into account <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b082dcef4e&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P121507 – ISR</a>.

**Political instability:** Pakistan's hybrid governance model has further consolidated in 2026, with PTI's exit from negotiations paralysing parliament's ability to pass IMF-required reforms and deepening public skepticism about democratic institutions (PAK-005). Pakistan remains under a USD 7 billion IMF Extended Fund Facility with stabilization described as fragile and import-compression-driven; FDI fell sharply to USD 808 million in H1 FY2026, with security analysts attributing the decline partly to violence in Balochistan and KPK (PAK-008). The CRISP social protection program depends on federal-provincial coordination and intergovernmental consensus-building, but the PAD identifies limited provincial ownership of federal programs as a risk, and the current political environment includes direct conflict between the federal government and the KP provincial government <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08839e75a&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174484 – PAD</a>. The ASPIRE education program's federal-provincial coordination mechanisms—including the Technical Steering Committee and Inter-Provincial Education Ministerial Conference—are vulnerable to political tensions between the federal government and the only PTI-governed province <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087bdd0fa&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173399 – PAD</a>. The Karachi BRT project faces high political and governance risk from overlapping mandates of multiple agencies under different political parties at federal, provincial, and municipal levels, with the PAD explicitly identifying risk of political disagreements affecting implementation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086da8231&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166732 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the CRISP program records macroeconomic risk escalating from Substantial to High, the beneficiary-centric payment mechanism off-track with zero progress due to State Bank approval delays, and 1.5 million Nashonuma beneficiaries having demonstrated compliance but payments delayed <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0887f499d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174484 – ISR</a>. The ASPIRE program shows delayed provincial cabinet endorsement of foundational literacy policies, with KP and Punjab policies awaiting cabinet approval and district-level grant mechanisms still unresolved between MoFEPT and the World Bank <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173399-6843ea91-d105-4aa2-9849-a36955383262.pdf" target="_blank">P173399 – ISR</a>. The Karachi BRT project records macroeconomic risk escalating from Substantial to High and institutional capacity rated Substantial, with the SMTA needing to avoid delays in contract management and procurement of BRT corridor packages <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088b18827&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166732 – ISR</a>.

**External risks and intra-state conflict:** Following a surge in TTP attacks including a suicide bombing in Islamabad killing 31, Pakistan declared open war with Afghanistan on February 27, 2026, conducting airstrikes on Kabul, Kandahar, and Paktia; a fragile Eid ceasefire brokered in March 2026 remains unresolved on core grievances, and China-mediated talks have failed to produce a formal agreement (PAK-002). India suspended the 1960 Indus Waters Treaty following the April 2025 Pahalgam attack, with the suspension remaining in force as of March 2026 and Pakistan escalating the dispute at the UN; the Indus Basin irrigates approximately 80 percent of Pakistan's agricultural land (PAK-007). The Khyber Pass Economic Corridor Project is directly exposed, as its core objective is to improve connectivity and trade across the Pakistan-Afghanistan border at Torkham, and the PAD explicitly identifies border skirmishes as a political and governance risk; the current open conflict directly threatens bilateral coordination mechanisms and cross-border beneficiary access <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b085b2e6ff&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P159577 – PAD</a>. The KP Irrigation Project covers northern KP districts including Chitral, Dir, Swat, and newly merged FATA areas proximate to the Afghanistan border, where cross-border military operations in March 2026 could disrupt implementation through displacement, security lockdowns, or infrastructure damage <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d6a502&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163474 – PAD</a>. The Energy Distribution Efficiency Improvement Project operates through PESCO in Peshawar and KP, which borders Afghanistan, with the PAD acknowledging PESCO operates in a province that has experienced conflict and that project areas include historically conflict-affected regions <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088be9483&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170230 – PAD</a>.
