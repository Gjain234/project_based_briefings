# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Governance Consolidation and Democratic Backsliding

Djibouti's removal of presidential age limits in October 2025—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term after more than two decades in power—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms. This political consolidation creates an environment of unpredictable policy continuity, weakened rule of law, and reduced institutional autonomy across government bodies. Several projects are directly exposed to this dynamic. The TVET and skills development project relies on a multi-ministry steering committee under the Economic Development Council, but the PAD's mitigation strategy—dependent on a "high-level steering committee"—does not address how to insulate project governance from executive capture as power concentrates further <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The public finance management reform project depends on a PFM Reform Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance, with mitigation relying on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that becomes fragile under constitutional manipulation and 2026 election dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project explicitly identifies that "ability to influence private sector finance mobilization depends on political will," with mitigation requiring "commitment at highest state level"—a strategy that becomes increasingly precarious as institutional accountability erodes <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The PFM project is experiencing a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with administrative delays in publishing governance decrees blocking project operationalization despite ministerial approval <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The digital connectivity project similarly carries a sustained "High" political and governance risk, with ARMD board appointments vacant since 2022 and the Digital Code's telecommunications regulation remaining unadopted despite repeated mission recommendations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's steering committee has become inactive, and governance coordination between government and development partners has stalled, with inter-ministerial formalization of project structures delayed as ministries await executive direction rather than operating through transparent protocols <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 2. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Accountability Mechanisms

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House—with journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested, and virtually no independent civil society remaining—creates a structural accountability deficit that pervades the entire project portfolio. Without independent oversight, monitoring systems, grievance mechanisms, and stakeholder engagement processes are systematically compromised. The TVET project's M&E system relies on "independent academic evaluation" and confidential grievance reporting, but these mechanisms are vulnerable to government pressure and self-censorship in an environment where independent verification is structurally impossible <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The PFM reform project's Component 3 explicitly targets civil society engagement in public finance oversight, yet the PAD does not acknowledge that "only marginal voices present on social media" and that civil society organizations cannot meaningfully participate in oversight functions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The education project establishes a grievance mechanism "for all stakeholders," but in a context where vulnerable groups—refugees, rural students, girls—face barriers to voicing concerns without fear of retaliation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. The food security project's grievance mechanism lacks confidentiality protections, independence from government pressure, and mechanisms for safe feedback in a repressive civic environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing. The TVET project shows zero progress on refugee-led CSO sub-projects, with refugee-led organizations facing the same operational constraints and potential harassment that have dismantled domestic civil society <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The corridor project has documented systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards affecting 150 daily workers, with no independent unions, labor advocates, or media scrutiny to expose violations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The digital connectivity project's grievance mechanism is non-functional, and environmental and social safeguards reporting is persistently delayed without external pressure for correction <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-9e33bd55-6107-428e-bde8-2dc02b8b55cf.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. The GBV prevention project launched without accounting systems in place, with financial reporting delayed and fiduciary controls compromised in an environment lacking independent institutional checks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. School Management Committees in the education project are functional in only 62% of schools, reflecting the broader collapse of community-level governance structures that depend on autonomous civic participation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>.

## 3. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Under Severe Resource Constraints

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers—with 85% concentrated in refugee villages, while simultaneously facing recurrent droughts, high public debt, and severely limited institutional capacity. This creates a structural mismatch between humanitarian obligations and service delivery capacity that directly undermines project implementation across the portfolio. The emergency contingency response project targets refugee and migrant populations but relies on SEGRC and MEFI—both identified as having "limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff"—creating a fundamental gap between mandate and institutional means <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The TVET project targets 3,165 refugees as direct beneficiaries but does not assess how recurrent droughts or resource scarcity would affect training delivery or beneficiary retention in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The food security project explicitly targets refugees for emergency food stock distribution but specifies no allocation percentages or distribution protocols between refugee villages and host communities amid resource scarcity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. The urban development project targets refugees and displaced persons as direct beneficiaries but does not assess how Djibouti's hosting of 32,000 displaced persons amid recurrent droughts may strain project delivery or create competing claims on infrastructure investments <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a>. These risks are concretely materializing. The health project shows refugee immunization rates stuck at 50% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation among refugee children declining from 10% to 5%—direct manifestations of insufficient capacity to maintain preventive health interventions for displaced populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The TVET project reports zero refugee enrollment in training programs and zero refugee employment outcomes (stuck at 5%), with the project unable to track refugee enrollment or completion data due to weak institutional monitoring capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The education project shows declining refugee student enrollment—from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025—with refugee schools not yet integrated into the national EMIS system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The food security project has achieved only 50% of its emergency food stock target and zero water infrastructure rehabilitation, with a June 2025 drought declaration triggering resource reallocation away from preparedness toward emergency response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban development project has stalled on refugee village restructuring plans for Ali Addeh and Holl Holl, with zero refugee-led CSO sub-projects implemented and zero vocational training certificates delivered to refugees <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a> <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>.

## 4. Severe Youth Unemployment and Structural Economic Exclusion

Youth unemployment exceeding 75%, combined with endemic corruption, high inequality, and limited private sector absorptive capacity, creates a structural economic environment in which project interventions targeting employment and livelihoods face systemic headwinds. The TVET project plans to place 23,775 trained graduates into employment but includes no labor market demand assessment, private sector capacity analysis, or contingency for limited job creation—a critical design gap given that the project's bilateral labor agreements with Kuwait and Qatar remain aspirational without evidence of employer demand <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food project targets youth as key beneficiaries but relies on technical assistance and business plan development without addressing the macro-level context of 75% youth unemployment, with no analysis of how corridor improvements translate to jobs for the unemployed youth population <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The social protection project targets youth training programs but lacks pre-defined eligibility criteria and transparent selection procedures, creating risk that benefits concentrate among already-privileged youth while excluding the most vulnerable [P508560 | PAD]. These risks are materializing across multiple projects. The TVET project's online gig work component has stalled entirely, with technical and coordination failures suggesting that supply-side training cannot overcome demand-side constraints including limited digital infrastructure and lack of formal employment pathways <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. Labor mobility agreements with Gulf states remain unadvanced, with bilateral negotiations with Kuwait and Qatar still pending and no signed agreements <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The social protection project has documented inequitable access to 400 youth training slots, with opaque selection procedures creating conditions for ethnic favoritism and elite capture <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. Rural savings groups (CREC) show persistent governance and financial management capacity constraints, undermining their function as economic inclusion mechanisms for marginalized rural youth <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The food security project has delivered zero agricultural inputs, livestock, or water infrastructure to vulnerable households, directly undermining livelihood pathways for economically marginalized populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The statistics project faces staffing constraints in macroeconomic planning that impair the government's capacity to design evidence-based interventions addressing youth unemployment—weakening the analytical foundation for job creation strategies <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0884c1bc2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>.

## 5. Ethnic Tensions, Political Exclusion, and Inclusive Implementation Risks

Djibouti's structural ethnic imbalance—with the Issa Somali group (approximately 60% of the population) dominating governance while the Afar minority (approximately 35%) remains politically marginalized—creates persistent risks of exclusionary implementation, elite capture, and community resistance across projects operating in ethnically mixed or politically sensitive areas. The TVET project's inclusive targeting of women, persons with disabilities, and refugees may create friction if ethnic representation in beneficiary selection or institutional governance is perceived as favoring particular groups, yet the PAD provides no explicit ethnic representation safeguards in steering committees <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The corridor project lists "populations living in corridor area" as beneficiaries but provides no disaggregation by ethnicity or targeted inclusion mechanisms for the Afar minority despite their structural political exclusion <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The education project targets 29,214 refugees across five regions and refugee villages, but does not detail how targeting will navigate ethnic sensitivities in a context where resource allocation to refugee education could become politically contested <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. The emergency response project explicitly targets vulnerable groups including refugees, migrants, and internally displaced persons across national locations, but ethnic tensions could undermine impartial distribution of emergency supplies across diverse populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing in implementation. The corridor project shows persistent "Substantial" stakeholder engagement risk, with coordination failures and community tensions that may reflect underlying ethnic fault lines if project benefits are perceived as skewed toward dominant groups <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The corridor project has also documented resettlement of informal traders at Arta without adequate consultation, with the absence of independent civil society compounding the risk that ethnic minorities' concerns go unheard in land use decisions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban development project's delayed refugee integration and social cohesion assessment prevents early identification of whether refugee settlement patterns are exacerbating existing ethnic fault lines in target slum areas <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The social protection project shows inadequate beneficiary participation documentation, with weak stakeholder mobilization potentially masking difficulties in reaching politically excluded communities, particularly Afar minorities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The statistics project faces governance risk from ethnic imbalances that undermine inclusive institutional reform, with structural exclusion creating legitimacy deficits that affect the broad-based stakeholder participation required for sustainable institutional outcomes <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0884c1bc2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>.
