**Djibouti City and Balbala (Urban Capital Region)**

The Governance and Institutional Capacity category dominates the risk profile for Djibouti's urban capital region, where the concentration of central government institutions creates both implementation dependencies and vulnerabilities. The October 2025 constitutional revision enabling indefinite presidential rule [DJ-001] has deepened political stagnation, directly undermining the institutional predictability that development projects require. The collapse of independent civil society and media freedom [DJ-004] removes the external accountability mechanisms that would otherwise check governance dysfunction. These country risks materialize acutely in the Public Finance Management project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>, where the PAD identifies a "High" political risk rating and relies on government commitment as its primary mitigation strategy—a fragile assumption given the constitutional manipulation underway. In practice, this risk has fully realized: the government failed to sign and publish required governance decrees despite Council of Ministers confirmation on February 25, 2025, blocking project effectiveness <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>, and delays in operationalizing PFM Reform Strategy governance structures are now affecting all donor-funded projects in Djibouti <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. Similarly, the Statistics and Economic Management project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a> identifies "systematic citizen engagement interventions" as a mitigation strategy for political risk, yet operates in a context where independent civil society is virtually absent—a fundamental implementation gap. Institutional capacity strain has materialized through staffing constraints slowing macroeconomic modeling and debt strategy development <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-4b12f487-05cf-4472-bbf3-012c7cb41ce1.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>, while political and governance risk has remained persistently Substantial across all review periods <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-4b12f487-05cf-4472-bbf3-012c7cb41ce1.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>. The Digital Finance and Connectivity project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088b5f99a&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174461 – PAD</a> faces compounding governance fragility: the ARMD regulatory authority lacks its primary operational tool—the Digital Code Book Two—which has not been adopted despite repeated mission recommendations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-a6aeb6a6-887c-41f9-8eb7-fb31c9e7d652.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>, and ARMD board appointments have remained vacant since 2022 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>, reflecting deliberate institutional subordination consistent with executive consolidation under DJ-001.

**Ali Sabieh and Southern Corridor Region**

The Service Delivery and Access category defines the risk landscape in Djibouti's southern region, where refugee concentration, corridor infrastructure, and cross-border dynamics converge. The country hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons with 85% residing in refugee villages [DJ-009], and the Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps in Ali Sabieh represent the primary concentration of this population. The FRUD-armé insurgent threat [DJ-005], which killed seven soldiers in October 2023, creates direct operational security risks for projects in this region, while the escalating Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis [DJ-006] threatens cross-border coordination and refugee population stability. The Djibouti-Addis Corridor project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> identifies "security/fragility (substantial)" risk from regional instability but relies only on "regular security risk assessments" without specific protocols for FRUD-armé threats to road rehabilitation sites or border operations at Guelileh. These risks have materialized severely: 150 daily laborers lack proper employment contracts and social security registration, with deplorable working conditions including excessive heat exposure <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>; open trenches remain unsecured due to ONEAD coordination failures creating serious accident risks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>; government quarterly reports omit non-conformities observed in the field <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>; and political and governance risk has remained persistently Substantial across all assessment periods <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The TVET Skills project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> concentrates 3,165 refugee beneficiaries in Ali Sabieh camps but has achieved zero refugee enrollment and zero refugee completion data, with the ISR noting this data "is currently not available" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The Urban Resilience project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a> targets refugee villages in this region but has delivered zero property access certificates to refugees—pending approval of village restructuring plans <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>—and zero refugee-led CSO sub-projects have been implemented despite a target of 60 <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>, reflecting the broader collapse of independent civil society [DJ-004] extending to refugee communities. The Education project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a> shows declining refugee enrollment from 596 in 2021-2022 to 384 in 2024-2025 (pending verification) <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>, a worsening trajectory directly reflecting resource constraints and institutional strain from hosting displaced populations.

**Obock, Tadjourah, and Northern Interior Regions**

The Economic Vulnerability and Social Cohesion and Protection categories jointly characterize the risk profile of Djibouti's northern and interior regions, where pastoral livelihoods, remote communities, and cross-cutting social protection challenges intersect. Youth unemployment exceeding 75% [DJ-002] and ethnic tensions between the Issa Somali majority and Afar minority [DJ-003]—who are concentrated in northern regions—create structural barriers to inclusive economic programming. The Agri-Food and Rural Development project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a> operates across three zones including the Northern Zone (Tadjourah and Obock) but asserts "no security risks identified for project implementation" despite FRUD-armé's demonstrated capacity for armed raids in proximate areas [DJ-005]—an assessment that appears to underestimate the threat. The Food Security project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a> has experienced the most acute realization of economic vulnerability risks: the government declared a drought emergency in June 2025, triggering project suspension and an official restructuring request <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>; only 11 of 25 targeted water infrastructure facilities have been rehabilitated with handover pending, while rehabilitation of the remaining 26 has been discontinued <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>; and the project's disbursement rate stood at only 15.2% one year before closure <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. Procurement delays are systemic, with the strategic food stock procurement spending 10 weeks in the procurement cycle <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the complaint management mechanism for sexual exploitation and abuse remains only 53% operationalized <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Social Safety Net projects [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire] face persistent implementation challenges in remote localities: poor road infrastructure causes logistical delays in in-kind transfer distribution during rainy seasons; viable income-generating activities cannot be identified due to weak market access; grievance mechanisms have structural weaknesses limiting effective beneficiary response; and youth training programs targeting 400 beneficiaries in interior regions carry risks of unequal access without pre-defined eligibility criteria or transparent selection procedures [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire]. The ethnic dimension of these risks remains unaddressed: the PAD for the Agri-Food project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a> specifies three geographically dispersed implementation zones but contains no mechanisms to ensure equitable benefit distribution across Issa and Afar populations, while the Sub-Project Selection Committee lacks explicit criteria for ethnic inclusivity—a gap that could reproduce structural exclusion at the project level.
