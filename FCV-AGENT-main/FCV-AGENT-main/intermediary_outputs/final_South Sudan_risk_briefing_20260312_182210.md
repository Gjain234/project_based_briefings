# South Sudan FCV Portfolio Briefing

## Paragraph 1: Collapse of the Peace Agreement and Return to Full-Scale War

The 2018 Revitalized Agreement on the Resolution of the Conflict in South Sudan (R-ARCSS) is at critical risk of full collapse. President Kiir's unilateral amendments to the agreement, cabinet reshuffles without consulting signatories, and the SSPDF's major military offensive launched in Jonglei State in late January 2026 have led the International Crisis Group to declare that South Sudan has "returned to war." This risk cuts across the entire portfolio. Projects relying on government-led implementation are acutely exposed: the Resilient Agricultural Livelihoods Project (RALP) depends on the Ministry of Agriculture and Food Security as lead implementing agency, and the PAD explicitly acknowledges that "protracted conflict, violence, and economic meltdown have eroded management and technical capacity of MAFS" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08861755b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P169120 – PAD</a>. The South Sudan Electricity Access Project similarly relies on the Ministry of Energy and Dams and a Project Steering Committee chaired by the Minister, with the PAD noting that "if government implementation becomes infeasible," a pre-selected third-party implementer would be required <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3c065209-7373-4a16-92d2-eff384787700.pdf" target="_blank">P178891 – PAD</a>. The Health System Transformation Project depends on a High-Level Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Health, which would cease to function under full-scale war <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2886229a-f8b3-472a-bcdc-2ee681b5fe59.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing. Implementation Status Reports for the education project document that macroeconomic risk has been upgraded from Substantial to High, directly linked to the January 2026 conflict escalation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178654-1d2a0a33-5408-4d44-962f-daf3b0692ffa.pdf" target="_blank">P178654 – ISR</a>. The PFM project records 9-month salary arrears for government counterparts, with the worsening from 4 to 9 months reflecting the diversion of fiscal resources to military operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176761-bf79f177-54c7-42fd-a88d-6f811275abe1.pdf" target="_blank">P176761 – ISR</a>. The agriculture project's Aide Memoire documents a delayed presidential endorsement of the Food Security Crisis Preparedness Plan, reflecting a government increasingly focused on military consolidation rather than civilian welfare systems <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169120-4884fdbc-7d3a-4d98-b147-22c863449688.pdf" target="_blank">P169120 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the peace agreement's collapse is both a forward-looking existential threat and an already-materializing constraint on institutional functionality.

## Paragraph 2: Intensified Military Operations, Humanitarian Access Crisis, and Attacks on Aid Operations

The SSPDF's major offensive in Jonglei State—involving repeated aerial bombardments of civilian areas, "no quarter" rhetoric by senior commanders, and ethnic militia mobilization—has created an acute operational crisis for the portfolio. Three projects have direct implementation presence in Jonglei: the Integrated Safety Net Project operates in Bor and Pibor counties <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088dd19cd&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P177663 – PAD</a>; the Livestock and Fisheries Development Project covers Bor County and Twic East <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-f81886be-210a-4bc0-9a92-5cf656fbbc43.pdf" target="_blank">P500553 – PAD</a>; and the Resilient Communities and Local Governance Project targets Pibor, Fangak, and Twic East <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-e8d67f4f-bc76-49af-9b6c-6099c748075b.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – PAD</a>. The PAD for the livestock project explicitly acknowledges a "narrow window of opportunity (6 months dry season) for implementation" and "limited access to some project areas during certain seasons"—conditions that have been entirely overtaken by active military operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-f81886be-210a-4bc0-9a92-5cf656fbbc43.pdf" target="_blank">P500553 – PAD</a>. Compounding the military offensive, the broader humanitarian access crisis has seen WFP suspend activities in Baliet County, an MSF-supported hospital bombed in Lankien on 3 February 2026, and the government ordering civilians and UN personnel to relocate from several Jonglei counties ahead of military operations. These risks are actively materializing across the portfolio. The Health System Transformation Project's Aide Memoire documents the closure of over 20 HSTP-supported health facilities due to aerial bombardment in New Fangak and ongoing security challenges, with unresolved pharmaceutical deliveries in Nasir and Ulang counties <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181385-31c255fc-69d1-4cc6-88c2-e37e28acd22f.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – Aide Memoire</a>. The hydromet project records that security constraints have prevented site assessments for infrastructure construction, with the security firm contracting delayed beyond its planned July 2025 timeline [P500441 | Aide Memoire; P180611 | Aide Memoire]. The Resilient Communities project's Implementation Status Report documents that volatile security is constraining civil works and that the Third-Party Monitoring Agent cannot access conflict-affected areas for independent verification <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177093-fdc25708-770e-40de-aa94-2679135bcf13.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – ISR</a>. The livestock project's Implementation Status Report confirms high security and environment/social risk ratings, with rangeland and water management tensions compounded by active military operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P500553-75d9db8b-09e8-406c-afeb-8555fb5454bf.pdf" target="_blank">P500553 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 3: Acute Economic Fragility, Oil Revenue Dependency, and Fiscal Collapse

South Sudan's near-total dependence on oil revenues—which constitute over 90 percent of government income and are routed entirely through Sudan's conflict-affected pipeline—creates a structural vulnerability that pervades the entire portfolio. A pipeline rupture in February 2024 caused severe economic contraction, with hyperinflation averaging approximately 143 percent in FY2024/25 and poverty rising to an estimated 92 percent. The renewed conflict escalation since January 2026 risks severing oil export routes again, and government troops are reportedly largely unpaid, with elite loyalty undermined by reduced fiscal revenues. Every project in the portfolio identifies macroeconomic risk as "High." The PFM Improvement and Strengthening Project PAD identifies "oil price volatility," "dwindling oil production," and "high debt distress risk (59.5% of GDP)" as core threats, with the project's sustainability depending on the government maintaining fiscal discipline under an IMF Staff-Monitored Program <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088d01007&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176761 – PAD</a>. The Health System Transformation Project PAD notes "irregular payment of civil service salaries including health workers" and "government commitment to health financing uncertain" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2886229a-f8b3-472a-bcdc-2ee681b5fe59.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – PAD</a>. The Financial Sector Development Project PAD identifies "heavy dependence on oil revenues for central government budget" and "high risk of debt distress (67% of GDP)" as the primary macroeconomic risks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-d2045e4c-b687-437a-8ee2-eb1edb36395a.pdf" target="_blank">P179278 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing with severity. The PFM project's Implementation Status Report documents 10-month salary arrears for government counterparts, with the project's core objective of improving budget implementation predictability directly undermined by fiscal collapse <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176761-bf79f177-54c7-42fd-a88d-6f811275abe1.pdf" target="_blank">P176761 – ISR</a>. The Health System Transformation Project's Aide Memoire records that the government's co-financing commitment of US$20 million is overdue, with the Ministry of Health's budget execution at 31 percent against a 50 percent target, directly attributable to oil revenue constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181385-31c255fc-69d1-4cc6-88c2-e37e28acd22f.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – Aide Memoire</a>. The education project's Implementation Status Report confirms macroeconomic risk escalation from Substantial to High, with the government's ability to sustain teacher payroll after project completion severely compromised <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178654-1d2a0a33-5408-4d44-962f-daf3b0692ffa.pdf" target="_blank">P178654 – ISR</a>. The Civil Service Reform Project records government failure to deposit required counterpart funding of US$3 million, signaling systemic cash flow constraints rather than administrative delay <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P500500-59292fc4-855f-47ed-84e5-0ca6c3bf985d.pdf" target="_blank">P500500 – Aide Memoire</a>. The financial sector project's Implementation Status Report maintains a persistent "High" macroeconomic risk rating, with the banking sector's viability directly threatened by currency instability and fiscal fragility <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P179278-6eaa8a2f-4164-43d4-a658-b3edad29f602.pdf" target="_blank">P179278 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 4: Mass Internal Displacement, Refugee Pressure, and Sudan Conflict Spillover

South Sudan is experiencing a compounding displacement crisis: approximately 280,000 people were displaced by fighting and airstrikes since late December 2025, with over 235,000 in Jonglei State alone, layered on top of 1.9 million pre-existing internally displaced persons and 2.3 million South Sudanese refugees in neighboring countries. Simultaneously, South Sudan hosts nearly 600,000 refugees—mostly from Sudan—and over 800,000 South Sudanese returnees who fled Sudan's conflict since April 2023. Sudan's ongoing war creates direct economic pressure through threats to the oil pipeline, with RSF attacks on Sudanese oil infrastructure in 2025 briefly threatening to shut South Sudan's export capacity entirely. Multiple projects are directly exposed to these dynamics. The Resilient Communities and Local Governance Project targets over 920,000 beneficiaries including IDPs, refugees, and host communities across 12 counties, with US$20 million from the IDA Window for Host Communities and Refugees allocated to Maban and Pariang counties hosting 95 percent of South Sudan's Sudanese refugees <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-e8d67f4f-bc76-49af-9b6c-6099c748075b.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – PAD</a>. The Health System Transformation Project targets approximately 330,000 refugees and host communities with US$105 million from the IDA Window for Host Communities and Refugees, operating in Upper Nile State (Maban, Renk) and Jonglei State (Jamjang, Pochalla) <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2886229a-f8b3-472a-bcdc-2ee681b5fe59.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – PAD</a>. The education project explicitly targets refugee-hosting areas in Ruweng Administrative Area and Upper Nile State, with Component 3 re-operationalizing 40 non-functional schools to serve approximately 5,000 out-of-school children <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-6bf0132a-5b36-4765-b031-3d0de461f77e.pdf" target="_blank">P178654 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The education project's Implementation Status Report documents that the development objective was modified to explicitly address refugee access to education, reflecting the acute pressure on education systems from displacement flows, while preparatory activities in refugee-hosting areas face persistent delays due to volatile security <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178654-1d2a0a33-5408-4d44-962f-daf3b0692ffa.pdf" target="_blank">P178654 – ISR</a>. The Health System Transformation Project's Implementation Status Report records that third-party monitoring agencies cannot be onboarded in displacement-affected zones, with data verification gaps in refugee and host community areas directly attributable to access constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181385-5b09fa0c-9926-43db-adf4-616d047b7fcb.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – ISR</a>. The Resilient Communities project's Implementation Status Report documents refugee-host community tensions in Pariang and Maban counties, with resource competition straining the project's integration objectives <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177093-fdc25708-770e-40de-aa94-2679135bcf13.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – ISR</a>. The agriculture project's Implementation Status Report records active engagement with 11,811 returnees and 830 refugees, with Crisis Response Window activation supporting 47,444 farmers facing acute food insecurity driven by displacement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169120-7aa9496d-b33a-4d6f-9215-13a79cf81b04.pdf" target="_blank">P169120 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 5: Political Repression, Governance Fragility, and Constrained Civic Space

President Kiir's government has used the National Security Service to escalate arrests, detentions, and harassment of civil society actors, journalists, and political opponents under the 2024 NSS Act. First Vice President Riek Machar remains under house arrest on politically motivated charges. At least 114 cases of censorship, harassment, and arbitrary arrests of civil society and journalists were documented between July 2022 and July 2025. Government demands for a 70 percent reduction in UNMISS international military contingents, closure of bases in Wau and Bentiu, and the grounding of UN-contracted aircraft have further constrained the security and oversight environment for the portfolio. These governance dynamics create systemic susceptibility across projects that depend on participatory planning, civil society engagement, and transparent institutional processes. The Resilient Communities project's PAD emphasizes "inclusive and transparent planning process" and "strengthening community institutions (BDCs/PDCs)" as core to project design, while acknowledging "tensions between government and opposition entities" as a high political risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-e8d67f4f-bc76-49af-9b6c-6099c748075b.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – PAD</a>. The PFM project explicitly includes "community monitoring groups," "budget literacy workshops," and "media partnerships" to support transparency—activities that face direct risk from NSS harassment of civil society participants <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088d01007&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176761 – PAD</a>. The livestock project's PAD acknowledges that "South Sudan's long history of conflict may affect engagement with local authorities and communities" and relies on a Stakeholder Engagement Plan that assumes civic space that is actively shrinking <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-f81886be-210a-4bc0-9a92-5cf656fbbc43.pdf" target="_blank">P500553 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing in governance dysfunction across the portfolio. The PFM project's Implementation Status Report documents that the National Legislative Assembly has failed to approve public procurement reforms, with the delays in constituting assembly committees reflecting the broader institutional capture and political repression that prevents normal legislative functioning <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176761-bf79f177-54c7-42fd-a88d-6f811275abe1.pdf" target="_blank">P176761 – ISR</a>. The energy project's Aide Memoire records contractor fraud, unauthorized payments, asset theft, and staff misconduct within the Project Implementation Unit, with the government's disregard for World Bank procurement directives reflecting the same pattern of institutional accountability erosion documented in the political repression risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178891-008ca349-4c37-4697-9301-9c6dbd23e9ef.pdf" target="_blank">P178891 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Civil Service Reform Project's Aide Memoire documents a delayed grievance mechanism establishment, weak financial management rated as Substantial risk, and government failure to deposit counterpart funding—all reflecting governance dysfunction in an environment where institutional checks are systematically undermined <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P500500-59292fc4-855f-47ed-84e5-0ca6c3bf985d.pdf" target="_blank">P500500 – Aide Memoire</a>. The agriculture project's Aide Memoire records absent internal audit arrangements, unresolved audit findings, non-compliance with national tax legislation, and inadequate environmental and social staffing at project sites, with the pattern of fiduciary weakness reflecting the broader governance environment where independent oversight mechanisms are compromised <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169120-4884fdbc-7d3a-4d98-b147-22c863449688.pdf" target="_blank">P169120 – Aide Memoire</a>.
