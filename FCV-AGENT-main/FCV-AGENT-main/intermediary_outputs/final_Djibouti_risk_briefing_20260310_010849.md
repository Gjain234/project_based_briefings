# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Governance Consolidation and Democratic Backsliding

Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms. This political consolidation creates direct risks for projects that depend on multi-ministerial coordination, sustained government commitment, and institutional autonomy. The TVET and skills development project is exposed because its governance architecture relies on a high-level steering committee under the Economic Development Council, an executive body, without independent oversight or civil society safeguards to insulate implementation from political capture <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The public finance management reform project faces analogous vulnerability: its governance structure depends on a PFM Reform Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance, and the PAD explicitly identifies vested interests within public administration as a high political risk, relying on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that weakens as executive power consolidates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project similarly flags that its ability to mobilize private sector finance depends on political will, and that line ministries may resist change—a risk that intensifies under indefinite presidential rule <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing. The PFM project is experiencing a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with administrative delays in publishing governance decrees blocking project operationalization despite ministerial approval <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The digital connectivity project carries a sustained "High" political and governance risk, with ARMD board appointments vacant since 2022 and the Digital Code's telecommunications regulation (Book Two) still unadopted despite repeated mission recommendations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's steering committee has been inactive, and governance coordination between government and development partners remains unresolved <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the pattern is consistent: formal institutional structures exist on paper but lack the operational independence and political priority to function effectively in an environment of deepening executive dominance.

## 2. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Media Freedom

Djibouti is classified as "Not Free" by Freedom House, with virtually no independent civil society remaining and journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested for criticizing the government. This systemic closure of civic space creates a structural implementation gap across the entire portfolio, as projects routinely rely on stakeholder engagement, grievance redress mechanisms, community feedback loops, and independent monitoring—all of which are severely compromised in this environment. The PFM reform project's Component 3 explicitly targets civil society engagement and citizen oversight of public finances, yet the PAD does not acknowledge that the country's civil society ecosystem is functionally non-existent, creating a fundamental design gap <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The education project establishes a grievance mechanism "for all stakeholders," but in a context where activists face arrest for criticism, vulnerable groups including refugees, rural students, and girls face structural barriers to voicing concerns <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. The corridor project includes a Stakeholder Engagement Plan and grievance redress mechanisms, but does not address how these will function when independent voices cannot safely monitor or challenge government resource allocation decisions <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing across the portfolio. The corridor project is experiencing persistent "Substantial" stakeholder engagement risk, with community consultation constrained by the absence of independent intermediaries <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's GBV action plans remain unimplemented, stakeholder mobilization plans are delayed, and the internal audit function has been compromised by lack of organizational independence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The social protection project shows weak grievance mechanisms and limited beneficiary participation, with communities reluctant to engage openly in a restricted political environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The GBV prevention project has experienced stalled NGO contract negotiations, capacity gaps in psychosocial service delivery, and delayed environmental and social safeguards reporting—all reflecting the institutional hollowing that results from the collapse of independent civil society <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. The statistics project faces INSTAD institutional overload, with compromised data quality reducing the transparency and public accountability that independent oversight would normally demand <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 3. Severe Youth Unemployment and Economic Inequality

Youth unemployment in Djibouti exceeds 75%, compounded by endemic corruption, high inequality, and limited private sector absorptive capacity. This structural economic fragility creates both demand-side and supply-side risks for projects targeting employment, livelihoods, and economic inclusion. The TVET project is directly exposed: it aims to place 23,775 trained graduates into employment, but the PAD acknowledges macroeconomic risk as "substantial" without conducting a labor market demand assessment or documenting employer absorption capacity—a critical design gap given the scale of structural unemployment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food project targets youth as direct beneficiaries but does not specify employment pathways or income-generation mechanisms within its livelihood restoration activities, creating risk that training will not translate into sustainable economic outcomes <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The corridor project identifies youth employment as a benefit but provides no youth employment targets, skills training provisions, or analysis of how corridor improvements will generate jobs for the 75%+ unemployed youth population <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing with significant consequences. The TVET project's refugee employment indicator is stuck at 5% against an 80% target, reflecting the structural inability of Djibouti's labor market to absorb trained graduates even with targeted interventions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The online gig work component has stalled entirely, suggesting that supply-side training cannot overcome demand-side constraints including limited digital infrastructure and low internet penetration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The social protection project is experiencing difficulty identifying viable income-generating activities in remote localities, with weak market access and limited economic diversification constraining livelihood programming <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project has documented exploitation of 150 daily workers through non-compliant contracts and lack of social security registration—a direct consequence of workers' minimal bargaining power in a context of extreme unemployment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The food security project's failure to deliver agricultural inputs, livestock, and water infrastructure to vulnerable households further compounds economic marginalization in a context where 75% youth unemployment leaves rural populations with few alternative livelihood options <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 4. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Amid Resource Constraints

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers—with 85% concentrated in refugee villages, primarily from Somalia, Ethiopia, and Yemen. Despite an open-door policy, the country faces recurrent droughts and severely limited public service capacity, creating structural strain on institutions responsible for both development programming and humanitarian service delivery. Multiple projects are directly exposed. The TVET project targets 3,165 refugees as direct beneficiaries concentrated in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps in Ali Sabieh region, but does not assess how resource scarcity or humanitarian access risks would affect training participation and completion rates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The emergency preparedness project (CERP) identifies institutional risk as "high" because SEGRC—the primary implementing agency for refugee-related emergency response—has limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff, creating a structural mismatch between mandate and resources <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The food security project explicitly targets refugees for emergency food stock distribution but does not specify allocation protocols between refugee villages and host populations or contingency plans for managing competing demands <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio with measurable impact. The health project shows refugee immunization rates at 50% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5%—concrete manifestations of the resource constraints and fragile public health infrastructure that characterize Djibouti's refugee hosting environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The TVET project has zero refugee enrollment against a target of 220, and zero refugee employment outcomes, reflecting the institutional incapacity to operationalize refugee training programs within resource-constrained camps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The urban development project shows stalled refugee village restructuring plans for Ali Addeh and Holl Holl, with pending validation creating delays in improving living conditions for the 85% of displaced persons in these settlements <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The education project shows declining refugee student enrollment—from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025—and persistent failure to integrate refugee schools into the national EMIS system, demonstrating how resource-constrained institutions struggle to extend service delivery to displaced populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The food security project has achieved only 50% of its emergency food stock target and zero water infrastructure rehabilitation, directly undermining humanitarian assistance capacity for the 32,000 displaced persons the country hosts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Domestic Security Threats from Insurgent Activity

The FRUD-armé splinter group conducted a raid on an army post in October 2023, killing seven soldiers and injuring four, demonstrating active armed insurgent capacity within Djibouti's territory. Combined with proximity to al-Shabaab and other terrorist organizations in Somalia, this security environment creates operational risks for projects with field presence in remote and border-proximate regions. The TVET project operates across six regions including Ali Sabieh and Obock—geographically proximate to areas of FRUD-armé activity—with 3,165 refugee beneficiaries concentrated in camps in Ali Sabieh, yet the PAD's security risk assessment is limited to "institutional protocols and safe learning environments" without specific threat assessment for these locations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The corridor project identifies "security/fragility (substantial)" risk from regional instability but relies only on "regular security risk assessments" without specific protocols for FRUD-armé threats to road rehabilitation sites, ITIS installations, or border operations at the Guelileh crossing <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The statistics project explicitly acknowledges security risks during census activities and notes that field teams must access communities across national territory, with FRUD-armé's demonstrated armed capability creating tangible risk to enumerators <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a>. The agri-food project operates in the Northern Zone (Tadjourah and Obock regions) with climate-proof rural infrastructure including boreholes and rural roads, yet the PAD asserts "no security risks identified" despite FRUD-armé's October 2023 raid in proximate areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. These risks are beginning to materialize in implementation. The statistics project's household survey implementation has encountered security concerns in refugee settlements, with field enumeration tensions and population displacement creating access barriers that required operational adjustments <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project has experienced coordination and planning difficulties, with safety violations on construction sites and inadequate HSE oversight—conditions that are exacerbated by the security environment and weak institutional accountability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The food security project's June 2025 drought declaration triggered project suspension and restructuring, with the security and humanitarian environment compounding implementation challenges in remote areas where insurgent activity creates additional access constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>.
