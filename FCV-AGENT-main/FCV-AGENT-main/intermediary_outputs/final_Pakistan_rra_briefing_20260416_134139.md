**Social unrest and protests:** Pakistan's political landscape is defined by a deepening civil-military hybrid governance crisis. Imran Khan remains imprisoned under conditions condemned by the UN Special Rapporteur on Torture, over 103 PTI workers face military trials, human rights lawyers were sentenced to 17 years in January 2026 for social media criticism, and the PTI withdrew from negotiations with the government in early 2026, entrenching political deadlock (PAK-004). The KP Chief Minister has publicly alleged systematic federal economic strangulation of the province, and the Human Rights Commission of Pakistan warned in January 2026 that suppression of dissent could seriously undermine civil liberties against a backdrop of 44.7 percent poverty (PAK-010). These dynamics directly threaten projects requiring federal-provincial coordination. The Merged Areas Development Project depends on coordination among five line departments under a governance structure where the PTI-led KP government is in direct conflict with federal authorities, creating risk that military security priorities override implementation schedules <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-107c8a45-eb40-4cbd-85f2-4e94744ec5dd.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – PAD</a>. The SPEED program's citizen engagement mechanisms—budget consultations, parent-teacher councils, and a provincial grievance redress system—assume civic space that is being actively contracted by security restrictions on demonstrations and mobile communications in KP <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088486e7b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P175727 – PAD</a>. The Sindh Social Protection Authority project faces escalating political and governance risk, with the ISR rating worsening from Substantial to High, reflecting the broader governance crisis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178532-6d993aa7-8a16-4819-90c3-4bf6693bb1bf.pdf" target="_blank">P178532 – ISR</a>. The CRISP social protection program's federal-provincial coordination mechanisms are directly undermined by the political deadlock, with the ISR confirming persistent Substantial governance risk and delayed provincial cabinet endorsements of social protection frameworks <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0887f499d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174484 – ISR</a>. The Khyber Pass Economic Corridor project's governance risk has similarly escalated, with the ISR noting the project's dependence on coordination between NHA, FATA Secretariat, and KP government in an environment of acute center-periphery conflict <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P159577-0c847e62-7ead-46cf-bd58-e100f1771a19.pdf" target="_blank">P159577 – ISR</a>.

**Violence between refugees, host communities, and the state:** Pakistan has deported over 146,000 Afghans in 2026 as of April, following over one million forced returns in 2025, with the suspension of Proof of Registration renewals leaving 1.3 million undocumented and exposed to deportation. MSF documented life-threatening humanitarian consequences including families avoiding medical care and women giving birth in uncontrolled conditions (PAK-005). Simultaneously, over 11,000 families—80–95 percent of Tirah Valley's population—were displaced by military anti-TTP operations in January 2026, with at least one woman reportedly dying of hunger at a transit point, and IDP registration remained ongoing as of March 2026 (PAK-006). The Refugees and Host Communities project (P166309) is funded through IDA's Regional Sub-Window for Refugees and Host Communities, with 519,303 refugees in target districts; the PAD identifies high refugee protection risk from potential changes to Pakistan's protection framework, but does not address the current scale of deportations approaching 1.4 million PoR card holders <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0879fa728&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166309 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms this risk is materializing: 7,835 Afghan refugee children are currently enrolled against a target of 12,000, with the ISR noting enrollment figures will be updated after the 2025–26 school year—a direct reflection of population instability from deportations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166309-e6c7abf6-7829-4ecf-b963-bf4cfa1c931c.pdf" target="_blank">P166309 – ISR</a>. The Refugee Affected and Hosting Areas project (P165542) directly targets 15,000 PoR card holders for visa policy benefits, but the ISR documents that CCAR lacks adequate organizational capacity to undertake its mandate in the changing refugee protection context, with no automated refugee data management system and staff capacity misaligned to functions as of September 2023—institutional gaps that are now acutely consequential given the deportation surge <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P165542-2226f908-40b4-4cb7-a8b5-21f422b5b997.pdf" target="_blank">P165542 – ISR</a>. The Balochistan Flood Emergency project (P180323) targets 2.7 million flood-affected people in a province hosting significant Afghan refugee populations, but the PAD does not address how forced deportations could affect beneficiary populations or create community instability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-cada74cb-95f5-4ca2-a46a-9560206ebad9.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – PAD</a>.

**Organized violence between political and sectarian groups:** Pakistan ranked first on the Global Terrorism Index 2026, with 1,139 deaths and 1,045 incidents in 2025—the highest fatality level in over a decade—driven primarily by TTP attacks accounting for over half of all deaths, with violence surging 76 percent in March 2026 to 146 attacks (PAK-002). On February 6, 2026, an ISKP suicide bomber killed 31 Shia worshippers at an Islamabad mosque, the deadliest such attack in over a decade in the capital, with ISKP explicitly targeting Pakistani Shia communities; the attack directly precipitated Pakistan's airstrikes on Afghanistan (PAK-009). The BLA launched Operation Herof 2 on January 31, 2026, conducting over 76 coordinated attacks across 14 locations including military installations, banks, and prisons, resulting in nearly 200 deaths, and conducted its first-ever maritime attack on April 12, 2026 (PAK-003). The Merged Areas Development Project operates in Bajaur, Khyber, Kurram, Mohmand, North Waziristan, and South Waziristan—all high-intensity TTP conflict zones—with the PAD acknowledging periodic security challenges in remote sites but not addressing the current intensity of violence or drone proliferation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-107c8a45-eb40-4cbd-85f2-4e94744ec5dd.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms zero progress on district administrative centers and sub-district service centers 24 months into implementation, with activities only expected to commence in the next six months, directly reflecting security and governance constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176780-319537c9-6ae2-4eee-8dbf-6bb4120b5e55.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – ISR</a>. The Balochistan Flood Emergency project operates in a province where BLA violence accounts for 55 percent of national terror deaths; the PAD acknowledges the security situation will restrict travel to project areas and relies on remote supervision tools, but the ISR documents escalating fiduciary risk (from Substantial to High) and stakeholder tensions (from Substantial to High), indicating that security-driven access constraints are materializing in implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180323-aca41980-8e57-40b5-96a1-1f120e049689.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – ISR</a>. The National Health Support Program operates in KP and Balochistan, with the PAD explicitly noting that insecurity affects staff recruitment, retention, and mobility—particularly for female health workers—and the ISR documents that referral system implementation is stalled in KP and Sindh, with supply chain disruptions causing medicine availability to fall from 70 to 58 percent at PHC facilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172615-d4815612-d79d-470e-a08b-47633da31938.pdf" target="_blank">P172615 – ISR</a>.

**Political instability:** Pakistan's civil-military hybrid governance system, with the Shehbaz Sharif government operating as a junior partner to the military establishment, has produced a governance environment characterized by systemic corruption in the lowest quartile globally on rule of law indicators, repeated IMF program failures to produce structural reform, and a 60 percent interest-revenue ratio (PAK-011). Pakistan's economy remains critically dependent on a USD 7 billion IMF Extended Fund Facility, with the IMF downgrading growth forecasts to 3.5 percent for FY2026–27 and raising inflation projections to 8.4 percent; FDI collapsed to USD 808 million in H1 FY2026 from USD 1.425 billion a year earlier (PAK-007). The SPEED program's core objective of improving public resource management for education and health in KP depends on sustained provincial fiscal commitment, but the ISR documents that multi-year budget integration was not completed due to the interim government, and supply chain disruptions caused medicine availability to fall from 70 to 53 percent at PHC facilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175727-191b6395-e711-43ac-a0c3-a0e4aa213f35.pdf" target="_blank">P175727 – ISR</a>. The Dasu Hydropower project faces escalating macroeconomic risk (from Substantial to High in the ISR), with currency depreciation directly affecting private capital mobilization calculations and the project's complex financing structure—which assumes 56 percent commercial financing—exposed to investor flight driven by security deterioration <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b082dcef4e&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P121507 – ISR</a>. The Karachi BRT project's macroeconomic risk has similarly escalated from Substantial to High, with the ISR noting that SMTA needs to avoid delays in contract management and procurement of BRT corridor packages, while the implementing agency remains understaffed at 35 of 170 approved positions <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088b18827&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166732 – ISR</a>. The KP Irrigated Agriculture Improvement Project faces persistent Substantial institutional capacity risk, with HEIS implementation at only 9 percent against a 45 percent target due to PC-1 revision approval delays, and many farmers withdrawing from the scheme due to high installation costs reflecting broader economic vulnerability <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0880f7690&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163474 – ISR</a>.

**External risks and intra-state conflict:** Pakistan declared "open war" against Afghanistan on February 27, 2026, after conducting airstrikes on Taliban military facilities in Kabul, Kandahar, and Paktia—the first time Islamabad directly targeted Afghan Taliban rather than affiliated militants. A mass-casualty strike near Kabul on March 16 drew international condemnation; a fragile Eid ceasefire was brokered by Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and Turkey on March 18, but China-mediated talks in Urumqi have failed to reach a formal agreement, leaving the risk of renewed escalation high (PAK-001). TTP attacks—precipitated by Afghan Taliban sanctuary and growing cooperation with al-Qaeda-aligned groups—have reached record levels, with the UN Security Council reporting the Afghan Taliban provides TTP "preferential treatment" (PAK-002). The Khyber Pass Economic Corridor project is existentially exposed to this risk: the PAD identifies border skirmishes between Pakistan and Afghanistan as a political and governance risk, and the project's core objective of expanding cross-border trade depends on bilateral mechanisms with Afghanistan that are now suspended amid active hostilities <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b085b2e6ff&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P159577 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms the project's cross-border conflict and security dynamics as a persistent High risk, with the PDO of expanding economic activity between Pakistan and Afghanistan directly threatened by the current interstate conflict <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P159577-0c847e62-7ead-46cf-bd58-e100f1771a19.pdf" target="_blank">P159577 – ISR</a>. The Merged Areas Development Project covers five districts directly bordering Afghanistan—Bajaur, Khyber, Kurram, Mohmand, and North Waziristan—where cross-border escalation has directly caused displacement and military operations; the PAD's framework approach to implementation assumes "low-grade conflict with occasional skirmishes" that is materially inconsistent with active cross-border air strikes and drone operations documented in March 2026 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-107c8a45-eb40-4cbd-85f2-4e94744ec5dd.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – PAD</a>. The Refugees and Host Communities project's border management component supports improvements at Torkham and complements the Afghanistan EZ-KAR project on passport issuance, but the ISR documents persistent High political and governance risk and worsening stakeholder coordination challenges that directly reflect the breakdown in Pakistan-Afghanistan bilateral relations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P165542-2226f908-40b4-4cb7-a8b5-21f422b5b997.pdf" target="_blank">P165542 – ISR</a>.
