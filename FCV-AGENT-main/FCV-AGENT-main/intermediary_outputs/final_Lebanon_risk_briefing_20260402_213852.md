# Lebanon Portfolio FCV Risk Briefing

## 1. Renewed Full-Scale Israel-Hezbollah Hostilities and Collapse of the November 2024 Ceasefire

Lebanon's most acute risk is the resumption of large-scale armed conflict following the March 2, 2026 escalation triggered by spillover from US-Israeli strikes on Iran. Israeli military operations have expanded well beyond previously held positions, displacement orders now cover approximately 14 percent of Lebanese territory, and by late March the UN Emergency Relief Coordinator warned the Security Council that Lebanon is at "breaking point" with most bridges south of the Litani River destroyed and entire villages flattened. The portfolio is comprehensively exposed. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project (LEAP) explicitly acknowledges that geopolitical risks from Middle East conflict may impede implementation in border areas, with project locations in southern Lebanon and the Bekaa Valley directly within active conflict zones; the PAD requires Lebanese Army clearance for explosive remnants of war before any investment can proceed and acknowledges that World Bank supervision missions are security-constrained <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project PAD states that works remain suspended and implementing agency operations severely disrupted, with a hard rule that no works will commence until conflict has ended and sites are safe <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a>. The Lebanon Integrated Solid Waste and Pollution Management Project targets municipalities in Tyre, Abbasiyeh, Sahel el Zahrani, and Nabatieh—all now within or adjacent to active conflict zones—requiring field presence for waste characterization, dumpsite rehabilitation, and facility upgrades that cannot safely proceed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a>. The Social Protection and Livelihoods project and the Lebanon Health Resilience Project similarly depend on national coverage including southern Lebanon and the Bekaa Valley for cash transfer delivery and vaccination campaigns respectively <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08813b443&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173367 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The Beqaa Valley Wastewater Project aide memoire documents that contractors suspended all sewage network works due to escalating military activities in the Beqaa region beginning September 2024, with the project unable to resume and facing closure deadline risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>. The LEAP project ISR records that the security situation has forced virtual supervision missions and prohibited work near active shelling, with UXO clearance required before any resumption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Beirut Heritage Initiative aide memoire flags a tight implementation timeline with zero slippage tolerance that is directly threatened by security-driven work stoppages <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176577-6c3b4a27-9cf9-4050-9a10-ed310209e452.pdf" target="_blank">P176577 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Social Protection project ISR documents that sporadic armed strikes on South Lebanon and Beirut's southern suburbs are creating direct implementation barriers for service delivery to vulnerable populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-bde1794c-f97c-48b9-a56e-1949644a584e.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 2. Mass Internal Displacement Exceeding One Million People

Lebanon is experiencing the largest wave of internal displacement in its modern history, with over one million people newly displaced since March 2, 2026—nearly one-fifth of the population—including more than 90,000 Syrian refugees displaced for a second time and 125,800 individuals sheltering in collective sites at 92 percent capacity. This displacement crisis strikes at the core targeting and delivery assumptions of the entire portfolio. The LEAP project was designed around an area-based approach prioritizing communities by damage intensity and economic potential, with Component 1 including decommissioning of IDP centers; the March 2026 displacement wave has rendered these prioritization criteria misaligned with actual population movements and made IDP center decommissioning premature or impossible <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Lebanon Health Resilience Project targets approximately 4.7 million beneficiaries including 1.5 million Syrian and Palestinian refugees, relying on mobile vaccination units and targeted awareness campaigns; mass displacement of over 1.2 million people, with 99 percent of newly displaced refugees being Syrian, directly prevents the project from maintaining beneficiary registries and delivering continuous vaccination services <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a>. The Social Protection project uses household-based targeting requiring residential verification and home visits to reach 147,000 Lebanese families; displacement orders covering 14 percent of Lebanese territory have made home verification visits impossible in affected zones and rendered baseline targeting data obsolete <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08813b443&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173367 – PAD</a>. The Solid Waste Management project identifies approximately 470,000 direct beneficiaries in selected service zones in southern Lebanon; with displacement orders covering these exact areas, waste management service continuity cannot be maintained when populations are under evacuation orders <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing. The Social Protection project ISR records zero progress on beneficiary enrollment for displaced Syrian populations, with the project unable to operationalize targeting mechanisms amid population flux <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0867b7011&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163476 – ISR</a>. The Beirut Heritage Initiative aide memoire documents that family disputes over property deed commitments are being driven in part by absentee owners who have left Lebanon, directly impeding rehabilitation works <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176577-6c3b4a27-9cf9-4050-9a10-ed310209e452.pdf" target="_blank">P176577 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Health Resilience Project ISR confirms that health system pressures from refugee and host community utilization are overwhelming public hospital capacity, with the project tracking severe gaps in pharmaceutical and equipment availability against targets <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a>.

## 3. Collapse of the Healthcare System Under Systematic Attacks and Supply Shortages

Since March 2, 2026, WHO has documented 64 attacks on healthcare infrastructure in Lebanon, killing at least 53 healthcare workers and injuring 91 others, with nine hospitals damaged, four hospitals and 51 primary healthcare centers forced to close entirely, and hospitals operating on medical supply inventories expected to run out within weeks. This collapse compounds a pre-existing baseline severely weakened by six years of economic crisis and mass emigration of healthcare workers. The portfolio's health-dependent projects face acute exposure. The LEAP project's Component 2 targets multi-sectoral restoration of critical infrastructure including health services through mobile clinics and equipment replacement, but the PAD acknowledges pervasive pre-conflict deficits across all covered sectors and depends on coordination with the Ministry of Public Health and a functional health sector for implementation—conditions that no longer exist in conflict-affected areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Lebanon Health Resilience Project's core implementation depends on operational health facilities and available healthcare workforce; Component 2 finances hospitalization bills for COVID-19 and related cases in formal and private hospitals, and Component 3 includes solar panels for hospitals—both of which require functioning facilities that are now closed or severely understaffed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a>. The Social Protection and Livelihoods project's Component 3 delivers psychosocial support, GBV response, and child protection services through primary healthcare centers and mobile medical units; the PAD notes that 51 percent of surveyed refugees access healthcare via PHCCs, and these facilities are now non-functional in conflict zones <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a>. The Beqaa Valley Wastewater Project requires recruitment and retention of technical staff including a wastewater engineer, customer relations officer, and financial officer for the Beqaa Water Establishment, against a backdrop of approximately 400 vacant positions already unfilled due to a government hiring freeze; the healthcare system collapse and mass displacement of skilled workers will further constrain recruitment <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a>. Implementation evidence confirms these risks are materializing. The Health Resilience Project ISR documents that the project has failed to deliver primary healthcare services to any vulnerable populations by project closing, with zero beneficiaries enrolled against a target of 20,000, and zero COVID-19 rapid response teams operational against a target of three <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0867b7011&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163476 – ISR</a>. The same project ISR records that hospital equipment availability stands at only 56 percent against a 70 percent target, with the gap widening as conflict intensifies pressure on health infrastructure <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a>.

## 4. Protracted Economic Crisis Blocking IMF Reform and Reversing Nascent Recovery

Lebanon's economic crisis—described by the World Bank as one of the worst globally since 1850—has seen real GDP decline more than 38 percent since 2019, with the Lebanese pound losing over 97 percent of its value and frozen bank deposits estimated at $82 billion. The World Bank's January 2026 Economic Monitor had projected 3.5 percent real GDP growth in 2025 and 4 percent in 2026, contingent on sustained reforms, modest reconstruction inflows, and political stability—all conditions that the March 2026 escalation has now fundamentally undermined. The portfolio's financial sustainability assumptions are built on this now-invalidated macroeconomic trajectory. The LEAP project identifies macroeconomic risk as high, noting ongoing economic crisis with triple-digit inflation now easing to double digits, Lebanese pound having lost 98 percent of its value, and reconstruction activity potentially driving up construction material and labor prices; the project denominates contracts in US dollars and maintains contingency financing for anticipated price increases, but these mitigations may be insufficient if economic crisis deepens beyond the 38 percent cumulative GDP decline <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Lebanon Agriculture and Rural Development project was designed based on World Bank January 2026 projections of 4 percent growth in 2026 contingent on sustained reforms and political stability; the project's USD 200 million financing and beneficiary payment mechanisms depend on economic stabilization that is now reversed, and the project shows zero disbursement as of August 2025 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-c14c98c6-d426-4177-9a31-f1acaae2fb97.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – PAD</a>. The Social Protection project identifies macroeconomic risk as substantial, noting Lebanon's severe economic crisis with currency depreciation exceeding 90 percent, triple-digit inflation, and shrinking employment; mitigation relies on preserving purchasing power through US dollar-denominated transfers and quarterly review of benefit amounts in Lebanese pounds, but the March 2026 escalation has reversed the modest 2025 gains <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a>. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project identifies macroeconomic risk as high due to structural currency mismatch between revenues in Lebanese pounds and costs approximately 90 percent in US dollars, with mitigation through a Cash Waterfall Mechanism with the Ministry of Finance and Banque du Liban to ensure access to foreign exchange for operational continuity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The Agriculture project ISR records zero farmers reached, zero SME loans approved, and zero infrastructure rehabilitated, with the project stalled since January 2025 due to political instability and macroeconomic fragility <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180334-4899e0cd-2b9b-432c-88d6-4a6b7c577697.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – ISR</a>. The Beqaa Valley Wastewater Project ISR documents escalation from moderate to high macroeconomic risk, with the project's financial sustainability and government counterpart funding of USD 5 million directly threatened by economic collapse <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-740ffba4-1843-48ae-936f-b5ec49d1902a.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – ISR</a>. The Social Protection project ISR confirms that NGO partner funding losses—driven by international development financing contraction—are forcing partners to scale back operations precisely when humanitarian demand has surged <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-bde1794c-f97c-48b9-a56e-1949644a584e.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Beirut Heritage Initiative ISR records that landowner compensation non-collection is being driven by Lebanese lira devaluation rendering compensation amounts negligible in real terms, with 52 percent of landowners unable or unwilling to collect <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Institutional Fragility, State Sovereignty Deficit, and Parliamentary Governance Crisis

Lebanon's new reform-oriented government—which emerged from a two-year presidential vacancy in early 2025—faces a compounding governance crisis: the Lebanese Armed Forces cannot enforce government decrees banning Hezbollah military activities, parliament voted 76-41 on March 9, 2026 to postpone constitutionally required elections by two years, and the state's inability to assert monopoly on the use of force has been publicly demonstrated. This institutional fragility strikes at the governance foundations on which the entire portfolio depends. The LEAP project identifies institutional capacity risk as high, noting that the Council for Development and Reconstruction has limited capacity for a project of this scale, with procurement approval taking nine months and bid evaluation two to eight months; the project depends on CDR reform measures including business process reengineering and digitalization, but the state's demonstrated inability to enforce its own authority undermines these mitigation strategies <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a>. The Public Finance Management project is designed to restore basic functions of domestic revenue mobilization and accountable allocation of public resources, explicitly aiming to renew the social contract between state and citizens through improved governance; the state sovereignty deficit and inability to enforce authority directly contradicts the project's core objective, and the project had not achieved parliamentary ratification as of the June 2025 ISR <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-5067fa60-78e4-4f5e-b6c1-5a428d852b01.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – PAD</a>. The Lebanon Energy Transition project identifies institutional capacity risk as high, noting that EDL and LRA lack experience implementing Bank-financed projects and that the recent economic crisis has accelerated depletion of qualified human resources from public entities; the project's steering committee is chaired by the Ministry of Energy and Water, but the government's inability to enforce disarmament decrees signals broader institutional authority gaps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c260299b-4517-48c7-a0c1-b95eb42d17a7.pdf" target="_blank">P180501 – PAD</a>. The Beirut Heritage Initiative identifies political risk as high, noting that parliamentary elections may cause disruptions to government workflow and that citizen mistrust toward government has escalated since October 2019; the project requires a Project Coordination Committee including representatives from the Ministry of Environment, Beirut Governorate, and the Municipality of Beirut, all of which are now operating under conditions of institutional paralysis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-829b3a34-aa4e-4b31-a4d1-5d469e9f50b4.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The Public Finance Management project ISR records that the IBRD Financing Agreement has not achieved effectiveness due to pending parliamentary ratification, with the project critical for the post-war recovery phase but blocked by the same parliament that just extended its own term by two years <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a>. The Beirut Heritage Initiative ISR documents that the project team has been unable to obtain necessary approvals from Beirut Governorate and Municipality for waste management infrastructure, with local governance obstruction reflecting the broader institutional paralysis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-f112aa75-2e9c-4c03-ac0e-46311eb3089b.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – ISR</a>. The LEAP project ISR records persistent high political and governance risk with stakeholder engagement severely constrained by security conditions and institutional fragmentation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a>. The Solid Waste Management project ISR documents that fiduciary risk has escalated from substantial to high, with governance delays in fund transfers between the Ministry of Finance and Ministry of Environment reflecting inter-ministerial coordination failures rooted in sectarian political fragmentation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-4727dec5-5d09-4ce2-8055-7dcb271aae12.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – ISR</a>.
