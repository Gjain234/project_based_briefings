## Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

**1. Governance Consolidation and Democratic Backsliding**

Djibouti's removal of presidential age limits in October 2025—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms. This constitutional manipulation signals a governance trajectory toward indefinite executive rule, weakening the rule-of-law predictability that development projects depend upon. Across the portfolio, projects are susceptible because their governance architectures rely on high-level steering committees chaired by ministers operating under the Economic Development Council or equivalent executive bodies, with no independent oversight or civil society safeguards to insulate implementation from political capture [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf) [P509082 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf) [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf) [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes). The public finance management reform project is particularly exposed, as its mitigation strategy rests explicitly on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that weakens as political attention shifts toward consolidating power ahead of the 2026 election cycle [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf). These risks are materializing: ISR evidence across multiple projects documents persistent "High" or "Substantial" political and governance risk ratings, with steering committees inactive, ministerial decrees unpublished despite approval, and PFM governance structures not yet operationalized [P180696 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf) [P175483 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf) [P174300 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf). The constitutional revision has compounded pre-existing institutional fragility, creating an environment where executive consolidation supersedes institutional modernization—manifesting in stalled regulatory reforms, delayed board appointments for the telecommunications regulator (vacant since 2022), and the non-adoption of the Digital Code's telecommunications regulation despite repeated mission recommendations [P174461 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf) [P174461 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-a6aeb6a6-887c-41f9-8eb7-fb31c9e7d652.pdf).

**2. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Accountability Mechanisms**

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House, with journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested, has effectively eliminated the independent oversight ecosystem that development projects require for transparent implementation. This risk cuts across the entire portfolio: projects in TVET, public finance, urban development, food security, and corridor infrastructure all rely on grievance redress mechanisms, stakeholder engagement plans, and independent monitoring and evaluation systems that presuppose a functioning civic space [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf) [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf) [P162901 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes) [P178988 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf) [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes). The PFM reform project's Component 3—explicitly designed to "strengthen oversight of public finances and civil society engagement"—faces a fundamental implementation gap, as the country context shows only marginal independent voices remain and the civil society partners the component assumes can meaningfully participate do not exist in practice [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf). These risks are actively materializing across the portfolio. The TVET project's steering committee has been inactive, its internal audit function lacks organizational independence, and its GBV action plans and stakeholder mobilization plans remain unimplemented [P175483 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf). The corridor project documents systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards affecting 150 daily workers, with no independent actors to monitor or enforce compliance [P174300 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf). The food security project's complaint management mechanism remains incomplete, and environmental and social safeguard documents are transmitted to the Bank with persistent delays [P178988 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf). The GBV prevention project launched without accounting software in place and has not met financial reporting deadlines [P178332 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-4908093d-8bfd-4f36-a29c-d9575d229601.pdf). Across projects, the absence of independent civil society is not a background condition—it is an active constraint on implementation quality, accountability, and beneficiary protection.

**3. Severe Youth Unemployment, Economic Inequality, and Labor Market Absorption Constraints**

With youth unemployment exceeding 75% and endemic corruption compounding poverty and inequality, Djibouti's structural economic fragility creates both demand-side and supply-side constraints for the portfolio. The TVET project's core theory of change—training 23,775 graduates for private sector absorption—is directly undermined by a labor market that cannot absorb even the existing unemployed population; the PAD acknowledges macroeconomic risk as "substantial" but documents no labor market demand assessment or employer absorption capacity analysis [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf). The housing project targets youth in construction trades and low-to-middle income households, but the 80% youth unemployment rate constrains both beneficiary capacity to engage in self-construction financing and the broader housing market dynamics the project depends upon [P176772 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-38023395-68a8-4eb6-8dd3-05bbe5a8c9ed.pdf). These risks are materializing with particular severity. The TVET project's refugee employment outcome indicator is stuck at 5% against an 80% target, reflecting the structural inability of the host economy to absorb trained workers regardless of skills interventions [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf). The online gig work component has achieved zero results, suggesting that supply-side training cannot overcome demand-side constraints including limited digital infrastructure and low internet penetration [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf). Labor mobility agreements with Kuwait and Qatar—a key project strategy for addressing domestic unemployment—remain unimplemented, with no bilateral agreements signed [P175483 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf). The social protection project documents persistent difficulty identifying viable income-generating activities in remote localities due to weak market access and limited economic diversification [P508560 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf) [P180999 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf). Across the portfolio, economic fragility is not merely a contextual backdrop but an active constraint on project effectiveness, with workers on the corridor project accepting exploitative informal arrangements precisely because 75% youth unemployment eliminates their bargaining power [P174300 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf).

**4. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Under Resource Constraints**

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers, with 85% concentrated in refugee villages—while simultaneously managing recurrent droughts and severely limited fiscal resources. This hosting burden creates a structural mismatch between humanitarian obligations and institutional capacity that runs through the entire portfolio. The CERP emergency response project explicitly identifies SEGRC as having "limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff" while simultaneously tasking it with coordinating emergency response for a population that includes 32,000 displaced persons plus 150,000 non-registered migrants [P509082 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf). The TVET project targets 3,165 refugees directly but documents no contingency for how humanitarian conditions—food security, shelter, competing survival priorities—might undermine training participation and completion [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf). The education project targets 29,214 refugees across three villages for integration into the national system but acknowledges "external shocks" as a moderate protection risk without detailing how resource scarcity would affect programming continuity [P166059 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes). These risks are materializing comprehensively. The health project documents refugee immunization rates at 50% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5%—concrete evidence that resource-constrained public services cannot sustain basic preventive health for refugee populations [P178033 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf). The education project shows refugee student enrollment declining from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025, refugee schools not integrated into the national EMIS system, and zero implementation of recommendations from the 2017 National Refugee Law [P166059 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf) [P162901 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes). The food security project's June 2025 drought declaration triggered project restructuring, with food stock acquisition at only 50% of target and zero achievement on water infrastructure rehabilitation for 26 facilities [P178988 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf). The statistics project requires boosted sampling of migrant and refugee households in both the national census and household budget survey, reflecting the complexity that displacement dynamics impose on basic data collection [P171777 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-4b12f487-05cf-4472-bbf3-012c7cb41ce1.pdf). Across the portfolio, the refugee hosting context is not a peripheral concern—it is a primary driver of implementation gaps.

**5. Ethnic Tensions, Political Exclusion, and Structural Marginalization**

The structural dominance of the Issa Somali ethnic group (approximately 60% of the population) in governance, combined with the systematic political marginalization of the Afar minority (approximately 35%), creates a persistent risk of exclusionary implementation across all projects operating at national scale or in ethnically mixed regions. Projects are susceptible because beneficiary selection, institutional governance, and resource allocation mechanisms are designed without explicit ethnic representation safeguards. The TVET project's multi-ministry steering committee under the Economic Development Council lacks documented ethnic balance provisions, creating vulnerability to political interference in beneficiary selection given the Issa-dominated institutional landscape [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf). The PFM reform project's civil society engagement component assumes inclusive stakeholder participation but provides no mechanisms to ensure Afar or minority representation in the Public Finance Reform Management Unit or technical committees [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf). The agri-food project operates across three geographically dispersed zones without addressing the ethnic composition of target areas or mechanisms for equitable benefit distribution across Issa and Afar populations [P178836 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf). These risks are materializing in ways that are difficult to observe precisely because monitoring systems are themselves compromised. The corridor project documents persistent "Substantial" stakeholder engagement risk, with evidence suggesting that communities along the corridor face barriers to authentic participation—a dynamic consistent with ethnic exclusion dynamics where marginalized groups cannot safely voice concerns [P174300 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf). The social protection project documents opaque youth training program selection without pre-defined eligibility criteria or accessibility safeguards, creating a direct mechanism through which ethnic favoritism could operate in a context where Issa dominance shapes access to state resources [P508560 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf) [P180999 – Aide Memoire](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf). The urban development project identifies "tensions between host communities and refugees" as a substantial risk but does not address how the Issa-Afar political imbalance may shape community acceptance of refugee integration in ethnically mixed slum areas [P162901 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes). The education project's declining refugee enrollment and non-functional School Management Committees (62% functionality) may partially reflect ethnic power imbalances in local governance structures that limit meaningful participation by marginalized communities [P166059 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf). Across the portfolio, the absence of ethnic-disaggregated targeting criteria and monitoring mechanisms leaves projects structurally exposed to reproducing the exclusionary dynamics that characterize Djibouti's political landscape.
