**Paragraph 1: Severe Youth Unemployment and Economic Inequality**

Djibouti's youth unemployment rate exceeding 75%, compounded by endemic corruption, high poverty, and structurally limited economic opportunities, poses a pervasive threat to the portfolio. Multiple projects are directly exposed to this risk through their reliance on labor market absorption and income-generation mechanisms that the broader economy cannot sustain. The TVET and skills development project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> targets the enrollment of nearly 24,000 trainees across diploma, short-term, and work-based pathways, yet the PAD acknowledges macroeconomic risk as substantial while providing no labor market demand assessment or contingency for limited private sector absorption—a critical gap given that youth unemployment structurally constrains the very employment outcomes the project is designed to produce. The food security and emergency preparedness project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a> similarly depends on government staff availability and overtime mobilization during emergencies, but in a context of 75% youth unemployment and endemic corruption, institutional incentive structures and human capital availability are severely compromised. The corridor development project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> identifies road transport providers and local traders as beneficiaries but provides no youth employment targets or analysis of how infrastructure improvements will translate into jobs for the most economically excluded cohort. These forward-looking vulnerabilities are now materializing across the portfolio. The TVET project has failed to enroll refugees in training programs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a> and has achieved only 5% refugee employment outcomes against targets <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>, with the PAD's aspirational labor mobility agreements with Gulf states showing no progress <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>—reflecting the structural impossibility of supply-side training interventions overcoming demand-side economic collapse. The social protection project has been unable to identify viable income-generating activities in remote localities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>, with youth training programs lacking transparent selection criteria and risking concentration of benefits among already-privileged youth rather than the most economically excluded <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project has documented systematic exploitation of 150 daily workers through non-compliant contracts and absence of social security registration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>, a direct consequence of workers' near-total lack of bargaining power in a 75%-unemployment labor market. The GBV prevention project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a> faces capacity gaps in psychosocial counseling services, with economic inequality correlating with elevated GBV risk precisely when service delivery infrastructure is most constrained. Taken together, these realized risks confirm that the portfolio's employment and livelihood objectives are structurally undermined by an economy that cannot absorb trained workers, and that economic desperation is enabling labor exploitation and service delivery failures across multiple sectors.

**Paragraph 2: Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Amid Resource Constraints**

Djibouti's hosting of approximately 32,000 displaced persons—85% concentrated in refugee villages—amid recurrent droughts and severely limited public resources creates a systemic implementation risk across the portfolio, as projects targeting refugee populations encounter institutional capacity ceilings that the host country system cannot overcome. The health and nutrition project [P178033 | PAD] directly serves refugee populations concentrated in Ali-Addeh, Holl-Holl, and Markazi camps, but the PAD's mitigation strategy relies on dialogue with UNHCR without assessing how deteriorating humanitarian conditions or resource scarcity would affect service delivery continuity. The emergency food security project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a> explicitly targets refugees for food stock distribution but provides no allocation protocols between refugee villages and host communities, nor contingency plans for managing competing demands under resource scarcity. The statistics modernization project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a> requires boosted sampling of migrant and refugee households in both the national census and household budget survey, yet the PAD does not address how resource-constrained refugee village environments will affect data collection logistics or quality. These design vulnerabilities are translating into concrete implementation failures. The health project has achieved only 12% immunization coverage against an 80% target for refugee populations, with vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>—a direct manifestation of the resource constraints and fragile public health infrastructure that characterize Djibouti's refugee hosting environment. The food security project has achieved only 50% of its emergency food stock acquisition target and zero progress on water infrastructure rehabilitation for 26 facilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>, while a June 2025 drought declaration has forced reallocation of preparedness resources to emergency response, compounding the strain on systems already stretched by displacement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>. The urban development project has stalled on refugee village restructuring plans for Ali Addeh and Holl Holl <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>, failed to deliver vocational training certificates to refugees <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>, and recorded zero implementation by refugee-led civil society organizations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>—reflecting the institutional fragility and resource limitations that prevent meaningful refugee agency in development programming. The statistics project has encountered security concerns and population displacement challenges in refugee settlements during field enumeration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>, and INSTAD's concurrent survey overload—including a dedicated migrants and refugees survey—is compromising data quality across all operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the refugee hosting context is not a peripheral risk but a central implementation constraint, with resource ceilings consistently preventing projects from reaching their most vulnerable target populations.

**Paragraph 3: Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Media Freedom**

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House, with virtually no independent civil society remaining and journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested for criticizing the government, creates a structural governance deficit that undermines the accountability, transparency, and stakeholder engagement mechanisms on which the entire portfolio depends. Projects across sectors are designed with grievance redress mechanisms, independent monitoring systems, and civil society engagement components that presuppose an enabling environment that does not exist in practice. The PFM reform project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a> explicitly targets civil society engagement and citizen oversight of public finances through Component 3, yet the PAD does not acknowledge that "only marginal voices present on social media" and systematic harassment of critics make meaningful civil society participation structurally impossible—creating a fundamental implementation gap between design intent and country reality. The TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> relies on independent academic evaluation and confidential GRM reporting, but both mechanisms are vulnerable to government pressure and self-censorship in an environment where independent verification is absent. The corridor project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> establishes a Stakeholder Engagement Plan and grievance redress mechanisms without acknowledging that the country's "virtually no independent civil society" context renders these instruments ineffective for vulnerable groups, particularly women traders and communities along the corridor who cannot voice concerns without fear of retaliation. These design vulnerabilities are materializing as systemic implementation failures. The PFM project has sustained a "High" political and governance risk rating throughout implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>, with governance structures for PFM reform facing political resistance and the steering committee unable to function effectively <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>—dynamics directly enabled by the absence of independent oversight that would otherwise create accountability pressure. The digital connectivity project has seen the telecommunications regulatory authority unable to appoint board members since 2022 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a> and has failed to adopt the Digital Code's telecommunications regulation book despite repeated mission recommendations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-9e33bd55-6107-428e-bde8-2dc02b8b55cf.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>, with institutional paralysis persisting precisely because there is no independent civil society or media pressure to demand regulatory modernization. The corridor project has documented critical construction safety violations unreported in official government submissions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>, systematic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards affecting 150 daily workers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>, and persistent stakeholder engagement risk rated Substantial <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>—all enabled by the absence of independent monitoring actors who would otherwise expose and correct these violations. The GBV prevention project has launched without accounting systems in place <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>, with stalled NGO contract negotiations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a> and capacity gaps in psychosocial counseling <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a> reflecting the institutional hollowing that results when independent civil society organizations—which typically anchor GBV service delivery—have been systematically dismantled. The social protection project has recorded weak grievance mechanisms <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>, inadequate beneficiary participation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>, and opaque youth training selection procedures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> that risk reproducing structural exclusions without any independent mechanism to identify or challenge them. Across the portfolio, the collapse of independent civil society is not merely a contextual backdrop but an active implementation constraint, converting project accountability mechanisms into nominal structures that cannot function as designed.
