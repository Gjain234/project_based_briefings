# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Governance Consolidation and Democratic Backsliding

Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms. This political consolidation creates direct implementation risks across the portfolio by weakening the multi-ministerial coordination architectures that most projects depend upon. The TVET and skills development project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> relies on a Project Steering Committee under the Economic Development Council, an executive body, without independent oversight safeguards—making it structurally vulnerable to political capture as executive power concentrates further. The public finance management reform project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a> depends on a PFM Reform Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance, with mitigation strategies premised on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that weakens as political attention shifts toward succession management and constitutional consolidation. The corridor development project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> explicitly identifies that "ability to influence private sector finance mobilization depends on political will," a vulnerability that intensifies under indefinite presidential rule. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The PFM project is experiencing a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with administrative delays in publishing governance decrees blocking project operationalization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The TVET project faces an inactive steering committee and stalled inter-ministerial coordination <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>, while the corridor project carries a sustained "Substantial" political and governance risk rating <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The digital connectivity project has seen ARMD board appointments vacant since 2022, with the regulatory authority unable to adopt the Digital Code Book Two—a pattern consistent with institutional subordination to executive authority <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-9e33bd55-6107-428e-bde8-2dc02b8b55cf.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, formal institutional structures are being created to satisfy external requirements without genuine operational effectiveness, reflecting a governance model that prioritizes form over substance.

## 2. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Media Freedom

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House, with journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested, creates a structural implementation deficit across the entire portfolio. Projects designed with stakeholder engagement, grievance redress mechanisms, and community feedback loops are operating in an environment where these mechanisms cannot function independently. The education project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a> establishes a grievance mechanism "for all stakeholders" but does not acknowledge that vulnerable groups—refugees, rural students, girls—face barriers to voicing concerns in a politically repressive environment. The slum upgrading project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a> relies on community facilitators and participatory processes without acknowledging that Djibouti's "virtually no independent civil society" fundamentally constrains the authenticity of these mechanisms. The statistics modernization project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a> explicitly depends on "systematic citizen engagement interventions" as a political risk mitigation strategy—yet the country context makes this approach structurally unavailable. These risks are extensively realized across the portfolio. The TVET project shows a compromised internal audit function lacking organizational independence, stalled GBV action plans, and delayed stakeholder mobilization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project documents systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards affecting 150 daily workers, with no independent actors to monitor or enforce compliance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The GBV prevention project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a> shows stalled NGO contract negotiations, capacity gaps in psychosocial counseling networks, missing accounting systems at project launch, and delayed financial reporting—all reflecting the institutional hollowing that accompanies collapsed civil society oversight. The education project shows only 62% functionality of School Management Committees and private Arabic schools refusing to integrate into the national EMIS system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>, while the slum upgrading project documents stalled refugee-led CSO participation with zero sub-projects implemented <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The digital connectivity project faces persistent high stakeholder engagement risk and procurement delays attributable to weak institutional accountability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>.

## 3. Ethnic Tensions and Political Exclusion

The structural dominance of the Issa Somali ethnic group (approximately 60% of the population) in governance, combined with the systematic political marginalization of the Afar minority (approximately 35%), creates implementation risks wherever projects involve beneficiary targeting, resource allocation, or community participation across ethnically diverse areas. The TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> targets beneficiaries through a multi-ministry coordination mechanism without explicit ethnic representation safeguards in steering committees, creating vulnerability to political interference in beneficiary selection. The emergency food security project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a> operates across six regions without addressing how ethnic composition of target areas might affect equitable distribution of water, food, and agricultural inputs. The corridor project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> lists "populations living in corridor area" as beneficiaries without disaggregation by ethnicity or targeted inclusion mechanisms for the Afar minority, despite the corridor traversing ethnically mixed territory. The social protection project [P508560 | PAD] faces risks that opaque beneficiary selection for youth training programs could reproduce structural ethnic inequalities, with Afar youth potentially excluded from economic opportunity programs. These risks are materializing in observable implementation patterns. The corridor project shows persistent stakeholder engagement difficulties rated "Substantial," with ethnic tensions plausibly contributing to coordination failures and trust deficits with communities along the route <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The slum upgrading project documents a delayed refugee integration and social cohesion assessment, preventing early identification of whether refugee settlement patterns are exacerbating existing ethnic fault lines <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The social protection project shows weak grievance mechanisms and inadequate beneficiary participation, with ethnic marginalization of the Afar minority creating barriers to inclusive engagement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The education project shows non-functional School Management Committees that may reflect ethnic power imbalances in local governance structures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>, while the TVET project documents disability enrollment tracking gaps that mirror broader patterns of institutional exclusion of marginalized groups <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>.

## 4. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Amid Resource Constraints

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers—with 85% concentrated in refugee villages, alongside an estimated 150,000 non-registered migrants. This hosting burden, sustained amid recurrent droughts and severely limited public resources, creates compounding implementation risks for projects that either directly target refugee populations or depend on government institutional capacity that is already strained by humanitarian obligations. The health project [P178033 | PAD] targets refugee immunization but operates through government health systems already stretched by displacement pressures. The TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> targets 3,165 refugees concentrated in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps in Ali Sabieh region, with security risk mitigation limited to "institutional protocols" without addressing how resource scarcity affects training participation and completion. The emergency food security project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a> explicitly targets refugees for emergency food stock distribution but does not specify allocation protocols between refugee villages and host populations or contingency plans for competing demands. The education project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a> acknowledges "external shocks that could harm the country's proactive participation on refugee issues" as a moderate protection risk, but does not assess how recurrent droughts would affect refugee education programming. These risks are extensively realized. The health project shows refugee immunization rates stuck at 50% against an 80% target, vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5% among refugee children, and a 12% immunization rate against an 80% target for a specific refugee health indicator <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The TVET project shows zero refugee enrollment in TVET programs against a target of 220, zero refugee employment outcomes against a 5% target, and persistent failure to track refugee employment data <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The education project shows declining refugee student enrollment from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025, with refugee schools not integrated into the national EMIS system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The slum upgrading project documents stalled property rights documentation in refugee villages, zero implementation by refugee-led CSOs, and zero implementation of refugee law recommendations from the 2017 National Refugee Law <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The emergency food security project shows critical shortfalls in food stock acquisition (50% of target), zero achievement on water infrastructure rehabilitation, and a June 2025 drought declaration triggering project restructuring <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Domestic Security Threats from Insurgent Activity

The FRUD-armé splinter group's October 2023 raid on an army post—killing seven soldiers and injuring four—demonstrates active domestic insurgent capacity that creates direct operational risks for projects with field activities, rural infrastructure, or remote beneficiary populations. The proximity of project implementation areas to zones of potential insurgent activity, combined with the broader terrorism threat from al-Shabaab in neighboring Somalia, creates security exposure that most project designs have underestimated. The TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> operates in Ali Sabieh and Obock regions geographically proximate to FRUD-armé activity, with 3,165 refugee beneficiaries concentrated in camps in these areas; the PAD rates security risk as "moderate" without a specific threat assessment for these locations. The corridor project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a> identifies "security/fragility (substantial)" risk from regional instability but relies only on "regular security risk assessments" without specific protocols for FRUD-armé threats to road rehabilitation sites or border operations at Guelileh. The agri-food development project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a> operates in the Northern Zone (Tadjourah and Obock regions) with climate-proof rural infrastructure including boreholes and rural roads, while asserting "no security risks identified for project implementation"—an assessment that does not account for FRUD-armé's demonstrated capability in northern areas. The statistics project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a> requires field enumeration teams to access communities across national territory for the population census and household surveys, with the PAD acknowledging "road safety concerns during census activities" but not addressing the specific threat from active insurgent groups. These risks are beginning to materialize in implementation. The statistics project documents security concerns in refugee settlements as a barrier to survey implementation, with field enumeration tensions creating operational challenges <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>. The social protection project faces difficult road access in rural regions that compounds security-related access constraints, with logistical delays in cash and in-kind transfer distributions to remote areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project shows persistent safety violations on construction sites, inadequate HSE oversight, and road safety risks that reflect the broader security and accountability environment in which field operations are conducted <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>.
