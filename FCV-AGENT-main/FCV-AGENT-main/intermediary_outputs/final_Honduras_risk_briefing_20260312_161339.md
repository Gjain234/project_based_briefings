# Honduras FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Contested Political Transition, Deep Polarization, and Governance Vacuum

Honduras entered 2026 under a new administration with a profound legitimacy deficit. President Asfura's razor-thin electoral victory was marred by fraud allegations, a delayed vote count, an explosive device attack inside the Legislative Palace, and the outgoing President Castro's attempt at an illegal recount. The incoming National Party holds only 49 of 128 congressional seats, requiring coalition support for any major legislation, while the opposition LIBRE party has refused to accept the result. Civil society protests on inauguration day were met with tear gas. Forty days into the administration, the National Autonomous University of Honduras publicly noted the absence of formal Health Ministry leadership and a lack of concrete governance signals across sectors. Across the portfolio, PAD evidence consistently identifies political and governance risk as Substantial, with projects flagging risks of delayed congressional approvals, shifts in government priorities following the November 2025 election, and challenges securing project inclusion in official budget allocations [P507958 | PAD; P181166 | PAD; P174328 | PAD; P175977 | PAD; P152057 | PAD; P181324 | PAD; P173125 | PAD; P169901 | PAD; P169161 | PAD; P176532 | PAD]. These risks are now materializing. Congressional ratification delays are blocking project effectiveness for at least one operation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P179909-3ce23136-04a2-44c0-b91e-572ac2bcbfd0.pdf" target="_blank">P179909 – ISR</a>. The transition from Castro to Asfura has directly disrupted implementation timelines, with one project's Spanish co-financier raising concerns about paralysis or cancellation during the electoral cycle <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181166-4f02f082-62f7-4fd1-ac62-d8e38db5ccef.pdf" target="_blank">P181166 – Aide Memoire</a>. Budget modifications and institutional restructuring are delaying measurement consultancies and construction contracts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169161-2969ae1d-d89f-4b4e-9f1d-6d5a59c1b7d6.pdf" target="_blank">P169161 – Aide Memoire</a>. Cash transfer payment disruptions during the transition echo a pattern documented in 2021 <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088251625&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – ISR</a>. The combination of a minority government, unresolved electoral legitimacy, and institutional inertia across ministries creates a compounding governance vacuum that is now visibly constraining project execution across the portfolio.

## 2. Systemic Corruption, Institutional Impunity, and Fiduciary Exposure

Honduras is classified as a "highly defective democracy" by the Bertelsmann Transformation Index, with structural impunity, institutional capture, and a judiciary that is under-resourced and affected by corruption and intimidation. President Asfura himself faces an ongoing embezzlement and money-laundering investigation from his tenure as mayor of Tegucigalpa. The internationally backed anti-corruption commission (CICIH) pledged under the Castro administration was never established, leaving structural impunity intact. Multiple projects in the portfolio carry Substantial or High fiduciary risk ratings, with PADs noting that implementing agencies—including SIT, RNP, SESAL, INVEST-H, and the Ministry of Education—lack prior World Bank project experience, have limited financial management capacity, and operate within weak governance structures [P507958 | PAD; P181324 | PAD; P176532 | PAD; P174328 | PAD; P169161 | PAD; P173125 | PAD; P175977 | PAD; P152057 | PAD]. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. Audit compliance failures and incomplete KPMG reports on cash transfer payments have been flagged <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P152057-94d3fde8-d108-42e4-b41e-08031cf57c23.pdf" target="_blank">P152057 – Aide Memoire</a>. Inter-institutional agreements with SESAL and SEDUC remain unsigned, with informal workarounds substituting for formal contracting <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P152057-94d3fde8-d108-42e4-b41e-08031cf57c23.pdf" target="_blank">P152057 – Aide Memoire</a>. Beneficiary selection governance gaps—including the exclusion of 17,000 households without formal committee authorization—have been identified <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P152057-94d3fde8-d108-42e4-b41e-08031cf57c23.pdf" target="_blank">P152057 – Aide Memoire</a>. Critical data security vulnerabilities in project management systems, including SQL injection exposure and absence of automatic backups, create conditions for undetected misappropriation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174328-9bad92a5-4a40-4385-a927-1a6e1543d574.pdf" target="_blank">P174328 – Aide Memoire</a>. Environmental licensing delays of over eight months at SERNA reflect the same institutional paralysis and potential capture affecting regulatory bodies [P174328 | Aide Memoire; P170469 | Aide Memoire]. The termination of approximately $167 million in U.S. governance and anti-corruption aid has removed a significant layer of external accountability, leaving projects more exposed to the structural impunity that pervades Honduras's public administration [P507958 | PAD; P174328 | PAD; P152057 | PAD].

## 3. Gang Violence, Organized Crime, and Drug Trafficking in Project Areas

Honduras's security landscape is defined by the expansion of organized crime into rural departments, the presence of over 63 criminal groups beyond MS-13 and Barrio 18, and extortion affecting more than 300,000 households nationally. Violence in Colón and Olancho departments increased 27% and 44% respectively during the Castro administration, driven by drug trafficking and land disputes. The state of emergency ended on January 26, 2026, and the police force has actually shrunk from 18,047 to 17,436 officers since 2022. Critically, Honduras is transitioning from a cocaine transit country to a producer, with coca cultivation and drug laboratories now documented in Colón, Olancho, Atlántida, Yoro, and Gracias a Dios departments—the precise geography of multiple portfolio projects. PADs for road corridor, agricultural, health, and social protection projects all acknowledge that project areas overlap with zones of organized crime, drug trafficking, and weak state presence [P507958 | PAD; P174328 | PAD; P176532 | PAD; P175977 | PAD; P168385 | PAD; P169161 | PAD]. The CA-13 corridor project explicitly identifies the Bajo Aguán region as affected by violent land disputes, organized crime, and drug trafficking, with a labor influx of over 4,000 workers in remote areas with limited supervision <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-16ccfa88-c5fd-4b6a-acfc-b69c54b5e6ac.pdf" target="_blank">P507958 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing. In the Moskitia and Atlantic Coast regions, COMRURAL II reports zero progress on Component 5 activities, with low local recruitment response and indigenous communities resisting project agreements—dynamics consistent with criminal territorial control and community fragmentation driven by gang violence [P174328 | Aide Memoire; P174328 | Implementation Status and Results Report]. School violence prevention training has been incorporated into the early childhood education project as a direct institutional response to gang presence in target departments <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169161-307bd03a-1abe-4fa6-827d-521a77f09929.pdf" target="_blank">P169161 – ISR</a>. The health project's priority regions—including Colón, Olancho, Atlántida, Yoro, and Gracias a Dios—are simultaneously the country's primary drug trafficking and coca cultivation zones, yet PADs lack conflict-sensitive operational protocols for staff safety and supply chain security in criminal-controlled areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-b7e241ff-57b5-48f8-bf67-ec450fd3e23a.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – PAD</a>.

## 4. Internal Displacement, Mixed Migration, and Remittance Shock

Honduras faces a compounding displacement and economic vulnerability crisis. Criminal groups in rural departments seize land, destroy crops, and forcibly displace families to secure trafficking routes and production zones. Approximately 1.6 million people face IPC Phase 3+ food insecurity, driven by prolonged drought in the Dry Corridor. Simultaneously, the Trump administration terminated Temporary Protected Status for approximately 72,000 Honduran beneficiaries, and deportations are projected to equal or exceed 2025 levels of roughly 100,000 across Central America. Remittances—representing 26–30% of GDP—are projected to contract by 3–5% in 2026, implying approximately 1% GDP contraction and 1.5% decline in private consumption. The Asfura government has compounded this by agreeing to receive third-country asylum seekers transferred by the U.S. and to repatriate Venezuelan nationals via Soto Cano Air Base. PADs across the portfolio acknowledge macroeconomic vulnerability and displacement risks, but most were prepared before the TPS termination and deportation wave, leaving beneficiary co-financing assumptions, labor market absorption projections, and social protection coverage estimates outdated [P507958 | PAD; P174328 | PAD; P152057 | PAD; P181324 | PAD; P176532 | PAD; P173125 | PAD; P169161 | PAD]. These risks are materializing. The social protection project suspended rural cash transfers during H1 2024 and has not resumed urban transfers since 2022, removing critical safety nets precisely when remittance shocks and deportation-driven labor market saturation are intensifying <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088251625&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – ISR</a>. The reversion from universal rural transfer coverage to selective targeting in 2024 has reduced protection for populations in the Dry Corridor facing acute food insecurity <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088251625&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – ISR</a>. The civil registry project faces a surge in demand from deportees and returnees requiring identification services, while RNP's governance structures and PIU staffing remain incomplete <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181324-909b5b4e-f005-43e1-a600-77e4ad8ea6e1.pdf" target="_blank">P181324 – ISR</a>. In the agricultural project, indigenous communities in the Moskitia—already experiencing displacement from criminal land seizures—are resisting project agreements, reflecting rational risk assessment in an environment where state protection is absent <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174328-9bad92a5-4a40-4385-a927-1a6e1543d574.pdf" target="_blank">P174328 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Public Health System Collapse and Cross-Sectoral Service Delivery Failure

Honduras's public health system is described by a joint World Bank, PAHO, and Lancet assessment as exhibiting deep structural weaknesses that leave it highly vulnerable to crisis. At the time of Asfura's inauguration, thousands of patients were on surgery waiting lists and medicine shortages were acute. No formal Health Ministry leadership had been appointed 40 days into the administration. On March 2, 2026, Asfura terminated the Cuba medical cooperation agreement under apparent U.S. pressure, and 168 Cuban doctors departed on March 4, removing a significant healthcare workforce from underserved communities. The IHSS—covering roughly one-third of the public health system—continues to suffer long waits, medicine shortages, and inadequate care with no structural reforms in sight. The health project (P176532) directly confronts this crisis: its PAD identifies Substantial macroeconomic and institutional capacity risks, noting that the government's commitment to absorb 30% additional health workers on SESAL payroll will limit budget available for required health system investments <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-b7e241ff-57b5-48f8-bf67-ec450fd3e23a.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – PAD</a>. These risks are now materializing in measurable outcome deterioration. Immunization coverage is declining across health regions, with the systemic collapse of supply chains, staffing, and institutional leadership directly undermining routine vaccination delivery <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176532-b7cbc274-26aa-491e-bb45-ffebf0abfed8.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – ISR</a>. Early antenatal care attendance has regressed, reflecting the structural barriers preventing pregnant women from accessing timely prenatal services in a system losing workforce capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176532-b7cbc274-26aa-491e-bb45-ffebf0abfed8.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – ISR</a>. Implementation delays stemming from micro-planning failures within SESAL units are documented, with the 2.5-month lag between congressional approval and project effectiveness reflecting institutional paralysis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176532-b7cbc274-26aa-491e-bb45-ffebf0abfed8.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – ISR</a>. Beyond the health project, the pattern of institutional collapse is cross-sectoral: the social protection project cannot secure SESAL's signature on cooperation agreements <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P152057-94d3fde8-d108-42e4-b41e-08031cf57c23.pdf" target="_blank">P152057 – Aide Memoire</a>, the civil registry project's birth registration component depends on health facility coordination that is now severely compromised <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-26698d51-7b79-45d6-aa2e-9655368e9797.pdf" target="_blank">P181324 – PAD</a>, and the recovery project's health facility reconstruction activities face a health system unable to staff or supply rebuilt facilities <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08810980a&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P175977 – PAD</a>. The departure of Cuban doctors and the governance vacuum in the Health Ministry represent a realized, acute constraint on the entire portfolio's enabling environment.
