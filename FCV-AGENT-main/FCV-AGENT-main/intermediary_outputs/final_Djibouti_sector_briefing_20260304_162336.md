# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing: Sector-Aligned Risk Assessment

---

**1. Governance and Public Financial Management**

The governance and public financial management (PFM) cluster represents the most structurally exposed sector in the Djibouti portfolio, operating against a backdrop of deepening political centralization, collapsed independent civil society, and persistent institutional fragility. The October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential term limits (DJ-001) directly threatens the multi-ministerial coordination architectures that PFM and digital governance projects depend upon, while the virtual absence of independent civil society (DJ-004) hollows out the accountability mechanisms these projects are designed to strengthen. The PFM Modernization Project explicitly identifies political risk as "High" and relies on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes" as its primary mitigation—a strategy that becomes increasingly fragile as executive power consolidates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The Digital Finance and Numerique (DFN) project similarly depends on regulatory reform through the ARMD authority, but the Digital Code (Book Two) governing telecommunications remains unadopted, leaving the regulatory body without its primary operational instrument <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088b5f99a&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174461 – PAD</a>. These design vulnerabilities have fully materialized in implementation: the PFM project cannot achieve legal effectiveness because the government has not signed and published required governance decrees in the Official Gazette despite Council of Ministers confirmation in February 2025, with delays cascading across all donor-funded PFM projects in Djibouti <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The DFN project's Component 2 is rated Modérément Insatisfaisante due to activity implementation delays, grievance redress mechanisms remain non-operationalized, and environmental and social quarterly reports for Q1 and Q2 2024 were not submitted by their deadlines <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-a6aeb6a6-887c-41f9-8eb7-fb31c9e7d652.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Statistics and Economic Management project (P171777) maintains a persistent "Substantial" political and governance risk rating across all review periods, with macroeconomic modeling progress slowed by staffing constraints and institutional capacity strain from multiple concurrent data collection activities threatening INSTAD's ability to maintain data quality <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-4b12f487-05cf-4472-bbf3-012c7cb41ce1.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across this cluster, the pattern is consistent: governance reforms that require institutional autonomy, civil society engagement, and multi-ministerial consensus are being implemented in an environment structurally hostile to all three conditions.

**2. Infrastructure and Connectivity**

The infrastructure and connectivity cluster—anchored by the Djibouti-Addis Corridor Project (P174300) and the digital connectivity investments under DFN/EARDIP (P174461)—faces compounding risks from regional security threats, diplomatic instability, and realized governance failures in project site management. The corridor project operates across RN1, RN5, and RN19 routes and the Guelileh border crossing in a context where FRUD-armé demonstrated lethal operational capacity in October 2023 (DJ-005), and where the escalating Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis (DJ-006) creates direct uncertainty for cross-border customs cooperation with Ethiopia <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The PAD identifies "security/fragility (substantial)" risk but mitigation relies only on "regular security risk assessments" without specific protocols for domestic insurgent threats to road rehabilitation sites or border operations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. Political and governance risk has remained at Substantial across all assessment periods for the corridor project, and stakeholder risks are similarly rated Substantial throughout, indicating persistent coordination tensions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. These design gaps have translated into severe realized implementation failures: field visits in April 2025 identified the absence of dedicated HSE agents, numerous unsecured trenches, inadequate separation between machinery and vulnerable road users, and inadequate nighttime signaling—all creating serious accident risks for workers and communities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. One hundred fifty daily laborers lack proper employment contracts, social security registration, and face deplorable working conditions including excessive heat exposure without adequate shelter, with significant inconsistencies in accident records <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. Government quarterly reports omit non-conformities observed in the field, indicating weak monitoring capacity or willingness to report implementation failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. For digital connectivity, institutional coordination delays in identifying beneficiary sites for internet capacity pre-purchase and slow inter-agency coordination between ANSIE, MCPT, and the Technical Committee are compounding the legislative blockage from the unadopted Digital Code <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-a6aeb6a6-887c-41f9-8eb7-fb31c9e7d652.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor's high road accident rate—thirteen traffic incidents unrelated to work zones since project start—and inaccurate geographic positioning of accident data further undermine the project's safety management capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**3. Social Protection and Food Security**

The social protection and food security cluster—comprising the PURCSA emergency food security project (P178988), the Filets Sociaux projects (P508560, P180999), and the CERP contingent emergency response (P509082)—operates at the intersection of Djibouti's most acute structural vulnerabilities: 75% youth unemployment (DJ-002), 32,000 displaced persons straining public services (DJ-009), recurrent drought, and institutional capacity deficits in the primary implementing agencies. The CERP PAD identifies institutional risk as "High" due to SEGRC's limited capacity from lack of funding and staff, creating a structural mismatch between the project's ambition to serve refugee and migrant populations and the baseline capacity of implementing institutions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The PURCSA project's design acknowledged macroeconomic risk as "substantial" and identified procurement-dependent emergency food stock mechanisms, but did not adequately account for the governance friction between project implementation and national procurement authorities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. These vulnerabilities have materialized comprehensively: in June 2025, the government declared a drought emergency that prompted an official restructuring request and suspension of PURCSA's main activities, with SDR 3,925,000 reallocated from the Food Shortage Preparedness Fund to the Emergency Food Stock component—reducing future preparedness capacity precisely when it is most needed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>. Prior to the drought declaration, the project's disbursement rate stood at only 15.2% one year before project closure, with the mid-term review postponed due to insufficient results <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. Strategic food stock procurement spent ten weeks in the procurement cycle, with the project requiring ministerial intervention to prioritize emergency files at the National Public Procurement Commission <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The complaint management mechanism for sexual exploitation and abuse was only 53% operationalized, and the project lacked a dedicated GBV specialist <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. For the Filets Sociaux projects, difficult road conditions during rainy seasons are causing logistical delays in in-kind transfer distribution, rural communities face constraints in identifying viable income-generating activities due to weak market access, and community grievance mechanisms present structural weaknesses limiting effective beneficiary response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. Rural savings and credit groups face governance and financial management capacity constraints requiring sustained support <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**4. Education, Skills, and Human Capital**

The education and skills cluster—spanning the TVET and Skills Development Project (P175483), the Inclusive Education Project (P166059), and the urban livelihoods project (P162901)—faces a distinctive risk profile shaped by the intersection of 75% youth unemployment (DJ-002), ethnic tensions affecting inclusive targeting (DJ-003), refugee hosting pressures (DJ-009), and the absence of independent civil society constraining accountability mechanisms (DJ-004). The TVET project's employment outcomes depend on private sector absorption of nearly 24,000 trained graduates, but no labor market demand assessment or private sector capacity analysis was documented in the PAD, and bilateral labor agreements and labor mobility programs remain aspirational without evidence of employer demand <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The Inclusive Education Project's integration of refugee education into the national system creates potential flashpoints if ethnic tensions escalate, given that resource allocation to refugee education could become politically contested in a context of Issa dominance and Afar marginalization <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. These design vulnerabilities have produced a cluster of realized implementation failures that are particularly acute for the most vulnerable beneficiaries: zero refugees have been enrolled or completed TVET training programs despite targets of 220 and 800 respectively, zero persons with disabilities have been enrolled despite targets of 47 and 160, and zero refugees have been engaged in apprenticeships or work-based learning despite a target of 180 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The online gig work initiative targeting 500 individuals is being deleted during restructuring due to significant delays and coordination failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The project steering committee remains inactive, the GBV action plan has not been transmitted despite training in June 2024, and the internal audit function is compromised by a conflict of interest in which the auditor reports to the coordinator whose processes they are mandated to review <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. For inclusive education, refugee enrollment in primary education shows a declining trend—from 596 in 2021-22 to 384 in 2024-25 (pending verification)—against a target of 37,000 out-of-school children, and only 62% of School Management Committees are functional <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The urban livelihoods project (P162901) shows zero refugee-led CSO sub-projects implemented against a target of 60, zero property access certificates delivered to refugees pending village restructuring plan approvals, and zero recommendations from sectoral action plans for the 2017 National Refugee Law implemented <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>.

**5. Health and Gender-Based Violence Response**

The health and GBV cluster—anchored by the health systems strengthening project (P178033) and the GBV prevention project (P178332)—operates in a context where coordination and planning deficiencies, refugee data gaps, and governance fragility directly undermine service delivery to the most vulnerable populations. The health project's design acknowledged political and governance risk as "Moderate" at approval, but this assessment did not adequately account for the structural coordination challenges inherent in Djibouti's fragmented multi-ministry health governance environment, nor the specific barriers to serving refugee populations (DJ-009) in a context where independent civil society cannot validate service delivery claims (DJ-004) <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088da7dd6&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P178033 – PAD</a>. The GBV project's design relied on NGO partnerships for community mobilization in a civic space where independent organizations face systematic harassment (DJ-004), and did not detail contingency arrangements for the procurement delays that are endemic across the Djibouti portfolio [P178332 | PAD]. These vulnerabilities have materialized in implementation: the health project's political and governance risk rating escalated from Moderate at approval to Substantial by April 2025, reflecting worsening governance fragility affecting project implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. Continued difficulties with coordination, planning, and outreach are impacting immunization service delivery and data quality, and disaggregated data for refugee populations receiving immunization services remains unavailable across multiple indicators <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. For the GBV project, negotiations with two NGOs for community mobilization against gender-based violence remain stalled at contract negotiation stage, with only 17 months remaining until project closure—reducing the available window to measure impact of prevention and response activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-4908093d-8bfd-4f36-a29c-d9575d229601.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. The first environmental and social monitoring report covering April 2023 to April 2024 remains outstanding, and the project lacks a dedicated GBV specialist, with the social specialist assuming this role despite identified capacity gaps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-4908093d-8bfd-4f36-a29c-d9575d229601.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across both projects, the pattern of weak grievance mechanisms, absent independent civil society oversight, and refugee data collection gaps reflects a systemic accountability deficit that the portfolio has not yet adequately addressed.
