# Sudan FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Active Multi-Front War and Its Direct Disruption to Project Operations

Sudan's ongoing war between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) has entered its fourth year with no resolution in sight, with Kordofan and Darfur emerging as the primary epicentres of escalating hostilities. Drone strikes on hospitals—including the March 2026 attack on Dilling General Hospital—and sustained aerial bombardment of El Obeid over five consecutive days illustrate the indiscriminate nature of violence in areas where the portfolio is most concentrated. Three projects carry direct exposure to this risk. The health system strengthening project explicitly identifies Darfur and Greater Kordofan as priority implementation areas, with the PAD acknowledging that 75% of health facilities in conflict-affected areas are already non-functional and that ongoing security risks from armed groups, extortion, and looting of project materials are central operational challenges <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The emergency safety net project targets West Kordofan and South Kordofan as implementation locations, with the PAD identifying active fighting, grand robbery, and looting of life-saving aid by soldiers as central implementation challenges <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The resilience and livelihoods project, operating in states currently assessed as neutral, nonetheless acknowledges that approximately 30% of Sudan's counties are directly affected by conflict and explicitly plans for possible location shifts if escalation reaches target areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing: implementation reporting for the health project documents that shifting frontlines are continuously forcing adaptation of facility targeting and supply chain approaches, with territorial changes creating unpredictable access constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The education project's Aide Memoire confirms that school closures driven directly by active conflict are preventing grant distribution and learning material delivery, with a Security Management Framework still pending clearance—a signal that unresolved security threats continue to block operational normalization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Implementation reporting for the safety net project similarly records escalating environment and social risks tied directly to active hostilities in project zones <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>.

## 2. Deliberate Obstruction of Humanitarian Access Paralyzing Service Delivery

Both warring parties are wilfully obstructing aid movement, attacking humanitarian workers and local volunteers, and restricting access to conflict-affected populations in violation of international humanitarian law. Drone strikes on WFP operations since early February 2026 and the UN Security Council's condemnation of starvation as a weapon of war underscore that obstruction is systematic and deliberate, not incidental. This risk cuts across the entire portfolio. The health project's PAD identifies a three-tiered service delivery model designed to adapt to changing accessibility, but this model presupposes some level of humanitarian access that warring parties are actively denying; the PAD also acknowledges that project supervision is challenging due to insecurity and that supply chains for medications, fuel, and materials are directly vulnerable to obstruction <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The safety net project's PAD acknowledges high security risk from military operations and violence toward aid workers, as well as bureaucratic impediments and politicization of aid, with mitigation relying on flexible geographic targeting and frontline worker autonomy—approaches that cannot substitute for physical access <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The resilience project's PAD notes reliance on UN humanitarian supply routes and acknowledges that restricted supply of goods if conflict escalates is a fiduciary risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the health project's ISR records that constrained access to health facilities and beneficiaries is forcing continuous operational recalibration, with the project unable to maintain consistent facility targeting <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The education project's Aide Memoire documents that limited access to schools is directly preventing school grant distribution, with conflict-related factors cited as the primary constraint <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The safety net project's ISR records that obstruction is directly impairing cash transfer delivery, with macroeconomic collapse reducing government capacity to maintain payment systems and forcing reliance on informal channels that warring parties can more easily intercept <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>.

## 3. De Facto State Fragmentation and Governance Vacuum Undermining Institutional Foundations

The RSF's proclamation of a parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" (Tasis State) in Darfur in August 2025, combined with the SAF-aligned government's return to Khartoum, has created competing governance authorities, legitimacy vacuums, and fragmented institutional capacity across the country. The African Union's refusal to recognize SAF chief al-Burhan as head of state deepens the legitimacy crisis and complicates any future governance framework. All portfolio projects are exposed to this structural risk. The health project's PAD identifies federal institutions as non-functional due to conflict and notes that reliance on state and locality-level institutions—which may have limited capacity—is the primary coordination strategy; the project explicitly avoids channeling funds through federal or state authorities, working instead through UN agencies and NGOs, but this does not resolve the underlying governance fragmentation affecting local institutional capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The safety net project operates under OP 7.30 (Dealings with De Facto Governments) and acknowledges that the government apparatus has relocated to Port Sudan with significant civil servant displacement, but does not specify how the project will navigate dual governance in RSF-controlled Darfur <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The resilience project identifies federal institutions as non-functional and relies on state and locality-level authorities that are operational but resource-constrained, without addressing the scenario where de facto fragmentation extends to target states <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. This risk is actively materializing across the portfolio: the health project's ISR records high political and governance risk as a realized implementation constraint, with the RSF's parallel administration and SAF-controlled institutions creating competing authorities that undermine coherent project coordination <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The safety net project's ISR documents political and governance instability as a high realized risk, with dual governance structures creating unclear jurisdictional boundaries and fragmented institutional legitimacy <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The energy and digital infrastructure project's ISR records that sector policy instability—driven directly by the absence of a unified national authority capable of establishing coherent policies—is a high realized risk, with competing power centers issuing conflicting directives <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>.

## 4. Famine Conditions and Macroeconomic Collapse Eroding Project Effectiveness

Famine (IPC Phase 5) has been confirmed in El Fasher (North Darfur) and Kadugli (South Kordofan), with 20 additional areas across Greater Darfur and Greater Kordofan projected at risk through 2026. Acute malnutrition rates exceeding 53% among children under five in Um Baru and approximately one-third in Kernoi signal catastrophic humanitarian deterioration in core project geographies. Simultaneously, hyperinflation at 211% (October 2024), currency depreciation, and a 20% economic contraction in 2023 are eroding the real value of any cash-based intervention. The health project's PAD identifies Darfur and Kordofan as priority locations and includes a severe acute malnutrition management component, but does not explicitly address famine-specific operational constraints or assess whether confirmed Phase 5 conditions will overwhelm project capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The safety net project targets IPC Phase 3 and 4 areas in Northern West Darfur, East Darfur, West Kordofan, and South Kordofan, with a cash transfer design providing US$10 per person per month intended to cover 50% of daily caloric needs—an amount that may be wholly inadequate if famine conditions worsen or if hyperinflation erodes purchasing power faster than anticipated <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The resilience project's food security component targets agricultural production in states not currently listed as famine zones, but the projected spread of famine to 20 additional areas creates risk that target areas could deteriorate into Phase 5 conditions during implementation, requiring a pivot to emergency response beyond project scope <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the safety net project's ISR records macroeconomic instability as a high realized risk, with hyperinflation and currency collapse directly undermining the purchasing power of cash transfer beneficiaries in a context of confirmed famine <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The resilience project's ISR documents that food security interventions targeting IDPs and host communities are being undermined by famine-driven social tensions and competing survival priorities that reduce community engagement capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>.

## 5. Mass Displacement Crisis Fragmenting Beneficiary Populations and Institutional Capacity

Sudan hosts the world's largest displacement crisis, with approximately 14 million people forcibly displaced since April 2023, including 7.3 million internally displaced persons and 4.2 million refugees in neighboring countries. Approximately 2.6 million displaced Sudanese have returned to areas such as Khartoum, Sennar, and Al Jazirah, often driven by hardship rather than safety, finding destroyed infrastructure and absent services. This crisis is simultaneously a targeting challenge, an institutional capacity challenge, and a social cohesion challenge for the portfolio. The health project's PAD explicitly targets 11.16 million displaced persons as primary beneficiaries but acknowledges that security challenges and population mobility may compromise beneficiary identification, and that frequent population movements fleeing conflict areas undermine the assumption of stable or predictable IDP populations on which fixed PHC facilities and mobile teams depend <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The safety net project targets 602,000 beneficiaries across 120,000 households with significant IDP populations, but acknowledges that widespread displacement may lead to social tensions between IDPs and host communities and that rapid displacement shifts—such as the 8,000 displaced from Kernoi in January 2026—could render biometric registration systems and beneficiary location verification obsolete during the 12-month transfer period <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The resilience project targets 560,000 IDP and host community individuals and acknowledges the risk of increased IDP mobility to project areas, relying on rapid local assessments during implementation rather than pre-implementation baseline stability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio: the resilience project's ISR records that IDP and host community social cohesion is a high realized risk, with resource competition between displaced and host populations intensifying as displacement-driven scarcity deepens <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The education project's ISR and Aide Memoire both document that mass displacement has fragmented the school-age population, scattered beneficiary communities, and destroyed educational infrastructure, resulting in only 7.3% fund utilization as of the reporting period and a procurement underspend of 98.3% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The safety net project's ISR records that institutional capacity constraints are directly compounded by the displacement crisis overwhelming the administrative, logistical, and financial systems required to implement emergency social protection at scale <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>.
