# Ethiopia Portfolio FCV Briefing

## 1. Active Armed Conflict in Amhara and Oromia Regions

Ethiopia's two most populous regions remain engulfed in active armed conflict that is directly degrading portfolio performance across multiple projects. In Amhara, the Fano insurgency—involving multiple militia factions, some of which signed a partial peace agreement in December 2025 while others continue armed resistance—has closed over 4,000 schools, displaced millions, and prompted government counterinsurgency operations including drone strikes on civilian areas. In Oromia, violence persists despite a December 2024 peace agreement with one OLA faction; over 25 civilians were killed in East Arsi Zone in October 2025 alone, and intercommunal clashes between Oromo and Somali communities have displaced over 288,000 people since July 2025. Both conflicts are explicitly acknowledged in project design documents as high-severity risks. The rural roads project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a> identifies "security (substantial): internal conflicts affecting project sites, staff safety, material transportation" and requires conflict-sensitive site selection across all 13 regions. The pastoral livelihoods project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-334703ce-834f-4cd4-bf49-4e941b00bb0a.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – PAD</a> explicitly lists "localized conflicts in Amhara and Oromia regions" as a high political risk and acknowledges that private sector actors are deterred from operating in conflict-prone areas. The TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a> notes that "security risks may hinder smooth implementation especially for PTCs in conflict regions," while the safety net program <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087f8ffa9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172479 – PAD</a> acknowledges "implementation affected by periodic political unrest and inter-ethnic conflict." These risks are now fully materializing. Aide memoire evidence confirms that unexploded ordnance in Amhara "hotspot woredas" is blocking public works implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>, that PSNP implementation in both Amhara and Oromia is directly constrained by active conflict <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>, and that security restrictions are preventing field monitoring across multiple projects <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. The WASH project has documented that internal conflict in Amhara is impeding staff movement and project activities <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the energy access project has required mini-grid site replacements due to security conditions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-45fbd416-22c6-4fe2-a001-21a06b2bddd9.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – Aide Memoire</a>. The IDP recovery project has been unable to finalize city selection and support modalities in conflict-affected areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>. Looking forward, the persistence of multiple Fano factions and the failure of partial peace agreements to stabilize Oromia suggest these operational constraints will intensify through the June 2026 election period, when insecurity is expected to worsen.

## 2. Mass Internal Displacement and Deteriorating Humanitarian Conditions

Ethiopia hosts between 3.3 and 4.7 million internally displaced persons—one of the world's largest IDP populations—with displacement driven by the conflicts described above, climate shocks, and intercommunal violence. Living conditions in formal and informal camps are described as dire, and the humanitarian funding environment has deteriorated sharply: WFP was forced to suspend treatment for 650,000 malnourished women and children in April 2025, and 3.6 million vulnerable people face loss of food assistance due to funding gaps exacerbated by the Trump administration's foreign aid freeze. This crisis creates compounding risks for the portfolio. Multiple PADs explicitly acknowledge displacement as a design consideration but underestimate its scale. The rural roads project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a> implements a "voluntary land donation protocol to avoid involuntary resettlement" and acknowledges "ongoing internal displacements due to conflicts," but its targeting of 11.3 million beneficiaries assumes population stability that does not exist. The TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a> commits to at least 15% IDP enrollment (approximately 30,000 beneficiaries) but operates in a context where 4.7 million people have been forced from their homes. The human development project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a> targets 512,000 drought-induced IDPs but faces a displacement reality three to nine times larger. Realized risks confirm that these design gaps are translating into implementation failures. The TVET project has achieved only 0.76% IDP enrollment against a 35% target <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-02907b3f-0a5c-484e-b00e-136cb8695aad.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – ISR</a>, with ISR evidence linking this directly to insecurity in Amhara and Oromia and the precarious conditions facing IDPs. The safety net program faces UXO contamination blocking public works in Tigray and Amhara hotspot woredas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>, and its Early Warning System dashboard has limited access among prioritization committee institutions, reducing coordinated shock response capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>. The WASH project documents that displacement adds pressure to localized water sources and that community management structures (WASHCOMs) are being undermined by population instability <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The humanitarian funding crisis is also directly constraining government counterpart financing: the safety net program faces macroeconomic stress from currency depreciation reducing the real value of government contributions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>, and the WASH project's US$50 million counterpart funding commitment is at risk as government budget constraints intensify <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The IDP recovery project has been unable to finalize targeting for Tigray and Somali region IDP caseloads <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the health project has failed to operationalize IDP clinics due to Ministry of Finance counter-funding delays <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>.

## 3. Political Crisis in Tigray and Fragile Peace Process in Northern Regions

The 2022 Pretoria Agreement has not produced stable governance in northern Ethiopia. Eritrean troops continue to commit sexual violence in occupied areas of Tigray, Tigrayans are being forcibly displaced from Western Tigray, and the pending referendum on Western Tigray's territorial status remains unresolved. More acutely, the National Election Board's deregistration of the TPLF from the June 2026 elections—while recognizing a rival party, Simret—has been labeled a "clear declaration of war" by Tigrayan leaders. Tensions between the TPLF and Tigray Defense Forces are escalating, and federal authority is described as "quickly shrinking" in Mekelle and other key cities. This political crisis creates direct governance risks for projects operating in or depending on coordination with Tigray. The TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a> explicitly targets Tigray as a conflict-affected region requiring PTC coverage and IDP beneficiary inclusion. The human development project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a> excludes Tigray from its coverage area—itself a signal of recognized implementation impossibility—while the digital economy project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08847bca4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171034 – PAD</a> explicitly excludes Tigray due to "ongoing conflict and communication blackout since November 2020," directly contradicting its stated objective of increasing digital inclusion for all Ethiopian citizens. The safety net program <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087f8ffa9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172479 – PAD</a> relies on "Federal and Regional Steering Committees" for coordination, structures that are being undermined by shrinking federal authority. Realized risks confirm that these vulnerabilities are materializing. The WASH project has documented asset loss, missing financial records, and inability to verify project assets in Tigray, with auditors unable to access the region <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The safety net program has excluded five contested woredas from program coverage due to territorial fragmentation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>, and account opening in Tigray has been delayed due to limited collaboration among implementing entities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. The IDP recovery project has required adapted implementation modalities in Tigray due to security considerations and post-conflict instability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the UNOPS-to-RPCU transition in Tigray is delayed precisely as governance structures weaken <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban safety net project has documented that federal authority shrinkage in Mekelle is directly threatening the coordination structures the project depends on <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>. Forward-looking risks are severe: if the TPLF's electoral exclusion triggers renewed armed conflict between Tigrayan factions, the entire northern portfolio faces potential suspension.

## 4. Governance Fragility, Electoral Legitimacy Crisis, and Civil Society Crackdown

Ethiopia's June 2026 elections are emerging as a critical governance risk for the portfolio. At least 44 journalists were abducted or detained in 2025, Deutsche Welle correspondents have been suspended, and media law changes have increased executive control over information. Ethiopia dropped to 145th out of 180 countries in the 2025 World Press Freedom Index. Major opposition parties are expected to boycott the elections as they did in 2021, insecurity may prevent voting in several regions, and the TPLF's exclusion threatens Tigrayan participation entirely. The severe cost-of-living crisis is identified as a potential driver of insurgency recruitment ahead of the vote. These dynamics create systemic risks for project governance, monitoring, and accountability mechanisms. The rural roads project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a> relies on an "Independent Verification Agent" and "third-party performance evaluation" for M&E, mechanisms that are structurally compromised when civil society space contracts. The digital economy project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08847bca4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171034 – PAD</a> acknowledges "upcoming elections" as a political risk but provides only "monitoring of political developments" as mitigation, with no contingency arrangements if ministerial capacity is disrupted. The TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a> depends on a "Project Steering Committee chaired by the MoLS Minister" and "Regional Skills Bureaus coordination"—governance structures vulnerable to political reshuffling. Realized risks confirm that governance deterioration is already affecting implementation. The rural roads project maintains a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with ISR evidence linking this to the systematic erosion of institutional checks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-7b125857-00f3-4f1b-a25e-a0ad429dba62.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's political and governance risk has remained "Substantial" throughout implementation, with ISR evidence connecting this to the media crackdown and electoral legitimacy crisis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-02907b3f-0a5c-484e-b00e-136cb8695aad.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – ISR</a>. The energy access project has seen declining governance ratings attributed to the same dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-a6dffcd4-0989-414f-875d-c153cf49eb19.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – ISR</a>. The digital economy project faces Ethio Telecom's resistance to regulatory reform and the stalling of privatization, both reflecting patterns of state consolidation of control over key infrastructure ahead of elections <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>. The safety net program's food procurement governance is contested, with the state-owned Ethiopian Trading Business Corporation's higher pricing and delivery delays creating fiduciary concerns <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. The WASH project documents woreda-level leadership reshuffling and staff turnover driven by political instability <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the livestock project faces persistent high political and governance risk ratings across multiple ISRs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P159382-d6896db2-88d8-43da-9163-01d9c113b5ec.pdf" target="_blank">P159382 – ISR</a>.

## 5. Cross-Border Spillover from Regional Conflicts and Humanitarian Funding Crisis

Ethiopia's FCV context is further complicated by two reinforcing external pressures. First, the Sudan conflict has driven over 163,000 individuals into Ethiopia as of January 2025, with 50,000+ new arrivals in 2025 alone, primarily from Sudan and South Sudan. Gambella region has experienced cross-border incursions by South Sudanese opposition forces protecting Nuer communities, and thousands of South Sudanese refugees entered Ethiopia in March 2025. Ethiopia now hosts over one million refugees amid growing political instability and funding shortfalls that undermine protection capacity. Second, the broader humanitarian funding crisis—driven by the Trump administration's foreign aid freeze and reduced international donor support—has created a resource environment in which 21 million people need humanitarian assistance but funding is critically insufficient. These pressures directly affect projects with refugee or border-region components. The refugee and host community project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088ddd75f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P178047 – PAD</a> explicitly targets Gambella as one of six implementation regions and acknowledges "cross-border tensions" and "cross-border mixed migration" as contextual risks. The human development project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a> commits to supporting 800,000 refugees and integrating them into national education and health systems. The urban safety net project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087e0afcb&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P169943 – PAD</a> targets refugee camps near urban centers including Kebribeyah and Sherkole, with 81,000 refugees in its beneficiary population. Realized risks confirm that cross-border dynamics are actively disrupting implementation. In Pugnido (Gambella), refugee program dropout and the need to re-target 3,175 beneficiaries is directly attributable to insecurity and forced relocation driven by South Sudanese conflict spillover <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>. The refugee and host community project has achieved only 6% refugee livelihood participation against a 30% target, with the humanitarian funding crisis and cross-border instability identified as primary drivers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. The project has also documented that Gambella and Benishangul Gumuz are inaccessible to World Bank supervision due to security conditions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban safety net project faces declining humanitarian assistance affecting refugee education and services, directly reflecting the country-level funding crisis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. The health project documents that Ministry of Finance counter-funding delays—a direct consequence of fiscal constraints driven by the humanitarian emergency—have prevented operationalization of mobile health teams and IDP clinics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>. The digital economy project faces customs duty pressures that can double contract prices, reducing the effective value of limited project resources in a context where every dollar of humanitarian and development funding must be maximized <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>.
