# Guinea FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Authoritarian Consolidation and Institutional Fragility

Guinea's junta has systematically dismantled democratic institutions since the September 2021 coup, culminating in the March 2026 dissolution of 40 political parties—including the three main opposition groups—by late-night decree, stripping them of legal status and assets. This follows a December 2025 presidential election in which all major opposition leaders were barred, a September 2025 constitutional referendum extending presidential terms to seven years, and the dissolution of elected municipal councils in March 2024. The cumulative effect is a party-state architecture with no meaningful institutional checks on executive power. Across the portfolio, PADs anticipated this trajectory: the energy access project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7b1cb57-9078-490e-8988-30bc9fe608c0.pdf" target="_blank">P511453 – PAD</a>, the emergency response project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509503-9e19c665-633a-4b5d-9057-1897309bb41a.pdf" target="_blank">P509503 – PAD</a>, the water project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a>, the fisheries project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ff8aaae6-4241-49f6-b294-04cfff550aaf.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – PAD</a>, the local governance project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-dbb6f3f7-06bb-4d73-a6df-8c71eb7f7711.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – PAD</a>, the mining governance project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08859ec01&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168613 – PAD</a>, the social protection project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08852e437&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168777 – PAD</a>, and the agribusiness project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087d59368&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – PAD</a> all rate political and governance risk as high or substantial, citing the transitional government context and potential for policy disruption. These risks are now materializing concretely. The local governance project documents that the replacement of elected municipal councils with special delegations has caused implementation delays <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-0d85cb88-e8fe-4bb3-b9b1-81c8c0178a90.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – ISR</a>, while frozen commune accounts since 2022 have blocked 45% of FNDL fund transfers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-0d85cb88-e8fe-4bb3-b9b1-81c8c0178a90.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – ISR</a>. The February 2024 government dissolution caused bureaucratic delays in fund transfers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the absence of formal project governance committees (COS/CODIR) has been flagged as an institutional vacuum <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>. The mining governance project records persistent high political risk ratings <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-8608b6ee-d8be-4d24-a9b0-3883596421e7.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – ISR</a>, and the agribusiness project similarly maintains a high governance risk rating throughout implementation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08872333c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – ISR</a>. The pattern is consistent: institutional consolidation is not merely a background condition but is actively degrading the administrative systems on which project implementation depends.

## 2. Security Force Violence, Protest Suppression, and Civic Space Closure

Since May 2022, Guinea's junta has maintained a blanket ban on public protests, and security forces have responded to violations with lethal force—killing at least 59 protesters and bystanders since 2022, including at least 20 in 2024, with documented killings of three children during Conakry demonstrations. Simultaneously, enforced disappearances have been used systematically to silence dissent: prominent FNDC activists Oumar Sylla and Mamadou Billo Bah have been missing since July 2024, civil society leader Abdoul Sacko was abducted and tortured in February 2025, and investigative journalist Habib Marouane Camara disappeared in December 2024. Six major private media outlets—including Djoma TV, Espace FM, and Sweet FM—had their licenses revoked in May 2024 and remain banned, creating what RSF describes as a "profound media vacuum." Together, these dynamics constitute a closed civic space that directly threatens project implementation across the portfolio. PADs for the energy access project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7b1cb57-9078-490e-8988-30bc9fe608c0.pdf" target="_blank">P511453 – PAD</a>, emergency response project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509503-9e19c665-633a-4b5d-9057-1897309bb41a.pdf" target="_blank">P509503 – PAD</a>, water project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a>, fisheries project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ff8aaae6-4241-49f6-b294-04cfff550aaf.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – PAD</a>, local governance project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-dbb6f3f7-06bb-4d73-a6df-8c71eb7f7711.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – PAD</a>, and health project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-aa26d346-cc66-4708-a538-60e0b30cdda4.pdf" target="_blank">P506072 – PAD</a> all identify stakeholder engagement, grievance redress mechanisms, and community participation as essential implementation modalities—yet all depend on a civic environment that no longer exists. The water project's resettlement component (US$13 million for project-affected persons) is particularly exposed: delayed or inadequate compensation could trigger protests that security forces would suppress with lethal force <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing: the local governance project's early warning system (SAPR) covers only 169 of 337 communes, leaving critical blind spots in protest-prone and border areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>. The fisheries project's newly established implementation team faces a governance environment where civil society partners—essential for community-led fisheries management—operate under documented threat <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180906-af1ea935-d30a-4433-ab02-ba43568d4457.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – ISR</a>. The health project's community health worker network and grievance documentation activities create staff visibility that carries personal security risk in the current environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-aa26d346-cc66-4708-a538-60e0b30cdda4.pdf" target="_blank">P506072 – PAD</a>. Media suppression further compounds these risks by eliminating the information channels through which projects communicate tariff reforms, connection procedures, and beneficiary eligibility to communities [P511453 | PAD; P179017 | PAD; P177095 | PAD].

## 3. Simandou Mining Boom: Revenue Governance Failure and Social Disruption

Guinea shipped its first iron ore from the $20 billion Simandou mega-project in December 2025, with the IMF projecting a 26% GDP lift by 2030. However, employment peaked at over 60,000 construction jobs in 2024–2025 and will fall to under 15,000 operational workers, creating a mass layoff dynamic of approximately 45,000 displaced workers concentrated in southeastern Guinea. Guinea holds only a 15% stake in the project, with Chinese interests dominant, raising fundamental questions about benefit distribution. The World Bank's July 2025 Guinea Economic Update warned of a "narrow window to ensure benefits of growth are widely shared." Across the portfolio, PADs anticipated this risk: the water project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a> identifies incomplete information on Simandou revenues as a macroeconomic risk; the mining governance project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08859ec01&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168613 – PAD</a> targets alternative income-generating activities for mining communities; the agribusiness project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087d59368&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – PAD</a> explicitly locates itself in Boké and Kindia mining regions to address social tensions from mining; and the social protection project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08852e437&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168777 – PAD</a> targets youth unemployment as an FCV driver. These risks are now materializing across multiple dimensions. The mining governance project's Environmental Management Agency (AGEE) lacks sustainable financing for independent monitoring missions of Simandou operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>—a direct governance gap at the moment the project is operationalizing. Institutional capacity for mining revenue collection is weak, with poor coordination across customs, tax, and central bank authorities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>. Resettlement and compensation frameworks remain non-operationalized <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>, leaving displaced mining communities without formal protections during the employment contraction phase. Environmental and social safeguard implementation stands at less than 20% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>, and inter-ministerial divergence between the Ministries of Mines and Infrastructure has blocked effective oversight of rail and port operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>. The local governance project's suspended recruitment of Local Development Planning Service Chiefs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a> further undermines the institutional capacity needed to manage social disruption from mass layoffs at the commune level. The agribusiness project faces contractor non-performance in the Mambia region <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08872333c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – ISR</a> and UN agency implementation delays in agricultural value chains <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08872333c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – ISR</a>—both in areas where mining-driven social disruption is most acute.

## 4. Chronic Food Insecurity and Humanitarian Vulnerability

Guinea is among 31 African countries requiring external food assistance as of the FAO's March 2026 assessment. Over 21.8% of Guinean households struggle with food insecurity, 6.1% of children under five suffer global acute malnutrition, and 24.4% are stunted. More than half the population lives in poverty. The FAO projects that West Africa's food insecurity could rise to 52.8 million people during the June–August 2026 lean season—coinciding with the critical implementation phase of multiple portfolio projects. Guinea remains heavily dependent on rice imports (40% of all food imports), exposing it to global price shocks. This humanitarian baseline creates structural vulnerability across the portfolio. The emergency response project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509503-9e19c665-633a-4b5d-9057-1897309bb41a.pdf" target="_blank">P509503 – PAD</a> is explicitly designed to address food insecurity affecting 17% of the population, targeting 225,000 households with food assistance and 300,000 with cash transfers—but the scale of the crisis may overwhelm project capacity, and the projected lean season escalation was not fully anticipated in project design. The health project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-aa26d346-cc66-4708-a538-60e0b30cdda4.pdf" target="_blank">P506072 – PAD</a> identifies chronic food insecurity as a fragility driver and includes nutrition and WASH support groups, but its assumption that free health care will stimulate demand for maternal and child health services may not hold if households lack food security to support pregnant women's nutrition. The social protection project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08852e437&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168777 – PAD</a> provides emergency cash transfers of 250,000 GNF (~$26 USD) per month—an amount that may prove insufficient if food prices spike during the lean season—and covers only 150,000 of an estimated 840,000 eligible households. The fisheries project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ff8aaae6-4241-49f6-b294-04cfff550aaf.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – PAD</a> targets 65,000 people including small-scale fishers and women processors who are among Guinea's most food-insecure groups; acute food insecurity could reduce community participation in project activities and increase pressure to divert project inputs toward immediate survival. The agribusiness project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087d59368&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – PAD</a> targets 145,000 farmers and rural households for commercial agriculture participation, but beneficiaries facing acute food insecurity may lack the capacity to invest in commercial activities or may consume project inputs for immediate survival. The water project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a> faces the additional challenge that food-insecure households in Greater Conakry may be unable to afford tariff increases for water services, undermining project financial sustainability.

## 5. Development Partner Tensions and Governance Conditionality Friction

A structural tension is emerging between multilateral development partners committed to governance conditionalities and the Doumbouya government, which is increasingly aligned with Chinese and Russian investors who operate without political conditions. The World Bank's July 2025 Guinea Economic Update explicitly called for reforms to make mining-driven growth "more inclusive and resilient." Russia maintains significant bauxite mining interests and has used Guinea as a logistics hub for arms shipments to Sahelian military juntas. The governance environment—party dissolution, protest bans, enforced disappearances, media suppression—directly conflicts with World Bank fiduciary standards and governance requirements. PADs across the portfolio anticipated this tension: the energy access project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7b1cb57-9078-490e-8988-30bc9fe608c0.pdf" target="_blank">P511453 – PAD</a> includes US$60 million in performance-based conditions linked to governance reforms and tariff restructuring; the local governance project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-dbb6f3f7-06bb-4d73-a6df-8c71eb7f7711.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – PAD</a> relies on government commitment to decentralization reform; the mining governance project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08859ec01&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168613 – PAD</a> depends on sustained government engagement with World Bank governance objectives; and the emergency response project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509503-9e19c665-633a-4b5d-9057-1897309bb41a.pdf" target="_blank">P509503 – PAD</a> explicitly contemplates use of OP 7.30 if circumstances require. These risks are materializing. The local governance project documents that the suspension of local public service reform—including recruitment of Local Development Planning Service Chiefs—signals government deprioritization of governance capacity-building <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>. The mining governance project records incomplete mining sector regulatory reforms (Protected Area Code, Water Code, mining permit exclusions from classified forests) despite explicit World Bank calls for action <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-8608b6ee-d8be-4d24-a9b0-3883596421e7.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – ISR</a>, weak E&S governance implementation at less than 20% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>, and a 25% overall disbursement rate reflecting institutional capacity constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-8608b6ee-d8be-4d24-a9b0-3883596421e7.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – ISR</a>. The agribusiness project's technical support firm demobilized without replacement planning <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08872333c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – ISR</a>, and FAO and WFP implementation delays in agricultural value chains suggest broader development partner coordination challenges <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08872333c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – ISR</a>. The social protection project's fiduciary risk has escalated during implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168777-23f25466-2716-4d90-b731-347460542b3c.pdf" target="_blank">P168777 – ISR</a>, consistent with the weakening of institutional accountability mechanisms that accompanies the governance deterioration. Across the portfolio, the practical question is whether the World Bank's governance leverage—through performance-based conditions, policy dialogue, and conditionality—remains effective in an environment where the government has demonstrated willingness to accept the costs of international isolation in exchange for political consolidation.
