# Guinea FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Authoritarian Consolidation and Institutional Legitimacy Crisis

Guinea's political environment has deteriorated sharply under President Doumbouya's junta, which dissolved 40 political parties in March 2026—including the three main opposition groups—following a December 2025 presidential election from which all major opposition leaders were barred. The junta has replaced all 34 civilian prefects with military officers and dissolved all 342 elected municipal councils, replacing them with appointed delegations. This systematic dismantling of democratic institutions creates profound uncertainty for the portfolio. The Guinea Energy Access Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7b1cb57-9078-490e-8988-30bc9fe608c0.pdf" target="_blank">P511453 – PAD</a> and the Contingency Emergency Response Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509503-9e19c665-633a-4b5d-9057-1897309bb41a.pdf" target="_blank">P509503 – PAD</a> both identify high political and governance risk, noting that the transitional government context creates uncertainty in decision-making and that governance disruptions have previously affected sector coordination and implementation. The Decentralization and Local Governance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-dbb6f3f7-06bb-4d73-a6df-8c71eb7f7711.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – PAD</a> relies explicitly on "transitional government assurances of continued support" and a commitment to decentralization reform—assurances that are increasingly hollow as the junta consolidates single-party control. The Agribusiness Development Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087d59368&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – PAD</a> depends on high-level inter-ministerial steering committees whose institutional autonomy is directly threatened by the same consolidation dynamics. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The Local Governance Project has experienced cascading implementation disruptions directly attributable to the dissolution of municipal councils and government transitions, with treasury approval delays blocking 45% of FNDL fund transfers to communes <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-0d85cb88-e8fe-4bb3-b9b1-81c8c0178a90.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – ISR</a>, local government accounts frozen since 2022 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the suspension of local public service reform including recruitment of Local Development Planning Service Chiefs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Mining and Natural Resources Governance Project has recorded a persistent 25% disbursement rate, with institutional capacity constraints linked to the same governance deterioration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-8608b6ee-d8be-4d24-a9b0-3883596421e7.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – ISR</a>. The Social Safety Net Project has seen its high political and governance risk rating sustained throughout implementation, with the junta's consolidation directly undermining the institutional pluralism and accountability mechanisms that social protection delivery requires <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168777-23f25466-2716-4d90-b731-347460542b3c.pdf" target="_blank">P168777 – ISR</a>.

## 2. Security Force Impunity, Protest Suppression, and Civic Space Contraction

Since imposing a protest ban in May 2022, Guinea's security forces have killed at least 59 people—including at least 20 in 2024—with no accountability for perpetrators. Simultaneously, enforced disappearances of civil society leaders, journalists, and activists have become systematic: FNDC activists Oumar Sylla and Mamadou Billo Bah have been missing since July 2024, civil society leader Abdoul Sacko was abducted and tortured in February 2025, and investigative journalist Habib Marouane Camara disappeared in December 2024. Guinea also recorded the largest single-year drop in the 2025 World Press Freedom Index, with six major private media outlets having their licenses revoked. Together, these dynamics create a climate of terror that directly threatens project implementation across the portfolio. The Energy Access Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7b1cb57-9078-490e-8988-30bc9fe608c0.pdf" target="_blank">P511453 – PAD</a> identifies substantial environmental and social risk, noting that community acceptance of smart metering and tariff increases is a key stakeholder concern—in a context where grievance expression is suppressed and security forces have used lethal force against protesters, community resistance to resettlement or tariff increases could escalate dangerously. The Water Supply Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2e1acf99-ec4d-4ffc-b49b-0c9ec31f74c2.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – PAD</a> operates in Greater Conakry with 2.6 million beneficiaries and explicitly notes that its tariff reform component could itself trigger community resistance; the PAD acknowledges that previous resettlement delays have caused implementation problems, and the project carries US$13 million in resettlement compensation obligations. The Fisheries Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ff8aaae6-4241-49f6-b294-04cfff550aaf.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – PAD</a> relies on security forces for marine protected area patrols and monitoring, control, and surveillance enforcement—creating direct exposure to documented patterns of excessive force. The Local Governance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-dbb6f3f7-06bb-4d73-a6df-8c71eb7f7711.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – PAD</a> depends on participatory budgeting, citizen engagement, and community facilitators operating in an environment where public assembly is prohibited. These risks are actively materializing. The Water Supply Project has recorded substantial environmental and social risk in implementation, with displacement-related tensions in Greater Conakry compounded by the protest ban <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P179017-70d0e4c6-d109-4724-94b5-d9be1036fa78.pdf" target="_blank">P179017 – ISR</a>. The Social Safety Net Project has documented that the climate of terror from enforced disappearances is undermining beneficiary confidence in state-led service delivery and constraining civil society partnerships essential for program outreach <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168777-23f25466-2716-4d90-b731-347460542b3c.pdf" target="_blank">P168777 – ISR</a>. The Mining Governance Project has recorded weak stakeholder engagement and transparency in mining governance, with the absence of institutionalized citizen consultation mechanisms directly linked to the suppression of civic space <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-8608b6ee-d8be-4d24-a9b0-3883596421e7.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – ISR</a>. The Local Governance Project's conflict early warning system covers only 169 of 337 communes, leaving significant blind spots precisely where protest dynamics and security force responses are most likely to escalate <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 3. Simandou Mining Boom: Revenue Governance, Mass Layoffs, and Social Disruption

Guinea's $23 billion Simandou iron ore project—75% Chinese-owned—shipped its first cargo in December 2025, with exports projected to eventually boost GDP by 26% by 2030. However, the project's transition from approximately 60,000 construction jobs to roughly 15,000 operational positions is generating acute social disruption in mining communities. Simultaneously, a broad cancellation of mining permits in 2025 affected compliant and non-compliant operators alike, triggering investor-state disputes. The IMF has warned that Simandou revenues must be invested in education and infrastructure to reduce poverty, but governance of the promised sovereign wealth fund remains opaque. These dynamics create compounding risks across the portfolio. The Mining and Natural Resources Governance Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08859ec01&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168613 – PAD</a> explicitly targets mining communities as beneficiaries and includes alternative income-generating activities for local communities, but its target of 500 beneficiaries is vastly insufficient relative to the scale of mass layoffs. The Agribusiness Development Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087d59368&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – PAD</a> is located in Boké and Kindia prefectures—the heart of Guinea's mining regions—where the PAD explicitly acknowledges that "mining activities attract large inflows of people seeking employment, creating social unrest." The Local Governance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-dbb6f3f7-06bb-4d73-a6df-8c71eb7f7711.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – PAD</a> depends on local planning institutions to manage the social transitions from mining-driven economic disruption, but the suspension of Local Development Planning Service Chief recruitment since April 2024 has hollowed out precisely this capacity. These risks are materializing with significant severity. The Mining Governance Project has recorded less than 20% implementation of environmental and social governance frameworks in the mining sector <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>, weak institutional capacity for environmental monitoring of Simandou operations with inadequate financing for the Environmental Management Agency's independent monitoring missions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>, non-operationalization of resettlement and compensation frameworks for mining-affected communities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>, and institutional capacity constraints in mining revenue collection across customs, tax, and central bank authorities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>. Inter-ministerial divergence between the Mines and Infrastructure ministries on rail and port control missions has further weakened regulatory oversight of the mega-project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-5b69ef5d-0ac1-4652-9e38-a491f1563ed9.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Local Governance Project's conflict early warning system gaps leave mining-affected communities potentially uncovered at the moment of greatest social disruption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 4. Chronic Food Insecurity, Poverty, and Humanitarian Vulnerability

Over 21.8% of Guinean households struggle with food insecurity, and more than half the population lives below the $3.65-a-day poverty line—a rate that rose 7 percentage points between 2019 and 2024, pushing 1.8 million additional people into poverty despite robust GDP growth. Guinea is listed among the countries most at risk during the 2026 lean season, with 6.1% of children under five suffering global acute malnutrition and 24.4% stunted. The country is heavily dependent on rice imports, representing 40% of all food imports, creating acute vulnerability to global price shocks. This humanitarian baseline creates structural vulnerability across the portfolio. The Contingency Emergency Response Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509503-9e19c665-633a-4b5d-9057-1897309bb41a.pdf" target="_blank">P509503 – PAD</a> identifies food insecurity affecting 17% of the population as a core target condition, but the scale of the crisis—with Guinea among 31 African countries requiring external food assistance and the lean season projected to affect up to 52.8 million people regionally—may overwhelm project capacity, which covers only 225,000 households for food assistance. The Social Safety Net Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08852e437&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168777 – PAD</a> provides emergency cash transfers of 250,000 GNF (approximately $26 USD) per month to 150,000 households against an estimated 840,000 eligible households—an 18% coverage rate that creates significant exclusion risk and potential social tensions over beneficiary selection. The Health System Strengthening Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-aa26d346-cc66-4708-a538-60e0b30cdda4.pdf" target="_blank">P506072 – PAD</a> identifies chronic food insecurity as a fragility driver and includes nutrition and maternal health services, but its assumption that free health care will stimulate demand may not hold if households lack food security to support pregnant women's nutrition or child health outcomes. The Fisheries Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ff8aaae6-4241-49f6-b294-04cfff550aaf.pdf" target="_blank">P180906 – PAD</a> targets 65,000 people including small-scale fishers and women processors who are among Guinea's most food-insecure groups. These risks are materializing in implementation. The Health Project has documented that the deepening food insecurity crisis is undermining beneficiary ability to access services and creating competing demands on household resources, with the projected June–August 2026 lean season posing acute risks to project outcomes during a critical implementation phase <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506072-a232a162-5ddd-476a-9b6b-49c8ebade3e3.pdf" target="_blank">P506072 – ISR</a>. The Social Safety Net Project has recorded that the humanitarian crisis is constraining beneficiary participation and that the emergency cash transfer amount may prove insufficient if food prices spike due to global shocks or regional instability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168777-23f25466-2716-4d90-b731-347460542b3c.pdf" target="_blank">P168777 – ISR</a>. The Mining Governance Project has noted that food-insecure populations dependent on natural resources face acute livelihood pressures that limit their capacity to participate in project activities and increase their vulnerability to project-induced restrictions on resource access <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168613-8608b6ee-d8be-4d24-a9b0-3883596421e7.pdf" target="_blank">P168613 – ISR</a>.

## 5. Jihadist Spillover from Mali and Cross-Border Security Deterioration

JNIM, the al-Qaeda-affiliated jihadist coalition, has recorded two dozen violent extremist incidents in Mali within 50 km of Guinea's border since 2024—in regions where little to no activity previously existed. Analysts warn that JNIM may use strengthened support zones in southern Mali to expand into Guinea, and the displacement of Malian conflict-affected populations into Guinea is compounding border instability. Guinea's security cooperation capacity is constrained by its alignment with Sahelian juntas and limited engagement with Western counterterrorism frameworks, creating an intelligence gap. Several projects carry meaningful exposure to this threat. The Contingency Emergency Response Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509503-9e19c665-633a-4b5d-9057-1897309bb41a.pdf" target="_blank">P509503 – PAD</a> provides national coverage across all regions of Guinea, including mountainous terrain and major river basins proximate to the Mali border, but the PAD identifies jihadist spillover only as a contingency risk without specific operational protocols for managing cross-border militant activity in border areas. The Mining and Natural Resources Governance Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08859ec01&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168613 – PAD</a> includes protected area management activities in the Upper Niger National Park, the Niger source area in Faranah, and the Niger-Tinkisso basin—all in eastern Guinea proximate to the Mali border—with rangers conducting patrols in areas that face elevated security risk from potential cross-border spillover. The Local Governance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-dbb6f3f7-06bb-4d73-a6df-8c71eb7f7711.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – PAD</a> has deployed a conflict early warning and response system, but its incomplete geographic coverage of only 169 of 337 communes likely leaves border communes and eastern regions inadequately monitored for cross-border militant activity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Agribusiness Development Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087d59368&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – PAD</a> includes 800 km of rural roads in Boké and Kindia, with some routes in areas proximate to Mali, and the PAD notes that infrastructure development may improve security through better connectivity—but this assumption has not been tested against active jihadist spillover. These risks are beginning to materialize. The Agribusiness Project has recorded contractor non-performance and project stalling in the Mambia region of eastern Guinea, which is proximate to the Mali border where JNIM has intensified operations; the inability to execute road rehabilitation contracts in this specific region plausibly reflects security deterioration from cross-border militant activity <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08872333c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164184 – ISR</a>. The Local Governance Project's early warning system gaps in border communes represent a realized operational vulnerability that reduces the portfolio's collective capacity to detect and respond to emerging security threats before they disrupt project activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177095-594cf5df-5372-4b2c-b80e-d1702874247b.pdf" target="_blank">P177095 – Aide Memoire</a>.
