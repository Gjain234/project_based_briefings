# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Governance Consolidation and Democratic Backsliding

Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms that poses systemic risk across the portfolio. Projects relying on multi-ministerial coordination and sustained political commitment are particularly exposed. The Public Finance Management Modernization Project depends on a PFM Reform Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance, and the PAD explicitly identifies "vested interests within public administration" as a high political risk, with mitigation resting on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that weakens as executive power consolidates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The TVET and Skills Development Project similarly relies on a high-level steering committee under the Economic Development Council, but the PAD provides no safeguards against executive interference in beneficiary selection or institutional governance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The Djibouti-Ethiopia Transport Corridor Project acknowledges that "ability to influence private sector finance mobilization depends on political will" and that "line ministries may lack incentives or resist change," vulnerabilities that intensify under indefinite presidential rule <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing: the PFM project's ISR documents a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with the constitutional revision directly undermining the institutional predictability required for reform implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The corridor project's ISR similarly records a "Substantial" political and governance risk, with constitutional manipulation correlating with weakened institutional autonomy across government bodies <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's Aide Memoire documents a non-functional steering committee and coordination gaps between government and development partners, with inter-ministerial processes increasingly dependent on executive discretion rather than formalized procedures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 2. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Accountability Mechanisms

The near-total absence of independent civil society and severe restrictions on media freedom—with Djibouti classified as "Not Free" by Freedom House and journalists regularly harassed or arrested—create structural accountability deficits that undermine monitoring, grievance redress, and stakeholder engagement across the portfolio. The PFM project's Component 3 explicitly targets "civil society engagement" and "citizen access to digital services," yet the PAD does not acknowledge that "only marginal voices present on social media" and independent organizations cannot meaningfully participate in oversight <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The Education System Strengthening Project establishes a grievance mechanism "for all stakeholders," but in an environment where activists face arrest for criticism, vulnerable groups including refugees, rural students, and girls face structural barriers to voicing concerns <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. The Djibouti-Ethiopia Transport Corridor Project includes a Stakeholder Engagement Plan and project-level grievance mechanisms, but does not address how these will function when communities fear government retaliation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing across the portfolio. The corridor project's Aide Memoire documents systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards affecting 150 daily workers, with no independent unions or media scrutiny to expose violations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The same project's Aide Memoire records inadequate government oversight of safety standards, with critical violations omitted from official documentation in the absence of independent monitoring <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's ISR documents a compromised internal audit function and non-implementation of GBV action plans, with the collapsed civil society environment enabling institutional gaps to persist without external pressure <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The GBV Prevention Project's Aide Memoire records stalled NGO contract negotiations, absent accounting systems at project launch, and delayed financial reporting—all reflecting the institutional hollowing that accompanies the suppression of independent oversight <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 3. Severe Youth Unemployment and Structural Economic Exclusion

Youth unemployment exceeding 75%, compounded by endemic corruption, high inequality, and limited private sector absorptive capacity, creates a structural economic environment that undermines the portfolio's employment and livelihood objectives while generating social pressure that complicates implementation. The TVET project's PAD acknowledges "macroeconomic uncertainties" and proposes mitigation through "regular engagement with private sector stakeholders," but documents no labor market demand assessment or contingency for limited job creation—a critical gap given that the project targets 23,775 trained graduates in an economy structurally unable to absorb them <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The Agri-Food Value Chain Project targets youth as direct beneficiaries but relies on technical assistance and business plan development without addressing the macro-level unemployment context, creating risk that training will not convert to income <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The Djibouti-Ethiopia Transport Corridor Project lists "road transport service providers and local traders" as beneficiaries but provides no youth employment targets or analysis of how corridor improvements translate to jobs for the 75%+ unemployed youth population <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing with measurable impact. The TVET project's ISR documents refugee employment outcomes stuck at 5% against targets, with the labor market structurally unable to absorb trained graduates regardless of skills interventions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The same project's Aide Memoire records a stalled online gig work component and zero progress on labor mobility agreements, suggesting that supply-side training cannot overcome demand-side constraints in Djibouti's constrained economy <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Social Safety Net Project's Aide Memoire documents persistent difficulty identifying viable income-generating activities in remote localities, with weak market access and limited economic diversification reflecting the same structural fragility driving national youth unemployment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project's Aide Memoire records exploitation of 150 daily workers through non-compliant contracts and lack of social security registration, directly enabled by the severe unemployment context that eliminates workers' bargaining power <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 4. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Under Resource Constraints

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—85% concentrated in refugee villages—amid recurrent droughts and severely limited public resources, creating compounding strain on service delivery infrastructure that directly affects projects targeting vulnerable populations. The Emergency Preparedness and Response Project depends on SEGRC and MEFI for implementation, yet the PAD identifies institutional risk as "high" due to "limited capacity from lack of funding and staff" in precisely the agencies responsible for coordinating emergency response to refugee-hosting areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The Education System Strengthening Project directly targets 29,214 refugees across three villages for integration into the national education system, but acknowledges "external shocks" as a moderate protection risk without detailing how recurrent droughts or resource scarcity would affect programming continuity <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. The Food Security and Resilience Project explicitly targets emergency food stocks for refugees but does not specify allocation protocols between refugee villages and host communities, or contingency plans for managing competing demands under resource scarcity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing with documented humanitarian impact. The Health and Nutrition Project's ISR records refugee immunization rates at 12% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5%—concrete manifestations of the resource constraints and fragile public health infrastructure that characterize the refugee hosting environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The Food Security Project's Aide Memoire documents a June 2025 drought declaration triggering project suspension, with only 50% of emergency food stock targets achieved and zero progress on water infrastructure rehabilitation for 26 facilities—directly reducing drought response capacity for the 32,000 displaced persons the country hosts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Urban Upgrading Project's ISR records zero implementation by refugee-led CSOs, stalled property rights documentation in refugee villages, and non-implementation of refugee law recommendations, reflecting the institutional fragility and resource limitations that prevent effective refugee integration programming <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's ISR documents zero refugee enrollment against a 220-person target, with resource-constrained institutions unable to operationalize training programs for displaced populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>.

## 5. Ethnic Tensions and Political Exclusion of Minority Populations

The structural dominance of the Issa Somali ethnic group (approximately 60% of the population) in governance, combined with the systematic political marginalization of the Afar minority (approximately 35%), creates persistent risks of exclusionary implementation, elite capture, and community resistance that cut across the portfolio. The TVET project's PAD identifies "political and governance challenges" as a substantial risk but does not address how ethnic balance will be maintained in steering committees or beneficiary selection, leaving the project vulnerable to political interference if resource allocation is perceived as favoring particular groups <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The Djibouti-Ethiopia Transport Corridor Project lists "populations living in corridor area" as beneficiaries but provides no ethnic-disaggregated targeting criteria or mechanisms to ensure Afar minority participation despite their structural political exclusion <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The Refugee and Host Community Support Project acknowledges "social conflict" as a substantial risk and notes a "conflict-sensitive targeting" approach, but does not detail how targeting will navigate ethnic sensitivities when allocating resources between 12,860 refugees (predominantly Somali) and 35,900 host community residents who include both Issa and Afar populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing in implementation. The corridor project's Aide Memoire documents a non-functional grievance mechanism and persistent stakeholder engagement difficulties rated "Substantial," with ethnic tensions and political exclusion dynamics creating trust deficits and coordination failures among communities along the corridor <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The Social Safety Net Project's Aide Memoire records opaque youth training program selection without pre-defined eligibility criteria or accessibility safeguards, creating a direct mechanism through which ethnic favoritism could operate in a context where Issa dominance shapes access to state resources <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Urban Upgrading Project's ISR documents a delayed refugee and host community social cohesion assessment, preventing early identification of whether refugee settlement patterns are exacerbating existing ethnic fault lines in target slum areas <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The Education project's ISR records private Arabic schools—often associated with minority communities—refusing to integrate into the national EMIS system, suggesting institutional fragmentation along ethnic lines consistent with broader patterns of community distrust of state institutions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>.
