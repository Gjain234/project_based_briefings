# Sudan FCV Portfolio Briefing

## P504629 — Health System Preservation and Service Delivery

Sudan's active multi-front war between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) poses an existential operational threat to this health project. Kordofan and Darfur — both designated priority implementation areas — have emerged as the epicentres of hostilities, with drone strikes directly targeting hospitals, including the attack on Dilling General Hospital in March 2026. The project design explicitly acknowledged that 75% of health facilities in conflict-affected areas were already non-functional, that facilities had been attacked and looted, and that ongoing security risks from armed groups, extortion, and theft would require a customized Security Management Framework and tiered access protocols <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The RSF's proclamation of a parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" in Darfur and parts of Kordofan further complicates implementation: the PAD identified federal institutions as non-functional and designed the project to work through UN agencies and NGOs rather than government channels, but the de facto fragmentation of state authority creates competing governance claims that undermine even sub-national coordination <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. These risks are now fully materializing. Implementation Status Reports confirm that shifting frontlines are continuously forcing adaptation of facility targeting and supply chain approaches, with high ratings recorded across political and governance risk, institutional capacity risk, and stakeholder engagement risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The project is also contending with a high residual financial management risk despite satisfactory ratings, with only 7.3% fund utilization — a direct consequence of access obstruction, displacement-driven institutional disruption, and the inability to deploy field teams safely <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. Procurement has been similarly paralyzed, with only $281,000 of $16.8 million in signed contracts disbursed as of April 2025, reflecting the near-impossibility of vendor mobilization and contract execution under active combat conditions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The confirmed famine in El Fasher and Kadugli — both within the project's priority geography — adds a further layer of operational strain: severe acute malnutrition management is a project component, but famine conditions overwhelm the capacity of health facilities to deliver nutrition interventions and reduce staff retention <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The project's SEA/SH risk framework anticipated the elevated risk of sexual exploitation in a context of displaced populations and weak governance, but the UN Fact-Finding Mission's identification of genocide hallmarks and the documentation of at least 330 cases of conflict-related sexual violence signal that the protective environment for beneficiaries and community health workers has deteriorated far beyond what mitigation measures can address <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. Third-party monitoring recruitment has been delayed due to budgetary constraints, creating a verification vacuum precisely when independent oversight is most critical <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. Looking forward, the risk of renewed cholera and waterborne disease outbreaks — with the underlying drivers of displacement, collapsed WASH services, and disrupted immunization fully intact — threatens to overwhelm the project's disease surveillance and emergency operations center components, which can identify outbreaks but lack the scale to contain them <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>.

## P505963 — Emergency Safety Net and Cash Transfer Operations

This cash transfer project targeting 602,000 beneficiaries across conflict-affected states faces a convergence of risks that are simultaneously anticipated in the project design and actively materializing in implementation. The PAD identified West Kordofan and South Kordofan as implementation locations and acknowledged the high security risk from ongoing armed conflict, including evidence of robbery and looting of life-saving aid by soldiers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The March 2026 escalation in Kordofan — now confirmed as a key epicentre of hostilities with sustained drone attacks — directly threatens the project's ability to reach beneficiaries in these states. The project's cash transfer design, providing approximately US$10 per person per month to cover 50% of daily caloric needs, was calibrated against IPC Phase 3 and 4 conditions; however, confirmed famine (IPC Phase 5) in El Fasher and Kadugli, and the projected spread to 20 additional areas across Greater Darfur and Greater Kordofan, means the transfer value is likely insufficient to meet survival needs in the most affected localities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. Implementation Status Reports confirm that macroeconomic instability — hyperinflation at 211% as of October 2024 and severe currency depreciation — is actively eroding the real purchasing power of cash transfers, with this risk rated high in current reporting <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The project operates under the World Bank's policy for dealings with de facto governments, reflecting the political fragmentation that the RSF's parallel administration has deepened: competing governance authorities in Darfur create unclear jurisdictional boundaries, complicate WFP and UNICEF's ability to obtain permissions, and fragment the institutional frameworks required for fiduciary accountability [P505963 | PAD; P505963 | Implementation Status and Results Report]. Fiduciary risk is rated high in implementation reporting, with dual governance structures creating competing claims over financial systems and payment mechanisms, while the suppression of civil society removes independent checks on fund management <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The project's Component 2, which links cash transfers to health service utilization for 30,000 pregnant and lactating women, depends on "relatively stable areas with functioning primary healthcare facilities" — a condition that the collapse of 37% of health facilities and ongoing attacks on healthcare infrastructure have rendered increasingly untenable <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. Deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties is rated as a high realized risk, with WFP operations themselves having been targeted by drone strikes since early February 2026, directly threatening the implementing agency's ability to deliver transfers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. Environmental and social safeguard risks are escalating across all dimensions — displacement, famine, health system collapse, and active conflict — and the severe humanitarian funding gap (only 5.5% of the $2.9 billion 2026 response plan funded) is constraining WFP and UNICEF's institutional capacity to sustain operations, with the funding per refugee having dropped from $11 to $4 per month since 2022 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>.

## P181490 — Integrated Social Services and Livelihoods Support

This project, targeting 560,000 IDP and host community beneficiaries across Kassala, Northern, and River Nile states, was designed around the premise that its target areas were currently neutral and not strongholds of either warring party — with relative security as a key selection criterion <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. While this geographic positioning provides some insulation from the worst of the fighting in Kordofan and Darfur, the PAD explicitly acknowledged that approximately 30% of Sudan's counties are directly affected by conflict and that escalation into target areas remained a credible risk requiring contingency relocation planning <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. Implementation Status Reports confirm that political and governance instability is now rated high, reflecting the de facto state fragmentation that has rendered federal institutions non-functional and created competing authority structures that undermine project coordination with state and locality-level counterparts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. Institutional capacity is similarly rated high risk, with the displacement of 14 million people having overwhelmed local government structures in displacement-affected areas and the collapse of health and education institutions reflecting systemic fragility across all service delivery sectors <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. A particularly acute realized risk is the emergence of salary arrears for frontline workers — teachers and health workers — which directly undermines the project's service delivery components and contributes to the broader health system collapse, with unpaid health workers unable to sustain nutrition screening and disease prevention services critical to famine response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The project's Community Development Committee (CDC) model, which requires minimum 40% women and 30% IDP representation, faces a structural threat from the arbitrary detention of political activists and retaliatory attacks against local emergency response volunteers documented across Gezira state and Khartoum — creating a climate of fear that deters participation in community governance structures [P181490 | PAD; P181490 | Implementation Status and Results Report]. Social cohesion between IDPs and host communities — a core project outcome — is under severe strain from the combination of resource scarcity, famine conditions, and the return of 2.6 million displaced persons to areas with destroyed infrastructure and absent services, with competition for limited project benefits intensifying tensions rather than alleviating them <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The project's food security component, designed to increase local agricultural production and support community cooperatives, faces the risk that famine conditions could spread to target states during implementation, requiring a pivot to emergency humanitarian response that is beyond the project's scope and for which the broader humanitarian system is catastrophically underfunded <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. Looking forward, the cross-border dynamics in border states such as Kassala — including weapons trafficking and displacement flows — add a layer of security risk that the project design did not fully assess, and the internationalisation of the conflict through cross-border recruitment and proxy involvement increases the unpredictability of the security environment in areas the project assumed would remain relatively stable <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>.

## P504621 — Education Continuity and Learning Support

This education project is confronting the most direct and unambiguous materialization of FCV risks in the Sudan portfolio: schools in target states remain closed due to active conflict, and the project's core delivery mechanism — school grant distribution — cannot function when the physical infrastructure for distribution does not exist or is inaccessible. The PAD anticipated this scenario, identifying conflict-related school closures, limited access, and population mobility as central implementation risks, and designed a learning continuity component to reach out-of-school children with materials in areas where schools cannot reopen [P504621 | PAD]. Implementation Status Reports confirm that school closures due to active conflict are a realized constraint preventing grant distribution, with the project adapting by attempting to deliver learning materials directly to primary school-age children in closed-school areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The Security Management Framework — a prerequisite for implementation in conflict-affected areas — remained pending clearance as of the most recent Aide Memoire, indicating that unresolved security threats in Kordofan and Darfur are preventing the project from finalizing its operational protocols <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Fund utilization stands at only 7.3%, with procurement at 98.3% underspend ($281,000 of $16.8 million signed contracts disbursed as of April 2025), reflecting the near-total operational paralysis caused by active conflict, deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access, and the institutional fragmentation created by competing governance authorities [P504621 | Aide Memoire; P504621 | Implementation Status and Results Report]. Third-party monitoring recruitment has been delayed due to budgetary constraints, creating a verification gap at precisely the moment when independent oversight is most needed to ensure school grants reach intended beneficiaries and are not diverted by conflict actors <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Fiduciary risk is rated high, with the RSF's parallel administration in Darfur and the SAF-aligned government in Khartoum creating dual governance structures that undermine unified financial oversight and accountability chains for school grant implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. Stakeholder engagement is rated high risk, with the suppression of civil society, arbitrary detention of activists, and the fragmentation of community structures across 14 million displaced persons making it impossible to maintain stable relationships with government counterparts, community leaders, or beneficiary groups <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The project's socio-emotional wellness component intersects with the collapsed health system and disease outbreak risk, as overcrowded displacement sites and disrupted WASH services that drive cholera and measles transmission also undermine the safe school environments necessary for learning continuity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The systematic attacks on civilians and the documented pattern of war crimes — including the UN Fact-Finding Mission's identification of genocide hallmarks — create a protection environment in which the project's beneficiaries, particularly girls and adolescents, face risks that the project's GBV and SEA/SH mitigation framework was not designed to address at this scale of impunity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## P507788 — Energy and Digital Infrastructure

This project faces a governance and institutional environment that has deteriorated to the point where the foundational conditions for infrastructure investment are absent across much of Sudan. Implementation Status Reports rate political and governance instability as high, reflecting the de facto state fragmentation in which the RSF's parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" controls most of Darfur and parts of Kordofan while the SAF-aligned government operates from Khartoum — creating competing authorities with no unified national framework capable of establishing coherent energy or digital sector policies <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. Sector policy instability is rated high, with the absence of a functioning peace process and the fragmentation of mediation efforts (the Quad rendered ineffective by Saudi-UAE rivalry, the AU Quintet unable to produce a ceasefire) meaning there is no credible pathway to the governance stabilization required for infrastructure sector strategy to be formulated, communicated, or enforced <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. Fiduciary risk is rated high, with dual governance structures creating competing claims over resources and institutional authority, the suppression of civil society removing independent oversight mechanisms, and the absence of unified monetary policy — a consequence of de facto partition — preventing the macroeconomic stabilization that infrastructure investment requires <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. Stakeholder engagement is rated high risk, with communities in RSF-controlled areas facing the risk of retaliation for perceived collaboration with SAF-aligned government counterparts, while development actors cannot identify legitimate and unified government partners across fragmented territorial control <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties extends to obstruction of institutional functioning more broadly, preventing government agencies from formulating or implementing sector policies and creating an environment where even well-resourced implementing agencies cannot maintain stable field presence or supply chains for infrastructure activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The stalled peace diplomacy and the non-constitution of the UN Panel of Experts supporting the Sudan Sanctions Committee — due to disagreements among Security Council members — signal that the accountability and early-warning mechanisms that might otherwise create pressure for governance stabilization are themselves non-functional, leaving the project's operating environment without any credible near-term trajectory toward the institutional coherence that energy and digital infrastructure investment requires <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>.
