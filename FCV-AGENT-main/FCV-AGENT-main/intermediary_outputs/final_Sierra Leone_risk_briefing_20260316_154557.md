# Sierra Leone FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Inclusive Basic Services Delivery and Local Governance Project (P172492)

Sierra Leone's governance landscape is defined by systemic institutional weakness across fiscal management, public procurement, and rule of law enforcement, as documented in the IMF's 2025–2026 Governance and Corruption Diagnostic. These vulnerabilities are compounded by deep SLPP-APC political polarization rooted in the disputed 2023 elections, ethno-regional fragmentation along Mende-dominated south/east and Temne-dominated north lines, and a pre-2028 electoral reform impasse that is eroding public trust in state institutions. Macro-economic fragility—characterized by high public debt, limited economic diversification, and constrained fiscal space—further limits the government's capacity to fund local service delivery. The dismantlement of USAID and sharp reduction in US foreign assistance since January 2025 has weakened civil society organizations that provide civic education and governance monitoring, while the February 2026 Parliamentary condemnation of the unlawful Netpage passport contract extension signals that procurement governance failures are occurring in real time.

The project is acutely susceptible to these risks. The PAD explicitly acknowledges that a 2019 Audit Service Sierra Leone review identified Le5.3 billion in cash losses across 22 local councils from fuel irregularities, unpaid withholding taxes, undocumented payments, and payroll fraud—vulnerabilities that directly threaten the integrity of the US$17 million local development grants component <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172492 – PAD</a>. The project's reliance on opposition-aligned local councils and a high-level Project Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance requires cross-party cooperation that is structurally threatened by the current political deadlock <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172492 – PAD</a>. Civil society organizations listed as direct beneficiaries and partners in citizen engagement and accountability monitoring face reduced capacity following the loss of US funding <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172492 – PAD</a>. The project's e-Government Procurement implementation and local revenue mobilization reforms assume a baseline of institutional trust and enforcement capacity that the IMF diagnostic has found to be absent <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172492 – PAD</a>.

These risks are materializing across multiple dimensions. Aide Memoire evidence documents active revenue sharing disputes between Chiefdoms and District Councils, reflecting the ethno-regional political fragmentation and patronage dynamics that the country risk identifies as structurally embedded <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – Aide Memoire</a>. Local revenue mobilization is constrained by taxpayer non-compliance and weak enforcement capacity, directly mirroring the IMF's findings of institutional weakness and limited rule of law <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – Aide Memoire</a>. Trust deficits and transparency concerns at the local level—particularly in remote communities where oversight capacity is weakest—are documented as active implementation constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – Aide Memoire</a>. ISR evidence confirms that the project's political and governance risk remains Substantial, driven by the same SLPP-APC confrontation, electoral institution credibility crisis, and procurement governance failures that define the country risk environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-1884d056-da29-49f2-ac93-9d9026ba75ad.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – ISR</a>. The project's institutional capacity for implementation and sustainability is also rated Substantial, reflecting the structural governance deficits that the IMF diagnostic flagged as systemic <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-1884d056-da29-49f2-ac93-9d9026ba75ad.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – ISR</a>. Looking forward, the failure to complete electoral reform before 2028 and the continued erosion of civil society capacity from aid withdrawal represent compounding risks to project sustainability that are unlikely to resolve within the implementation period.

## 2. Integrated and Resilient Urban Mobility Project (P168608)

Sierra Leone's political environment is defined by a deep SLPP-APC confrontation that has produced criminal charges against opposition leadership, a one-day suspension of the APC by the PPRC, and stalled Tripartite Committee dialogue as of March 2026. This polarization is structurally rooted in ethno-regional divisions and is compounded by an electoral institution credibility crisis—the contested appointment of the ECSL Chief Electoral Commissioner and the pending Constitutional Amendment Bill—that is eroding public trust in governance institutions ahead of 2028. Systemic procurement vulnerabilities, documented by the IMF and exemplified by the February 2026 Parliamentary condemnation of the Netpage passport deal, create a high-risk environment for project fiduciary integrity. Macro-economic fragility, with public debt at 73.6% of GDP and a high risk of debt distress, constrains local councils' capacity to sustain project infrastructure post-completion. The dismantlement of USAID has weakened civil society organizations that provide GBV response, third-party monitoring, and community engagement support.

The project is particularly susceptible because five of its seven beneficiary cities are governed by opposition-controlled mayors, including Freetown where the bulk of investments are concentrated <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168608 – PAD</a>. The PAD explicitly identifies this as a Political and Governance risk and relies on engagement with all political stakeholders as mitigation—a strategy that is directly threatened by the current criminalisation of opposition figures and deepening institutional distrust <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168608 – PAD</a>. The project's Fiduciary risk is rated Substantial, with documented weaknesses including limited PFMU staff capacity, lack of procurement record-keeping systems, and insufficient competition in procurement processes <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168608 – PAD</a>. The project's GBV Action Plan and specialized NGO support for SEA/SH response depend on civil society partners whose funding has been directly impacted by USAID withdrawal <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168608 – PAD</a>. The project also covers Port Loko, which is geographically proximate to the Falaba District border area where Guinean forces detained 16 Sierra Leonean soldiers in February 2026, creating a potential security disruption risk for project activities <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168608 – PAD</a>.

Risks are actively materializing. ISR evidence confirms that the project's Political and Governance risk has been escalated, driven by the deepening SLPP-APC confrontation, eroded electoral institution credibility, IMF-documented procurement vulnerabilities, and weakened judicial independence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf" target="_blank">P168608 – ISR</a>. The macroeconomic risk has similarly been escalated from Substantial to High, reflecting the structural economic fragility—high debt, extractive industry dependence, limited diversification—that constrains government fiscal capacity and MDA engagement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf" target="_blank">P168608 – ISR</a>. Limited MDA ownership and engagement is documented as an active implementation constraint, consistent with the governance fragility and institutional capacity deficits identified in the IMF diagnostic <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf" target="_blank">P168608 – ISR</a>. Persistent coordination challenges in emergency preparedness implementation reflect broader institutional fragility in inter-departmental effectiveness <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf" target="_blank">P168608 – ISR</a>. Looking forward, the combination of opposition-controlled city councils, deepening political polarization, and constrained fiscal space for local council recurrent cost financing creates a compounding sustainability risk that will intensify as the 2028 electoral cycle approaches.

## 3. Digital Transformation for Resilience Project (P177077)

Sierra Leone's governance environment is characterized by systemic procurement vulnerabilities, selective anti-corruption enforcement, and weakened institutional accountability—all documented in the IMF's January 2026 Governance and Corruption Diagnostic. The removal of the respected Auditor-General in late 2024 under disputed proceedings has weakened a key accountability anchor. Political polarization between SLPP and APC, including the February 2026 criminal charges against the APC Secretary-General, creates an environment where inter-ministerial coordination and whole-of-government digital transformation face political interference risks. Macro-economic fragility, with structural weaknesses in private sector reform and austerity-oriented budgeting, constrains government capacity to sustain institutional reforms. The dismantlement of USAID has reduced civil society monitoring capacity and development partner coordination that the project depends on for stakeholder engagement and oversight.

The project is susceptible across multiple risk dimensions. The PAD identifies Fiduciary risk as Substantial, noting limited fiduciary capacity in the Ministry of Information and Communications, weak complaint redress systems, and limited knowledge of World Bank procurement regulations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>. The project requires coordination across multiple MDAs through a Project Steering Committee co-chaired by the Ministers of Finance and MIC—a governance structure that is directly threatened by political deadlock and the risk of political interference in PSC and Technical Committee meetings <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>. The project's digital government services components, including e-Parliament and e-Justice blueprints, could become flashpoints if perceived as serving ruling party interests in electoral management, given the current ECSL credibility crisis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>. Youth employment pilots and digital skills training targeting youth aged 15–35 face risk of politicization along ethno-regional lines if beneficiary selection is captured by patronage networks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>. The project's reliance on civil society participation for stakeholder engagement and grievance monitoring is undermined by the loss of USD 1 million in US funding to a Freetown-based democracy advocacy organization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>.

These risks are materializing. ISR evidence confirms that the project has been downgraded to Moderately Satisfactory, with implementation delays attributed to governance fragility, procurement weaknesses, and institutional capacity constraints that directly reflect the IMF diagnostic findings <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177077-b39248ac-6b56-450d-ba7c-dff84b78d19f.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – ISR</a>. Zero progress has been recorded on MDAs utilizing shared digital government services and on government-to-citizen and government-to-business service delivery, indicating that the whole-of-government digital transformation objective is stalled <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177077-b39248ac-6b56-450d-ba7c-dff84b78d19f.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – ISR</a>. The National Computer Emergency Response Team (CIRT) has not been operationalized and spectrum management systems remain non-functional, reflecting the same institutional capacity deficits and structural reform gaps that the World Bank's November 2025 economic update identified as constraining private sector development <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177077-b39248ac-6b56-450d-ba7c-dff84b78d19f.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – ISR</a>. Looking forward, the combination of procurement governance failures, weakened civil society oversight, and the approaching 2028 electoral cycle—which could further politicize digital government initiatives—creates a compounding risk to project outcomes that requires urgent attention.

## 4. Financial Inclusion Development Project (P177947)

Sierra Leone's macro-economic environment presents severe risks to financial sector stability and MSME viability. The IMF's Extended Credit Facility arrangement has anchored some fiscal reforms, but the country exhibits high inflation (37% in January 2023), significant currency depreciation (66% in 2022), a public debt-to-GDP ratio that reached 94%, and banks highly exposed to government debt. These structural vulnerabilities make the country prone to external shocks from commodity price movements and imported inflation. Governance and corruption vulnerabilities—including systemic procurement weaknesses, selective anti-corruption enforcement, and the removal of the Auditor-General—create a high-risk environment for project fiduciary integrity. Ethno-regional polarization and elite capture risks threaten equitable distribution of MSME credit and financial access points. Judicial independence concerns undermine the credibility of regulatory frameworks and contract enforcement mechanisms that underpin financial sector operations.

The project is susceptible to these risks across its core components. The PAD identifies Macroeconomic risk as High, noting that economic shocks directly impair MSME credit performance and reduce project effectiveness <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>. The project's subsidized line of credit (US$10 million) for MSMEs depends on enforceable credit contracts and dispute resolution mechanisms that are undermined by weakened judicial independence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>. The PAD explicitly identifies the risk of elite capture and favoritism in MSME credit selection, which is amplified by the current ethno-regional polarization and patronage dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>. The Bank of Sierra Leone's supervisory capacity for MFIs and NBFIs is rated as Substantial risk, with staff lacking adequate experience in risk management—a gap that is compounded by the loss of USAID-funded technical assistance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>. Rural agricultural MSMEs targeted by the project face chronic food insecurity and repeated climate shocks (2022, 2023, 2024) that directly threaten loan repayment capacity and project sustainability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>.

No ISR or Aide Memoire evidence is available for this project in the current evidence base, limiting the ability to assess whether these risks are actively materializing. However, the forward-looking risk picture is concerning: the combination of ongoing macro-economic fragility, structural reform gaps in the private sector, weakened institutional accountability, and the approaching 2028 electoral cycle—which could further politicize MSME credit allocation—creates a compounding risk environment. The project's reliance on the IMF ECF program as a macro-economic anchor is vulnerable to political disruption if fiscal discipline weakens ahead of elections, and the structural weaknesses in BSL supervisory capacity are unlikely to be resolved within the project's implementation period without sustained technical assistance that the USAID withdrawal has made less available.

## 5. Inclusive Agricultural Value Chain Development Project (P153437)

Sierra Leone's agricultural sector faces compounding risks from chronic food insecurity, climate-driven shocks, macro-economic fragility, and systemic governance vulnerabilities. Over one million Sierra Leoneans are severely food insecure per WFP monitoring data, and repeated climate events in 2022, 2023, and 2024 displaced thousands and damaged crops. Agriculture employs over 60% of the population but operates predominantly at subsistence level, making smallholder farmers highly vulnerable to weather shocks and commodity price movements. The IMF's Governance and Corruption Diagnostic identified systemic procurement weaknesses and limited rule of law enforcement that directly threaten project fiduciary integrity. The February 2026 Parliamentary condemnation of the Netpage passport deal signals that procurement governance failures are occurring across government institutions. Government counterpart funding capacity is constrained by high public debt and limited fiscal space.

The project is susceptible across its major components. The PAD identifies Fiduciary risk as Substantial, noting weak financial management systems, inadequate internal controls, risks of fraud and corruption, and weak procurement capacity at some implementing agencies <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08412c2b7&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P153437 – PAD</a>. The project's complex procurement modalities—including performance-based road contracts (OPRC) for 500–600 km of feeder roads and SLADF fund management—create multiple corruption exposure points <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08412c2b7&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P153437 – PAD</a>. The project's targeting of 50,000 smallholder farmers across all four provinces, including 40% women and youth, is vulnerable to elite capture along ethno-regional and political lines in a context of deep SLPP-APC polarization <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08412c2b7&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P153437 – PAD</a>. The project's sustainability depends on government budget allocation for feeder road maintenance—a commitment that is directly threatened by declining government revenues and macro-economic fragility <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08412c2b7&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P153437 – PAD</a>.

These risks are materializing extensively. Aide Memoire evidence documents a contractor claiming US$3.78 million in delay costs, citing extreme weather conditions and equipment deficiencies, with the contractor unable to maintain adequate equipment on site—reflecting both climate-driven agricultural shocks and macro-economic fragility affecting private sector capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. Government counterpart funding gaps are documented, with US$6.944 million in committed counterpart funds not yet released, directly reflecting fiscal governance weaknesses and constrained fiscal space <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. Unresolved financial accountability issues totaling US$141,000 in grant advances and overpaid contract amounts have persisted since March 2024, directly reflecting the systemic procurement governance failures identified in the IMF diagnostic <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. Pending compensation payments of US$31,661 remain unresolved, creating community grievances that are symptomatic of fiscal governance and payment processing weaknesses <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. A ferry was grounded due to heavy rainfall and rising water levels, isolating communities and forcing reliance on unsafe wooden canoes—a direct manifestation of climate-driven infrastructure disruption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. Access roads to completed bridges have deteriorated due to underinvestment in maintenance, reflecting the structural fiscal constraints and governance gaps that prevent sustained public infrastructure upkeep <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. A contractor's failure to submit required environmental and social safeguard reports and a lost-time injury at a bridge construction site signal weak enforcement of contractual compliance standards <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. A project financing gap of US$10.944 million has emerged, with the government's commitment to bridge this gap remaining unclear—a direct consequence of macro-economic fragility and structural fiscal constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. Looking forward, the combination of climate vulnerability, government counterpart funding uncertainty, procurement governance failures, and the approaching 2028 electoral cycle—which could further politicize agricultural resource allocation—creates a severe compounding risk to project completion and sustainability.

## 6. Electricity Access Expansion Project (P171059)

Sierra Leone's energy sector faces risks from political interference in utility governance, systemic procurement weaknesses, land administration failures, and macro-economic fragility that threatens EDSA's financial viability. The IMF's Governance and Corruption Diagnostic identified institutional weaknesses and limited rule of law enforcement across public sector institutions, including utilities. The February 2026 Parliamentary condemnation of the Netpage passport deal signals persistent procurement governance failures. Climate vulnerability—Sierra Leone has the second most intensive rainfalls in Africa—creates risks of flooding, landslides, and coastal erosion that affect infrastructure construction and operations. The dismantlement of USAID has weakened health and civil society institutions that complement the project's electrification of 200 health facilities and 500 schools.

The project is susceptible to these risks across its major components. The PAD identifies Fiduciary risk as Substantial, noting that EDSA's FM system and capacity are assessed as inadequate and that procurement capacity to implement World Bank regulations is not strong <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08819710d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171059 – PAD</a>. The project's largest single activity—the Waterloo distribution network expansion, representing one-third of project cost—is located in an area informed by the 2017 landslides and floods, creating climate vulnerability for the project's most significant investment <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08819710d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171059 – PAD</a>. The project's reliance on EDSA's financial viability for operations and maintenance sustainability is directly threatened by macro-economic fragility and the government's constrained fiscal space <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08819710d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171059 – PAD</a>. Land acquisition and involuntary resettlement risks are identified, with dispute resolution depending on judicial independence that is under threat from executive interference <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08819710d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171059 – PAD</a>.

These risks are materializing across multiple dimensions. Aide Memoire evidence documents a land dispute at the Newton Solar Park site, where competing land claims emerged after contractor mobilization despite prior government conveyance and stakeholder consultations—reflecting weak institutional capacity to enforce property rights and resolve disputes transparently <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf" target="_blank">P171059 – Aide Memoire</a>. Ministerial-level intervention has been required to resolve the Newton land claims, suggesting institutional fragmentation and weak inter-agency coordination consistent with IMF findings <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf" target="_blank">P171059 – Aide Memoire</a>. ## 13. Private Sector Development and Competitiveness Project (P164212)

Sierra Leone's private sector development faces risks from political polarization that undermines public-private dialogue, systemic governance and corruption vulnerabilities that threaten fiduciary integrity, macro-economic fragility that constrains SME access to finance and investment, ethno-regional polarization that affects equitable benefit distribution, and the dismantlement of USAID that has weakened civil society organizations providing governance monitoring and private sector development support. The IMF's Governance and Corruption Diagnostic identified procurement vulnerabilities and limited rule of law enforcement that directly threaten the project's digitalization of business registration, construction permits, and tourism destination upgrade components. The February 2026 Parliamentary condemnation of the Netpage passport deal exemplifies the procurement governance failures that the PAD's High fiduciary risk rating anticipated.

The project is susceptible across its major components. The PAD identifies Fiduciary risk as High, noting an overall weak fiduciary environment with limited evidence of value for money, economy, efficiency, effectiveness, integrity, transparency, and accountability, and weak data controls in FM systems <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087bc19f1&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164212 – PAD</a>. The project's SME subgrants and tourism destination upgrades involve procurement of IT systems, civil works contracts, and service providers that are vulnerable to the same governance failures documented in the passport deal episode <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087bc19f1&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164212 – PAD</a>. ## 14. Governance Reform and Accountability Project (P503960)

Sierra Leone's governance reform environment is defined by deep institutional fragility, political polarization, and a pattern of executive interference in accountability institutions. The removal of the Auditor-General in late 2024 under disputed proceedings weakened a key accountability anchor. The IMF's Governance and Corruption Diagnostic identified systemic vulnerabilities in fiscal governance, public procurement, and rule of law enforcement. The contested appointment of the ECSL Chief Electoral Commissioner and the stalled Constitutional Amendment Bill signal institutional reform paralysis ahead of the 2028 elections. The February 2026 criminal charges against the APC Secretary-General and the PPRC's politically-motivated suspension of the APC exemplify the criminalisation of opposition activity that undermines the political consensus required for governance reform.

The project is susceptible to these risks given its core focus on institutional strengthening and governance reform. Projects of this type depend on sustained political will, cross-party cooperation, and the credibility of accountability institutions—all of which are directly threatened by the current political environment. The pending Audit Service Sierra Leone Regulation requiring presidential assent faces risk of delay or conditionality if the government perceives it as constraining executive discretion, consistent with the broader pattern of executive interference in accountability institutions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P503960-34c13115-e0c3-4d57-9d40-6ef9de132fd6.pdf" target="_blank">P503960 – ISR</a>. Legislative reforms pending presidential assent face politicization risk in a context where the government has demonstrated willingness to use institutional processes as political tools <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P503960-34c13115-e0c3-4d57-9d40-6ef9de132fd6.pdf" target="_blank">P503960 – ISR</a>.

These risks are materializing. ISR evidence confirms that the project's Political and Governance risk is rated High, driven by the convergence of SLPP-APC political deadlock, eroded electoral institution credibility, IMF-documented governance vulnerabilities, weakened judicial independence, ethno-regional polarization, and procurement governance failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P503960-34c13115-e0c3-4d57-9d40-6ef9de132fd6.pdf" target="_blank">P503960 – ISR</a>. The pending Audit Service Sierra Leone Regulation has not received presidential assent, with delays potentially reflecting the same pattern of executive resistance to accountability-strengthening measures that characterized the removal of the Auditor-General <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P503960-34c13115-e0c3-4d57-9d40-6ef9de132fd6.pdf" target="_blank">P503960 – ISR</a>. Multiple legislative reforms remain pending presidential assent, with the broader legislative reform impasse paralleling the stalled Constitutional Amendment Bill and signaling a pattern of institutional reform stalling <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P503960-34c13115-e0c3-4d57-9d40-6ef9de132fd6.pdf" target="_blank">P503960 – ISR</a>. Looking forward, the combination of executive resistance to accountability-strengthening legislation, deepening political polarization, eroded institutional credibility, and the approaching 2028 electoral cycle—which will intensify political pressures on governance institutions—creates a severe compounding risk to project outcomes. The project's success depends on precisely the institutional reforms and political consensus that the current environment is most actively undermining.
