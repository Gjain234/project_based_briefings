**Governance Consolidation and Institutional Fragility**

Djibouti's most pervasive country-level risk is the progressive concentration of executive power and the erosion of institutional checks. The October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—signals a governance trajectory toward indefinite rule, compounded by the near-total collapse of independent civil society and severe restrictions on media freedom. These dynamics create a permissive environment for institutional dysfunction across all sectors. Projects across the portfolio are structurally exposed: the TVET and skills project relies on a multi-ministry steering committee under the Economic Development Council, but the PAD's mitigation strategy—a "high-level steering committee"—provides no safeguards against executive capture or political interference in beneficiary selection <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The public finance management reform project depends on a PFM Reform Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance, with mitigation resting on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that becomes fragile under conditions of constitutional manipulation and succession uncertainty <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The corridor infrastructure project similarly identifies that "ability to influence private sector finance mobilization depends on political will," with mitigation requiring "commitment at highest state level" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing concretely: the PFM project cannot achieve effectiveness because the government has not published required governance decrees in the Official Gazette despite Council of Ministers approval in February 2025, with delays affecting all donor-funded PFM initiatives in Djibouti <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. Political and governance risk ratings have escalated from Moderate to Substantial on the health project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a> and remain persistently High on the PFM project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a> and Substantial across the corridor and social cohesion portfolios [P174300 | Implementation Status and Results Report; P506969 | Implementation Status and Results Report]. The TVET project's steering committee remains inactive, with the Bank in discussions on alternative governance arrangements <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>, while the EFTP governance modernization framework has been formally signed but remains operationally ineffective <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The internal audit function for the TVET project is structurally compromised, with the auditor reporting to the project coordinator whose processes they are mandated to review <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. Forward-looking, the April 2026 presidential election cycle and succession uncertainty—including recurring health concerns about President Guelleh—introduce additional unpredictability into ministerial continuity and government prioritization of development commitments across a portfolio that depends heavily on sustained political engagement.

**Severe Youth Unemployment, Economic Inequality, and Structural Labor Market Failure**

Djibouti's youth unemployment rate exceeding 75%, combined with endemic corruption, high public debt, and limited economic diversification, creates a structural economic environment that fundamentally constrains the absorptive capacity for project-generated employment and livelihood outcomes. Multiple projects are designed to address symptoms of this crisis without adequate analysis of the underlying demand-side constraints. The TVET project targets 23,775 trained graduates across diploma, short-term, and work-based learning pathways, but the PAD acknowledges "macroeconomic uncertainties" without providing a labor market demand assessment, private sector capacity analysis, or contingency for limited job creation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food project similarly targets youth as direct beneficiaries but does not specify youth employment pathways or income-generation mechanisms within livelihood restoration activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The corridor project lists "road transport service providers and local traders" as beneficiaries but provides no youth employment targets or analysis of how corridor improvements translate to jobs for the unemployed youth population <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio: zero bilateral labor agreements have been signed against a target of six, and zero short-term migrant workers have been deployed against a target of 250 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The online gig work component targeting 500 individuals is being deleted during restructuring due to "significant delays" and "technical constraints and coordination with implementing partners" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. Remote rural areas face persistent difficulty identifying viable income-generating activities due to weak market access and limited economic diversification, directly undermining productive inclusion activities [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire]. The food security project's disbursement rate stands at only 15.2% one year before project closure, with the mid-term review postponed due to lack of results <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. Youth training programs in interior regions carry documented risks of unequal opportunity and inequitable treatment, with the Bank recommending proactive risk management to ensure fair selection [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire]. The June 2025 drought declaration—triggering project suspension and restructuring of the food security project—compounds economic vulnerability by diverting resources from preparedness to emergency response and disrupting agricultural livelihood restoration activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>. Structurally, the portfolio faces a fundamental tension: supply-side training interventions cannot overcome demand-side constraints in an economy where formal sector employment creation is severely limited by debt, corruption, and limited diversification.

**Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Under Compounding Resource Constraints**

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers—with 85% concentrated in refugee villages, primarily from Somalia, Ethiopia, and Yemen. Despite maintaining an open-door policy, the country faces recurrent droughts, limited fiscal resources, and fragile public service infrastructure, creating systemic strain on service delivery to both refugee and host populations. This risk is the most directly realized across the portfolio. The TVET project targets 3,165 refugees as direct beneficiaries, but zero refugees have been enrolled in TVET programs, zero have completed training, and zero have been engaged in apprenticeships or work-based learning against a target of 180 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. Refugee enrollment data remains unavailable and is described as pending for the next ISR <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The education project shows declining refugee enrollment—from 596 in 2021-2022 to 384 in 2024-2025 (pending verification)—against a target of 37,000 out-of-school children, with refugee schools not yet integrated into the national EMIS system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The urban development project has delivered zero property access certificates to refugees, zero refugee-led CSO sub-projects, and zero implemented recommendations from sectoral action plans for operationalizing the 2017 National Refugee Law <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The refugee integration and social cohesion assessment remains incomplete <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The food security project explicitly targets refugees for emergency food stock distribution but lacks documented allocation protocols between refugee villages and host communities, and the June 2025 drought has triggered restructuring that reallocates SDR 3,925,000 from preparedness to emergency response, reducing long-term resilience <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>. The CERP emergency response project identifies SEGRC as having "limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff" precisely when it must coordinate emergency response across refugee-hosting areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The statistics project required HCR support to facilitate field access to refugee populations for survey implementation, with the migrant and refugee survey among concurrent activities straining INSTAD's institutional capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>. Forward-looking, the escalating Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis—including ambassador recalls and Djibouti's military alignment with Mogadishu—risks triggering new displacement waves from Somalia that would further strain hosting capacity precisely when the portfolio's refugee-serving components are already underperforming.

**Security Threats from Domestic Insurgency and Regional Instability**

Djibouti faces active domestic security threats from FRUD-armé, which conducted a raid on an army post in October 2023 killing seven soldiers, alongside proximity to al-Shabaab and other terrorist organizations in Somalia. The country's strategic position—hosting eight foreign military bases in a hyper-congested garrison environment—creates additional friction and deconfliction challenges that complicate the operating environment for development projects. Multiple projects operate in geographic areas of elevated security risk without adequate threat-specific mitigation. The TVET project operates in Ali Sabieh and Obock regions, where 3,165 refugee beneficiaries are concentrated in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps, but the PAD rates security risk as only "moderate" and limits mitigation to "institutional protocols and safe learning environments" without addressing FRUD-armé specifically <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food project operates in the Northern Zone (Tadjourah and Obock) and includes climate-proof rural infrastructure, but the PAD asserts "no security risks identified for project implementation" despite FRUD-armé's demonstrated capacity to conduct armed raids in proximate areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The corridor project identifies "security/fragility (substantial)" risk from regional instability but relies only on "regular security risk assessments" without specific protocols for FRUD-armé threats to road rehabilitation sites or border operations at Guelileh <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The statistics project explicitly acknowledges "security risks from regional instability" and "road safety concerns during census activities" for field teams conducting the Population Census and household surveys across national territory <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing in implementation: the corridor project has recorded thirteen traffic incidents on the corridor since project start, with the Bank emphasizing the importance of road safety standards <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. Critical safety violations were identified during April 2025 site visits—absence of dedicated HSE agents, unsecured trenches, inadequate separation between machinery and vulnerable road users—with government quarterly reports failing to document non-conformities observed in the field <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. One hundred fifty daily laborers on the corridor project lack proper employment contracts and social security registration, with significant inconsistencies in accident records <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The escalating Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis adds a regional dimension: the corridor project's cross-border operations at Guelileh depend on Ethiopia collaboration "conditional on mutual understanding between customs authorities," but the PAD does not assess how Djibouti's military alignment with Mogadishu could destabilize this bilateral arrangement <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. Forward-looking, the combination of active domestic insurgency, regional proxy dynamics from eight foreign military presences, and the Djibouti-Somaliland crisis creates a compounding security environment that the portfolio's risk assessments have systematically underweighted.
