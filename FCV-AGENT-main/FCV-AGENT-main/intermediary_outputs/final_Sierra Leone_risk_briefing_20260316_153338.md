# Sierra Leone FCV Portfolio Briefing

## Paragraph 1: Governance and Corruption Vulnerabilities Across the Portfolio

Sierra Leone's systemic governance and corruption weaknesses—documented in the IMF's 2025–2026 Governance and Corruption Diagnostic, which identified institutional fragility, procurement vulnerabilities, selective anti-corruption enforcement, and the removal of the Auditor-General—represent the most pervasive FCV risk across the portfolio. Every project faces exposure: the WASH project acknowledges that "GoSL advancing PFM reforms but faces challenges including budget deviations, procedural overrides, inefficient investment management, and unplanned political spending; limited independence of auditor general and Audit Service Sierra Leone from executive branch poses high FM risk" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>; the digital transformation project rates fiduciary risk as Substantial, citing "limited fiduciary capacity in MIC" and a "weak complaint redress system" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>; and the education project rates procurement risk as High, noting "weak capacity, delays in approvals, limited knowledge of World Bank procedures, insufficient competition" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087a46502&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – PAD</a>. The February 2026 Parliamentary Public Accounts Committee condemnation of the unlawful Netpage passport contract extension confirms that these vulnerabilities are not merely latent—they are actively materializing. Aide Memoire evidence from the agriculture project documents unresolved fiduciary issues including US$141,000 in outstanding grant advances and contract overpayments persisting since March 2024 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>, while the local governance project's Aide Memoire records taxpayer non-compliance and low enforcement capacity at district council level <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – Aide Memoire</a>. ISR evidence from the digital transformation project shows implementation downgraded to Moderately Satisfactory, with zero progress on MDAs utilizing shared digital services <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177077-b39248ac-6b56-450d-ba7c-dff84b78d19f.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – ISR</a>, and the health project's ISR documents a non-functional grievance mechanism and manual documentation without reporting <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. Across the portfolio, governance fragility is both a forward-looking structural constraint and a realized implementation drag.

## Paragraph 2: SLPP-APC Political Deadlock and Institutional Instability

Sierra Leone's deep political confrontation between the ruling SLPP and opposition APC—rooted in the disputed 2023 elections, the February 2026 criminal remand of APC Secretary-General Lansana Dumbuya on incitement charges, the PPRC's politically motivated one-day suspension of the APC, and the stalled Tripartite Committee dialogue—creates a pervasive governance instability risk for projects dependent on multi-party institutional coordination. The WASH project was "prepared in close collaboration with all IAs including opposition-led FCC and WARDC," and its Project Steering Committee requires high-level representatives from multiple ministries meeting at least bi-annually <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>; political deadlock directly threatens this coordination architecture. The urban resilience project explicitly flags "upcoming elections with mayors in five of seven beneficiary cities from opposition party, including Freetown where bulk of investments focused" as a Substantial political risk <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168608 – PAD</a>, while the roads and agriculture project identifies "political interference in project implementation and fund management" as a governance risk <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08412c2b7&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P153437 – PAD</a>. The education project PAD identifies "high level of politicization in education system management at all levels" as a Substantial risk <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087a46502&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – PAD</a>. These susceptibilities are materializing: the education project's ISR documents that political and governance risk has escalated, with the broader political confrontation—including criminal charges against opposition leadership—directly undermining institutional stability <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087b90655&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – ISR</a>. The health project's ISR records an escalation from Moderate to Substantial political risk, explicitly connected to the deepening SLPP-APC confrontation and the February 2026 arrest of opposition figures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. The urban resilience project's ISR similarly records an escalation of political and governance risk, with the Parliamentary condemnation of procurement violations and deepening polarization cited as contributing factors <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf" target="_blank">P168608 – ISR</a>. Pending legislative reforms—including the Audit Service Sierra Leone Regulation awaiting presidential assent—face additional risk of delay in this environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P503960-34c13115-e0c3-4d57-9d40-6ef9de132fd6.pdf" target="_blank">P503960 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 3: Chronic Food Insecurity, Climate Shocks, and Macro-Economic Fragility

Sierra Leone's structural economic fragility—characterized by high public debt (public debt-to-GDP ratio reaching 94% at appraisal for the financial inclusion project), heavy dependence on extractive industries, limited diversification, and exposure to commodity price shocks—combines with chronic food insecurity affecting over one million Sierra Leoneans and repeated climate-driven agricultural shocks (2022, 2023, 2024) to create compounding implementation and sustainability risks across the portfolio. The financial inclusion project rates macroeconomic risk as High, noting "high inflation (37% in January 2023), currency depreciation (66% in 2022), fiscal deficit nearly 10% of GDP," and warns that these conditions impair MSME loan repayment capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>. The social protection project identifies "inflation reducing real value of transfers" and "tight fiscal situation limiting government budget allocation to social protection" as Substantial macroeconomic risks <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088cebb9f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176789 – PAD</a>. The local governance project explicitly warns that "risk that despite investment in better systems, LCs are not provided meaningful additional funding to deliver basic services role, undermining systems investments" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172492 – PAD</a>. Climate risks are materializing concretely: the agriculture project's Aide Memoire documents a contractor citing "extreme weather conditions" as a cause of implementation delays, a ferry grounded by heavy rainfall and rising water levels isolating communities from market access, and the collapse of a diversion route <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. The energy project's Aide Memoire records multiple land acquisition disputes, delayed Resettlement Action Plans, and EDSA recruitment failures for critical management positions—all consistent with the structural capacity constraints the macro-economic fragility risk predicts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf" target="_blank">P171059 – Aide Memoire</a>. Government counterpart funding gaps are a cross-cutting materialized risk: the agriculture project documents a US$6.944 million counterpart funding shortfall and a US$10.944 million financing gap <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>, while the energy project records delayed access road preparation and inter-agency coordination failures with the Sierra Leone Roads Authority <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf" target="_blank">P171059 – Aide Memoire</a>. The local governance project's ISR confirms that Substantial macroeconomic risk persists, with government fiscal constraints limiting meaningful additional funding to local councils <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-1884d056-da29-49f2-ac93-9d9026ba75ad.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 4: USAID Dismantlement, Aid Withdrawal, and Civil Society Capacity Erosion

The dismantlement of USAID and sharp reduction in US foreign assistance since January 2025—which has already caused a Freetown-based democracy advocacy organization to lose US$1 million in civic education funding, left children arriving at hospitals with untreated severe malaria due to absent diagnostic tests, and reduced Sierra Leone's health budget (approximately half-funded by donors) by a significant margin—creates a cross-cutting risk to projects dependent on civil society participation, complementary donor coordination, and health system functionality. The WASH project relies on a "donor coordination platform to be established and maintained" and community engagement through the Wan Fambul Framework, while also monitoring waterborne disease outcomes that depend on a functioning health system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>. The digital transformation project depends on "close collaboration with UN Foundation DIAL, UNICEF, Gates Foundation" and civil society participation in its Stakeholder Engagement Plan <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>. The land administration project identifies "CSO involvement in design and implementation" and "public sensitization campaigns" as key mitigation strategies for conflict and governance risks, with civil society representatives embedded in the Project Technical Committee <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088db0cd6&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P177031 – PAD</a>. The education project targets vulnerable girls facing GBV and dropout risks, with a GBV Action Plan dependent on civil society service providers <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087a46502&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – PAD</a>. These susceptibilities are materializing: the education project's Aide Memoire documents an NGO consortium withdrawal—potentially connected to US aid cuts—that has shifted responsibility for 120,000 out-of-school children to an under-resourced Ministry of Basic and Senior Secondary Education <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167897-f4984073-1dc6-4a44-a2fa-8b6ab0ccc16b.pdf" target="_blank">P167897 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the ISR confirms that fund unavailability has delayed back-to-school incentives and accelerated curriculum delivery <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087b90655&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – ISR</a>. The health project's ISR records that institutional capacity constraints at the Ministry of Health are directly exacerbated by the loss of donor-funded technical assistance, with the health budget's donor dependency creating a compounding financing crisis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. The health project's Aide Memoire documents SEA/SH coordination delays that may reflect reduced donor-funded technical support capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf" target="_blank">P171059 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the urban resilience project's Aide Memoire records an inability to formalize a GBV service provider relationship with RAINBO Initiatives due to cost disagreements plausibly linked to the NGO's own funding pressures from aid withdrawal <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P164212-dcf52c93-27c3-41b0-b33d-28e42d82cff8.pdf" target="_blank">P164212 – Aide Memoire</a>.
