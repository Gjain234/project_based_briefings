# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Governance Consolidation and Democratic Backsliding

Djibouti's October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms. This political consolidation creates direct risks for projects that depend on multi-ministerial coordination, sustained government commitment, and institutional autonomy. The TVET and skills development project is exposed because its governance architecture relies on a high-level steering committee under the Economic Development Council, an executive body, without independent oversight or civil society safeguards to insulate implementation from political capture <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The public finance management reform project faces analogous vulnerability: its PFM Reform Steering Committee is chaired by the Minister of Finance, and the PAD explicitly identifies vested interests within the public administration as a high political risk, with mitigation resting on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that weakens as executive power consolidates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project similarly flags that its ability to mobilize private sector finance depends on political will, and that line ministries may lack incentives or resist change—vulnerabilities that intensify under indefinite presidential rule <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. In implementation, these risks are materializing: the PFM project is experiencing a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with administrative delays in publishing governance decrees blocking project operationalization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The digital economy project has seen ARMD board appointments stalled since 2022, with the regulatory authority unable to adopt the Digital Code Book Two—a pattern consistent with institutional subordination to executive authority <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-c15be353-62d2-4f55-928e-a77385dc1393.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's steering committee has been inactive, with governance coordination between government and development partners (World Bank, USAID) remaining unresolved <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the concentration of executive power is translating into institutional stagnation, delayed reform implementation, and weakened project governance structures that lack the independence to function effectively.

## 2. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Media Freedom

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House—with journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested, and virtually no independent civil society remaining—creates a structural accountability deficit that pervades the entire portfolio. Projects relying on stakeholder engagement, grievance redress mechanisms, community participation, and independent monitoring are systematically exposed. The education project's grievance mechanism and stakeholder engagement strategy are undermined because vulnerable groups—refugees, rural populations, girls—face barriers to voicing concerns in a restricted political environment <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. The PFM reform project's Component 3 explicitly targets civil society engagement for public finance oversight, yet the PAD does not acknowledge that the country context makes meaningful independent participation structurally impossible <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The corridor project's stakeholder engagement plan and grievance redress mechanisms are similarly compromised, with no acknowledgment that communities along the corridor cannot safely voice concerns without risk of government retaliation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. In implementation, these risks are manifesting across multiple dimensions. The TVET project shows zero refugee enrollment and completion data, absent disability tracking, and a stalled online gig work component—all reflecting weak institutional accountability in the absence of independent monitoring <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The corridor project has documented systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards affecting 150 daily workers, with no independent labor advocates or media scrutiny to expose violations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban upgrading project's refugee-led CSO sub-projects have achieved zero implementation, consistent with the hostile environment for autonomous civil society organization <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The GBV prevention project launched without accounting systems in place, with delayed financial reporting and stalled NGO contract negotiations—all reflecting the institutional fragility that pervades an environment without independent oversight <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. The digital economy project's grievance mechanism and environmental and social safeguards reporting remain non-functional, with the absence of civil society oversight enabling persistent non-compliance without external corrective pressure <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-9e33bd55-6107-428e-bde8-2dc02b8b55cf.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the collapse of independent civil society is not merely a contextual backdrop—it is actively degrading the accountability infrastructure on which project implementation depends.

## 3. Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Amid Severe Resource Constraints

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—23,000 refugees and 8,000 asylum-seekers, with 85% concentrated in refugee villages—alongside 150,000 non-registered migrants, placing acute strain on already fragile public services, infrastructure, and institutional capacity. This hosting burden intersects with virtually every project in the portfolio. The emergency contingency response project is directly exposed because it relies on SEGRC and MEFI—both identified as having limited capacity due to lack of funding and staff—to coordinate emergency response for a population that includes these displaced persons <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The TVET project targets 3,165 refugees as direct beneficiaries in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps, but the PAD does not address how resource constraints or humanitarian access risks would affect training delivery or beneficiary retention <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The food security project explicitly targets refugees for emergency food stock distribution but provides no allocation protocols between refugee villages and host communities, nor contingency plans for managing competing demands under resource scarcity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. In implementation, these risks are among the most concretely realized in the portfolio. The health project shows refugee immunization rates stuck at 50% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5%—direct manifestations of the resource constraints and service delivery fragility that characterize the hosting environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The TVET project has failed to enroll any refugees in training programs, with zero employment outcomes for refugee graduates, reflecting the structural inability of the host country system to absorb refugee labor even with targeted interventions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The education project shows declining refugee student enrollment—from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025—with refugee schools not yet integrated into the national EMIS system, demonstrating how resource-constrained institutions struggle to extend service delivery to displaced populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The urban upgrading project has stalled on refugee village restructuring plans for Ali Addeh and Holl Holl, with property rights documentation delayed and vocational training certificates undelivered to refugee beneficiaries <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The food security project has achieved only 50% of its emergency food stock target and zero water infrastructure rehabilitation, directly reducing drought response capacity for the 32,000 displaced persons the country hosts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-79f711cb-4676-4798-95e4-844f5162676d.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The scale and consistency of these failures across the portfolio indicate that Djibouti's refugee hosting burden has moved well beyond a contextual risk factor into an active constraint on development project delivery.

## 4. Severe Youth Unemployment, Economic Inequality, and Institutional Fragility

Youth unemployment exceeding 75%, endemic corruption, high public debt, and structural economic inequality create a fragile economic environment that constrains both the demand side and the institutional supply side of development project implementation. Projects targeting employment outcomes face a labor market that cannot absorb trained graduates, while projects relying on government institutional capacity face agencies stretched thin by fiscal constraints and limited human capital. The TVET project's employment outcomes depend on private sector absorption of nearly 24,000 trained graduates, but the PAD contains no labor market demand assessment or contingency for limited job creation in a context of 75% youth unemployment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food project's economic opportunity component faces structural constraints because youth unemployment and inequality dynamics limit demand for income-generating activities in remote areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The corridor project identifies job losses from decreased goods volume through Djibouti ports as a macroeconomic risk, suggesting that corridor improvements may be offset by broader economic contraction rather than generating the employment benefits anticipated <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. In implementation, these structural constraints are materializing with particular severity. The social protection project has been unable to identify viable income-generating activities in remote localities due to weak market access and limited economic diversification—a localized manifestation of the economy-wide inability to create sustainable employment pathways <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's refugee employment outcomes remain at 5% against targets, with the online gig work component stalled due to limited digital infrastructure and low internet penetration—reflecting the project's underestimation of structural economic barriers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The corridor project has documented exploitation of 150 daily workers through non-compliant contracts and lack of social security registration, directly enabled by the severe youth unemployment context in which workers with no alternatives accept exploitative informal arrangements <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The statistics project faces staffing constraints in macroeconomic planning capacity, with limited institutional capacity for debt strategy formulation—undermining the government's ability to design evidence-based economic policies to address the unemployment crisis <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0884c1bc2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>. The GBV prevention project has experienced financial management deterioration, with missing accounting software and delayed reporting, reflecting the broader human capital deficit and institutional capacity constraints that characterize an economy with endemic corruption and limited skilled labor <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-285d7898-9c2d-4514-b7ba-c25e1c82a9dd.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. The convergence of demand-side labor market failure and supply-side institutional fragility means that economic inequality is simultaneously undermining project outcomes and the institutional capacity needed to deliver them.

## 5. Active Security Threats and Regional Diplomatic Instability

Djibouti faces a compounding security environment: the FRUD-armé insurgent group—classified as a terrorist organization by the National Assembly—conducted a raid killing seven soldiers in October 2023, while the country's escalating diplomatic crisis with Somaliland, marked by ambassador recalls and alleged logistical support for anti-Somaliland militias, creates regional instability with direct operational implications for the portfolio. Projects operating in remote regions, refugee-hosting areas, and cross-border corridors are particularly exposed. The TVET project operates in Ali Sabieh and Obock—regions geographically proximate to FRUD-armé activity—with 3,165 refugee beneficiaries concentrated in camps where the PAD identifies security risk as only "moderate" without specific threat assessment for insurgent activity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The corridor project's infrastructure along RN1, RN5, and RN19 and the One-Stop Border Post at Guelileh faces acknowledged "security/fragility (substantial)" risk from regional instability, but mitigation relies only on "regular security risk assessments" without specific protocols for FRUD-armé threats to road rehabilitation sites or border operations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. The emergency contingency response project notes that FRUD-armé's demonstrated capacity to conduct armed raids poses direct risk to implementation teams and supply chains for distributing water, food, and medicines across rural areas nationwide <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The urban upgrading project's refugee component is exposed to the Djibouti-Somaliland crisis because the project targets Somali refugees who could face increased restrictions or secondary displacement if regional tensions escalate into broader conflict <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a>. In implementation, security and diplomatic risks are beginning to intersect with project delivery. The corridor project's ISR documents a persistent "Substantial" political and governance risk rating, with the Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis explicitly identified as directly threatening cross-border coordination capacity essential for corridor functionality—deteriorating regional relations reduce political will for the bilateral customs cooperation on which the Guelileh border post depends <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's labor migration component—targeting bilateral agreements with Kuwait and Qatar—faces headwinds from Djibouti's diplomatic positioning, as regional isolation and strained international alignments may reduce the country's negotiating leverage with Gulf labor market partners <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The statistics project's field enumeration teams face documented security concerns in refugee settlements, with the survey implementation risk explicitly identifying security in settlements as a potential barrier to census and household survey operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-8dfe1d10-65dd-4d7a-96a0-701ceb6e77ed.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – Aide Memoire</a>. The combination of active domestic insurgency, escalating regional diplomatic crisis, and the country's transformation into a congested proxy theatre for eight foreign military powers creates a security environment that is progressively constraining the operational space available for development project implementation across the portfolio.
