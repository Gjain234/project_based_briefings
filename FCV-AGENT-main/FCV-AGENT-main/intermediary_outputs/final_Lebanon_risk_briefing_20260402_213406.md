Lebanon's portfolio of World Bank projects faces five distinct and compounding country-level risks that are simultaneously shaping project design vulnerabilities and driving realized implementation failures across the active lending program.

**Renewed Full-Scale Hostilities and Active Military Operations**

The collapse of the November 2024 ceasefire on 2 March 2026 and the resumption of large-scale Israel-Hezbollah conflict represents the most acute and immediate threat to the entire Lebanon portfolio. By late March 2026, over 1,238 people had been killed, Israeli ground operations had expanded beyond previously held positions, and displacement orders covered approximately 14 percent of Lebanese territory—including southern Lebanon, the Bekaa Valley, and Beirut's southern suburbs. Every project in the portfolio was designed with some acknowledgment of geopolitical risk, but none anticipated active warfare of this scale. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project (LEAP) <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> explicitly identifies geopolitical risks from Middle East conflict as a threat to implementation in border areas, with project locations in southern Lebanon and the Bekaa Valley directly within active conflict zones; the PAD's mitigation strategy—requiring Lebanese Army clearance for explosive remnants of war before investment—has become operationally moot under active hostilities. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a> had already suspended all works as of November 2024 due to conflict escalation, with the PAD stating that "no works will commence until conflict has ended and sites are safe"; the March 2026 escalation has indefinitely extended this suspension. The Integrated Solid Waste Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> has project sites explicitly in Tyre, Abbasiyeh, Sahel el Zahrani, and Nabatieh—all within active conflict zones—making field activities for waste characterization, dumpsite rehabilitation, and facility upgrades physically impossible. Implementation evidence confirms these risks are fully materializing: the Beqaa Valley sewage network contractor suspended all works due to military escalation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>; LEAP project teams have been forced to conduct virtual missions and are prohibited from working near active shelling, with UXO clearance required before any resumption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>; and the Integrated Solid Waste Management Project has placed Component 2 activities on hold with implementation jeopardy flagged <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a>. The UN Special Coordinator's March 2026 assessment that reconstruction progress "has come to a screeching halt" captures the portfolio-wide impact.

**Mass Internal Displacement and Collapse of Beneficiary Targeting**

Since 2 March 2026, over 1.2 million people have been displaced—including more than 300,000 children—with 94 percent of collective shelters at full capacity and hundreds of thousands sheltering in vehicles or with host communities. This displacement crisis, the largest in Lebanon in more than two decades, directly undermines the beneficiary identification, targeting, and service delivery mechanisms of virtually every project in the portfolio. The LEAP <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> was designed around an area-based approach prioritizing districts by damage intensity and economic potential, with Component 1 including decommissioning of IDP centers; the March 2026 escalation has created 1.2 million newly displaced persons whose movement patterns bear no relationship to the project's baseline targeting criteria. The Lebanon Health Resilience Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a> targets approximately 4.7 million people including 1.5 million Syrian and Palestinian refugees, relying on mobile vaccination units and large-scale vaccination marathons; with 99 percent of newly displaced persons being Syrian refugees and displacement orders covering project implementation areas, beneficiary registries are obsolete. The Social Protection Enhancement Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a> explicitly targets forcibly displaced persons across all workstreams, with 86 percent of recorded GBV incidents in the first half of 2023 involving displaced Syrians and 35 percent of mental health hotline callers being refugees; secondary displacement of over 90,000 refugees within Lebanon and cross-border flight of 180,000 Syrians has fragmented the beneficiary base beyond the project's case management capacity. Implementation evidence confirms the scale of disruption: the Social Protection Enhancement Project ISR documents that NGO partner sustainability is threatened by the macroeconomic and displacement environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a>; the Lebanon Health Resilience Project ISR shows persistent gaps in hospital equipment availability and refugee health service delivery <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a>; and the Integrated Solid Waste Management Project aide memoire documents that security constraints in Nabatieh and Tyre have forced a shift to virtual data collection, with in-person activities suspended <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Healthcare System Near-Collapse Under Systematic Attack**

Since 2 March 2026, WHO has documented at least 75 attacks on healthcare infrastructure in Lebanon, resulting in 51 deaths and 126 injuries among on-duty healthcare personnel, with five hospitals and 50 primary healthcare centers closed and nine hospitals damaged. This systematic destruction of health infrastructure creates cascading implementation risks across the portfolio, both for projects directly targeting health services and for those relying on functioning health systems for worker safety, referral pathways, and social service delivery. The Lebanon Health Resilience Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a> depends on operational primary healthcare centers and mobile medical units for vaccination and case management; with 51 PHCCs now closed, the project's core delivery mechanism has collapsed in conflict-affected areas. The LEAP <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> includes rapid recovery of health lifeline services through mobile clinics and equipment replacement as a Component 2 priority; the documented attacks on 64 healthcare facilities and mass displacement of health workers make rapid recovery operationally impossible. The Social Protection Enhancement Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a> sustains a national mental health hotline and delivers specialized health services through PHCCs and mobile medical units, with 51 percent of surveyed refugees accessing healthcare via PHCCs; the closure of these facilities in conflict zones has severed the project's primary service delivery channel. Implementation evidence shows these risks are materializing: the Lebanon Health Resilience Project ISR documents persistent failure to reach the 70 percent hospital equipment target, with only 56 percent achieved, and zero COVID-19 rapid response teams operational against a target of three <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a>; the Social Protection Enhancement Project ISR flags ongoing security and access challenges to reaching GBV survivors and mental health beneficiaries in conflict-affected areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a>; and the Integrated Solid Waste Management Project aide memoire documents occupational health and safety violations at the Karantina Sorting Facility, including unreported incidents and inadequate PPE, in a context where emergency medical response capacity has collapsed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**State Sovereignty Deficit and Institutional Paralysis**

The Lebanese government's unprecedented March 2026 decree banning Hezbollah's military activities and ordering surrender of weapons has not been enforced, with the Lebanese Armed Forces commander objecting to direct confrontation. This gap between political declarations and implementation on the ground reflects a structural sovereignty deficit rooted in Lebanon's confessional governance system that directly undermines every project's reliance on state institutional capacity. The LEAP <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> identifies institutional capacity as a high risk, noting that the Council for Development and Reconstruction has limited capacity for a project of this scale, with procurement approvals taking nine months and bid evaluation two to eight months; the state's demonstrated inability to enforce its own decrees signals that CDR reform measures and the autonomous PMU structure may be insufficient to overcome structural governance constraints. The Public Financial Management and Revenue Mobilization Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-5067fa60-78e4-4f5e-b6c1-5a428d852b01.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – PAD</a> aims to restore basic functions of domestic revenue mobilization and accountable allocation of public resources, explicitly seeking to renew the social contract between state and citizens; the state's inability to enforce monopoly on force directly contradicts the project's core objective of restoring state fiscal authority. The Integrated Solid Waste Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> requires a Project Steering Committee including seven ministries and coordination with local authorities and municipalities; with state authority absent or contested in southern Lebanon and the Bekaa Valley, these coordination mechanisms cannot function effectively. Implementation evidence confirms the depth of institutional dysfunction: the Public Financial Management Project ISR documents that parliamentary ratification of the IBRD financing agreement remains pending, with the project not yet effective despite approval <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a>; the LEAP ISR flags persistent high political and governance risk with stakeholder engagement severely constrained <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a>; and the Integrated Solid Waste Management Project ISR documents that local governance obstruction—including the inability to obtain approvals from Beirut Governorate and Municipality for waste management interventions—has blocked implementation of key activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-4727dec5-5d09-4ce2-8055-7dcb271aae12.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – ISR</a>. The Beirut Urban Resilience Project aide memoire further documents that procurement delays stemming from external coordination challenges among government stakeholders led to a USD 2.2 million budget cancellation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Economic Recovery Reversal and Financing Framework Collapse**

The World Bank's January 2026 Lebanon Economic Monitor had projected 3.5 percent real GDP growth in 2025 and 4 percent in 2026, contingent on sustained reforms, modest reconstruction inflows, and political stability. The March 2026 escalation has fundamentally undermined all three conditions simultaneously. Lebanon's cumulative GDP decline has exceeded 38 percent since 2019, public debt surpassed 357 percent of GDP, and the banking sector carries over USD 72 billion in accumulated losses; the renewed conflict has halted the nascent recovery and diverted international financing from development to humanitarian response. The LEAP <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> identifies macroeconomic risk as high, with contracts denominated in US dollars and contingent financing set aside for price increases; these mitigations assume a functioning market and contractor availability that no longer exist in conflict-affected areas. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project for Reconstruction <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> faces a USD 750 million financing gap between appraised needs and available IBRD financing, with the PAD's mitigation relying on proactive efforts to identify additional financing from development partners; the March 2026 escalation has triggered a USD 308.3 million humanitarian flash appeal that is critically underfunded at 14 percent for UNHCR Lebanon, creating direct competition for the same limited international financing pool. The Agriculture and Rural Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-c14c98c6-d426-4177-9a31-f1acaae2fb97.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – PAD</a> was designed based on macroeconomic projections now rendered obsolete, with all project disbursements and beneficiary payments denominated in USD to preserve purchasing power; the project shows zero disbursement against targets for farmers reached, SME loans approved, and infrastructure rehabilitated <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180334-4899e0cd-2b9b-432c-88d6-4a6b7c577697.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – ISR</a>. Implementation evidence confirms the portfolio-wide financial deterioration: the Beqaa Valley Water Project ISR documents escalation from moderate to high macroeconomic risk, with government counterpart funding capacity severely compromised <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-740ffba4-1843-48ae-936f-b5ec49d1902a.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – ISR</a>; the Social Protection Enhancement Project ISR documents that NGO partners are experiencing core funding losses from international donors pivoting to humanitarian response, with some partners scaling back operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-bde1794c-f97c-48b9-a56e-1949644a584e.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – Aide Memoire</a>; and the Beirut Urban Resilience Project aide memoire documents that Lebanese lira depreciation has reduced land compensation amounts to between USD 27 and USD 728 per plot, making compensation economically meaningless to landowners and blocking project land acquisition <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Beirut Critical Environmental Recovery Project ISR further documents that Parliament has not ratified the follow-on LEAP financing, directly preventing transfer of contaminated rubble management activities and leaving asbestos-contaminated debris unaddressed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-f112aa75-2e9c-4c03-ac0e-46311eb3089b.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – ISR</a>.
