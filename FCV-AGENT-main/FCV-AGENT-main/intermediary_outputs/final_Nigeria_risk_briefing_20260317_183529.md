# Nigeria FCV Portfolio Briefing

## Paragraph 1: ISWAP-Boko Haram Insurgency Escalation and Northeast Displacement Crisis

Nigeria's northeast faces a compounding security and humanitarian emergency driven by the tactical rehabilitation of ISWAP and affiliated Boko Haram factions. Between March 8–10, 2026, at least six simultaneous coordinated attacks struck military forward operating bases across Borno and Yobe states, with analysts assessing an unprecedented level of command-and-control sophistication including kinetic drones, IED deployment, and feint operations. Concurrently, the Borno State government's policy of closing IDP camps by 2026 is increasingly untenable: approximately 2.3 million IDPs remain in the BAY states, with 10,636 new arrivals recorded in Adamawa State in a single week in January 2026, and returnees face renewed attack risk and limited economic opportunities that fuel re-recruitment into insurgent ranks. Multiple portfolio projects are structurally exposed to these dynamics. The Nigeria Livestock Productivity and Resilience Support Project explicitly targets Borno, Adamawa, and Yobe as priority implementation zones, with the PAD acknowledging that "project activities in conflict-prone areas may be precluded by escalation of violence" and requiring field presence for Livestock Service Centers and animal health services <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-03827105-585b-4750-9095-203bbba388b8.pdf" target="_blank">P160865 – PAD</a>. The Nigeria Rural Access and Agricultural Marketing Scale-Up Project similarly identifies the Northeast as a conflict-affected area, noting that "increased security risks in recent years are a major constraint on supervision" and requiring a Security Advisor and State-level Security Risk Management Plans as disbursement conditions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-49277aa9-28d4-4f97-83e9-33ee698d511e.pdf" target="_blank">P180640 – PAD</a>. The Nigeria Irrigation and Water Resources Management Project covers Borno, Taraba, and Yobe, with the PAD rating security risk as "High" and documenting a "shortage of service providers/contractors/suppliers in some states due to fragility and violence" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-85088c47-4863-4599-b4b5-096fa6542e7d.pdf" target="_blank">P179684 – PAD</a>. The HOPE-GOVERNANCE Program for Results operates across all 36 states including the BAY states, with Results Area 3 requiring deployment of teachers and health workers in these zones, yet the PAD provides no specific security protocols for conflict-affected areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9cbd9942-ddb2-4f82-8a59-2ef33c093615.pdf" target="_blank">P181476 – PAD</a>. These forward-looking risks are materializing: the livestock project's Aide Memoire documents that the project team is experiencing insecurity challenges and has been compelled to partner with state security advisers for field operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P160865-5d69e8bf-f4da-431b-ba10-53ce0eb15708.pdf" target="_blank">P160865 – Aide Memoire</a>. The RAMP-3 rural roads project records deteriorating implementation performance, with the Aide Memoire noting that ongoing displacement flows and insurgent resurgence are directly constraining project delivery in the northeast <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P163353-3689df6b-f804-48f3-9226-71fc691bb9fd.pdf" target="_blank">P163353 – Aide Memoire</a>. The SABER business environment program's ISR flags persistent "High" sector strategy and policy risk, with escalating insurgency in Borno and Yobe diverting state resources and institutional bandwidth away from reform implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177442-89be90fb-8aa4-4b5f-a3ce-2ee220563321.pdf" target="_blank">P177442 – ISR</a>. The Nigeria Girls Secondary Education Program notes that Borno is conflict-affected by Boko Haram insurgency and that the security environment creates "limited capacity for supervision in conflict-affected areas," with the ISR documenting that institutional capacity constraints in conflict-affected states are directly impeding project execution <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170664-53a3c7a3-0d3e-4579-b3c6-4c02d2df3c34.pdf" target="_blank">P170664 – ISR</a>. Across the portfolio, the insurgency's tactical evolution is outpacing project security mitigation strategies designed for a less sophisticated threat environment, and the displacement crisis is undermining beneficiary targeting, WUA formation, and service delivery continuity in ways that project designs did not adequately anticipate.

## Paragraph 2: Northwest Banditry, Jihadist Convergence, and Cross-Border Spillover

Nigeria's northwest is rapidly developing into a second major insurgency theater, with the convergence of bandit networks and jihadist organizations—particularly the Lakurawa group, designated a terrorist organization in 2025 and linked to the Islamic State Sahel Province—creating an interconnected threat landscape. Between July 2024 and June 2025, 2,938 people were kidnapped in the Northwest, representing over 60 percent of reported kidnapping incidents nationwide. In February 2026, attacks in Anka and Bukkuyum areas of Zamfara State killed at least 50 people and abducted others, while a separate attack on Woro killed more than 100 and kidnapped 176. Compounding this, Niger's withdrawal from the Multinational Joint Task Force in March 2025 has compromised intelligence sharing and created operational vacuums in the Nigeria-Niger-Benin tri-border area, with Lakurawa killing at least 34 people in Arewa Dandi, Kebbi State on February 18, 2026. Portfolio exposure is extensive. The livestock project targets all 36 states with priority focus on northern pastoral corridors including Sokoto, Kebbi, and Zamfara, and Component 3 includes transnational agreements on livestock mobility requiring cross-border coordination in a context where Niger has withdrawn from regional security cooperation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-03827105-585b-4750-9095-203bbba388b8.pdf" target="_blank">P160865 – PAD</a>. The irrigation project explicitly identifies "banditry, kidnapping" as a security concern in North-West states (Jigawa, Katsina, Kebbi, Sokoto, Zamfara) and acknowledges a shortage of contractors due to fragility and violence, while Component 3 involves dam rehabilitation in Sokoto and Kebbi without addressing cross-border jihadist dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-85088c47-4863-4599-b4b5-096fa6542e7d.pdf" target="_blank">P179684 – PAD</a>. The RAMP-3 rural roads project lists Kano, Katsina, Sokoto, and Kebbi as participating states facing "violent kidnappings," with security risk rated "High" and documented kidnapping incidents at construction sites <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08758e688&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163353 – PAD</a>. The Nigeria Girls Secondary Education Program operates in Katsina and Kebbi, where the PAD identifies girls as vulnerable to "long distances to schools placing girls at risk of sexual exploitation and abuse," yet the mitigation of perimeter fencing and CPTED principles is wholly inadequate against organized mass kidnapping operations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087bc19f3&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170664 – PAD</a>. The health program (NPHCDA) identifies North-West as a focus area for community health worker deployment without addressing kidnapping risks to field personnel <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e2f0b5f0-ac80-427a-84a6-b627b845cc54.pdf" target="_blank">P504693 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing. The AGILE girls' education project's Aide Memoire documents that Project Implementation Unit representatives from Sokoto and Kebbi states were unable to travel safely to the June 2025 implementation support mission due to security conditions—a direct consequence of Lakurawa expansion and jihadist-bandit convergence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170664-c11acf71-7091-47be-a6ec-64c2fc58c258.pdf" target="_blank">P170664 – Aide Memoire</a>. The health project's Aide Memoire records that insecurity in Zamfara State has necessitated the establishment of mobile brigades and temporary health posts for displaced communities, with the February 2026 attacks in Anka and Bukkuyum directly cited as the security environment driving this adaptive response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167156-220e582d-2367-4076-a727-867da1bca3c9.pdf" target="_blank">P167156 – Aide Memoire</a>. The livestock project's ISR documents that conflict monitoring activities have been delayed, impairing the project's ability to map the complex actor landscape in the Northwest where bandit networks and jihadist groups are converging <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P160865-96299c20-cebe-401a-8e6e-851ed58ced23.pdf" target="_blank">P160865 – ISR</a>. The RAMP-3 ISR records that Sokoto State—one of only three states to have operationalized its State Rural Road Agency—is simultaneously experiencing severe jihadist and bandit activity, with institutional capacity potentially undermined by security disruptions and resource diversion to emergency response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P163353-59619518-733b-41e5-a411-2ca992991687.pdf" target="_blank">P163353 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 3: Farmer-Herder Intercommunal Violence and Middle Belt Displacement

Farmer-herder intercommunal violence in Nigeria's Middle Belt and North-Central regions continues to escalate, with at least 38 people killed in Benue and Plateau states between February 3–26, 2026, following a pattern of mass casualty events throughout 2025 including 52 killed in Bokkos LGA in April and 59+ killed in Guma LGA in June. The Middle Belt now hosts over 600,000 IDPs, with IOM DTM recording 215 new IDP arrivals in Guma LGA alone in the week of January 19–25, 2026. This violence is fundamentally resource-driven—competition over grazing land, water access, and agricultural territory—and is being intensified by climate-induced resource scarcity and population pressure. Portfolio exposure is direct and structural. The livestock project explicitly targets areas affected by farmer-herder conflicts in North-Central and Middle Belt zones, with Component 3 ($100 million) specifically designed to address conflict mitigation through water point construction, rangeland management, conflict committees, and early warning systems—yet the PAD acknowledges "potential for conflict to spiral out of control in implementing areas" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-03827105-585b-4750-9095-203bbba388b8.pdf" target="_blank">P160865 – PAD</a>. The irrigation project identifies North-Central states (Benue, Kogi, Kwara, Nasarawa, Plateau) as project locations and explicitly notes "herder-farmer disputes over land and cattle" as a security concern, with Component 2 involving rehabilitation of 40,000 hectares of irrigated command area and WUA formation that could become flashpoints for resource competition <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-85088c47-4863-4599-b4b5-096fa6542e7d.pdf" target="_blank">P179684 – PAD</a>. The RAMP-3 rural roads project identifies Plateau, Kwara, and Kogi as participating states facing "herder-farmer conflicts" and "ethnic/communal clashes," with 1,625 km of rural road construction requiring sustained on-site presence in conflict-affected areas <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08758e688&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163353 – PAD</a>. The SABER business environment program's FRILIA component requires "community consultation and stakeholder engagement in land acquisition and investment processes" in regions experiencing active intercommunal violence, with the PAD not addressing how these consultative processes will function safely <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-88d393cd-37bc-4496-8b3f-9add2bcf17c9.pdf" target="_blank">P177442 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing in ways that directly undermine project objectives. The livestock project's ISR reveals that the farmer-herder conflict incidence indicator has shown zero improvement—remaining at the 10 percent baseline through July 2025—despite 64 percent completion of conflict mediation platform targets, indicating that localized mediation efforts are insufficient to counter the structural deterioration of intercommunal relations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P160865-96299c20-cebe-401a-8e6e-851ed58ced23.pdf" target="_blank">P160865 – ISR</a>. The ISR further documents that only 9 of 22 conflict mediation platforms have been established, that livestock mobility agreements have achieved 0 percent adoption against a 60 percent target, and that stock routes remain unprotected from encroachment—the precise institutional failures that perpetuate the resource competition driving deadly violence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P160865-96299c20-cebe-401a-8e6e-851ed58ced23.pdf" target="_blank">P160865 – ISR</a>. The livestock project's Aide Memoire records that community conflict committees have been established and trained in early warning and early response mechanisms, representing a positive adaptation, but notes that the grievance redress system remains non-operational, removing a critical institutional safety valve <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P160865-5d69e8bf-f4da-431b-ba10-53ce0eb15708.pdf" target="_blank">P160865 – Aide Memoire</a>. The RAMP-3 ISR documents that several underperforming states (Kogi, Osun, Oyo) are located in or adjacent to the Middle Belt, with active conflict and displacement of populations directly constraining project implementation capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P163353-59619518-733b-41e5-a411-2ca992991687.pdf" target="_blank">P163353 – ISR</a>. The AGILE girls' education project's Aide Memoire records a sexual exploitation incident and systemic GBV reporting gaps in the context of active intercommunal violence and mass displacement in the Middle Belt, where over 600,000 IDPs face heightened vulnerability to sexual violence in overcrowded, insecure IDP settlements <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170664-c11acf71-7091-47be-a6ec-64c2fc58c258.pdf" target="_blank">P170664 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## Paragraph 4: Political Fragility, Democratic Backsliding, and Economic Hardship

Nigeria's governance environment has deteriorated significantly under the Tinubu administration, with the declaration of a state of emergency in Rivers State suspending elected officials, the arrest of at least 16 military officers on alleged coup plot charges in October 2025, and the persistent use of the Cybercrimes Act against journalists and critics. Massive opposition party defections to the ruling APC throughout 2025 have weakened democratic checks ahead of 2027 elections. Simultaneously, the 2023 fuel subsidy removal and currency liberalization have contributed to Nigeria's worst cost-of-living crisis in 30 years, with inflation peaking near 35 percent in 2025, soaring food prices, and at least three nationally significant labor strikes including a 29-day nationwide doctors' strike in November 2025. These twin dynamics—political centralization and economic collapse—are creating compounding institutional fragility across the portfolio. The power sector reform program identifies "Political and Governance (High)" risk, noting that "high degree of inter-agency coordination required" and that "changes in administration could impact PSRP implementation," while the PAD's macroeconomic risk assessment acknowledges that tariff increases "may reduce purchasing power of metered households" and could trigger "social conflict if reforms fail" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087a0da57&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P164001 – PAD</a>. The HOPE-GOVERNANCE program identifies "political (high)" risk including "high levels of state autonomy in Nigerian federalism" and notes that "DLI 5 (hiring more workers) could be affected by fiscal contraction," with Results Area 2 requiring publication of citizen-friendly budgets and audit reports in a context of restricted civil space <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9cbd9942-ddb2-4f82-8a59-2ef33c093615.pdf" target="_blank">P181476 – PAD</a>. The SABER business environment program identifies "Political and Governance (substantial)" risk, noting that "significant changes in state administrations may lead to reduced political commitment or policy stance changes" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-88d393cd-37bc-4496-8b3f-9add2bcf17c9.pdf" target="_blank">P177442 – PAD</a>. The WASH program identifies "High" political instability risk and documents that the National WASH Fund was not established during the 2023 political transition, with the water bill requiring re-presentation to the new National Assembly [P170734 | PAD]. These risks are materializing pervasively. The power sector ISR documents persistent "High" political and governance risk, with NERC regulatory approvals delayed (Fit and Proper regulations, Least Cost Power Development Plan incomplete), TCN unbundling incomplete, and the government unable to fully meet disbursement-linked requirements for tariff shortfall funding—directly reflecting the institutional fragility and fiscal constraints of the Tinubu administration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P164001-cd19cb1f-810e-43f5-8669-4d77b0ca28d5.pdf" target="_blank">P164001 – ISR</a>. The HOPE-GOVERNANCE ISR documents that several states have not constituted Steering Committees or Technical Committees, that implementing MDA leadership changes and desk officer substitutions are undermining institutional continuity, and that conflicting coordination instructions across HOPE Programs reflect systemic governance dysfunction <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181476-13130bb8-9edf-499b-9dd9-03a946debcb9.pdf" target="_blank">P181476 – ISR</a>. The WASH program ISR records escalation of political and governance risk from Substantial to High, with state governments engaging in unauthorized fund transfers and instructions to use parallel market currency conversions—directly reflecting the economic crisis and governance fragility documented at the national level <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P170734-815b82dc-054d-478b-a7d5-77999943c2a6.pdf" target="_blank">P170734 – ISR</a>. The SABER ISR documents persistent "Substantial" institutional capacity risk, with only 32 of 36 states absorbing funds and weak state-level capacity for implementing PPP frameworks and land administration digitization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177442-89be90fb-8aa4-4b5f-a3ce-2ee220563321.pdf" target="_blank">P177442 – ISR</a>. The NG-CARES social protection program ISR records that Anambra State's CARES coordination platforms have lost institutional functionality, with the ISR connecting this to broader governance fragility and, in the Southeast context, to IPOB separatist tensions and civic space repression <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174114-3f0ddef9-46ec-4fdd-887a-6c462a77f417.pdf" target="_blank">P174114 – ISR</a>. The RAMP-3 ISR documents that only 2 of 19 participating states have operationalized road sector reforms (State Road Funds and Rural Access Roads Agencies), with the pattern of non-compliance reflecting the same governance fragility and state capacity erosion documented at the national level <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P163353-59619518-733b-41e5-a411-2ca992991687.pdf" target="_blank">P163353 – ISR</a>.

## Paragraph 5: Acute Humanitarian Funding Collapse, Food Crisis, and Climate-Induced Displacement

Nigeria faces a convergence of humanitarian crises that is overwhelming both state capacity and development project sustainability. Nearly 35 million Nigerians are projected to face acute food insecurity during the 2026 lean season, including 5.8 million in the BAY states, with 15,000 people in Borno State at risk of catastrophic hunger (IPC-5) for the first time in nearly a decade. Approximately 3 million children under five are projected to suffer from severe acute malnutrition in 2026, including 1 million in the BAY states, while WFP has been forced to scale down nutrition programs affecting more than 300,000 children due to funding shortfalls—with humanitarian funding for Nigeria collapsing from nearly $1 billion a few years ago to less than $200 million expected in 2026. Compounding this, the 2025 flooding affected 34 of Nigeria's 36 states, impacting 3 million people, displacing 896,000 individuals, destroying 226,000 homes and 1.3 million hectares of farmland mid-harvest, and triggering secondary cholera outbreaks following the Maiduguri Alau dam overflow. Portfolio exposure to these compounding crises is systemic. The livestock project targets pastoral and agro-pastoral communities facing acute food insecurity, with the PAD's macroeconomic risk assessment noting "reduced government fiscal capacity" and Component 2's credit line mechanisms assuming beneficiary liquidity that the humanitarian crisis has eliminated <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-03827105-585b-4750-9095-203bbba388b8.pdf" target="_blank">P160865 – PAD</a>. The irrigation project identifies "food insecurity with 26.5 million projected food insecure in 2024" as a fragility factor and aims to enhance food production through irrigation modernization, but WUA mobilization and scheme rehabilitation require beneficiary participation and investment that acute malnutrition and humanitarian collapse are making impossible <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-85088c47-4863-4599-b4b5-096fa6542e7d.pdf" target="_blank">P179684 – PAD</a>. The health program identifies "climate change as risk multiplier" and includes solar power installation and WASH improvements as climate resilience measures, but the PAD does not address implementation risks from 2025 flooding affecting 34 states or secondary cholera outbreaks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e2f0b5f0-ac80-427a-84a6-b627b845cc54.pdf" target="_blank">P504693 – PAD</a>. The women's economic empowerment program acknowledges "climate-related displacement risks" and includes climate-smart agriculture components, but does not address how acute flood shocks displacing 896,000 people could disrupt WAG continuity and CIF fund management <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e7bb7cb7-6726-472c-b6d4-e7d4dc5834be.pdf" target="_blank">P179447 – PAD</a>. The landscape restoration project identifies "Climate and Natural Hazards (Substantial)" risk and notes that "extreme weather events (floods, droughts) may impact implementation," with Component B's community-led landscape restoration (350,000 hectares) directly vulnerable to flood damage and climate variability <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdc3c4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P175237 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The health project's Aide Memoire documents that insecurity-driven displacement in Zamfara State has necessitated mobile brigades and temporary health posts, with the underlying displacement dynamics reflecting both armed violence and climate-induced displacement from compounding crises <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167156-220e582d-2367-4076-a727-867da1bca3c9.pdf" target="_blank">P167156 – Aide Memoire</a>. The health project's ISR records persistent "High" macroeconomic risk, with the humanitarian funding collapse and acute food insecurity creating competing fiscal pressures that limit government resources available for health system sustainability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167156-5c6eda9e-dbe0-4e33-8162-3fe11568c7c6.pdf" target="_blank">P167156 – ISR</a>. The NG-CARES ISR explicitly acknowledges that fuel subsidy reforms, foreign exchange rate unification, and climate change are concurrent stressors amplifying fragility, with the program's continued support need directly reflecting the persistent erosion of citizen welfare documented in the economic hardship risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174114-3f0ddef9-46ec-4fdd-887a-6c462a77f417.pdf" target="_blank">P174114 – ISR</a>. The power sector ISR documents that the government's inability to fully fund tariff shortfalls reflects the same constrained fiscal space that has forced humanitarian funding to collapse, with both representing competing demands on a severely limited government budget <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P164001-cd19cb1f-810e-43f5-8669-4d77b0ca28d5.pdf" target="_blank">P164001 – ISR</a>. The women's economic empowerment ISR records that the project's macroeconomic instability risk is directly connected to Nigeria's cost-of-living crisis, with beneficiaries unable to mobilize savings as households prioritize immediate consumption—a dynamic that the 2025 floods, which destroyed 1.3 million hectares of farmland, have further intensified <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P179447-d906a35e-5888-4d62-a304-67dc84909385.pdf" target="_blank">P179447 – ISR</a>. The landscape restoration ISR documents elevated fiduciary risk across northern states, with flooding and climate shocks overwhelming state capacity for routine financial management functions while diverting resources to emergency response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175237-98777e8e-70ee-433a-a9c5-0470e6000ac1.pdf" target="_blank">P175237 – ISR</a>.
