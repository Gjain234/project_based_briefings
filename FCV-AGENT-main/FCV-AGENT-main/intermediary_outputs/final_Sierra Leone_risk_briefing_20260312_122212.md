# Sierra Leone FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Governance and Corruption Vulnerabilities

Sierra Leone's governance landscape is characterized by systemic institutional weaknesses documented in the IMF's 2025–2026 Governance and Corruption Diagnostic, which identified procurement vulnerabilities, limited rule of law enforcement, selective anti-corruption application, and weakened fiscal oversight—including the removal of the respected Auditor-General in late 2024. These structural deficits create a high-risk operating environment for the entire portfolio. Projects are broadly susceptible: the WASH project flags "limited independence of auditor general and Audit Service Sierra Leone from executive branch" as a high FM risk alongside procurement challenges across a US$40 million envelope <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>; the roads project identifies "weak institutional capacity" and "procurement delays and higher-than-expected bid prices" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c83ae70a-5a33-45ba-a77a-95ccca1d4fda.pdf" target="_blank">P178683 – PAD</a>; the digital transformation project explicitly rates fiduciary risk as Substantial, noting "limited fiduciary capacity in MIC" and a "weak complaint redress system" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>; and the local governance project cites a 2019 audit identifying Le5.3 billion in cash losses across 22 local councils <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088616f2d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172492 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing. The agriculture project's aide memoire documents US$141,000 in unresolved grant advances and contract overpayments persisting since March 2024, contractor non-compliance with environmental reporting, and government counterpart funding gaps <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. The energy project records land disputes requiring ministerial intervention, delayed resettlement action plans, and EDSA recruitment failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf" target="_blank">P171059 – Aide Memoire</a>. The local governance project's aide memoire confirms taxpayer non-compliance, low enforcement capacity, and trust deficits in local revenue systems <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – Aide Memoire</a>. The health project's ISR documents a non-functional grievance mechanism and persistent DHMT capacity constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. The February 2026 Parliamentary condemnation of the unlawful Netpage passport contract extension—consistent with IMF findings—signals that procurement governance failures are occurring in real time and are not confined to project-level implementation [P177077 | PAD; P164212 | Aide Memoire].

## 2. Political Deadlock, Electoral Credibility Erosion, and Pre-2028 Instability

Sierra Leone faces compounding political risks rooted in the disputed 2023 elections: the SLPP-APC confrontation has intensified with the February 2026 criminal remand of APC Secretary-General Lansana Dumbuya on incitement charges, the PPRC's politically motivated one-day suspension of the APC, and stalled Tripartite Committee dialogue as of March 2026. Simultaneously, the contested appointment of ECSL Chief Electoral Commissioner Edmond Sylvester Alpha—opposed by civil society and the APC as procedurally unlawful—and the pending Constitutional Amendment Bill 2025 have deepened institutional credibility deficits ahead of the 2028 general elections. Projects are susceptible across multiple dimensions. The WASH project depends on a Project Steering Committee requiring cooperation from opposition-led councils (FCC and WARDC), and the PAD explicitly acknowledges "2023 elections resulted in political uncertainty and ongoing tensions with strong party divisions" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>. The urban resilience project flags that five of seven beneficiary cities have opposition-controlled mayors, including Freetown where the bulk of investments are concentrated <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168608 – PAD</a>. The digital transformation project identifies "difficulty organizing timely PSC/TC meetings" and "potential policy reversal or political interference" as substantial risks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-e23e5acd-ed71-4830-b0ce-b480db6c7feb.pdf" target="_blank">P177077 – PAD</a>. The social protection project notes that the Inter-Agency Forum is chaired by the Vice President, creating potential for executive political pressure on targeting decisions <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088cebb9f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176789 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing: the education project's ISR documents that political and governance risk has been elevated to High, with the ISR explicitly connecting this to the broader governance environment <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087b90655&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – ISR</a>. The health project's ISR records an escalation from Moderate to Substantial political risk, directly referencing the deepening SLPP-APC confrontation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. The governance project's ISR similarly sustains a Substantial political and governance risk rating, with the contested ECSL appointment and stalled constitutional reform cited as contributing factors <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-1884d056-da29-49f2-ac93-9d9026ba75ad.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – ISR</a>. The private sector development project's ISR notes pending legislative reforms awaiting presidential assent in a context where the pattern of executive pressure on institutions creates risk of delay <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P503960-34c13115-e0c3-4d57-9d40-6ef9de132fd6.pdf" target="_blank">P503960 – ISR</a>.

## 3. Macro-Economic Fragility, Debt Vulnerability, and Structural Reform Gaps

Sierra Leone's economy exhibits structural fragility characterized by high public debt (reaching 94% of GDP at project appraisal for the financial inclusion project), heavy dependence on extractive industries, limited economic diversification, and subsistence agriculture exposure to commodity price shocks. The IMF Extended Credit Facility provides some fiscal anchor, but the supplementary 2025 budget was criticized as austerity-oriented for citizens while preserving favorable terms for political insiders. Projects are broadly susceptible to macroeconomic deterioration affecting counterpart funding, contractor performance, and operational sustainability. The financial inclusion project rates macroeconomic risk as High, noting "high inflation (37% in January 2023), currency depreciation (66% in 2022), fiscal deficit nearly 10% of GDP" and warns that MSME loan repayment capacity could be impaired <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>. The WASH project identifies risks of "inflation-depreciation spiral" and "external risks from global commodity prices" threatening O&M sustainability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>. The urban resilience project flags "rapid decline in fiscal space could impede delivery of critical infrastructure and services" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886b6a73&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168608 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing. The agriculture project's aide memoire documents a US$10.944 million financing gap with uncertain government commitment to bridge it, government counterpart funding delays of US$6.944 million, and a US$3.1 million bridge construction funding shortfall <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P153437-7c4c655a-c6f8-47aa-b397-3bb60505369e.pdf" target="_blank">P153437 – Aide Memoire</a>. The energy project records EDSA's inability to recruit a Chief Information Officer and Supervisory Engineer, reflecting structural human capital constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf" target="_blank">P171059 – Aide Memoire</a>. The health project's ISR confirms "constrained fiscal space for resource mobilization" and "very limited government health spending (less than 5% of government budget)" as active implementation constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. The local governance project's aide memoire confirms that revenue sharing tensions between chiefdoms and district councils reflect structural fiscal federalism deficits that impede domestic resource mobilization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 4. USAID Dismantlement and Global Aid Cuts Undermining Health and Civil Society

The dismantlement of USAID since January 2025 and the sharp reduction in US foreign assistance have created acute resource gaps in Sierra Leone's health and civil society sectors. A Freetown-based democracy advocacy organization lost US$1 million in civic education funding; field reports from March 2026 describe children arriving at hospitals with untreated severe malaria due to absent USAID-funded diagnostic tests; and Sierra Leone's health budget is approximately half-funded by donors, making it acutely exposed. Projects are susceptible through multiple pathways: reduced civil society capacity for monitoring and grievance oversight, weakened health system complementarity, and eroded donor coordination platforms. The WASH project relies on a "donor coordination platform to be established and maintained" and community engagement through the Wan Fambul Framework, both of which depend on a functioning civil society ecosystem <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a01bb309-cb69-4828-b27f-10b83ffbb568.pdf" target="_blank">P507588 – PAD</a>. The education project targets vulnerable girls facing GBV and dropout risks, with a GBV Action Plan dependent on civil society partners <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087a46502&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – PAD</a>. The health project depends on donor-funded technical assistance for BSL supervisory capacity and financial literacy campaigns <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing significantly. The education project's aide memoire documents that an NGO consortium (including Save the Children) withdrew from supporting 120,000 out-of-school children, with MBSSE unable to absorb the mandate—a withdrawal plausibly connected to US aid cuts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167897-f4984073-1dc6-4a44-a2fa-8b6ab0ccc16b.pdf" target="_blank">P167897 – Aide Memoire</a>. The education project's ISR confirms fund unavailability for back-to-school incentives and accelerated curriculum delivery, directly harming food-insecure and marginalized children <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087b90655&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167897 – ISR</a>. The health project's ISR records that USAID withdrawal has directly exacerbated "very limited government health spending" and constrained health facility readiness, with the connection rated at 0.95 confidence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172102-c330a705-beb1-4281-8c32-7180588ece0f.pdf" target="_blank">P172102 – ISR</a>. The energy project's aide memoire notes SEA/SH coordination delays that may reflect reduced donor-funded technical support capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171059-fe1e8709-0a89-400a-9c3d-6122f578f52f.pdf" target="_blank">P171059 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Ethno-Regional Political Polarisation, Youth Marginalisation, and Judicial Independence

Two interconnected structural fragility drivers—ethno-regional polarisation and weakened judicial independence—create cross-cutting risks for the portfolio. Sierra Leone's two-party system remains deeply structured along Mende-dominated (SLPP, south/east) and Temne-dominated (APC, north) lines, with patrimonialism deepening exclusion of youth (75% of population under 35, 18% overall NEET rate). Simultaneously, the judiciary faces credibility risks from executive interference: the suspension of Supreme Court Judge Halloway following his complaints about the Chief Justice, and the February 2026 remand of APC Secretary-General Dumbuya with bail initially denied, signal courts being used as instruments of political pressure. Projects are susceptible to both risks. The roads project identifies "elite capture in market infrastructure" and "social exclusion of vulnerable groups" as substantial environmental and social risks across districts with different ethno-regional compositions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-c83ae70a-5a33-45ba-a77a-95ccca1d4fda.pdf" target="_blank">P178683 – PAD</a>. The social protection project targets 13,000 youth in public works across all 16 districts but provides no district-level allocation targets to ensure equitable ethno-regional distribution <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088cebb9f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176789 – PAD</a>. The land administration project notes that "60% of High Court cases arise from land disputes" and establishes GRM and ADR mechanisms whose effectiveness depends on judicial independence <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088db0cd6&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P177031 – PAD</a>. The financial inclusion project flags "risk of elite capture and favoritism in MSME credit selection" as an environmental and social risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ef18fc52-8299-4710-b421-f8b4287b16b4.pdf" target="_blank">P177947 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing. The local governance project's aide memoire documents revenue sharing tensions between chiefdoms and district councils that reflect ethno-regional political fragmentation and patronage dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172492-e7af4b5d-dbaa-4d2f-bbde-cc936e0d240e.pdf" target="_blank">P172492 – Aide Memoire</a>. The education project's aide memoire records teachers absconding from law enforcement—a direct manifestation of weak rule of law enforcement—and delayed GRM follow-up monitoring that leaves youth and marginalized groups without institutional grievance channels <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167897-f4984073-1dc6-4a44-a2fa-8b6ab0ccc16b.pdf" target="_blank">P167897 – Aide Memoire</a>. The land administration project's ISR documents a procurement specialist resignation causing delays in land records digitization, and consultant underperformance requiring contract cancellation, both consistent with weak institutional enforcement capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177031-4a89cbc6-c019-4f68-a3f1-6619f122f0ee.pdf" target="_blank">P177031 – ISR</a>. The urban resilience project's ISR records limited MDA ownership and persistent emergency preparedness coordination gaps, reflecting the same institutional fragility <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168608-6948d3d6-7895-4755-b550-cc1f6dce6453.pdf" target="_blank">P168608 – ISR</a>.
