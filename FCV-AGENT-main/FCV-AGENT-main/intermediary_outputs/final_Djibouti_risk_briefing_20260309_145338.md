# Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

**Risk 1: Political Consolidation and Governance Fragility Under Indefinite Presidential Rule**

Djibouti's removal of presidential age limits in October 2025—enabling President Guelleh to seek a sixth term in April 2026—represents a fundamental deterioration in institutional checks and democratic norms. Across the portfolio, projects anticipated this risk: governance structures relying on multi-ministerial coordination, executive-chaired steering committees, and sustained political commitment are inherently vulnerable when executive power concentrates without constitutional constraint. The TVET and skills development project flagged that its high-level steering committee under the Economic Development Council lacked safeguards against political capture <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The public finance management reform project identified vested interests and political resistance as a high-rated risk, with mitigation resting almost entirely on demonstrated government commitment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The contingency emergency response project noted that governance arrangements spread across ministries with unclear mandates could be further destabilized by political consolidation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. In implementation, these concerns are materializing. The PFM project is experiencing a persistent "High" political and governance risk rating, with administrative delays in publishing governance decrees and weak operationalization of reform structures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The statistics modernization project similarly carries a sustained "Substantial" governance risk, with institutional fragility undermining evidence-based policymaking <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-4b12f487-05cf-4472-bbf3-012c7cb41ce1.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>. The corridor development project's political and governance risk has remained "Substantial" throughout implementation, with institutional coordination failures and stakeholder engagement difficulties linked to the tightening political environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's steering committee has been inactive, with governance coordination between government and development partners stalled <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the pattern is consistent: formal governance structures exist on paper but lack the institutional autonomy and accountability to function effectively under an increasingly centralized executive.

**Risk 2: Severe Youth Unemployment and Economic Inequality as a Structural Implementation Constraint**

With youth unemployment exceeding 75% and endemic corruption compressing economic opportunity, Djibouti's economic fragility creates both demand-side and supply-side constraints for the portfolio. Projects designed to address employment outcomes face a structural paradox: the very conditions they seek to remedy also undermine their implementation. The TVET project acknowledged macroeconomic risk as "substantial," noting that labor market absorption capacity is severely constrained, yet proposed no labor market demand assessment or contingency for limited job creation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The agri-food development project identified youth as key beneficiaries but provided no analysis of how structural unemployment would affect uptake or sustainability of income-generating activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project noted that road improvements may not translate to youth employment without targeted mechanisms, while macroeconomic risk acknowledged potential job losses from decreased port volumes <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. In implementation, these risks are actively materializing. The TVET project's refugee employment indicator is stuck at 5% against an 80% target, with the broader labor market unable to absorb trained graduates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The online gig work component has stalled entirely, reflecting that supply-side training cannot overcome demand-side structural barriers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The food security project has seen drought-driven reallocation of resources, with agricultural input and livestock delivery failing to reach target households, deepening livelihood insecurity for economically vulnerable populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor project has documented exploitation of 150 daily workers through non-compliant contracts and absence of social security registration—a direct consequence of workers' inability to refuse exploitative arrangements in a 75%-unemployment environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The social protection project is struggling to identify viable income-generating activities in remote localities, reflecting the economy-wide inability to create sustainable employment pathways <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Risk 3: Refugee and Asylum-Seeker Hosting Under Severe Resource Constraints**

Djibouti hosts approximately 32,000 displaced persons—85% concentrated in refugee villages—while simultaneously managing recurrent droughts and fragile public services. This hosting burden creates compounding pressures across the portfolio, as projects targeting refugee populations encounter institutional capacity ceilings that the country's resource base cannot sustain. The health services project identified refugee-specific service delivery as a core implementation challenge, noting that 85% of displaced persons reside in villages with limited infrastructure <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088da7dd6&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P178033 – PAD</a>. The TVET project directly targets 3,165 refugees for skills training but acknowledged that resource constraints and competing humanitarian priorities could undermine training participation and completion <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The education project noted that external shocks—including recurrent droughts—could disrupt refugee education programming, with mitigation relying on dialogue with UNHCR and partners <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>. The food security project explicitly planned emergency food stocks for refugee populations but lacked documented allocation protocols between refugee villages and host communities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the resource constraint is translating into concrete service delivery failures. The health project shows refugee immunization rates at 50% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The TVET project has zero refugee enrollment against a target of 220, with institutional capacity unable to operationalize refugee training programs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The education project shows declining refugee student enrollment—from 596 to 384 between 2021 and 2025—with refugee schools still not integrated into the national education management information system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The urban development project has stalled on refugee village restructuring plans for Ali Addeh and Holl Holl, with zero vocational training certificates delivered to refugees and refugee-led civil society organizations failing to implement any sub-projects <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The food security project has achieved only 50% of its emergency food stock target and zero water infrastructure rehabilitation, directly reducing drought response capacity for refugee and host populations alike <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Risk 4: Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Media Freedom Undermining Accountability Mechanisms**

Djibouti's classification as "Not Free" by Freedom House—with journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested—creates a structural accountability deficit that pervades the entire portfolio. Projects relying on stakeholder engagement, grievance redress mechanisms, independent monitoring, and civil society participation are operating in an environment where these functions have been systematically dismantled. The PFM reform project identified this as a fundamental implementation gap: Component 3 explicitly targets civil society engagement for public finance oversight, yet the PAD does not acknowledge that independent civil society is virtually non-existent <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a>. The statistics project noted that its mitigation strategy depends on "systematic citizen engagement interventions" that cannot function without civil society <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171777 – PAD</a>. The TVET project's M&E system relies on "independent academic evaluation" and confidential grievance reporting, both of which are vulnerable to government pressure or self-censorship in the current environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The corridor project established stakeholder engagement plans and grievance redress mechanisms without acknowledging that the civic space required for these to function has been eliminated <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. In implementation, the consequences are visible across the portfolio. The corridor project has documented absent HSE agents, systematic labor non-compliance, safety violations unreported in official submissions, and weak environmental and social compliance reporting—all enabled by the absence of independent oversight <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's GBV action plans are missing, stakeholder mobilization plans are delayed, and the internal audit function has been compromised by lack of organizational independence <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The GBV prevention project launched without accounting software, has delayed financial reporting, stalled NGO contract negotiations, and capacity gaps in psychosocial service delivery—all reflecting the institutional hollowing that accompanies collapsed civil society <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-4908093d-8bfd-4f36-a29c-d9575d229601.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>. The social protection project shows weak grievance mechanisms, limited beneficiary participation, and opaque youth training selection procedures without pre-defined eligibility criteria <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban development project's refugee-led CSO sub-projects have achieved zero implementation, consistent with the hostile environment for autonomous civil society organizations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>.

**Risk 5: Ethnic Tensions and Political Exclusion Creating Targeting and Cohesion Vulnerabilities**

The structural imbalance between Issa Somali dominance (approximately 60% of the population) and Afar political marginalization (approximately 35%) creates persistent risks of exclusionary implementation, elite capture, and contested resource allocation across the portfolio. Projects operating in ethnically mixed regions or targeting vulnerable populations face the risk that institutional governance structures—dominated by the ruling ethnic group—will reproduce existing inequities at the project level. The TVET project's multi-ministry coordination structure lacks explicit ethnic representation safeguards in steering committees, creating potential for political interference in beneficiary selection <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>. The contingency emergency response project targets the entire national population including refugees and vulnerable households, but does not address how ethnic balance will be maintained in resource distribution across regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509082-335ace4f-041e-4d8b-8b24-01c7157e4078.pdf" target="_blank">P509082 – PAD</a>. The refugee and host community integration project acknowledged "social conflict" as a substantial risk and adopted conflict-sensitive targeting, but did not detail how targeting would navigate ethnic sensitivities between predominantly Somali refugee populations and ethnically mixed host communities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – PAD</a>. The corridor development project lists corridor populations as beneficiaries without disaggregation by ethnicity or targeted inclusion mechanisms for the Afar minority <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174300 – PAD</a>. In implementation, ethnic exclusion dynamics are manifesting in several forms. The corridor project's stakeholder engagement risk has remained "Substantial," with persistent coordination failures that may reflect trust deficits between ethnically diverse communities and government-led project structures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>. The social protection project shows inadequate beneficiary participation and opaque youth training selection procedures, creating conditions under which ethnic favoritism could operate without detection <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P180999 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban development project's delayed refugee integration and social cohesion assessment means the project cannot identify whether refugee settlement patterns are exacerbating existing ethnic fault lines <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. The education project shows private Arabic schools—often associated with minority communities—refusing to integrate into the national education management system, suggesting institutional fragmentation along ethnic and community lines <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The food security project's water distribution non-compliance across six regions, with regional prefects deviating from planned routes, may reflect ethnic-based resistance to centralized resource allocation controlled by the dominant political group <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>.
