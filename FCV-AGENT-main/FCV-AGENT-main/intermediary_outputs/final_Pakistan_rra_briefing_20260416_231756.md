**Social unrest and protests:** Pakistan's social unrest landscape has intensified sharply in early 2026. Deadly protests erupted in Gilgit-Baltistan on 1 March following the killing of Iran's Supreme Leader, leaving 12–20 dead and 80 injured; protesters stormed and burned the UNDP office in Skardu and attacked the UNMOGIP field station, while separate clashes at the US Consulate in Karachi left 10 dead (PAK-004). PTI's KP chapter has warned of a "major movement" for Imran Khan's release, and human rights lawyers have received 17-year sentences for social media criticism of state actions in Balochistan (PAK-005). Youth unemployment, 44.7 percent poverty, and deepening radicalisation networks—including the arrest of a female would-be suicide bomber in Khuzdar—compound the structural drivers of unrest (PAK-012). These dynamics directly threaten portfolio projects. The Merged Areas Development Project operates in KP where civil society space is contracting and bans on demonstrations and mobile communications disruptions during operations constrain the participatory planning and community engagement that underpin its village council model <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-107c8a45-eb40-4cbd-85f2-4e94744ec5dd.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – PAD</a>. The Sindh Social Protection Authority project similarly faces a worsening political and governance risk trajectory, with the rating escalating from Substantial to High and sector strategies and policies risk rising from Moderate to Substantial <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178532-6d993aa7-8a16-4819-90c3-4bf6693bb1bf.pdf" target="_blank">P178532 – ISR</a>. **Violence between refugees, host communities, and the state:** Pakistan has deported more than 146,000 Afghans in 2026 as of mid-April, following over one million forced returns in 2025; suspension of Proof of Registration renewals has rendered 1.3 million Afghans undocumented and exposed nearly two million to removal risk (PAK-006). UNHCR and MSF document that deportation fear is preventing refugees from accessing medical care, causing trauma-induced miscarriages and preventable deaths. Simultaneously, tens of thousands of civilians have been displaced from KP's Tirah Valley and border communities by military operations and cross-border shelling (PAK-007). The Refugees and Host Communities project is directly exposed: it is funded through IDA's Regional Sub-Window for Refugees and Host Communities (US$125 million of US$200 million total), with 519,303 refugees in target districts, and the PAD explicitly identifies the risk that Pakistan's protection framework could be changed due to the evolving regional situation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0879fa728&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166309 – PAD</a>. ISRs confirm that 7,835 Afghan refugee children are currently benefiting from project interventions against a target of 12,000, flood rehabilitation works remain incomplete across 12 districts, and fiduciary risk has escalated from Moderate to High <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166309-e6c7abf6-7829-4ecf-b963-bf4cfa1c931c.pdf" target="_blank">P166309 – ISR</a>. The Afghan Refugees Management project, which directly targets approximately 15,000 PoR card holders for visa policy benefits, faces an existential tension: the accelerating deportation campaign directly contradicts the voluntary repatriation principle the project supports, and the CCAR has shown no progress in organisational capacity since 2019 <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0875fcabb&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P165542 – PAD</a>. ISRs record that political and governance risk remains persistently High, stakeholder risk has worsened, institutional capacity has deteriorated, and no automated refugee data management system exists <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P165542-2226f908-40b4-4cb7-a8b5-21f422b5b997.pdf" target="_blank">P165542 – ISR</a>. The Balochistan Flood Emergency project targets approximately 2.7 million flood-affected people in a province that is also a major Afghan refugee-hosting area, yet the PAD does not address how forced deportations may affect beneficiary populations or project staff <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-cada74cb-95f5-4ca2-a46a-9560206ebad9.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – PAD</a>; ISRs show fiduciary and stakeholder risks have both escalated to High <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180323-aca41980-8e57-40b5-96a1-1f120e049689.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – ISR</a>.

**Organized violence between political and sectarian groups:** A suicide bomb attack on a Shia mosque in Islamabad on 6 February 2026 killed at least 31 people and wounded 169—the deadliest such attack in over a decade—with ISKP claiming responsibility and explicitly targeting Pakistani Shias (PAK-004). The BLA launched Operation Herof 2.0 on 31 January 2026, a series of 12 or more coordinated attacks across Balochistan targeting military installations, banks, prisons, and police stations, resulting in approximately 200 deaths; on 12 April, BLA insurgents conducted the first maritime attack on a Pakistan Coast Guard patrol boat (PAK-003). TTP violence surged to 146 attacks in March 2026 alone—a 76 percent increase over February—with tactics evolving to include quadcopter drones, IEDs, and targeted killings (PAK-002). The Balochistan Flood Emergency project operates in the province recording 55 percent of national terror deaths, and the PAD acknowledges that the current security situation will restrict and may prevent travel to project areas, relying on remote supervision tools (GEMS) to mitigate access constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-cada74cb-95f5-4ca2-a46a-9560206ebad9.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – PAD</a>; ISRs confirm persistent High political and governance risk and slow physical progress due to procedural delays <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180323-aca41980-8e57-40b5-96a1-1f120e049689.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – ISR</a>. The KP Merged Areas Development Project operates in eight districts—including Bajaur, Khyber, Kurram, Mohmand, North and South Waziristan—that are the primary TTP operational theatre; the PAD acknowledges periodic security challenges in remote sites and requires Frontier Constabulary protection for field staff <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-107c8a45-eb40-4cbd-85f2-4e94744ec5dd.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – PAD</a>. The National Health Support Programme operates in KP and explicitly acknowledges that insecurity affects staff recruitment, retention, and mobility, with particular impact on female health workers <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088dba71e&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172615 – PAD</a>; ISRs record persistent Substantial political and governance risk, referral system implementation stalled in KP and Sindh, and health information system integration lagging in both provinces <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172615-d4815612-d79d-470e-a08b-47633da31938.pdf" target="_blank">P172615 – ISR</a>. The Dasu Hydropower project in Upper Kohistan District, KP, involves a 7-year construction period with 5,000+ workers and a 250 km transmission line corridor; the PAD identifies potential for theft or sabotage of equipment but does not address the emerging drone threat <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0828b816d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P121507 – PAD</a>; ISRs show political and governance risk has escalated to High, fiduciary risk has risen to High, and environment and social stakeholder risk has escalated to High, with only 33–35 percent of affected persons reporting their suggestions have been taken into account <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b082dcef4e&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P121507 – ISR</a>.

**Political instability:** Pakistan's civil-military hybrid governance has grown more entrenched in 2026, with Imran Khan still jailed, PTI leaders facing mass arrests, and human rights lawyers sentenced to 17 years for social media criticism (PAK-005). The KP provincial government—the only PTI-governed institution—is in direct political conflict with the federal government and military, with the KP Chief Minister alleging systematic economic strangulation through withheld constitutional financial allocations (PAK-009). Pakistan ranked in the lowest global quartile on political stability and rule of law, and most prime ministers have historically not completed a full term (PAK-009). This governance fragility pervades the portfolio. The SPEED programme in KP depends on the Finance Department of a provincial government in direct political conflict with federal authorities; the PAD identifies political instability as a risk to sustained PFM reforms, and multi-year budgeting integration was not completed due to the interim government <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088486e7b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P175727 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175727-191b6395-e711-43ac-a0c3-a0e4aa213f35.pdf" target="_blank">P175727 – ISR</a>. **External risks and intra-state conflict:** Pakistan declared "open war" with Afghanistan in late February 2026 after striking Taliban military facilities in Kabul, Kandahar, and Paktia province; a March 16 airstrike on what Afghan officials described as a drug rehabilitation centre killed more than 400 people; a fragile Eid ceasefire brokered by Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and Turkey held from 18–23 March but no formal agreement was reached (PAK-001). India-Pakistan tensions resurfaced through the suspension of the Indus Waters Treaty following military clashes in May 2025, with President Zardari warning of threats to food security and livelihoods (PAK-010). Pakistan ranked first on the Global Terrorism Index 2026, with TTP responsible for over half of all deaths and a UN Security Council report warning of potential extra-regional threat (PAK-002). The Khyber Pass Economic Corridor project is the most directly exposed: its core objective is to improve connectivity and trade across the Pakistan-Afghanistan border at Torkham, and the PAD explicitly identifies border skirmishes between Pakistan and Afghanistan as a political and governance risk; the Torkham crossing was closed during the March 2026 hostilities and only reopened on 31 March <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b085b2e6ff&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P159577 – PAD</a>.
