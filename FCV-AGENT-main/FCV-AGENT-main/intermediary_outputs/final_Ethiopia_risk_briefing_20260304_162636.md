# Ethiopia FCV Portfolio Briefing: Sector-Aligned Risk Analysis

---

## 1. Security and Conflict Dynamics

The security cluster represents the most acute and pervasive risk layer across the Ethiopia portfolio. Active armed conflict in Amhara (Fano insurgency), Oromia (OLA factions), and Tigray (TPLF-TDF tensions) creates a multi-front insecurity environment that directly constrains implementation across virtually every project. Country-level risks ETH_2026_003 and ETH_2026_004 document over 4 million children out of school in Amhara, 288,000 displaced in Oromia since July 2025, and continued civilian casualties despite partial peace agreements. PADs across the portfolio acknowledge these dynamics: P176303 identifies "security (substantial): internal conflicts affecting project sites, staff safety, material transportation" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a>; P178047 designates all Tigray woredas as High Risk of Ongoing Conflict (HROC) areas requiring differentiated implementation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088ddd75f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P178047 – PAD</a>; and P177881 notes "security risks may hinder smooth implementation especially for PTCs in conflict regions" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a>. Realized implementation evidence confirms these risks have materialized at scale. In Amhara, active conflict has directly prevented WASH project staff movement <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>, blocked public works monitoring <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>, and required explosive ordnance removal before project activities can proceed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>. In Tigray, the political crisis and shrinking federal authority have caused account-opening delays in 23 woredas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>, asset loss and document destruction <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the exclusion of five contested woredas from program coverage entirely <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>. Security constraints have also rendered Gambella and Benishangul Gumuz inaccessible to World Bank supervision <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>, and mini-grid sites have required replacement due to insecurity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-45fbd416-22c6-4fe2-a001-21a06b2bddd9.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Ethiopia-Eritrea tensions (ETH_2026_001) add a further layer of risk, particularly for projects in Afar and Tigray, where PADs acknowledge "geopolitical tensions in Horn of Africa" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-334703ce-834f-4cd4-bf49-4e941b00bb0a.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – PAD</a> and proxy conflict dynamics could disrupt operations in border-adjacent regions.

## 2. Humanitarian Response and Social Protection

The humanitarian cluster is defined by the intersection of mass displacement, acute funding shortfalls, and the social protection architecture attempting to bridge development and emergency response. ETH_2026_005 and ETH_2026_007 document 3.3–4.7 million internally displaced persons in dire camp conditions, WFP suspension of treatment for 650,000 malnourished women and children, and a funding environment described as Ethiopia's most severe humanitarian crisis. PADs across the portfolio explicitly acknowledge displacement as a structural implementation factor: P172479 includes provisions for displaced PSNP beneficiaries and protocols for hosting displaced families <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087f8ffa9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172479 – PAD</a>; P172284 targets 512,000 drought-induced IDPs and supports 800,000 refugees <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a>; and P177881 mandates that at least 15% of 200,000 direct beneficiaries be IDPs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a>. Realized risks reveal that these design provisions have been overwhelmed by the scale of the crisis. The TVET project (P177881) achieved only 0.76% IDP enrollment against a 35% target, with barriers including lack of documentation, mobility constraints, and competing survival needs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-02907b3f-0a5c-484e-b00e-136cb8695aad.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – ISR</a>. The PSNP (P172479) faces a critical coordination gap: transitory caseload beneficiaries are excluded from Humanitarian Response Plan planning during the July–December lean season, leaving vulnerable populations without coordinated support precisely when needs peak <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. The EDRMC's institutional capacity constraints in confirming beneficiary numbers delay shock response disbursement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>, while the Early Warning System dashboard is inaccessible to key prioritization committee institutions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>. The humanitarian funding crisis also directly constrains government counterpart financing: the PSNP faces macroeconomic stress from ETB/USD conversion rates <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>, and the One WASH project's US$50 million counterpart commitment is at risk as government budget constraints intensify <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d2b493&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – PAD</a>.

## 3. Governance, Institutions, and Political Economy

The governance cluster captures the systemic erosion of institutional capacity, political legitimacy, and accountability mechanisms that cuts across all sectors. ETH_2026_002, ETH_2026_006, and ETH_2026_010 document the TPLF's deregistration from 2026 elections, the detention of 44 journalists in 2025, media law changes increasing executive control, and legitimacy concerns that could trigger post-election instability. PADs acknowledge these dynamics with varying specificity: P172479 identifies "political turmoil, inter-ethnic conflict, weakened upward accountability of subnational governments" as high political risks <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087f8ffa9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172479 – PAD</a>; P171034 flags "upcoming elections" and "political transition" as substantial risks <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08847bca4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171034 – PAD</a>; and P167794 notes "political and social unrest creating difficulties in mobility and access to communities" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d2b493&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – PAD</a>. Realized implementation evidence shows governance fragility manifesting across multiple dimensions. Political and governance risk ratings have been sustained at "High" or "Substantial" across P176303, P171034, P167794, and P180076 [P176303 | Implementation Status and Results Report; P171034 | Implementation Status and Results Report; P167794 | Implementation Status and Results Report; P180076 | Implementation Status and Results Report]. Newly established regions (SW Ethiopia, South Ethiopia, Central Ethiopia) exhibit staff deployment rates of only 26–51%, with weak sector bureau coordination undermining WASH implementation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The digital project (P171034) faces Ethio Telecom's resistance to regulatory reform <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>, government pressure to reallocate US$30 million in project funds <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>, and mobile money market restrictions that exclude competitors from government payment systems <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>. Fiduciary governance weaknesses are pervasive: inter-fund borrowing, unreconciled balances, and equipment misappropriation have been documented in P159382 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P159382-193d3e2f-b7e4-4661-9760-e815aba99fb2.pdf" target="_blank">P159382 – Aide Memoire</a>; fraud and corruption complaint handling systems are non-functional at implementing entities in P176303 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-f8f4167e-72a4-4049-a5c0-0b3f0fd456c6.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – Aide Memoire</a>; and the client unilaterally cancelled an independent audit process in P159382 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P159382-193d3e2f-b7e4-4661-9760-e815aba99fb2.pdf" target="_blank">P159382 – Aide Memoire</a>. The systematic crackdown on civil society (ETH_2026_006) further undermines independent verification agents, grievance redress mechanisms, and third-party monitoring across the portfolio.

## 4. Infrastructure and Energy Access

The infrastructure cluster encompasses rural roads, urban water and sanitation, energy access, and digital connectivity—sectors where physical implementation in conflict-affected terrain creates compounding operational risks. ETH_2026_003 and ETH_2026_004 directly threaten construction activities, contractor mobility, and supply chain logistics across Amhara and Oromia. PADs in this cluster acknowledge the challenge: P176303 requires "conflict-sensitive site selection and implementation procedures" and "standard operating procedures for conflict situations" across all 13 regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a>; P167794 identifies "insecurity limiting contractor access to sites" as a security risk <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d2b493&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – PAD</a>; and P171742 acknowledges "political disruption and social unrest" as high risks affecting energy access implementation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08839e83a&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171742 – PAD</a>. Realized risks confirm severe implementation deterioration. The rural roads project (P176303) has failed to establish financial management arrangements in conflict-affected regions, with FM systems unable to be operationalized due to institutional fragility <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-7b125857-00f3-4f1b-a25e-a0ad429dba62.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – ISR</a>. The energy access project (P171742) has seen implementation ratings decline to Moderately Unsatisfactory despite 80% of contracts under implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-a6dffcd4-0989-414f-875d-c153cf49eb19.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – ISR</a>, with mini-grid sites requiring replacement due to security constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-45fbd416-22c6-4fe2-a001-21a06b2bddd9.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – Aide Memoire</a> and large-scale public facilities electrification facing bidder qualification challenges <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171742-45fbd416-22c6-4fe2-a001-21a06b2bddd9.pdf" target="_blank">P171742 – Aide Memoire</a>. The One WASH project (P167794) documents active conflict in Amhara impeding staff movement and project implementation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>, asset loss and document destruction in conflict-affected woredas <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>, and procurement constraints due to internet disruptions and security restrictions in conflict-affected woredas <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The corridor highway project (P148850) faces contractor payment delays with a US$18.09 million government obligation outstanding <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08588ff6c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P148850 – Aide Memoire</a>, work suspension, and unresolved resettlement action plans across multiple conflict-affected cities <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08588ff6c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P148850 – Aide Memoire</a>. Currency depreciation since July 2024 has further eroded the real value of government counterpart commitments across infrastructure projects [P171034 | Aide Memoire; P176303 | Aide Memoire].

## 5. Human Capital Development and Livelihoods

The human capital cluster encompasses education, health, skills development, and pastoral livelihoods—sectors where service delivery to conflict-affected and displaced populations is both most urgently needed and most operationally constrained. ETH_2026_003 through ETH_2026_005 document 3.2–4 million children out of school across Amhara and Oromia, non-operational IDP clinics, and deteriorating nutrition outcomes amid the humanitarian funding crisis. PADs in this cluster reflect ambitious targeting of vulnerable populations: P172284 targets 2.2 million people in 29 woredas including conflict-affected areas and 800,000 refugees <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a>; P175167 targets IDPs and conflict-affected populations for RMNCH services and health facility rehabilitation [P175167 | PAD]; and P180076 targets 600,000 pastoral households across eight lowland regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-334703ce-834f-4cd4-bf49-4e941b00bb0a.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – PAD</a>. Realized implementation evidence reveals systematic underperformance driven by FCV dynamics. The health project (P175167) has restored only 2 of 262 conflict-damaged health facilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>, with IDP clinics remaining non-operational due to Ministry of Finance counter-funding delays <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a> and mobile health team establishment blocked by the same fiscal constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>. Data quality assurance activities cannot be completed due to security constraints in conflict-affected regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>. The human capital project (P172284) faces delayed woreda selection for conflict-affected and refugee-hosting areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172284-c3e2849b-c5b6-4a37-b729-d72e2d877d90.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – Aide Memoire</a>, unfinalized environmental and social risk management instruments <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172284-c3e2849b-c5b6-4a37-b729-d72e2d877d90.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – Aide Memoire</a>, and weak coordination between regional and federal implementing agencies <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172284-c3e2849b-c5b6-4a37-b729-d72e2d877d90.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – Aide Memoire</a>. The pastoral livelihoods project (P180076) documents private sector disengagement from conflict-prone areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>, only 49% of conflict-prone woredas assessed for resource-related conflict management <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>, and sector bureau capacity constraints in Oromia diverting administrative resources to conflict response <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>. Cross-border spillover (ETH_2026_009) adds further pressure: refugee livelihood participation in P178047 stands at only 6% against a 30% target <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>, and urban refugee data gaps in Addis Ababa prevent accurate targeting for employment programs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>.
