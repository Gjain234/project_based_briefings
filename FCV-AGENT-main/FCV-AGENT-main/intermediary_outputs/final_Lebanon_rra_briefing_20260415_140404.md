**Social unrest and protests:** Lebanon's social fabric is under extreme stress from the convergence of mass displacement, economic collapse, and active armed conflict. Over 1.2 million people have been internally displaced since March 2, 2026, with government-run collective shelters restricting entry to Lebanese nationals and leaving Syrian, Palestinian, and migrant populations reliant on underfunded civil society organizations—a practice generating acute inter-communal tensions and social stigma against refugees. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project (LEAP) <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> explicitly identifies social exclusion risks from its area-based prioritization approach, noting that civil society concerns about prioritized areas and the exclusion of marginalized groups could undermine implementation. The Lebanon Social Protection Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08813b443&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173367 – PAD</a> similarly flags the risk that female-headed households, at-risk children, and GBV survivors may be excluded from targeting mechanisms under conditions of mass displacement. In implementation, the Social Recovery Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a> records sporadic armed strikes on South Lebanon and Beirut's southern suburbs continuing to threaten beneficiary access and staff safety, while NGO partners have lost core financing from US and other development partners, forcing operational scale-backs precisely when social protection demand is highest. <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-bde1794c-f97c-48b9-a56e-1949644a584e.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – Aide Memoire</a> The Lebanon Social Protection Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173367-72c123cf-9083-4eb3-a51a-8b251cf4b86f.pdf" target="_blank">P173367 – ISR</a> shows persistent high political and governance risk ratings, with zero progress across all PDO indicators including GBV support and child protection services, reflecting the collapse of social service delivery infrastructure under conflict conditions.

**Violence between refugees, host communities, and the state:** Lebanon hosts approximately 1.35 million Syrian refugees and 250,000 Palestinian refugees—the highest per-capita refugee burden globally—and the March 2026 escalation has generated secondary displacement of over 200,000 people into Syria and displaced 90,000 refugees a second time within Lebanon. Clashes in Ein El Hilweh Camp on March 29–30 resulted in two fatalities, road barricades, and suspension of UNRWA operations, while evacuation orders for Palestinian camps in Tyre (Burj Shemali, Rashidieh, El Buss) displaced approximately half their populations. The Lebanon Health Resilience Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a> identifies 1.5 million Syrian and Palestinian refugees as core beneficiaries and explicitly tracks refugee hospital utilization rates, acknowledging that high vaccine hesitancy among Syrian refugees and unequal access for vulnerable groups represent substantial social risks. The Lebanon Social Recovery Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a> notes that 86 percent of recorded GBV incidents in the first half of 2023 involved displaced Syrians, and 35 percent of mental health hotline callers are refugees, making this population central to project design. In implementation, the Health Resilience Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a> shows zero beneficiaries enrolled among the 180,000 displaced Syrian target population and zero conflict-related hospitalization costs financed against a target of 6,000, indicating complete failure to reach refugee populations. <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0867b7011&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163476 – ISR</a> The Social Recovery Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-bde1794c-f97c-48b9-a56e-1949644a584e.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – Aide Memoire</a> documents that several supported NGOs have lost core financing previously sourced from US resources, directly reducing capacity to serve refugee and displaced populations at the moment of maximum need.

**Organized violence between political and sectarian groups:** Hezbollah's unilateral decision on March 2, 2026 to launch rockets against Israel without government consent—prompting PM Salam to call the launches "irresponsible" and order arrests that were never made—has exposed the Lebanese state's inability to enforce its monopoly on force. The Lebanese Armed Forces commander objected to direct confrontation with Hezbollah, leaving government disarmament decrees unenforced and creating a critical gap between political declarations and implementation. UNIFIL peacekeepers have suffered multiple casualties (three Indonesian peacekeepers killed, Ghanaian peacekeepers wounded), face increasing denials of freedom of movement, and operate under a mandate expiring December 2026 with no renewal agreement. The Lebanon Reconstruction and Recovery Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> explicitly acknowledges that geopolitical risks from Middle East conflict may impede implementation in border areas, requires Lebanese Army clearance for ERW before investment in project areas, and identifies the state's inability to enforce monopoly on force as a core governance risk. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a> identifies the Hezbollah disarmament impasse as creating a sovereignty deficit that undermines implementing agencies CDR and BMLWE, noting that the crisis has forced allocation of limited human and financial resources to emergency response. In implementation, the Reconstruction and Recovery Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a> records persistent high ratings across political and governance, stakeholder, institutional capacity, and macroeconomic risk categories, with the project still in early implementation as renewed hostilities have halted the reconstruction momentum it was designed to support. The Beqaa Water Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a> documents that contractors suspended all works on the Zahle networks and Anjar contracts from September 23, 2024 due to military escalation in the Beqaa region, that a planned High-Level Meeting could not be held due to the security situation, and that unexploded ordnance clearance is now required before any work resumption.

**Political instability:** Lebanon's parliament voted 76 to 41 on March 9, 2026 to extend its own mandate by two years, postponing elections constitutionally required by May 2026—the fourth such extension in a decade. This eliminates a key reform milestone tied to World Bank and international partner conditionality and removes electoral accountability at a moment when Hezbollah's military weakness might otherwise have reduced its political influence. The Lebanon Public Finance Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-5067fa60-78e4-4f5e-b6c1-5a428d852b01.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – PAD</a> identifies parliamentary ratification of the loan agreement as a prerequisite for project effectiveness and notes that past challenges with loan agreement signing and parliamentary ratification have caused delays. The Lebanon Agriculture and Rural Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-c14c98c6-d426-4177-9a31-f1acaae2fb97.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – PAD</a> similarly identifies political and governance risk as high, noting that Lebanon's caretaker government context and political paralysis impede structural reforms. In implementation, the Public Finance Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a> records that the IBRD Financing Agreement is undergoing government internal clearances before parliamentary ratification, with the effectiveness deadline extended to August 2025 at government request—and the project notes that timely commencement is "critical for the post-war recovery phase." <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a> The Agriculture Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180334-4899e0cd-2b9b-432c-88d6-4a6b7c577697.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – ISR</a> shows the project was not effective as of February 2025, over seven months after the planned effectiveness date, with zero progress across all PDO indicators including farmers adopting technologies, SME loans approved, and rural roads rehabilitated—a complete implementation stall reflecting the constraining effects of political instability and governance fragility.

**External risks and intra-state conflict:** The March 2, 2026 escalation—triggered by Hezbollah rocket and drone strikes against Israel following the killing of Iranian Supreme Leader Khamenei—has killed at least 2,089 people including 165 children and 87 medical workers, destroyed most bridges south of the Litani River, forced closure of five hospitals and 51 primary healthcare centers, and generated displacement orders covering approximately 14 percent of Lebanon's territory. The Lebanon-Syria border crossings at Masnaa were closed from April 4 following IDF displacement orders, disrupting a key humanitarian corridor, while over 200,000 people have crossed into Syria since March 2, creating reverse displacement flows into a country still undergoing its own fragile post-Assad transition. The Solid Waste Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> explicitly identifies project locations in the Union of Municipalities of Tyre and Nabatieh—now active conflict zones—and requires field presence for waste characterization, dumpsite rehabilitation, and facility upgrades that cannot proceed safely. The Beirut Critical Environmental Recovery Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-829b3a34-aa4e-4b31-a4d1-5d469e9f50b4.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – PAD</a> identifies security risks from ongoing political instability and notes that the management of asbestos-contaminated rubble at the Bakalian site cannot be completed within the project timeline. In implementation, the Solid Waste Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a> documents that security escalation in South Lebanon has caused slippage in Component 2 implementation, that the ESIA for the Nabatieh sanitary landfill was delayed due to security issues, and that a stakeholder operating an adjacent open dump is threatening to damage the Srar Sanitary Landfill if not included in the project. <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-4727dec5-5d09-4ce2-8055-7dcb271aae12.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – ISR</a> The Beirut Critical Environmental Recovery Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-c459ed1c-d09c-47f2-831e-37821e0b095a.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – Aide Memoire</a> records that the unstable security situation in Nabatieh and Tyre has forced data collection to shift to virtual meetings and remote tools, that Component 2 implementation has been jeopardized by the security situation, and that occupational health and safety violations at the Karantina Sorting Facility—including unreported incidents requiring hospitalization—reflect the institutional fragility of reconstruction operations under active conflict conditions.
