# FCV Portfolio Briefing: Djibouti

## 1. Constitutional Revision Enabling Indefinite Presidential Rule

In October 2025, Djibouti removed the presidential age limit through constitutional revision, enabling President Ismail Omar Guelleh—in power since 1999—to seek a sixth term in April 2026. This fundamental governance deterioration directly undermines institutional checks and democratic norms, creating an environment of political uncertainty and reduced rule-of-law predictability.

**Project Susceptibility:** Five active World Bank projects depend on sustained government commitment, institutional continuity, and predictable policy frameworks. The Public Finance Management Reform project [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf) explicitly identifies political risk as "high," relying on government commitment to institutional reforms that require sustained political priority. The Education project [P166059 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes) depends on ministerial leadership stability and multi-year implementation coordination. The Corridor Development project [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes) requires inter-ministerial coordination and sustained bilateral engagement with Ethiopia. The Slum Upgrading project [P162901 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes) relies on government institutional capacity and resource allocation for land regularization and infrastructure delivery. The Refugee Integration project [P506969 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf) requires stable governance arrangements and political commitment to refugee services.

**Materialization Evidence:** The constitutional revision has already manifested in project implementation challenges. The PFM Reform project [P180696 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf) shows persistent "High" political and governance risk, with administrative delays in formalizing governance decrees despite ministerial approval. The Education project [P166059 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf) documents ministerial reshuffles as a "substantial" governance risk affecting project continuity. The Corridor project [P174300 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf) reports "Substantial" political and governance risk with persistent stakeholder engagement challenges. The Slum Upgrading project [P162901 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes) shows stalled land regularization since October 2023, coinciding with the constitutional revision period. These realized risks demonstrate that political consolidation is directly translating into institutional instability affecting project execution.

## 2. Severe Youth Unemployment and Economic Inequality

Youth unemployment in Djibouti exceeds 75%, compounded by high rates of inequality, poverty, and endemic corruption. Rising debt and limited economic opportunities create significant domestic pressure, particularly among a young population with few prospects. This structural economic crisis directly constrains the government's fiscal capacity and institutional effectiveness.

**Project Susceptibility:** Six projects explicitly target youth employment, skills development, or livelihood restoration, making them directly vulnerable to this risk. The TVET project [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf) targets 23,775 trained graduates for labor market absorption, but acknowledges "macroeconomic uncertainties" and proposes only "regular engagement with private sector stakeholders" without labor market demand assessment. The Slum Upgrading project [P162901 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes) targets youth in construction trades and low-to-middle income households, but faces implementation challenges if economic inequality persists. The Agri-Food project [P178836 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf) targets youth as direct beneficiaries but lacks macro-level unemployment context in mitigation strategies. The Corridor project [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes) identifies employment and livelihood benefits as project outcomes but provides no youth employment targets or mechanisms to ensure inclusive benefit distribution. The Food Security project [P178988 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf) targets livelihood restoration but does not specify youth employment pathways. The Statistics project [P171777 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087c0f9e4&stream=Yes) depends on government capacity to generate labor market data for evidence-based policymaking.

**Materialization Evidence:** The TVET project [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf) shows zero refugee employment outcomes (stuck at 5%) despite training completion, reflecting the structural inability of the labor market to absorb trained workers. The Agri-Food project [P178836 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178836-d1c431e1-fdc9-401c-a9e2-deed25b49034.pdf) reports difficulty identifying viable income-generating activities in remote localities, indicating that supply-side training interventions cannot overcome demand-side constraints. The Food Security project [P178988 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf) shows minimal financial execution and persistent procurement delays, suggesting institutional capacity constraints linked to resource scarcity. The Corridor project [P174300 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf) documents systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards, with 150 daily workers lacking social security registration—a direct manifestation of youth economic desperation and weak labor protections. The Slum Upgrading project [P162901 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes) shows stalled property rights documentation and delayed livelihood activities, undermining economic integration pathways for vulnerable populations.

## 3. Ethnic Tensions and Political Exclusion

Ethnic tensions are a primary driver of Djibouti's politics, with the Issa Somali ethnic group (approximately 60% of population) dominating governance while the Afar minority (approximately 35%) remains politically marginalized. This structural imbalance fuels grievances and limits inclusive political participation, creating institutional fragmentation that undermines project implementation.

**Project Susceptibility:** Four projects explicitly target vulnerable populations across ethnically diverse regions, creating implementation friction if ethnic representation in beneficiary selection or institutional governance is perceived as favoring or excluding particular groups. The TVET project [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf) targets refugees (predominantly Somali) and persons with disabilities but lacks explicit ethnic representation safeguards in steering committees. The Refugee Integration project [P506969 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf) targets 12,860 refugees and 35,900 host community residents, explicitly aiming to "strengthen social cohesion," but does not address how ethnic tensions might affect project acceptance or benefit distribution. The Slum Upgrading project [P162901 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes) targets 115,000 slum inhabitants including refugees and displaced persons but does not address ethnic composition of target areas or mechanisms to ensure equitable benefit distribution. The Corridor project [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes) lists "populations living in corridor area" as beneficiaries but provides no disaggregation by ethnicity or targeted inclusion mechanisms for marginalized groups.

**Materialization Evidence:** The Refugee Integration project [P506969 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506969-c23fc0d1-df16-470e-9ddf-fdd831536a8b.pdf) reports "substantial" social conflict risk and notes project addresses "both refugees and host communities to strengthen social cohesion," but implementation tensions between refugee and host populations persist. The Slum Upgrading project [P162901 – ISR](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes) documents stalled refugee-led CSO participation (zero implementation), suggesting systemic barriers to inclusion of marginalized populations. The Corridor project [P174300 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf) shows persistent "Substantial" stakeholder engagement risk with coordination challenges across diverse constituencies. These realized risks suggest that ethnic dynamics are creating implementation friction despite project design intentions to be inclusive.

## 4. Collapse of Independent Civil Society and Media Freedom

Djibouti has virtually no independent civil society remaining, with only marginal voices present on social media. Freedom House classifies the country as Not Free, citing severe restrictions on political competition and media freedom, with journalists and activists regularly harassed or arrested for criticizing the government. This institutional hollowing removes accountability mechanisms and transparency that are foundational to effective project governance.

**Project Susceptibility:** All six active projects depend on stakeholder engagement, grievance mechanisms, and transparent accountability structures that are structurally compromised in this governance environment. The TVET project [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf) establishes a grievance mechanism with "confidential reporting" but effectiveness is undermined by absence of independent civil society to validate or amplify concerns. The PFM Reform project [P180696 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf) Component 3 explicitly targets "Strengthen Oversight of Public Finances and Civil Society Engagement," but assumes civil society organizations can meaningfully participate in oversight—an assumption contradicted by the country context. The Education project [P166059 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes) includes "Mobilisation des parties prenantes et diffusion de l'information" but operates in an environment where community participation and transparent feedback mechanisms face pressure or self-censorship. The Corridor project [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes) includes "Stakeholder Engagement Plan" and "project-level grievance redress mechanisms" but does not acknowledge that restricted civic space may limit effectiveness of these mechanisms. The Food Security project [P178988 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf) establishes a Grievance Redress Mechanism but does not address how beneficiaries will safely report concerns in an environment where activists face harassment.

**Materialization Evidence:** The PFM Reform project [P180696 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-78d5caca-0d39-443d-8d7b-742a76e39ce8.pdf) shows persistent "High" political and governance risk with weak institutional capacity for implementing governance structures that depend on transparency and civil society oversight. The TVET project [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf) reports inactive steering committee and delayed stakeholder mobilization plans, reflecting institutional constraints in convening autonomous stakeholder engagement. The Education project [P166059 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf) shows low functionality of School Management Committees (62%), suggesting weak community-level governance structures in a context of restricted civic space. The Corridor project [P174300 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf) documents systemic non-compliance with labor and social protection standards, with critical safety violations omitted from official government reports—a manifestation of weak institutional accountability and limited independent oversight. The Food Security project [P178988 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf) shows weak community grievance mechanisms and limited beneficiary participation, reflecting the broader institutional environment of constrained civic space.

## 5. FRUD-armé Insurgent Activity and Terrorism Threat

The splinter group FRUD-armé launched a raid on an army post in October 2023, killing seven soldiers and injuring four. Classified as a terrorist group by the National Assembly, FRUD-armé represents an ongoing domestic security threat amid Djibouti's proximity to al-Shabaab and other terrorist organizations in Somalia. This active insurgent presence creates direct operational risks to project implementation in rural and border-proximate areas.

**Project Susceptibility:** Four projects operate in geographically dispersed rural regions and refugee-hosting areas that may be vulnerable to security disruption. The TVET project [P175483 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf) operates across six regions including Ali Sabieh and Obock, geographically proximate to areas of potential FRUD-armé activity, with 3,165 refugee beneficiaries concentrated in Ali-Addeh and Holl-Holl camps in Ali Sabieh region. The Agri-Food project [P178836 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf) operates in Northern Zone (Tadjourah and Obock regions) with climate-proof public infrastructure (boreholes, solar pumping, rural roads) in remote areas potentially vulnerable to insurgent activity. The Corridor project [P174300 – PAD](http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088bdcd4b&stream=Yes) acknowledges project is in "CONFLICT-AFFECTED AREAS: yes" with vulnerability to "regional spillovers from Horn of Africa conflicts." The Food Security project [P178988 – PAD](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ab0d00ef-27b4-4e55-b4d9-d19643e5cfb2.pdf) operates across six regions and rural areas where security incidents could disrupt access to beneficiaries and supply chains.

**Materialization Evidence:** The Agri-Food project [P178836 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178836-d1c431e1-fdc9-401c-a9e2-deed25b49034.pdf) asserts "no security risks identified for project implementation" despite FRUD-armé's October 2023 raid on army posts, suggesting risk assessment may underestimate the threat. The Corridor project [P174300 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf) documents persistent security concerns and road safety risks but does not detail specific FRUD-armé threat mitigation. The Food Security project [P178988 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf) acknowledges "regional instability affecting trade routes and economic activity" but does not address specific security protocols for operations in areas with insurgent presence. The TVET project [P175483 – ISR](https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf) notes project operates in "conflict-affected areas (partial)" but does not detail threat assessment for Ali Sabieh region where refugee beneficiaries are concentrated. These gaps suggest that security risks from active insurgent groups are not being adequately incorporated into project risk management frameworks.

---

**Conclusion:** Djibouti's FCV context presents five interconnected country-level risks that are actively materializing across the World Bank portfolio. Constitutional manipulation is creating institutional instability affecting project governance; severe youth unemployment is constraining labor market absorption of trained beneficiaries; ethnic tensions are creating implementation friction in inclusive targeting; collapsed civil society is removing accountability mechanisms; and active insurgent threats are creating operational security challenges. These risks are not theoretical—they are manifesting as stalled implementation, unachieved outcomes, and governance failures across multiple projects. Sustained engagement requires explicit risk mitigation strategies that acknowledge the severity of Djibouti's governance deterioration and its direct impact on development project effectiveness.
