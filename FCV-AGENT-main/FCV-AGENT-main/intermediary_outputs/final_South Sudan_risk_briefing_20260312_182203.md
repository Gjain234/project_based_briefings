# South Sudan FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Collapse of the R-ARCSS Peace Agreement and Return to Full-Scale War

South Sudan's 2018 Revitalized Agreement on the Resolution of the Conflict (R-ARCSS) has effectively collapsed following President Kiir's arrest of First Vice President Riek Machar in March 2025, systematic exclusion of SPLM-IO officials from power-sharing, and a major SSPDF offensive launched in Jonglei State in late January 2026 involving aerial bombardments of civilian areas. The UN Security Council was warned in February 2026 that the country stands "on the brink of collapse," and the International Crisis Group assessed that South Sudan had "returned to war." Every project in the portfolio is acutely susceptible to this risk. Projects spanning all ten states—including the Enhancing Community Resilience and Local Governance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-e8d67f4f-bc76-49af-9b6c-6099c748075b.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – PAD</a>, the South Sudan Electricity Access Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3c065209-7373-4a16-92d2-eff384787700.pdf" target="_blank">P178891 – PAD</a>, the Health System Transformation Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2886229a-f8b3-472a-bcdc-2ee681b5fe59.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – PAD</a>, the Education Sector Transformation Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-6bf0132a-5b36-4765-b031-3d0de461f77e.pdf" target="_blank">P178654 – PAD</a>, and the Public Financial Management Modernization Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088d01007&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176761 – PAD</a>—depend on functional government institutions, stable Project Steering Committees, and predictable policy environments that are incompatible with full-scale war. The PFM project explicitly links its reform agenda to R-ARCSS implementation, making the agreement's collapse a direct threat to its theory of change <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088d01007&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176761 – PAD</a>. Materialization is confirmed across multiple projects: the PFM project reports 9–10 months of civil service salary arrears, delayed budget submissions, and failure to constitute the National Assembly on schedule <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176761-bf79f177-54c7-42fd-a88d-6f811275abe1.pdf" target="_blank">P176761 – ISR</a>; the health project documents government co-financing arrears overdue since October 2024 and Ministry of Health budget execution at only 31% of target <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181385-31c255fc-69d1-4cc6-88c2-e37e28acd22f.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – Aide Memoire</a>; and the agriculture project records persistent weak MAFS institutional capacity and delayed presidential endorsement of the Food Security Crisis Preparedness Plan <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169120-4884fdbc-7d3a-4d98-b147-22c863449688.pdf" target="_blank">P169120 – Aide Memoire</a>. The return to active conflict is now the defining constraint on portfolio-wide implementation.

## 2. Escalating Military Offensive, Atrocity Risks, and Humanitarian Access Crisis in Jonglei and Upper Nile States

The SSPDF's January 2026 offensive in Jonglei State—involving aerial bombardments of civilian areas, "no quarter" rhetoric from senior commanders, and a massacre in Abiemnom County that killed 178 civilians—has created an acute security and humanitarian access crisis across the portfolio's most concentrated implementation zones. WFP suspended activities in Baliet County, Upper Nile State, after repeated attacks on humanitarian convoys; MSF reported the bombing of a hospital in Lankien as the tenth attack on medical facilities in twelve months; and OCHA documented a sharp deterioration in humanitarian access in January 2026. Projects are directly exposed because their target counties overlap precisely with the conflict epicenter. The community resilience project targets Fangak, Twic East, Pibor, Maban, Baliet, and Renk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-e8d67f4f-bc76-49af-9b6c-6099c748075b.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – PAD</a>; the livestock project covers Bor County, Twic East, and Fashoda <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-f81886be-210a-4bc0-9a92-5cf656fbbc43.pdf" target="_blank">P500553 – PAD</a>; the health project operates in Maban, Renk, Jamjang, and Pochalla <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2886229a-f8b3-472a-bcdc-2ee681b5fe59.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – PAD</a>; and the energy project targets the Greater Upper Nile Region including Jonglei, Unity, and Ruweng <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3c065209-7373-4a16-92d2-eff384787700.pdf" target="_blank">P178891 – PAD</a>. Risks are actively materializing: the health project documents the closure of over twenty HSTP-supported facilities due to security challenges, including aerial bombardment in New Fangak causing facility closure and population displacement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181385-31c255fc-69d1-4cc6-88c2-e37e28acd22f.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – Aide Memoire</a>; pharmaceutical deliveries to Nasir and Ulang counties remain unresolved due to access denial <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181385-31c255fc-69d1-4cc6-88c2-e37e28acd22f.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – Aide Memoire</a>; the community resilience project cannot deploy its Third Party Monitoring Agent to remote conflict-affected areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177093-fdc25708-770e-40de-aa94-2679135bcf13.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – ISR</a>; and the hydromet project has been unable to complete security risk assessments required before construction can begin <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180171-3f71edda-0b3c-44d1-b96c-bb39b74b4af1.pdf" target="_blank">P180611 – Aide Memoire</a>. The humanitarian access crisis is not a background condition but an active implementation barrier across the portfolio.

## 3. Mass Internal Displacement and Cross-Border Spillover from Sudan's Conflict

South Sudan's displacement crisis has reached catastrophic scale: 3.2 million internally displaced persons as of 2025 (a 40% increase over the prior year), with 280,000 newly displaced since late December 2025—235,000 of them in Jonglei State alone—alongside nearly 600,000 Sudanese refugees and over 800,000 South Sudanese returnees from Sudan concentrated in Upper Nile and Unity states. Sudan's ongoing civil war compounds this through pipeline disruption (the Petrodar pipeline was damaged in February 2024 and only partially restored), RSF presence along the porous border, and the risk that the two conflicts could merge. Projects are structurally exposed because they target displacement-affected populations and operate in the highest-burden states. The community resilience project serves 920,000 beneficiaries including IDPs, refugees, and host communities across twelve counties, with US$20 million from the IDA Window for Host Communities and Refugees specifically targeting Maban and Pariang <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-e8d67f4f-bc76-49af-9b6c-6099c748075b.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – PAD</a>; the health project serves 330,000 refugees and host communities concentrated in Jamjang and Bunj <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2886229a-f8b3-472a-bcdc-2ee681b5fe59.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – PAD</a>; the education project has modified its development objective to explicitly include refugee and host community children <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178654-1d2a0a33-5408-4d44-962f-daf3b0692ffa.pdf" target="_blank">P178654 – ISR</a>; and the WASH project operates in refugee settlements experiencing vandalism, limited repair capacity, and coordination gaps among government, humanitarian, and development actors <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180171-3f71edda-0b3c-44d1-b96c-bb39b74b4af1.pdf" target="_blank">P500441 – Aide Memoire</a>. Materialization is confirmed: the education project reports no school rehabilitation work commenced in refugee-hosting areas as of September 2025 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178654-1d2a0a33-5408-4d44-962f-daf3b0692ffa.pdf" target="_blank">P178654 – ISR</a>; the health project shows Boma Health Initiative coverage at only 16% in refugee areas against a 26% target for antenatal care <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181385-5b09fa0c-9926-43db-adf4-616d047b7fcb.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – ISR</a>; and the community resilience project documents active tensions between refugee and host communities over infrastructure allocation in Pariang and Maban <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177093-fdc25708-770e-40de-aa94-2679135bcf13.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – ISR</a>. The Sudan spillover is simultaneously a fiscal shock—oil revenues remain severely constrained—and a humanitarian pressure multiplier across the portfolio.

## 4. Acute Food Insecurity, Health System Collapse, and the USAID Funding Vacuum

Approximately 7.7 million people face acute food insecurity during lean months, with parts of Luakpiny/Nasir County in Upper Nile State at IPC Phase 5 famine risk. The dismantling of USAID in early 2025—the largest single humanitarian donor—has directly collapsed emergency response systems: ProPublica documented that funding cuts contributed to South Sudan's worst cholera epidemic in its history, with 36,000 infections and over 600 deaths in the first three months alone. WFP is providing only 50–70% rations to 2.7 million people against a US$398.9 million funding shortfall. Projects are susceptible because they operate in the same geographic and population space as the humanitarian crisis, and because the collapse of complementary systems undermines the enabling environment for development programming. The agriculture project explicitly targets IPC Phase 3+ households and has activated its Crisis Response Window to support 47,444 farmers, with 66% female beneficiaries <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169120-7aa9496d-b33a-4d6f-9215-13a79cf81b04.pdf" target="_blank">P169120 – ISR</a>; the health project's nutrition component targets populations where acute malnutrition rates reach 31.1% among refugee children under five and 63.4% among refugee pregnant and lactating women <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-2886229a-f8b3-472a-bcdc-2ee681b5fe59.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – PAD</a>; and the livestock project operates in pastoral communities where food insecurity is structurally embedded <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-f81886be-210a-4bc0-9a92-5cf656fbbc43.pdf" target="_blank">P500553 – PAD</a>. Materialization is confirmed: the health project documents that government health worker incentive payments are delayed, Boma Health Worker attrition is rising, and County Health Department governance is unstable—all directly linked to the fiscal and health system collapse <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181385-31c255fc-69d1-4cc6-88c2-e37e28acd22f.pdf" target="_blank">P181385 – Aide Memoire</a>. The agriculture project has activated emergency flood response mechanisms and faces persistent fiduciary weaknesses including ineligible expenditures and absent internal audit functions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169120-4884fdbc-7d3a-4d98-b147-22c863449688.pdf" target="_blank">P169120 – Aide Memoire</a>. The USAID vacuum has created a humanitarian gap that development projects cannot fill but are increasingly being pressured to absorb.

## 5. Governance Collapse: Political Repression, Fiscal Fragility, and UNMISS Degradation

South Sudan's governance environment has deteriorated across three mutually reinforcing dimensions. First, the National Security Service has escalated repression under the 2024 NSS Act, with at least 114 documented cases of censorship, harassment, and arbitrary arrests of civil society actors and journalists between 2022 and 2025, directly threatening the participatory planning, third-party monitoring, and grievance redress mechanisms that projects depend on. Second, the economy faces structural collapse: inflation averaged 143% in FY2024/25, poverty has risen to 92% of the population, and over 90% of government revenue derives from oil exports transiting Sudan's conflict-affected pipeline—with NilePet operating as a "black box" and oil revenues pre-sold through 2027. Third, UNMISS has been simultaneously degraded by government hostility (a demand to reduce military contingents by 70%, closure of bases in Wau and Bentiu, and grounding of four aircraft in December 2025) and UN-wide austerity (a 15% budget cut translating to a 25% reduction in uniformed personnel and closure of field offices). Projects are susceptible because they rely on civil society engagement, government co-financing, and the security umbrella that UNMISS provides. The PFM project's community monitoring groups, budget literacy workshops, and media partnerships require functioning civic space <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088d01007&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176761 – PAD</a>; the central bank strengthening project depends on BoSS institutional independence that is incompatible with political repression <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-d2045e4c-b687-437a-8ee2-eb1edb36395a.pdf" target="_blank">P179278 – PAD</a>; and the civil service reform project faces stakeholder resistance to pay restructuring in a context where government troops are largely unpaid <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P500500-27e0959b-29c3-405a-a132-a7a152fe1689.pdf" target="_blank">P500500 – ISR</a>. Materialization is confirmed across the portfolio: the PFM project reports recurrent reassignment of trained technical counterparts without official communication, 9-month salary arrears, failure to roll out a Grievance Redress Mechanism, and a Finance Ministry restructuring that has not been reflected in project design <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176761-16ef46cf-8fa6-4044-bc47-479902ca2cbd.pdf" target="_blank">P176761 – Aide Memoire</a>; the central bank project carries persistent High fiduciary and macroeconomic risk ratings <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P179278-6eaa8a2f-4164-43d4-a658-b3edad29f602.pdf" target="_blank">P179278 – ISR</a>; the energy project documents contractor fraud, unauthorized payments, asset theft, and a payment dispute between JEDCO and MoED <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178891-008ca349-4c37-4697-9301-9c6dbd23e9ef.pdf" target="_blank">P178891 – Aide Memoire</a>; and the community resilience project has not established O&M and DNP management modalities for post-project sustainability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177093-fdc25708-770e-40de-aa94-2679135bcf13.pdf" target="_blank">P177093 – ISR</a>. The convergence of repression, fiscal collapse, and peacekeeping degradation represents a systemic governance failure that no individual project-level mitigation can adequately address.
