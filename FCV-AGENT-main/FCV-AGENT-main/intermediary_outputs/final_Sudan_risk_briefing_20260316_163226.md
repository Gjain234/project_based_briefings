# Sudan FCV Portfolio Briefing

## P504629 – Health System Preservation and Service Delivery

Sudan's active multi-front war between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) poses an existential operational threat to this health project. Kordofan and Darfur—both designated priority implementation areas—have emerged as the epicentres of hostilities, with drone strikes directly targeting hospitals, including the attack on Dilling General Hospital in March 2026. The project design explicitly acknowledged that 75% of health facilities in conflict-affected areas were already non-functional at appraisal, that facilities had been attacked and looted, and that ongoing security risks from armed groups, extortion, and theft would require a customized Security Management Framework and tiered access protocols <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The mass displacement crisis—with over 11 million internally displaced persons identified at appraisal—further complicates beneficiary identification, as population mobility undermines targeting accuracy and creates social tensions between IDPs and host communities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. Famine conditions confirmed in El Fasher and Kadugli, both within project priority zones, threaten staff retention, beneficiary access, and the effectiveness of the severe acute malnutrition management component <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The project also identified high risks of sexual exploitation and abuse in the FCV context, given the engagement of community health workers and cash transfer mechanisms with vulnerable displaced women and girls <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. These risks are now clearly materializing. Implementation Status Reports confirm that shifting frontlines are continuously forcing adaptation of facility targeting and supply chain approaches, with high ratings recorded across political and governance, institutional capacity, stakeholder engagement, and other risk categories <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The RSF's proclamation of a parallel government in Darfur and Kordofan has fragmented the governance environment, undermining coordination with recognized authorities and deepening institutional capacity constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. Deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties—including drone strikes on WFP operations—is directly constraining the project's ability to maintain consistent service delivery and supply replenishment to remaining functional facilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The severe humanitarian funding gap, with Sudan's 2026 response plan receiving only 5.5% of required funds, threatens the complementary services on which health interventions depend for effectiveness <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. Looking forward, the risk of renewed cholera and waterborne disease outbreaks ahead of the rainy season, combined with collapsed immunization systems and overcrowded displacement sites, will further overwhelm project capacity in ways that the disease surveillance component alone cannot contain <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>.

## P505963 – Emergency Safety Net and Cash Transfer Operations

This cash transfer project targeting 602,000 beneficiaries across conflict-affected states faces compounding and mutually reinforcing risks that are actively materializing across all major risk dimensions. At appraisal, the project acknowledged high security risks from ongoing armed conflict, including grand robbery and looting of life-saving aid by soldiers, and identified West Kordofan and South Kordofan—now confirmed epicentres of hostilities with drone strikes on hospitals—as implementation locations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The project's cash transfer design, providing US$10 per person per month to cover 50% of daily caloric needs, was already calibrated for IPC Phase 3 and 4 conditions; the confirmation of famine (IPC Phase 5) in El Fasher and Kadugli, and the projected spread to 20 additional areas across Greater Darfur and Greater Kordofan, directly undermines the adequacy of transfer values and the viability of market-based delivery in famine zones <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The project operates under the World Bank's policy for dealings with de facto governments, reflecting the political fragmentation acknowledged at design; the RSF's subsequent proclamation of a parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" in Darfur has deepened this fragmentation, creating competing authority claims over project areas and complicating WFP and UNICEF's ability to obtain permissions and coordinate across dual governance structures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. High risks of sexual exploitation and abuse were identified at appraisal given large IDP populations, weak GBV response mechanisms, and power differentials during cash distribution, with particular concern about women's safe access to health facilities required under the conditional transfer component <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. These risks are now fully realized. Implementation Status Reports confirm high ratings across political and governance, macroeconomic, institutional capacity, fiduciary, environmental and social, and other risk categories <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. Hyperinflation and currency depreciation are eroding the real purchasing power of cash transfers, while the severe humanitarian funding gap—with WFP forced to reduce food rations to survival minimums—is compounding the macroeconomic environment in which transfers must function <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. Deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access, including drone strikes on WFP operations since February 2026, is directly constraining the project's ability to reach beneficiaries in Kordofan and Darfur, where access is documented as increasingly restrained <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The suppression of civil society and arbitrary detention of local volunteers and activists is narrowing the pool of reliable NGO and community-based implementing partners on whom the project's community targeting and grievance mechanisms depend <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The combination of stalled peace diplomacy, proxy dynamics involving the UAE and Saudi Arabia, and the non-constitution of the UN Sanctions Panel of Experts removes any near-term prospect of the governance stabilization on which project sustainability depends.

## P181490 – Integrated Service Delivery for IDPs and Host Communities

This project, targeting 560,000 IDP and host community beneficiaries across Kassala, Northern, and River Nile states, was designed around the premise that its target areas were currently neutral and not strongholds of either warring party—a selection criterion that reflects the project team's awareness that geographic security is the primary determinant of operational viability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The PAD identified high political and governance risk from non-functional federal institutions, reliance on resource-constrained state and locality-level authorities, and the risk of conflict escalation into target areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The mass displacement crisis—with over 5.5 million IDPs in target states at appraisal, 64% in urban centres—creates dynamic and unpredictable beneficiary populations that challenge targeting accuracy and risk exacerbating social tensions between IDPs and host communities over project benefits <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The project's food security component, which aims to increase local agricultural production and support community consumer cooperatives, faces the risk that famine conditions spreading from Darfur and Kordofan could overwhelm its developmental approach and require a pivot to emergency response beyond project scope <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The project's community-led governance model through Community Development Committees, with minimum quotas for women and IDPs, is vulnerable to arbitrary detention and retaliatory attacks against local volunteers and activists documented by both warring parties <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing across multiple dimensions. Implementation Status Reports confirm high ratings for political and governance instability, institutional capacity, and IDP-host community social cohesion <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The de facto fragmentation of state authority between the SAF-aligned government and the RSF's parallel administration is undermining the coherence of state and locality-level institutions on which the project depends for coordination <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. Salary arrears for frontline health workers and teachers—a realized risk documented in implementation reporting—are directly contributing to the collapse of the health system and reducing service delivery capacity for displaced populations and returnees <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The severe humanitarian funding gap, with only 5.5% of the 2026 response plan funded, is intensifying resource competition between IDPs and host communities and constraining the project's ability to deliver equitable services to both groups <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The project's reliance on UN humanitarian supply routes and the UN security umbrella is increasingly precarious given documented drone strikes on WFP operations and the deliberate obstruction of aid movement in Kordofan and Darfur, which could affect supply chains even to the project's relatively more stable target states.

## P504621 – Learning Continuity and School Grants

This education project faces a fundamental implementation blockage: schools in conflict-affected target areas remain closed due to active hostilities, preventing the distribution of school grants and the delivery of learning materials to primary school-age children. The project's design acknowledged the conflict-driven learning crisis, incorporating a learning continuity component for out-of-school children and a Security Management Framework requirement, but the SMF remained pending clearance as of the most recent Aide Memoire review—a delay that itself reflects the unresolved security threats in Kordofan and Darfur where escalating violence is documented <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The mass displacement of 14 million people, including 7.3 million IDPs, has fragmented the school-age beneficiary population across multiple locations and countries, making beneficiary identification and material delivery logistically extremely difficult <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties extends to educational services, with restrictions on aid movement creating systemic barriers to reaching schools and distributing grants in conflict zones <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The project's financial management and procurement performance reflects these operational realities starkly: fund utilization stood at only 7.3%, and procurement expenditure reached just US$281,000 of US$16.8 million in signed contracts as of April 2025—a 98.3% underspend rate driven by active conflict, access obstruction, and governance fragmentation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Third-party monitoring recruitment was delayed due to budgetary constraints, creating a verification vacuum precisely when oversight is most critical in an environment of systematic obstruction and civil society suppression <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Implementation Status Reports confirm high ratings across institutional capacity, stakeholder engagement, fiduciary, and other risk categories <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The RSF's parallel administration in Darfur and the SAF-aligned government in Khartoum create competing jurisdictions that fracture the institutional accountability chains required for school grant management <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The suppression of civil society and arbitrary detention of activists and local volunteers—including retaliatory attacks against emergency response volunteers—is narrowing the pool of trusted local partners and community representatives available for school selection, learning hub coordination, and grievance redress <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The systematic attacks on civilians and documented impunity create conditions where schools cannot safely reopen even in areas not directly experiencing active combat, as the pervasive insecurity environment deters both students and teachers from returning <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. Without resolution of the security situation and clearance of the Security Management Framework, the project's core grant distribution objective cannot be achieved at meaningful scale.

## P507788 – Energy and Digital Infrastructure

This project faces a governance and institutional environment that is fundamentally hostile to the implementation of energy and digital infrastructure investments. Implementation Status Reports confirm high ratings across political and governance instability, stakeholder engagement, fiduciary risk, and sector policy instability—a comprehensive risk profile that reflects the structural collapse of the operating environment rather than project-specific implementation failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The RSF's proclamation of a parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" in Darfur and the SAF-aligned government's return to Khartoum have created competing governance authorities with no unified national framework capable of establishing coherent energy or digital sector policies <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. Infrastructure projects require clear institutional counterparts, enforceable regulatory frameworks, and stable policy environments; none of these conditions currently exist in Sudan, where territorial control determines which government's directives apply and where both parties issue conflicting policy signals <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The failure of multiple parallel peace initiatives—the US-led Quad, the AU Quintet, and bilateral Egyptian efforts—means there is no credible near-term pathway to the governance stabilization on which sector policy coherence depends <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. Stakeholder engagement is severely constrained by the de facto partition of the country, with communities aligned with different de facto authorities, government actors operating under military control, and civil society suppressed through arbitrary detention and political repression <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. Fiduciary risk is compounded by the absence of unified legitimate authority to oversee financial management and procurement, with dual governance structures creating competing claims on resources and unclear accountability chains across fragmented territorial control <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The suppression of civil society removes independent monitoring of financial management and procurement processes, creating conditions where fiduciary controls cannot function effectively regardless of institutional intent <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties—including attacks on aid workers and restrictions on movement in Kordofan and Darfur—extends to the broader operational environment for development activities, limiting the ability to conduct field assessments, engage communities, and maintain supply chains for infrastructure investments. The combination of active multi-front warfare, de facto partition, stalled diplomacy, and systematic civil society suppression creates a risk environment in which meaningful infrastructure implementation is not currently feasible without fundamental changes to the security and governance context.
