# Honduras Portfolio FCV Briefing

## 1. Gang Violence, Organized Crime, and Internal Displacement

Honduras faces a severe and expanding organized crime threat that directly imperils portfolio delivery across multiple projects. The end of the state of emergency on January 26, 2026, has removed a key security buffer, while the police force has actually shrunk to 17,436 officers—leaving the state structurally under-resourced against 63+ active criminal groups. Violence in Colón and Olancho departments increased 27% and 44% respectively during the Castro term, and extortion now affects over 300,000 households nationally. Forced displacement driven by criminal land seizures, drug trafficking, and gender-based violence is ongoing in Colón, Olancho, Atlántida, and Yoro—the same departments where several portfolio projects operate. The CA-13 road corridor project is acutely exposed: the PAD explicitly identifies the Bajo Aguán region as affected by "organized crime, drug trafficking, and weak state presence," with an estimated 4,000+ labor influx workers in remote areas with limited supervision capacity, and acknowledges that criminal groups seize land and displace families along the project corridor <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-16ccfa88-c5fd-4b6a-acfc-b69c54b5e6ac.pdf" target="_blank">P507958 – PAD</a>. The civil registry project's mobile enrollment brigades are designed to operate in rural areas including Colón and Olancho, where gang violence could disrupt team operations, compromise beneficiary safety, and enable criminal interference in enrollment of minors <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-26698d51-7b79-45d6-aa2e-9655368e9797.pdf" target="_blank">P181324 – PAD</a>. The health project covers 15 priority regions including Colón, Olancho, Yoro, and Atlántida, but the PAD provides no conflict-sensitive operational protocols for staff safety or supply chain security in gang-controlled areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-b7e241ff-57b5-48f8-bf67-ec450fd3e23a.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – PAD</a>. The rural recovery project explicitly identifies "crime and gang violence" as a substantial risk during disaster response and reconstruction, noting that project workers and local communities face security threats <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08810980a&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P175977 – PAD</a>. The agricultural competitiveness project acknowledges that "violence and security issues could significantly impact implementation" in departments including Colón, Atlántida, Cortés, and Yoro <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08727296c&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P168385 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing: the COMRURAL II project reports zero progress on Component 5 activities in the Moskitia and Atlantic Coast regions, where gang violence and drug trafficking operations are concentrated and local recruitment responses have been negligible <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174328-0b10c145-c009-4dcc-9632-f51dc2d9f149.pdf" target="_blank">P174328 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174328-9bad92a5-4a40-4385-a927-1a6e1543d574.pdf" target="_blank">P174328 – Aide Memoire</a>. The early childhood education project has incorporated a school crime and violence prevention curriculum unit as a direct institutional response to gang presence in target departments <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169161-307bd03a-1abe-4fa6-827d-521a77f09929.pdf" target="_blank">P169161 – ISR</a>. The CA-13 corridor project's ISR confirms substantial environmental and social risks in a fragile context, with land clearance delays plausibly connected to criminal territorial control in Colón and Olancho <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507958-45d3e1b1-f9de-4993-9973-a637d8ba1143.pdf" target="_blank">P507958 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181166-f47a33e2-4d5e-436f-ad6a-4cee5e45577c.pdf" target="_blank">P181166 – ISR</a>.

## 2. Systemic Corruption, Institutional Impunity, and Governance Collapse

Honduras's classification as a "highly defective democracy" with structural impunity, institutional capture, and a judiciary under-resourced and affected by corruption and intimidation creates pervasive implementation risk across the entire portfolio. President Asfura himself is under investigation for embezzlement from his tenure as mayor of Tegucigalpa, and 40 days into his administration, UNAH reported no formal Health Ministry leadership had been appointed and governance signals were absent. The anti-corruption commission (CICIH) pledged under Castro was never established, leaving structural impunity intact. Every project in the portfolio carries substantial fiduciary and governance risk rooted in this environment. The CA-13 road project's PAD identifies SIT's lack of World Bank experience, weak FM governance structure, and risk of non-compliance with Bank policies as substantial concerns in a context of systemic impunity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-16ccfa88-c5fd-4b6a-acfc-b69c54b5e6ac.pdf" target="_blank">P507958 – PAD</a>. The civil registry project's PAD flags RNP's lack of prior World Bank experience, the need to create a PIU from scratch, and substantial risks around biometric data protection—all amplified by the corruption and institutional capture documented at the national level <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-26698d51-7b79-45d6-aa2e-9655368e9797.pdf" target="_blank">P181324 – PAD</a>. The health project's PAD identifies SESAL's limited experience managing World Bank initiatives and the UAP's limited capacity as substantial risks, while noting that the government's commitment to absorb 30% additional health workers on payroll creates a risk of inadequate funding for basic health system functions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-b7e241ff-57b5-48f8-bf67-ec450fd3e23a.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – PAD</a>. The social protection project's PAD rates fiduciary risk as "Alta," citing complex inter-institutional arrangements and the risk that violence and security issues could significantly impact implementation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ba0766&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – PAD</a>. These risks are actively materializing: the civil registry project's ISR confirms substantial political and governance risk ratings, with RNP delays in establishing governance structures and staffing the PIU <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181324-909b5b4e-f005-43e1-a600-77e4ad8ea6e1.pdf" target="_blank">P181324 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181324-909b5b4e-f005-43e1-a600-77e4ad8ea6e1.pdf" target="_blank">P181324 – ISR</a>. The social protection project's ISRs document suspended urban cash transfers since 2022, inconsistent education transfer payments, a stalled Social Protection Policy blocked by "complex institutional clearances," and a cancelled RUP certification process requiring a replacement registry (SIRBHO) <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088251625&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – ISR</a> <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088251625&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – ISR</a> <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088251625&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – ISR</a>. The COMRURAL II project's Aide Memoire documents critical data security vulnerabilities in the project management system—SQL injections, weak session management, and lack of automatic backups—that create direct exposure to fund misappropriation in a high-impunity environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174328-9bad92a5-4a40-4385-a927-1a6e1543d574.pdf" target="_blank">P174328 – Aide Memoire</a>. ## 3. Public Health System Crisis and Collapse of Service Delivery

Honduras's public health system is documented as "on the verge of collapse," with thousands of patients on surgery waiting lists, acute medicine and supply shortages, and no formal Health Ministry leadership appointed 40 days into the Asfura government. On March 2, 2026, Asfura terminated the Cuba medical cooperation agreement, and 168 Cuban doctors departed on March 4—removing a significant healthcare workforce from underserved communities. The IHSS, covering roughly one-third of the public health system, continues to suffer long waits, medicine shortages, and inadequate care with no structural reforms in sight. This crisis creates direct and indirect exposure across the portfolio. The health project's PAD identifies the government's commitment to absorb additional health workers as creating a risk of inadequate funding for basic health system functions, and the project's Component 1 activities (maternal, neonatal, and adolescent health services) depend on facility rehabilitation and health worker deployment that are contingent on SESAL's ability to maintain and deploy workforce <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-b7e241ff-57b5-48f8-bf67-ec450fd3e23a.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – PAD</a>. The CA-13 road project's PAD identifies community health and safety risks for 4,000+ workers in remote areas with limited health infrastructure, but does not detail contingencies for non-functional health systems <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-16ccfa88-c5fd-4b6a-acfc-b69c54b5e6ac.pdf" target="_blank">P507958 – PAD</a>. The social protection project's inter-institutional Technical Committee includes the Secretaría de Salud, whose participation is required for health conditionalities in the TMC program—conditionalities that are now undermined by the health system's institutional paralysis <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ba0766&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing severely: the health project's ISRs document declining immunization coverage, regression in early antenatal care attendance, and a 2.5-month delay between Congressional approval and project effectiveness followed by micro-planning delays within SESAL units <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176532-b7cbc274-26aa-491e-bb45-ffebf0abfed8.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176532-b7cbc274-26aa-491e-bb45-ffebf0abfed8.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176532-b7cbc274-26aa-491e-bb45-ffebf0abfed8.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – ISR</a>. The project's overall implementation progress has been downgraded, with the ISR confirming that SESAL's institutional capacity constraints are directly impeding execution <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176532-b7cbc274-26aa-491e-bb45-ffebf0abfed8.pdf" target="_blank">P176532 – ISR</a>. The social protection project's Aide Memoire documents unsigned inter-institutional agreements with SESAL, preventing the project from formalizing health sector coordination <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P152057-94d3fde8-d108-42e4-b41e-08031cf57c23.pdf" target="_blank">P152057 – Aide Memoire</a>. The early childhood education project's Aide Memoire documents a risk of educator desertion from community-based centers, reflecting the same institutional fragility and inadequate compensation dynamics affecting health worker retention <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169161-2969ae1d-d89f-4b4e-9f1d-6d5a59c1b7d6.pdf" target="_blank">P169161 – Aide Memoire</a>. ## 4. Remittance Shock, Economic Vulnerability, and U.S. Policy Spillovers

Honduras is acutely exposed to U.S. immigration policy shifts, with remittances exceeding $9.7 billion (26–30% of GDP) in 2024–2025 and approximately 85% of senders based in the United States. The Trump administration terminated Temporary Protected Status for approximately 72,000 Honduran beneficiaries, and the Ninth Circuit's February 2026 stay of a federal court reinstatement means TPS is currently terminated. The Inter-American Dialogue projects a 3–5% decline in remittance senders in 2026, implying approximately 1% GDP contraction and 1.5% decline in private consumption. Mass deportations—projected to equal or exceed 2025 levels of approximately 100,000 across Central America—will flood Honduras's already saturated labor market. Simultaneously, the Trump administration terminated approximately $167 million in U.S. foreign assistance for Honduras, including most economic and governance aid and support for civil society organizations working on democracy and human rights. A Honduran congressional investigation has targeted former U.S. civil society partners for allegedly "destabilizing" the government, creating a chilling effect on civic space. The CA-13 road project's PAD identifies macroeconomic risk from decelerating GDP growth and remittance deceleration, with limited contingency planning for remittance collapse affecting the 615,000 beneficiaries in Atlántida and Colón <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-16ccfa88-c5fd-4b6a-acfc-b69c54b5e6ac.pdf" target="_blank">P507958 – PAD</a>. The civil registry project's PAD identifies macroeconomic risk from persistent inflation and unemployment, but does not account for the magnitude of the TPS termination shock projected during implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-26698d51-7b79-45d6-aa2e-9655368e9797.pdf" target="_blank">P181324 – PAD</a>. The social protection project's PAD explicitly identifies "stricter U.S. immigration policy may reduce family remittances and domestic consumption, increase labor market pressures" as a key macroeconomic risk affecting the project's poorest beneficiaries <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ba0766&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – PAD</a>. The COMRURAL II project's PAD identifies beneficiary contributions of $18.1 million (12.3% of total project cost), making beneficiaries' economic capacity critical—a capacity directly undermined by the remittance shock <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088637d10&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P174328 – PAD</a>. The water utility project's PAD identifies macroeconomic risk from remittance decline and notes that the project depends on utility revenues and municipal counterpart funding that will be constrained by economic contraction <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0879fb266&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173125 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing: the social protection project's ISRs document suspended urban cash transfers, inconsistent rural payments, and a stalled Social Protection Policy—all occurring as the economic shock from TPS termination and deportations intensifies <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088251625&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P152057 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P152057-94d3fde8-d108-42e4-b41e-08031cf57c23.pdf" target="_blank">P152057 – Aide Memoire</a>. The COMRURAL II project's ISR documents a Ministry of Finance reallocation of $25 million to a Disaster Risk Management credit and a fund reassignment triggered by Tropical Storm Sara that eliminated Component 1 agribusiness activities, reducing the project's capacity to support rural livelihoods precisely when remittance income is contracting <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168385-47877c23-05d0-4772-ad5f-4d882d42d059.pdf" target="_blank">P168385 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168385-47877c23-05d0-4772-ad5f-4d882d42d059.pdf" target="_blank">P168385 – ISR</a>. The water utility project's ISR documents four years of zero civil works implementation and no progress on any indicators, with the project's cost-recovery model dependent on household purchasing power that is being eroded by the remittance shock <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173125-77c9a8ff-92f6-47b4-a337-afe8f8cac813.pdf" target="_blank">P173125 – ISR</a>.

## 5. Contested Political Transition, Polarization, and Geopolitical Realignment

President Asfura was inaugurated on January 27, 2026, following a razor-thin election victory marred by fraud allegations, delayed vote counts, and an explosive device attack inside the Legislative Palace. His party holds only 49 of 128 congressional seats, requiring coalition support for major legislation, while the LIBRE party has yet to accept the result. Civil society organizations protested on inauguration day, and a feminist march the following day was met with tear gas. Asfura's sharp rightward foreign policy pivot—including signaling ICSID re-accession, pledging to restore diplomatic relations with Taiwan, and terminating the Cuba medical mission under apparent U.S. pressure—has created domestic political tensions and a legitimacy deficit from the outset. The Trump administration's explicit electoral intervention, including the pardon of convicted drug trafficker and former President Hernández, has been decried by civil society and opposition parties as a violation of Honduran sovereignty. The DPF operation's ISR documents Congressional ratification delays and elevated stakeholder risk directly connected to the polarized legislative environment and institutional mistrust of the new administration <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P179909-3ce23136-04a2-44c0-b91e-572ac2bcbfd0.pdf" target="_blank">P179909 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P179909-3ce23136-04a2-44c0-b91e-572ac2bcbfd0.pdf" target="_blank">P179909 – ISR</a>.
