**Political instability:** President Guelleh's April 2026 sixth-term election with 97.81% of the vote, enabled by an October 2025 constitutional amendment removing the 75-year age limit, has deepened democratic backsliding and created succession uncertainty around a 78-year-old leader whose stepson is being groomed as a potential successor. The Economic and Financial Governance Project (P180696) is the most directly exposed: the PAD rates political risk as High, the project depends on a PFM Reform Steering Committee chaired by the Minister of Finance, and the mitigation strategy relies on "government commitment demonstrated through past successes"—an assumption that is structurally fragile under conditions of political consolidation. <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a> The Education Sector Support Project (P166059) explicitly identifies ministerial reshuffles as a substantial governance risk, with project coordination relying on the Secretary General as project lead and a steering committee whose continuity is vulnerable to political transitions. <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a> The Digital Finance and Connectivity Project (P174461) has maintained a High political and governance risk rating across all review periods, and the ARMD regulatory board appointments have remained pending since 2022 despite being a legal project covenant.
