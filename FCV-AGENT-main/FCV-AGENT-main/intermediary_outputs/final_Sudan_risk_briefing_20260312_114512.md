# Sudan FCV Portfolio Briefing

## Paragraph 1: Active Multi-Front War and Deliberate Obstruction of Humanitarian Access

Sudan's ongoing war between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) has entered its fourth year with no resolution in sight, with Kordofan and Darfur identified as key epicentres of hostilities, drone strikes on hospitals, and a new front opening in Blue Nile state as of early 2026. All four projects in the portfolio are directly susceptible to this risk. The Emergency Health and Nutrition Project explicitly identifies health facilities in Darfur and Greater Kordofan as priority implementation areas, acknowledging that facilities have been "previously attacked and looted, leading to deaths of patients and health workers," and that a three-tiered service delivery model must adapt to shifting security conditions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The Emergency Safety Net Project similarly targets West and South Kordofan, with the PAD acknowledging "high security risk from ongoing armed conflict" and "evidence of grand robbery, thefts, and looting of life-saving aid by soldiers" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The Resilience and Livelihoods Project notes that conflict may escalate into its target states and that approximately 30% of Sudan's counties are directly affected by hostilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The Education Emergency Project identifies conflict-related school closures as a central implementation constraint [P504621 | PAD]. These risks are actively materializing across the portfolio. The Emergency Health Project's ISR documents that shifting frontlines are continuously forcing adaptation of facility targeting and supply chain approaches <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The Education Project's Aide Memoire confirms that schools remain closed in conflict-affected areas, with only 7.3% of funds utilized as of April 2025, and that the Security Management Framework required for school grant disbursement had not yet received clearance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Compounding the direct violence, both warring parties are deliberately obstructing humanitarian access — attacking WFP operations with drone strikes since February 2026 and restricting movement in Kordofan and Darfur. The Emergency Health Project's PAD acknowledges reliance on "UN humanitarian corridor facilitation" and a tiered access model, but this model presupposes a minimum level of access that is being systematically denied <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The Safety Net Project's ISR confirms that deliberate obstruction is constraining cash transfer delivery and beneficiary verification <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The Education Project's ISR and Aide Memoire both document that access restrictions are preventing procurement execution and learning material distribution, with $281,000 disbursed of $16.8 million in signed contracts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Forward-looking, the geographic spread of hostilities to Blue Nile and Sennar — states targeted by the Resilience Project — signals that no project area can be considered stable, and the absence of any ceasefire mechanism means these operational disruptions are structural rather than temporary.

## Paragraph 2: De Facto State Fragmentation, Governance Vacuum, and Political Repression

Sudan's political landscape has fractured into competing governance structures, with the RSF's proclamation of a parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" (Tasis State) in Darfur and parts of Kordofan in August 2025 creating dual administrative authorities with conflicting legitimacy claims, while stalled peace diplomacy across the Quad, AU Quintet, and bilateral channels has produced no ceasefire as of March 2026. Every project in the portfolio is susceptible to this fragmentation. The Emergency Health Project's PAD identifies "federal institutions non-functional due to conflict" and notes that the project avoids channeling funds through federal or state authorities, instead working through UN agencies and NGOs — yet this does not resolve the underlying governance fragmentation affecting local coordination in RSF-controlled Darfur and Kordofan <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The Safety Net Project explicitly operates under OP 7.30 (Dealings with De Facto Governments), acknowledging "political and institutional fragmentation from ongoing war" and the relocation of the government apparatus to Port Sudan, but does not specify how it will navigate RSF-aligned parallel authorities in implementation zones <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The Resilience Project identifies "federal institutions non-functional" and relies on state and locality-level authorities that may themselves be contested or displaced <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The Education Project's PAD notes that institutional capacity constraints are compounded by governance fragmentation, with the PIU unable to maintain coherent coordination across fragmented territorial control [P504621 | PAD]. These risks are materializing. Across all four ISRs, political and governance risk is rated High, with the Safety Net Project's ISR documenting that governance instability is directly impairing institutional capacity for safety net delivery <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The Energy and Digital Infrastructure Project's ISR records high ratings for political and governance instability, stakeholder engagement, fiduciary risk, and sector policy instability — all directly traceable to the legitimacy vacuum created by competing administrations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The Education Project's ISR documents that stakeholder engagement is rated High risk, with fragmented governance creating competing authorities for school selection and grant distribution <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. Compounding governance fragmentation, both warring parties are engaging in arbitrary detention of over 3,000 civilians in Gezira state, retaliatory attacks on local emergency response volunteers, and systematic suppression of civil society — directly constraining the community engagement mechanisms, CDC-based governance models, and NGO partnerships that all four projects depend upon <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. Without a political settlement, the governance vacuum will deepen, and the institutional counterparts and local partners on which the portfolio depends will remain fragmented, suppressed, or at risk.

## Paragraph 3: Mass Displacement, Famine, and Collapsed Health System

Sudan hosts the world's largest displacement crisis — approximately 14 million forcibly displaced since April 2023, including 7.3 million IDPs — while confirmed famine (IPC Phase 5) in El Fasher and Kadugli and acute malnutrition surpassing thresholds in Um Baru and Kernoi signal a catastrophic humanitarian convergence. The health system has collapsed, with 37% of facilities non-functional as of January 2026 and 201 verified attacks on healthcare facilities since April 2023. The Emergency Health Project is most directly susceptible: it explicitly targets 9 million beneficiaries including IDPs and pregnant women in Darfur and Kordofan, acknowledges that "75% of health facilities in conflict-affected areas are non-functional," and includes a severe acute malnutrition (SAM) management component — yet the PAD does not fully assess how confirmed famine conditions in project priority areas will overwhelm nutrition intervention capacity or compromise staff retention <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The Safety Net Project targets IPC Phase 3 and 4 areas in Northern West Darfur, East Darfur, West Kordofan, and South Kordofan, with cash transfers designed to provide 50% of daily caloric needs at US$10 per person per month — a transfer value that the PAD acknowledges is vulnerable to hyperinflation (211% as of October 2024) and that may be wholly inadequate in confirmed Phase 5 famine zones <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The Resilience Project's food security component targets agricultural production in states neighboring famine zones, with the PAD acknowledging that food prices have increased 50% since the war started and that the project "cannot fully mitigate" the macroeconomic crisis <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The Education Project's Aide Memoire and ISR document that disease outbreaks and health system collapse are contributing to school closures and reduced community engagement capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. These risks are actively materializing. The Emergency Health Project's ISR confirms that shifting frontlines are preventing consistent facility targeting and supply replenishment, directly contributing to the 37% non-functionality rate <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The Safety Net Project's ISR documents that macroeconomic instability is eroding the real purchasing power of cash transfers, with hyperinflation and currency depreciation rendering planned transfer amounts insufficient in famine-affected zones <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The Resilience Project's ISR records that IDP integration and social cohesion indicators are under severe strain, with food scarcity driving competition between displaced and host populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The Safety Net Project's ISR further documents that environmental and social safeguard risks are rated High, with disease outbreaks in overcrowded displacement sites creating compounding vulnerabilities for beneficiaries <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. With 20 additional areas projected to reach famine conditions across Greater Darfur and Greater Kordofan through 2026, and the upcoming rainy season threatening renewed cholera outbreaks, the humanitarian convergence facing the portfolio will intensify before it improves.

## Paragraph 4: Severe Humanitarian Funding Gap and Macroeconomic Collapse

Sudan's 2026 humanitarian response plan requires $2.9 billion but had received only 5.5% of required funds as of early February 2026, WFP has been forced to reduce food rations to survival minimums, and UNFPA has withdrawn from more than half of the 93 health facilities it supported due to funding cuts — all within a macroeconomic environment of 20% economic contraction in 2023, 15% in 2024, and hyperinflation of 211% as of October 2024. The entire portfolio operates within this ecosystem of catastrophic underfunding and macroeconomic collapse. The Emergency Health Project's PAD acknowledges "sustainability heavily dependent on long-term external financing" and identifies macroeconomic risk as substantial, noting economic contraction, hyperinflation, currency depreciation, and frozen donor aid — but does not address how the project will maintain operations if broader humanitarian funding collapses further <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The Safety Net Project's PAD identifies high macroeconomic and fiduciary risks, noting that "worsening economic conditions risk real purchasing power of cash transfers" and that the project's US$100 million budget operates within a context where WFP itself is being forced to reduce rations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The Resilience Project's PAD acknowledges "severe economic deterioration from conflict, hyperinflation, currency depreciation, halt of domestic/international trade, frozen donor aid, rising unemployment" and notes the project will use contractual obligations in US dollars to counteract exchange rate impacts — but does not address the risk that implementing partners may be diverted to emergency humanitarian operations as the funding gap widens <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The Education Project's Aide Memoire documents that budgetary constraints delayed the recruitment of the Third-Party Monitor, creating a verification vacuum precisely when oversight is most critical <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. These risks are materializing across the portfolio. The Safety Net Project's ISR documents that macroeconomic instability is rated High, with currency depreciation and inflation directly eroding cash transfer effectiveness and creating fiduciary management challenges <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The Emergency Health Project's ISR confirms that institutional capacity constraints are compounded by resource scarcity, with underfunding forcing agencies to reduce services and close facilities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The Safety Net Project's ISR records that institutional capacity risk is rated High, with the critical funding shortfall directly translating to staffing deficits, closed service delivery points, and overwhelmed implementing partners <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The Education Project's ISR documents a 98.3% procurement underspend rate, with only $281,000 of $16.8 million in signed contracts disbursed — a figure that reflects not only security constraints but the operational paralysis created by resource scarcity and institutional fragility <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. With donor fatigue deepening, USAID cuts reducing available financing, and funding per refugee dropping from $11 to $4 per month between 2022 and 2025, the portfolio faces a structural financing environment that will constrain implementation regardless of security improvements.
