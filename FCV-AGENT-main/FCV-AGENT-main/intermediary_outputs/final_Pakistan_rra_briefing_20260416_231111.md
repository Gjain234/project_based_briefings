**Social unrest and protests:** Pakistan's social unrest landscape has intensified sharply in early 2026. Deadly protests erupted in Gilgit-Baltistan on March 1 following the killing of Iran's Supreme Leader, leaving 12–20 dead and 80 injured; protesters stormed UN offices including the UNDP office in Skardu and a UNMOGIP field station, while separate clashes at the US Consulate in Karachi left 10 dead (PAK-004). PTI's KP chapter has warned of a "major movement" for Imran Khan's release, and human rights lawyers have received 17-year prison sentences for social media criticism, signalling a contracting civic space (PAK-005). IMF-required energy tariff reforms and fiscal consolidation carry additional social backlash risks amid 44.7 percent poverty (PAK-008). **Violence between refugees, host communities, and the state:** Pakistan has deported over 146,000 Afghans in 2026 as of mid-April, following more than one million forced returns in 2025; suspension of Proof of Registration renewals has rendered 1.3 million Afghans undocumented and exposed nearly two million to removal risk (PAK-006). UNHCR and MSF document that deportation fear is preventing Afghan refugees from accessing medical care, causing trauma-induced miscarriages and preventable deaths (PAK-006). Tens of thousands of civilians were also displaced from KP's Tirah Valley in late January 2026 ahead of military anti-TTP operations, with thousands more sheltering in a railway tunnel during March border shelling (PAK-007). These dynamics directly threaten projects serving refugee and displaced populations. The Refugees and Host Communities Project is funded through IDA's Regional Sub-Window for Refugees and Host Communities and targets 519,303 refugees in four KP districts; the PAD identifies high refugee protection risk from potential changes to Pakistan's Protection Framework, but does not address the current scale of deportations approaching 1.4 million PoR card holders <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0879fa728&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166309 – PAD</a>. The Strengthening Refugee and Host Community Resilience Project directly targets approximately 15,000 PoR card holders for visa policy benefits, but the accelerating deportation campaign and suspension of PoR renewals are undermining the project's beneficiary base and voluntary repatriation principles <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0875fcabb&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P165542 – PAD</a>. The Balochistan Flood Emergency project targets 2.7 million flood-affected people in a province hosting significant Afghan refugee populations, yet the PAD does not address how forced deportations may affect beneficiary composition <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-cada74cb-95f5-4ca2-a46a-9560206ebad9.pdf" target="_blank">P180323 – PAD</a>. **Organized violence between political and sectarian groups:** A suicide bomb attack on a Shia mosque in Islamabad on February 6, 2026 killed at least 31 people and wounded 169—the deadliest such attack in over a decade—with ISKP claiming responsibility and explicitly targeting Pakistani Shias (PAK-004). TTP violence surged to 146 attacks in March 2026 alone, a 76 percent increase over February, with tactics evolving to include quadcopter drone use, targeted killings, and IEDs; Pakistan ranked first on the Global Terrorism Index 2026 with 1,139 deaths in 2025 (PAK-002). The BLA launched Operation Herof 2.0 on January 31, 2026—12 or more coordinated attacks across Balochistan targeting military installations, banks, and prisons, resulting in approximately 200 deaths—and conducted its first maritime attack in April 2026 (PAK-003). Projects with infrastructure and staff in KP and Balochistan face acute exposure. The Khyber Pass Economic Corridor operates in Khyber Agency and KP along the Pakistan-Afghanistan border, requires Frontier Constabulary protection, and involves a 48 km construction corridor with 500–700 workers; the PAD acknowledges security risk but does not address TTP drone capabilities or the current intensity of cross-border violence <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b085b2e6ff&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P159577 – PAD</a>. The Dasu Hydropower Project is located in Upper Kohistan District, KP, with a 7-year construction period, 5,000+ workers, and a 250 km transmission line; the PAD identifies security risk but does not address emerging drone threats to construction sites or the transmission corridor <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0828b816d&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P121507 – PAD</a>. **Political instability:** Pakistan's civil-military hybrid governance has grown more entrenched in 2026, with Imran Khan still jailed, PTI leaders facing mass arrests, and the KP provincial government—the only PTI-governed institution—in direct political conflict with the federal government and military (PAK-005, PAK-009). PTI's withdrawal from negotiations in early 2026 has deepened political deadlock, while the KP Chief Minister has publicly accused the federal government of systematic economic strangulation through withheld constitutional financial allocations (PAK-009). Pakistan's Transparency International ranking remains poor, and most prime ministers have historically not completed a full term (PAK-009). Projects dependent on federal-provincial coordination and sustained political commitment face structural exposure. The SPEED Programme for Results in KP depends on the Finance Department of the PTI-governed KP provincial government and requires coordination across Planning, Education, and Health Departments; the PAD identifies moderate political and governance risk but does not address the current direct conflict between KP and federal authorities <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088486e7b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P175727 – PAD</a>. The National Health Support Programme requires coordination between federal MoNHSR&C and provincial Health Departments in KP, Punjab, and Sindh, with Provincial Steering Committees and Technical Working Groups that are vulnerable to the KP-federal political fracture <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088dba71e&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172615 – PAD</a>. The ASPIRE education programme depends on a Technical Steering Committee with senior representatives from all provincial education departments and an Inter-Provincial Education Ministerial Conference, both of which are directly threatened by the KP-federal political deadlock <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087bdd0fa&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173399 – PAD</a>. **External risks and intra-state conflict:** Pakistan declared "open war" with Afghanistan in late February 2026 after striking Taliban military facilities in Kabul, Kandahar, and Paktia province; a March 16 airstrike on what Afghan officials described as a drug rehabilitation centre in Kabul killed more than 400 people; a fragile Eid ceasefire brokered by Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and Turkey held March 18–23 but resolved no core grievances, and China-mediated talks in Urumqi also failed (PAK-001). India-Pakistan tensions resurfaced through disputes over the suspended Indus Waters Treaty, with Pakistan accusing India of "weaponising water" and the IMF's Resilience and Sustainability Facility specifically noting water resource management as a macro-critical vulnerability (PAK-010). TTP cross-border insurgency, backed by Afghan Taliban sanctuary, generated 1,045 incidents in 2025 and a 76 percent surge in March 2026 (PAK-002). Projects in border-adjacent districts face the most direct exposure. The Merged Areas Development Project operates in five districts directly bordering Afghanistan—Bajaur, Khyber, Kurram, Mohmand, North Waziristan, South Waziristan—where cross-border shelling, airstrikes, and displacement have been documented in early 2026; the PAD's "framework approach" assumes low-grade conflict rather than the active interstate hostilities now underway <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-107c8a45-eb40-4cbd-85f2-4e94744ec5dd.pdf" target="_blank">P176780 – PAD</a>.
