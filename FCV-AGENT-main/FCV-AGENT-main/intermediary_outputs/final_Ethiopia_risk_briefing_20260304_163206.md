# Ethiopia FCV Portfolio Briefing

## 1. Persistent Armed Conflict in Amhara and Oromia Regions

Ethiopia's two most populous regions remain engulfed in active armed conflict that is directly degrading portfolio performance across virtually every sector. In Amhara, the Fano insurgency—involving multiple factions, some of which signed a partial peace agreement in December 2025 but most of which continue armed resistance—has closed over 4,000 schools, displaced millions, and subjected civilians to drone strikes and military operations. In Oromia, violence persists despite a December 2024 peace agreement with one OLA faction, with over 25 civilians killed in East Arsi Zone in October 2025 and 32 killed in a single day in August, alongside 288,000 people displaced since July 2025 from intercommunal clashes between Oromo and Somali communities. Projects across the portfolio were designed with explicit awareness of these risks: the rural roads project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a> identifies "security (substantial): internal conflicts affecting project sites, staff safety, material transportation" and requires conflict-sensitive site selection across all 13 regions; the pastoral livelihoods project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-334703ce-834f-4cd4-bf49-4e941b00bb0a.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – PAD</a> acknowledges "localized conflicts in Amhara and Oromia regions" as a high political risk; the TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a> notes that "security risks may hinder smooth implementation especially for PTCs in conflict regions"; and the PSNP <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087f8ffa9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172479 – PAD</a> operates in both regions with provisions for conflict-affected beneficiaries. These risks are now fully materializing. The PSNP Aide Memoire documents that implementation in Amhara and Oromia is directly constrained by active conflict, with UXO contamination in Amhara hotspot woredas preventing public works and requiring demining before activities can proceed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. The WASH project reports that internal conflict in Amhara is directly impeding staff movement and project implementation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>, with asset damage in Amhara and Afar regions unassessable due to access constraints <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's ISR records that IDP enrollment stands at only 0.76% against a 35% target, with Amhara and Oromia conflict dynamics identified as primary barriers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-02907b3f-0a5c-484e-b00e-136cb8695aad.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – ISR</a>. The refugee and host community project documents that security constraints are preventing field travel in Amhara and Oromia <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>, and procurement delays in DRDIP II are directly attributed to conflict in both regions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. The livestock and fisheries project similarly reports that security constraints in Amhara and Oromia are limiting private sector engagement and conflict management activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the combination of active insurgency, counterinsurgency operations, and intercommunal violence is producing a consistent pattern of access denial, staff flight, asset loss, and beneficiary displacement that is degrading implementation quality and results achievement.

## 2. Political Crisis in Tigray and Fragile Post-Conflict Dynamics in Northern Regions

Tigray faces a compounding crisis: the fragile 2022 Pretoria Agreement has not produced stabilization, and a new political emergency has emerged with the TPLF's deregistration from the June 2026 elections—which Tigrayan leaders labeled a "clear declaration of war"—alongside escalating tensions between the TPLF and Tigray Defense Forces and rapidly shrinking federal authority in Mekelle and other key cities. Simultaneously, Eritrean troops continue to commit sexual violence in occupied areas of Tigray, Tigrayans continue to be forcibly displaced from Western Tigray, and the pending referendum on Western Tigray's fate remains unresolved. These dynamics were anticipated in project design: the PSNP <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087f8ffa9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172479 – PAD</a> identifies "political (high): ongoing conflicts, internal displacements, ethnic tensions, political instability affecting implementation" and relies on federal-regional-woreda steering committees that are now compromised; the TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a> explicitly targets Tigray as a conflict-affected region requiring PTC coverage and notes that "volatile security situation affects service delivery"; the human capital project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a> explicitly excludes Tigray from project locations, itself a reflection of recognized implementation impossibility; and the digital project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08847bca4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171034 – PAD</a> excludes Tigray due to "ongoing conflict and communication blackout since November 2020." Realized risks are severe and documented across multiple instruments. The WASH project's Aide Memoire records that assets in Tigray cannot be verified, financial documents from woredas are missing, and the project cannot assess asset status or damage <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The PSNP ISR documents that five contested woredas are excluded from program coverage due to territorial fragmentation, and account opening in Tigray's 23 woredas has stalled due to limited collaboration among implementing entities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>. The urban safety net project documents delayed recovery grant distribution to IDPs in Mekelle due to the fragile post-conflict environment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>, while the WASH project's Aide Memoire records document destruction during conflict in three Amhara woredas and persistent inability to settle expenditures <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's ISR records persistent "Substantial" political and governance risk ratings, with fiduciary controls at polytechnic colleges in Tigray compromised by contested political authority <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-02907b3f-0a5c-484e-b00e-136cb8695aad.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – ISR</a>. The UNOPS-to-RPCU transition in Tigray is stalled precisely as governance structures weaken <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, Tigray represents the most acute governance vacuum, where the combination of post-conflict fragility, new political crisis, and Eritrean military presence is producing systematic implementation failure.

## 3. Mass Internal Displacement and Deteriorating Humanitarian Conditions

Ethiopia hosts one of the world's largest internally displaced populations, with 3.3 million IDPs and 4.7 million people forced from their homes due to conflicts and climate impacts. IDPs in Tigray staged protests demanding safe returns while hundreds of thousands remain in precarious conditions in formal and informal camps. The humanitarian situation has deteriorated sharply, with WFP forced to suspend treatment for 650,000 malnourished women and children and 3.6 million people facing loss of food assistance due to the Trump administration's foreign aid freeze and reduced international donor support. Projects across the portfolio were designed with awareness of displacement but at scales that have been overtaken by events: the PSNP <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087f8ffa9&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172479 – PAD</a> includes provisions for displaced beneficiaries and protocols for hosting displaced families; the pastoral livelihoods project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-334703ce-834f-4cd4-bf49-4e941b00bb0a.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – PAD</a> explicitly identifies "displaced populations" as a vulnerable group; the TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a> sets a target of at least 15% IDP enrollment (approximately 30,000 of 200,000 beneficiaries); and the human capital project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a> identifies 512,000 drought-induced IDPs as a target group. Realized risks are pervasive and severe. The TVET project's ISR records IDP enrollment at 0.76% against a 35% target, with the Aide Memoire confirming that IDPs face documentation barriers, mobility constraints, and competing survival needs that prevent participation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-02907b3f-0a5c-484e-b00e-136cb8695aad.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-a2d54917-aedd-4d6b-bdb7-01bbf1399378.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – Aide Memoire</a>. The PSNP ISR documents that EDRMC institutional capacity constraints are delaying shock response disbursement, while the Early Warning System dashboard is inaccessible to prioritization committee institutions, reducing coordinated response capacity <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>. The health project's ISR records that IDP clinics are non-operational due to Ministry of Finance counter-funding delays, and only 2 of 262 health facilities have been restored to pre-conflict service levels <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175167-992833b3-d61e-459e-9188-466f35c2618c.pdf" target="_blank">P175167 – ISR</a>. The WASH project's Aide Memoire documents that displacement in drought-prone woredas is directly impeding project access, with physical progress at only 17.65% <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The PSNP Aide Memoire records that the transitory caseload is excluded from Humanitarian Response Plan planning during the July-December lean season, creating a protection gap for the most vulnerable precisely when needs peak <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-33203b57-3884-48f8-a304-7fc05ebf92c8.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – Aide Memoire</a>. The rural roads project's ISR records persistent high macroeconomic risk driven by the same fiscal constraints that are forcing humanitarian suspensions, with government counterpart funding of US$107.3 million at risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-7b125857-00f3-4f1b-a25e-a0ad429dba62.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – ISR</a>. The scale of displacement has systematically exceeded project design assumptions, and the humanitarian funding crisis is now cascading into development project implementation through government co-financing shortfalls, institutional capacity diversion, and beneficiary inaccessibility.

## 4. Cross-Border Spillover from Sudan Conflict and Regional Instability

Ethiopia's Gambella region and eastern border areas face mounting pressure from the Sudan conflict, which has driven over 163,000 individuals into Ethiopia as of January 2025, with 50,000 new refugee arrivals in 2025 alone, primarily from Sudan and South Sudan. Cross-border incursions by South Sudanese opposition forces protecting Nuer communities in Gambella have raised risks of renewed violence, and thousands of South Sudanese refugees entered Ethiopia in March 2025. Ethiopia hosts over one million refugees amid growing political instability and funding shortfalls that undermine protection capacity. Projects in border regions were designed with awareness of these dynamics: the pastoral livelihoods project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-334703ce-834f-4cd4-bf49-4e941b00bb0a.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – PAD</a> lists Gambella as a project location and explicitly notes "consideration of geopolitical tensions in Horn of Africa" and "cross-border tensions" as contextual risks; the refugee and host community project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088ddd75f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P178047 – PAD</a> identifies Gambella as one of six target regions and acknowledges "security (substantial)" risk including cross-border threats; the human capital project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0611b230-d318-48f2-930a-55190479c46f.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – PAD</a> commits to supporting 800,000 refugees and integrating them into national education and health systems; and the urban safety net project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087e0afcb&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P169943 – PAD</a> explicitly targets refugee camps near urban centers including Kebribeyah and Sherkole with 81,000 refugee beneficiaries. Realized risks are directly documented. The pastoral livelihoods project's Aide Memoire records that Gambella is inaccessible to World Bank supervision due to conflict dynamics, and Benishangul Gumuz similarly cannot be accessed for field monitoring <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-b359290e-2e82-42ba-a499-e9b275ae75f5.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – Aide Memoire</a>. The refugee and host community project's Aide Memoire documents that declining humanitarian assistance is directly impacting refugee education and services, with refugee livelihood participation at only 6% against a 30% target <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178047-b0d0fe8e-7b17-4207-831a-5f4c80491177.pdf" target="_blank">P178047 – Aide Memoire</a>. The urban safety net project's Aide Memoire records that refugee program dropout in Pugnido (Gambella) required re-targeting of 3,175 beneficiaries due to insecurity and forced relocation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>, and that CBE branch capacity constraints in Pugnido are preventing cash transfer delivery to refugee beneficiaries <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P169943-fc642666-20d5-4d3e-950f-b6a65b97177a.pdf" target="_blank">P169943 – Aide Memoire</a>. The human capital project's ISR records that refugee-hosting areas face service delivery pressures from the influx, with secondary school enrollment and diarrhea treatment indicators lagging in high refugee host areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172284-40fb699b-071f-488d-92d0-0a7c5aeb0050.pdf" target="_blank">P172284 – ISR</a>. The WASH project's PAD acknowledges that "displacement adds pressure to localized water sources" and the project's water resources management component faces increased demand from refugee populations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d2b493&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – PAD</a>. The combination of ongoing refugee arrivals, cross-border security incidents, and collapsing humanitarian funding is systematically overwhelming project implementation capacity in border regions.

## 5. Governance Fragility, Electoral Legitimacy Crisis, and Macroeconomic Deterioration

Ethiopia faces a convergence of governance and macroeconomic risks that are undermining the enabling environment for the entire portfolio. The June 2026 elections carry high risk of triggering further instability: major opposition parties may boycott as they did in 2021, the TPLF's exclusion threatens Tigrayan participation, insecurity may prevent voting in several regions, and the government has conducted systematic crackdowns on independent media—with at least 44 journalists detained in 2025, Deutsche Welle correspondents suspended, and media law changes increasing executive control—while Ethiopia dropped to 145th out of 180 countries in the 2025 World Press Freedom Index. Simultaneously, the macroeconomic environment has deteriorated sharply, with the Birr weakening significantly since July 2024, high inflation, foreign exchange shortages, and a severe cost-of-living crisis that could fuel recruitment for regional insurgencies. Projects across the portfolio identified these risks: the rural roads project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-a04969dd-32f6-453f-b2d3-310f5643586b.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – PAD</a> rates macroeconomic risk as "high" citing "high inflation, foreign exchange shortages, currency distortions affecting project costs" and requires US$107.3 million in government counterpart funding; the TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-7e6589de-f796-4cc4-8c29-ff61d3c2e295.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – PAD</a> establishes a Project Steering Committee chaired by the MoLS Minister and depends on Regional Skills Bureaus coordination; the WASH project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086d2b493&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – PAD</a> relies on a National WASH Steering Committee chaired by the MoWIE Minister and requires US$50 million in counterpart funding; and the digital project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08847bca4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171034 – PAD</a> identifies "political (high)" risk from "upcoming elections" and depends on Ministry of Innovation and Technology leadership. Realized risks are documented across multiple dimensions. The rural roads project's ISR records persistent "High" political and governance risk ratings, with financial management arrangements not yet established in conflict-affected regions and currency depreciation creating fiscal stress on government matching fund commitments <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-7b125857-00f3-4f1b-a25e-a0ad429dba62.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176303-f8f4167e-72a4-4049-a5c0-0b3f0fd456c6.pdf" target="_blank">P176303 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's ISR records persistent "Substantial" political and governance risk, with the Project Steering Committee meeting only once and providing minimal guidance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-02907b3f-0a5c-484e-b00e-136cb8695aad.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177881-a2d54917-aedd-4d6b-bdb7-01bbf1399378.pdf" target="_blank">P177881 – Aide Memoire</a>. The digital project's Aide Memoire documents that Ethio Telecom privatization has stalled, the state-owned incumbent is resisting regulatory reform, Safaricom Ethiopia faces an uneven regulatory playing field, and the government has requested reallocation of US$30 million away from digital job creation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171034-2b31c1cd-3768-4621-a1dd-4599e54b1019.pdf" target="_blank">P171034 – Aide Memoire</a>. The WASH project's ISR records persistent "Substantial" political and governance risk, with government matching fund delays and woreda-level leadership reshuffling disrupting implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P167794-f8ff51f0-6541-4d6a-944c-966e7779455e.pdf" target="_blank">P167794 – ISR</a> <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef997b&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P167794 – Aide Memoire</a>. The PSNP's ISR documents macroeconomic instability affecting program financing, with high ETB/USD conversion rates reducing the real value of government contributions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172479-521e2e7f-369f-4f5b-b998-4d81919b9b17.pdf" target="_blank">P172479 – ISR</a>. The livestock project's ISR records persistent high macroeconomic risk linked to the cost-of-living crisis and reduced donor support <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180076-2646766e-1d32-49db-8d05-cc6efb166efc.pdf" target="_blank">P180076 – ISR</a>. The convergence of pre-election political repression, institutional legitimacy deficits, and macroeconomic deterioration is creating a systemic governance environment in which project coordination structures are fragile, counterpart financing is unreliable, independent monitoring is constrained, and the political consensus necessary for sustained implementation is eroding.
