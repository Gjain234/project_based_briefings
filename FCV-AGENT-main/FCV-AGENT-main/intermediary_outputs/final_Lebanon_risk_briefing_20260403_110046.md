Lebanon's portfolio of World Bank projects faces five distinct and compounding country-level risks that are simultaneously shaping project design vulnerabilities and materializing as concrete implementation failures across the active lending program.

**Renewed Full-Scale Israel-Hezbollah Hostilities and Active Military Operations**

The collapse of the November 2024 ceasefire on 2 March 2026 and the subsequent resumption of large-scale Israeli military operations across southern Lebanon, the Bekaa Valley, Nabatieh, and Beirut's southern suburbs represent the most acute and immediate threat to portfolio delivery. By late March 2026, over 394 people had been killed, most bridges south of the Litani River destroyed, and entire villages flattened, with the IDF declaring intent to eliminate the Hezbollah threat and seize additional strategic positions. At the design stage, multiple projects explicitly acknowledged this exposure. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> identified geopolitical risks from Middle East conflict as a direct threat to implementation in southern Lebanon and the Bekaa Valley, requiring Lebanese Army clearance for explosive remnants of war before any investment could proceed. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a> noted that works remained suspended and implementing agency operations severely disrupted as of November 2024, with a hard rule that no works would commence until conflict had ended and sites were declared safe. The Beqaa Valley Water Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a> flagged that ongoing conflict creates security concerns limiting contractor mobility and Bank supervision missions. The Lebanon Solid Waste and Environmental Management Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> identified project locations in Tyre, Abbasiyeh, Sahel el Zahrani, and Nabatieh—all now within or adjacent to active conflict zones. These risks are now fully materializing. Contractors suspended all sewage network works in the Beqaa due to escalating military activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>. Component 2 activities across multiple projects have been put on hold due to security conditions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>. Bank supervision missions have shifted to virtual modalities, and project teams are prohibited from working near areas of active shelling, with unexploded ordnance clearance required before any resumption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-903aa505-bae1-44ca-bf98-11a99cbf798e.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project's reconstruction momentum has effectively collapsed, with the UN Special Coordinator confirming in March 2026 that progress has come to a screeching halt <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a>.

**Mass Internal Displacement Exceeding One Million People**

Since 2 March 2026, Israeli evacuation orders covering the entire area south of the Litani River, Beirut's southern suburbs, and parts of the Bekaa Valley have triggered the largest wave of internal displacement in Lebanon's modern history, with UNHCR estimating over one million newly displaced by late March—nearly one-fifth of the population—and 90,000 Syrian refugees displaced for a second time. This displacement crisis was anticipated in project design but at far smaller scale. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> identified 900,000 displaced during the 2023–2024 conflict as a baseline vulnerability, noting that the project's area-based approach and prioritization criteria could become misaligned with actual displacement patterns and that decommissioning of IDP centers might be premature if displacement continued. The Vaccination and Health System Resilience Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a> targeted approximately 4.7 million beneficiaries including 1.5 million Syrian and Palestinian refugees, with mobile vaccination units designed for areas of high refugee concentration—a delivery model now fundamentally disrupted by population movements. The Social Protection and Jobs Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08813b443&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173367 – PAD</a> relied on home visits to verify all applicants, a methodology rendered inoperable when beneficiary households are displaced. The Solid Waste Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1e05d46a-942a-40a9-b99e-73b57a786469.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – PAD</a> identified approximately 470,000 direct beneficiaries in selected service zones in southern Lebanon, populations now under evacuation orders. Implementation evidence confirms these vulnerabilities are materializing. The Social Protection Project's cash transfer delivery mechanisms are disrupted as beneficiaries in the Bekaa Valley and southern Lebanon have fled their registered addresses <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173367-72c123cf-9083-4eb3-a51a-8b251cf4b86f.pdf" target="_blank">P173367 – ISR</a>. The Vaccination Project's refugee beneficiary tracking has been undermined by the secondary displacement of Syrian refugees and the cross-border outflow of over 180,000 Syrians into Syria <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a>. The Solid Waste Project cannot deliver services to displaced populations or in evacuation zones, and waste management service continuity has collapsed in project areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-4727dec5-5d09-4ce2-8055-7dcb271aae12.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – ISR</a>.

**Collapse of the Healthcare System Under Systematic Attacks and Supply Shortages**

Since 2 March 2026, WHO documented 64 attacks on healthcare infrastructure, killing at least 53 healthcare workers and injuring 91 others, with nine hospitals damaged, four hospitals and 51 primary healthcare centers forced to close entirely, and remaining facilities operating on critically low medical supply inventories. This collapse compounds a pre-existing baseline severely weakened by six years of economic crisis and mass emigration of healthcare workers. At the design stage, the Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> targeted rapid recovery of health lifeline services through mobile clinics, equipment replacement, and restoration of primary healthcare centers—a component now directly undermined by the systematic destruction of the very facilities the project sought to rehabilitate. The Vaccination Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9c1bfedd-5761-415c-9838-dc89cfe988b8.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – PAD</a> identified that 51 percent of surveyed refugees access healthcare via primary healthcare centers, and its Component 2 depended on payment of hospitalization bills and procurement of medicines and equipment through formal and private hospitals—mechanisms that cannot function when hospitals are closed or operating under emergency conditions. The Social Protection Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a> specified delivery of specialized health services via primary healthcare centers and mobile medical units, with the national mental health hotline as a core component. Implementation evidence confirms the healthcare system collapse is directly impeding project delivery. The Vaccination Project shows zero beneficiaries served under its hospitalization cost financing component, with the project unable to operationalize healthcare financing mechanisms amid the displacement surge and facility closures <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0867b7011&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P163476 – ISR</a>. The Social Protection Project's mental health and GBV referral pathways to medical services are non-functional as primary healthcare centers in conflict zones have closed <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a>. The health system capacity shortfall—with hospitals at only 56 percent of the equipment target against a 70 percent goal—reflects both pre-existing governance fragility and the acute disruption caused by active conflict operations affecting supply chains and staff availability <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178587-8133ed2c-dff3-4ee7-a615-063ca255128b.pdf" target="_blank">P178587 – ISR</a>.

**Parliamentary Election Postponement and Deepening Institutional Paralysis**

On 9 March 2026, Lebanon's parliament voted 76–41 to postpone the scheduled May 2026 parliamentary elections by two years—the country's fourth parliamentary self-extension in a decade—citing force majeure created by war and displacement. Critics, including Lebanese Forces leader Geagea and Free Patriotic Movement leader Bassil, condemned the extension as a power-retention maneuver that violates constitutional rights and undermines democratic accountability. This risk was identified across the portfolio as a structural governance vulnerability. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> flagged that past challenges with loan agreement signing and parliamentary ratification had caused delays, and that parliamentary ratification of the loan agreement was a prerequisite for project effectiveness. The Greater Beirut Water Supply Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-46a4ccd6-8f02-40fa-b05f-21af84f07850.pdf" target="_blank">P504170 – PAD</a> explicitly noted that signing of the loan agreement and parliamentary ratification may be delayed due to conflict. The Fiscal Management and Public Finance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-5067fa60-78e4-4f5e-b6c1-5a428d852b01.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – PAD</a> was designed to operate despite ongoing political stalemate but did not address scenarios of complete legislative breakdown. The Beqaa Valley Water Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a> identified continuous engagement with the Council of Ministers and Parliament as the primary mitigation for political risk. Implementation evidence confirms this risk is materializing with direct project consequences. The Fiscal Management Project has not achieved effectiveness due to pending parliamentary ratification of the IBRD Financing Agreement, with the project described as critical for the post-war recovery phase yet blocked by the same parliament that just extended its own term <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181155-3aec5db1-a5d2-44c4-b63f-102a196768dd.pdf" target="_blank">P181155 – ISR</a>. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project shows persistent high political and governance risk ratings, with the March 2026 conflict escalation and parliamentary dysfunction compounding each other to prevent institutional coordination <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P509428-8183afa5-2d83-4fd5-9788-2230e5216342.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – ISR</a>. The Solid Waste Project faces delays in Parliament ratification of follow-on financing, with contaminated rubble management activities pending transfer to the Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project that itself awaits parliamentary action <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176635-f112aa75-2e9c-4c03-ac0e-46311eb3089b.pdf" target="_blank">P176635 – ISR</a>.

**Protracted Economic Crisis Blocking Recovery Financing and Eroding Project Sustainability**

Lebanon's economic crisis—described by the World Bank as one of the worst globally since 1850—has seen real GDP decline more than 38 percent since 2019, the Lebanese pound lose over 97 percent of its value, and frozen bank deposits estimated at $82 billion. The World Bank's January 2026 Economic Monitor had projected 3.5 percent real GDP growth in 2025 and 4 percent in 2026, contingent on sustained reforms, modest reconstruction inflows, and political stability—conditions the March 2026 escalation has now fundamentally undermined. At the design stage, this risk was universally identified as high across the portfolio. The Lebanon Emergency Assistance Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-92339148-e75a-4dca-8bc2-580a6104a46a.pdf" target="_blank">P509428 – PAD</a> noted a $750 million financing gap between appraised needs and available IBRD financing, with project contracts denominated in US dollars as the primary mitigation for currency depreciation risks. The Social Protection Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-6dadd285-c9a6-442f-8faa-3a5cdb52d0ab.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – PAD</a> identified Lebanon's severe economic crisis with currency depreciation exceeding 90 percent and triple-digit inflation as a substantial macroeconomic risk, with mitigation through US dollar-denominated designated accounts and quarterly review of benefit amounts in Lebanese pounds. The Agricultural and Rural Development Project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IDU-c14c98c6-d426-4177-9a31-f1acaae2fb97.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – PAD</a> relied on World Bank January 2026 projections of 4 percent growth in 2026 as the macroeconomic baseline for project viability. The Beqaa Valley Water Project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08452b660&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P147854 – PAD</a> required government counterpart funding of $5 million and depended on tariff increases and cost recovery improvements for financial sustainability. Implementation evidence confirms the economic crisis is actively eroding project outcomes. The Social Protection Project's macroeconomic risk has escalated to Substantial, with the purchasing power of cash transfers threatened by renewed inflation and currency volatility as the conflict reverses the modest 2025 gains <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506127-d9f2a824-f45b-42d8-baa2-906f33700549.pdf" target="_blank">P506127 – ISR</a>. The Agricultural Project shows zero disbursement and zero implementation across all indicators—no farmers reached, no SME loans approved, no infrastructure completed—with the macroeconomic environment having rendered the project's financial intermediation model inoperable <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180334-4899e0cd-2b9b-432c-88d6-4a6b7c577697.pdf" target="_blank">P180334 – ISR</a>. The Beqaa Valley Water Project's macroeconomic risk has escalated from Moderate at approval to High, with the government's ability to provide counterpart funding and sustain tariff policies directly threatened by the conflict-driven economic reversal <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P147854-740ffba4-1843-48ae-936f-b5ec49d1902a.pdf" target="_blank">P147854 – ISR</a>. Landowner compensation under the Zahle sewer network has become effectively uncollectable, with Lebanese lira depreciation reducing compensation amounts to between $27 and $728 per plot—amounts so negligible that 52 percent of landowners have not collected their payments, creating grievance risks that threaten project implementation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172770-d2ffb9b1-6857-4db5-a132-6f8948899097.pdf" target="_blank">P172770 – Aide Memoire</a>.
