# Somalia Portfolio FCV Risk Briefing

## 1. Al-Shabaab Resurgence and Humanitarian Access Constraints

Al-Shabaab's February 2025 Shabelle Offensive has reversed significant government territorial gains, recapturing areas in Middle Shabelle, Lower Shabelle, and Hiraan not under militant control since 2019. The group is now maneuvering to encircle Mogadishu, demonstrated by a March 2025 IED assassination attempt on President Mohamud inside the capital. Concurrently, OCHA recorded 139 humanitarian access incidents between January and September 2025, with severe restrictions along the Shabelle River corridor, Hiraan, and Galgaduud—areas that overlap directly with World Bank project implementation zones. Multiple projects anticipated these threats at design stage. The Somalia Inclusive and Resilient Communities project explicitly targets "hard-to-reach and rural areas along the stabilization continuum" and acknowledges that al-Shabaab controls large swaths of rural territory, requiring regular travel by project personnel to fragile districts <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1c1564c9-a880-45d6-9b95-a7f1b4568685.pdf" target="_blank">P508408 – PAD</a>. The Somalia Electricity Sector Recovery Project notes that "much of Somalia remains in conflict affecting access to project sites and insecurity for staff of government agencies and contractors," with flexibility in site selection as a mitigation measure <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088c01d81&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173088 – PAD</a>. The Somalia Education for Human Capital Development Project acknowledges that "heightened security risks make parts of country inaccessible for project implementation and supervision" <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08863e060&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172434 – PAD</a>. These risks are now materializing. Implementation evidence confirms that security constraints are limiting project area access and slowing implementation pace across the education portfolio <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172434-92591eed-4886-40e9-916c-13eb17066b3d.pdf" target="_blank">P172434 – ISR</a>. The urban resilience portfolio documents that insecurity is forcing costly airlifts of equipment rather than road transport, directly increasing project costs <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088219289&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170922 – Aide Memoire</a>. A fatality incident on a project site in Garowe has been recorded <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088219289&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170922 – Aide Memoire</a>, and a terror attack near the Kismayo project site required strict security mitigation measures <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088219289&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170922 – Aide Memoire</a>. The AUSSOM funding crisis—with the mission receiving only half its required 2025 budget and no 2026 funding pledged—compounds these risks by degrading the security environment that projects depend upon. If AUSSOM collapses, no amount of private security contracting will substitute for the mission's stabilization function across project implementation areas.

## 2. Federal-State Fragmentation and Electoral Deadlock

Somalia's governance architecture is fracturing at a critical moment. Puntland suspended cooperation with the federal government in 2023 and Jubaland maintains parallel security arrangements, while President Mohamud's constitutional reform agenda—widely perceived as centralizing executive power ahead of May 2026 elections—has collapsed high-level dialogue with opposition blocs as of February 2026. Parliamentary sessions on constitutional amendments were halted by scuffles in January 2026, and discussions of unilateral term extension have been reported. This fragmentation is not a background condition but an active implementation constraint across the portfolio. Projects spanning multiple federal member states designed their governance structures around inter-governmental cooperation that is now actively deteriorating. The Somalia Inclusive and Resilient Communities project relies on a Project Steering Committee co-chaired by rotating FMS Ministers of Interior and a Technical Working Group with FMS Directors General as co-chairs <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1c1564c9-a880-45d6-9b95-a7f1b4568685.pdf" target="_blank">P508408 – PAD</a>. The Somalia Strengthening Intergovernmental Fiscal Relations project depends on the Inter-Governmental Fiscal Forum and fiscal transfer mechanisms requiring sustained federal-state political cooperation <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0879e0e01&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173731 – PAD</a>. The Somalia Water for Agropastoral Communities project requires coordination across six federal member states with varying capacities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-63dd48bb-2de2-45f6-884c-8c9a581a5515.pdf" target="_blank">P177627 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing comprehensively. The fiscal transfer execution rate stands at only 69.9%, with Jubbaland State transfers withheld for two years <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf" target="_blank">P173731 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176956-f77e1459-fbec-4c57-918d-bc1e83399e83.pdf" target="_blank">P173731 – Aide Memoire</a>. The fiscal transfer policy has been repeatedly rejected at the Inter-Governmental Fiscal Transfer Committee and has failed to reach the Federal Ministerial Finance Forum, with no FMFF meetings held in 2025 <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173731-b8cf0b3f-7b60-4926-bbe4-93675685b15a.pdf" target="_blank">P173731 – ISR</a>. Puntland has failed to submit civil service wage bill data and sign functional assignment agreements required for project participation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176956-f77e1459-fbec-4c57-918d-bc1e83399e83.pdf" target="_blank">P173731 – Aide Memoire</a>. Implementation in Sool and Sanaag—territories claimed by both the federal government and Somaliland—is stalled due to unresolved administrative responsibility between the FGS National Project Coordination Unit and Somaliland Project Implementation Unit <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177627-2f23d4e6-e7c6-417c-bd70-af917ff4c80b.pdf" target="_blank">P177627 – Aide Memoire</a>. Audit recommendation implementation rates in FMS Ministries of Finance stand at only 10% <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177298-cbe2c81d-1766-4a9e-9b9c-8db997576511.pdf" target="_blank">P177298 – ISR</a>. Across the portfolio, political and governance risk ratings are uniformly elevated, with projects reporting that governance instability is the primary constraint on implementation progress <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P508408-dcd3a157-59c0-4c83-b21d-34583800ce9c.pdf" target="_blank">P508408 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P173119-5cec1334-a6ca-404f-a406-0ef855ea39e3.pdf" target="_blank">P173119 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176898-9fb8ece5-6386-4b3f-b0bb-80a8c254cb08.pdf" target="_blank">P176898 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P172031-40354814-08a2-485a-8b27-dbe5154f2444.pdf" target="_blank">P172031 – ISR</a> <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087783da4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170922 – ISR</a>.

## 3. Severe Drought, Mass Displacement, and Health System Collapse

Somalia is experiencing its worst drought in at least 40 years, with a fourth consecutive failed rainy season, rainfall 60% below average in northern regions, and the Deyr season cereal harvest 83% below the long-term average. As of February 2026, 6.5 million people—approximately one in three Somalis—face high levels of hunger, including 2 million in Emergency (IPC Phase 4) conditions. An estimated 1.85 million children under five face acute malnutrition. Climate-related shocks have overtaken conflict as the leading cause of new displacement, with nearly 4 million IDPs nationally and close to 500,000 people having abandoned their homes since September 2025. Forced evictions compound vulnerability: over 141,900 IDPs were evicted from temporary shelters between January and August 2025, with 81% of evictions in Banadir region. Simultaneously, Somalia's health system is projected to lose at least 35% of functioning facilities by mid-2026 due to funding cuts and conflict disruption, with 79 supplementary feeding sites and 182 health facilities in Puntland already facing closures and staff shortages. Projects across the portfolio were designed with awareness of these dynamics but are now confronting conditions that exceed baseline assumptions. The Somalia Safety Net Project explicitly links cash transfers to complementary nutrition services (SAM/MAM treatment) and targets 900,000 children under five requiring urgent nutrition support <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086f839bc&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P171346 – PAD</a>. The Somalia Primary Health Care Project targets 1.84 million beneficiaries including IDPs and nomads, with services designed to reach hard-to-reach populations <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886c3194&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172031 – PAD</a>. The Somalia Inclusive and Resilient Communities project includes climate adaptation investments and climate risk monitoring systems <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-1c1564c9-a880-45d6-9b95-a7f1b4568685.pdf" target="_blank">P508408 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing severely. The Federal Minister of Finance has frozen Female Health Worker recruitment due to sustainability concerns, halting the Marwo Caafimaad Program expansion precisely when health system capacity is collapsing <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176956-f77e1459-fbec-4c57-918d-bc1e83399e83.pdf" target="_blank">P173731 – Aide Memoire</a>. Medical supply disruptions are affecting health service delivery in Puntland and Hirshabelle, requiring new supply agreements and stock management interventions <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176956-f77e1459-fbec-4c57-918d-bc1e83399e83.pdf" target="_blank">P173731 – Aide Memoire</a>. Three federal member states—Galmudug, Hirshabelle, and Southwest—have failed to meet minimum health ministry appropriations criteria <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176956-f77e1459-fbec-4c57-918d-bc1e83399e83.pdf" target="_blank">P173731 – Aide Memoire</a>. The vaccination project reports zero progress on health facility designation and equipment <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176956-8f1dfdd6-a289-431e-b9b0-15ea55e5eccc.pdf" target="_blank">P176956 – ISR</a>. The urban resilience project has activated its Contingency Emergency Response Component and reached 1.05 million beneficiaries (82% IDPs) through emergency cash assistance, with the project's CERC activation reflecting the scale of the humanitarian emergency <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087783da4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170922 – ISR</a>. Displacement-driven rapid urbanization around Baidoa has required mid-project design modifications to infrastructure capacity <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088219289&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170922 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 4. Somalia-UAE Diplomatic Rupture and Somaliland Geopolitical Polarization

On January 12, 2026, Somalia's Council of Ministers annulled all bilateral agreements with the UAE—spanning port operations at Berbera, Bosaso, and Kismayo, as well as defense and security cooperation—citing UAE conduct described as undermining Somalia's sovereignty. The rupture was triggered by the UAE's perceived facilitation of Israel's December 2025 recognition of Somaliland and the transit of a Yemeni separatist leader through Somali airspace. Puntland, Jubaland, and Somaliland promptly rejected the federal decision within hours, exposing the limits of Mogadishu's authority. The UAE had been paying salaries for at least 3,400 soldiers and backing the Puntland Maritime Police Force's counterterrorism campaign. Somalia has sought to fill the gap through military cooperation MOUs with Qatar and Saudi Arabia, but these are unlikely to immediately replace Emirati training, salary support, and airpower. Israel's recognition of Somaliland—the first globally—has simultaneously drawn Somalia into broader Gulf geopolitical rivalry and intensified Somaliland's political separation from Mogadishu. Projects operating across Puntland, Jubaland, and Somaliland anticipated federal-state coordination challenges but not this acute a rupture. The Somalia Water for Agropastoral Communities project includes Puntland Water Development Agency as an implementing partner and operates across Jubaland and Puntland <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-63dd48bb-2de2-45f6-884c-8c9a581a5515.pdf" target="_blank">P177627 – PAD</a>. The Somalia Education for Human Capital Development Project has implementation sites in Puntland (Taleex, Xingalool) and Jubaland (Baardheere, Afmadow), with Somaliland Ministry of Education as an implementing agency <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08863e060&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172434 – PAD</a>. The Somalia Strengthening Intergovernmental Fiscal Relations project relies on two separate implementing entities—the Federal Ministry of Finance and Somaliland Ministry of Finance—whose coordination is now complicated by Somaliland's heightened political separation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0187c8e3-e606-4407-b94f-d27e9320d601.pdf" target="_blank">P177298 – PAD</a>. These risks are materializing. Implementation evidence documents that governance coordination between the FGS National Project Coordination Unit and Somaliland PIU has broken down over implementation modalities in disputed Sool and Sanaag territories, with no resolution on administrative responsibility <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177627-2f23d4e6-e7c6-417c-bd70-af917ff4c80b.pdf" target="_blank">P177627 – Aide Memoire</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177627-fbbfa6e1-4f42-4b2f-985f-98a91aaca38e.pdf" target="_blank">P177627 – ISR</a>. The Somaliland recognition dispute and resulting geopolitical polarization are identified as active constraints on project implementation in Somaliland <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177298-cbe2c81d-1766-4a9e-9b9c-8db997576511.pdf" target="_blank">P177298 – ISR</a>. The Somalia Primary Health Care Project has contingent financing of US$7 million reserved for Somaliland activities pending FMoH-Somaliland agreement on fund flows—an agreement that is now at heightened risk of non-completion given Somaliland's dismissal of federal authority <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886c3194&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172031 – PAD</a>. Payment delays in Garowe (Puntland) requiring new inter-governmental agreements and monthly oversight reflect the practical consequences of the rupture on project fund flows <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088219289&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170922 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## 5. Islamic State-Somalia's Puntland Threat and Media Freedom Suppression

Two distinct but reinforcing risks are compounding the portfolio's governance and security challenges. First, Islamic State-Somalia (IS-S) has evolved from a localized group into a key node in IS's global network, operating from strongholds in Puntland's Cal Miskaad and Al-Madow mountain ranges with an estimated 1,000–1,600 fighters, approximately 60% of whom are foreign fighters. AFRICOM conducted coordinated airstrikes targeting IS-S on February 16, 2026, while Puntland's Operation Hilaac continues ground operations with limited federal or AU support. Somalia's January 2026 cancellation of UAE security agreements—including those backing the Puntland Maritime Police Force—threatens to further degrade the counterterrorism capacity that had been containing IS-S. Projects operating in Puntland face direct exposure. The Somalia Primary Health Care Project covers Nugaal region in Puntland, where IS-S operates from mountain strongholds, creating acute security risks for health workers and facility access <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0886c3194&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P172031 – PAD</a>. The Somalia COVID-19 Vaccination Project lists Garowe (Puntland) as a specific project location and targets all Federal Member States for vaccination campaigns <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a76c27&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P176956 – PAD</a>. The Somalia Resilience and Flood Recovery Project includes Puntland as one of six project locations, with government access beyond urban areas already restricted <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08790306f&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173315 – PAD</a>. Second, Somalia's authorities have intensified suppression of independent reporting: in March 2025, the Minister of Information announced a ban on reporting on security threats; in January–February 2026, NISA officers and Presidential Guard personnel blocked journalists from covering parliamentary sessions on constitutional amendments on at least three occasions; and the National Union of Somali Journalists documented at least 41 press freedom violations in the first half of 2025 alone. This suppression undermines the transparency and accountability mechanisms that projects depend upon for stakeholder engagement, grievance redress, and public communication about beneficiary selection and fund allocation. Projects with explicit transparency objectives are particularly exposed. The Somalia Strengthening Intergovernmental Fiscal Relations project supports budget transparency and citizen engagement mechanisms <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-0187c8e3-e606-4407-b94f-d27e9320d601.pdf" target="_blank">P177298 – PAD</a>, while the Somalia Urban Resilience Project emphasizes public disclosure of procurements and transparent consultation processes <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b08735b2ef&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170922 – PAD</a>. Implementation evidence confirms that media suppression is contributing to governance risk ratings across the portfolio <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171346-39ff3c88-94c1-4a7b-9012-8caec3d57659.pdf" target="_blank">P171346 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P168115-be5f1569-0d1c-44e9-b1c2-0a42a38fa9a4.pdf" target="_blank">P168115 – ISR</a> <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088a396a8&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P173315 – ISR</a> <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087783da4&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P170922 – ISR</a>, and that the broader governance dysfunction it reflects is undermining fiduciary accountability mechanisms and institutional capacity for project oversight <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P176956-8f1dfdd6-a289-431e-b9b0-15ea55e5eccc.pdf" target="_blank">P176956 – ISR</a> <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P177298-cbe2c81d-1766-4a9e-9b9c-8db997576511.pdf" target="_blank">P177298 – ISR</a>.
