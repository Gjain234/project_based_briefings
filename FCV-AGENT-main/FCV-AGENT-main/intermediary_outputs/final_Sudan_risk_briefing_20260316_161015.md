## Sudan Emergency Health and Nutrition Project (P504629)

Sudan's active multi-front war between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) poses the most immediate and severe threat to this health and nutrition project. Kordofan and Darfur — both designated priority implementation areas — have emerged as epicentres of hostilities, with drone strikes directly targeting hospitals (including Dilling General Hospital) and sustained attacks across El Obeid and North Darfur. The PAD explicitly anticipated this exposure, noting that multiple health facilities in conflict areas had already been attacked and looted, that 75% of health facilities in conflict-affected areas were non-functional, and that ongoing security risks from armed groups, violence, extortion, and theft required a customized Security Management Framework <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. These risks are now fully materializing: the ISR confirms that shifting frontlines are continuously forcing the project to adapt facility targeting and supply chain approaches, with access to health facilities and beneficiaries severely constrained <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties — including drone strikes on WFP operations and systematic restrictions on aid movement in Kordofan and Darfur — compounds this directly. The PAD foresaw this risk, noting that insecurity affects delivery of medications, supplies, and fuel, and that project supervision would be challenging, with mitigation relying on UN humanitarian corridor facilitation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that access obstruction is now a realized operational constraint, with high residual risk ratings across governance, institutional capacity, and stakeholder engagement categories <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. Sudan's de facto state fragmentation — with the RSF's parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" controlling most of Darfur and parts of Kordofan — further undermines the project's governance and coordination architecture. The PAD identified that federal institutions were non-functional and that the project would need to rely on state and locality-level institutions, deliberately avoiding channeling funds through federal or parastatal entities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that political and governance risk is rated High and is actively materializing, with institutional capacity constraints and stakeholder tensions reflecting the fragmented authority landscape <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The mass displacement crisis — Sudan hosts the world's largest, with over 14 million forcibly displaced — directly threatens beneficiary identification and targeting. The PAD acknowledged that security challenges and population mobility may compromise beneficiary identification, and that frequent population movements fleeing conflict areas represent a core technical design risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. Confirmed famine in El Fasher and Kadugli — both within project priority areas — adds a further layer of operational stress: the PAD's nutrition component (including severe acute malnutrition management) will face severely reduced effectiveness when food insecurity is the primary driver of malnutrition and when famine conditions are spreading to an additional 20 areas across Greater Darfur and Greater Kordofan <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The ISR flags famine-affected zones as contributing to the project's high "Other" risk category <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. The project also faces acute risks from systematic sexual violence and impunity. The PAD rated sexual exploitation and abuse risk as high, noting that health services, community engagement, and cash transfers create SEA/SH exposure in an FCV context with displaced populations and weak governance <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>. The collapsed health system — with 37% of facilities non-functional and 201 verified attacks on healthcare since April 2023 — is both the problem the project seeks to address and the environment that makes addressing it so difficult; the ISR confirms that institutional capacity constraints tied to health system collapse are rated High <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504629-f8992420-6b24-4296-bf70-918431a5811f.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – ISR</a>. Finally, the severe humanitarian funding gap — Sudan's 2026 response plan received only 5.5% of required funds as of February 2026 — threatens the complementary services (food, water, shelter) that health interventions depend upon to be effective, a risk the PAD acknowledged but did not fully resolve <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-3f523f6e-fd66-4613-ab40-20cf7c482908.pdf" target="_blank">P504629 – PAD</a>.

## Sudan Emergency Safety Net Project (P505963)

This cash transfer project, targeting 602,000 beneficiaries across multiple conflict-affected states including West Kordofan, South Kordofan, Northern West Darfur, and East Darfur, faces a convergence of country-level risks that are actively materializing across nearly every implementation dimension. The active multi-front war is the foundational threat: the PAD identified high security risk from ongoing armed conflict, noting evidence of robbery, theft, and looting of life-saving aid by soldiers, and described security as a "central implementation challenge" <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that environment and social risks, institutional capacity, fiduciary management, and governance risks are all rated High, with active conflict in Kordofan and Darfur directly constraining field operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. Confirmed famine in El Fasher and Kadugli — both within or adjacent to project target areas — creates a particularly acute challenge for the project's cash transfer design. The PAD targeted IPC Phase 3 and 4 areas and acknowledged that worsening economic conditions risk eroding the real purchasing power of cash transfers, and that project sustainability depends on minimum stability levels <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that macroeconomic instability — hyperinflation at 211% as of October 2024, currency depreciation, and disrupted supply chains — is a realized risk actively undermining the purchasing power of transfers <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. With famine now confirmed at IPC Phase 5 in project-adjacent localities, the US$10 per person per month transfer amount may be structurally insufficient to meet survival needs. The deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties is directly constraining WFP and UNICEF's ability to reach beneficiaries. The PAD acknowledged high security risk from military operations and violence toward civilians and aid workers, as well as bureaucratic impediments and politicization of aid, with mitigation relying on flexible geographic targeting and frontline worker autonomy <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that access obstruction is a realized constraint, with institutional capacity of implementing agencies rated High risk, reflecting the compounding effect of access restrictions, displacement, and resource scarcity on WFP and UNICEF operations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. Sudan's de facto state fragmentation — with the RSF's parallel administration controlling Darfur and parts of Kordofan — is a realized governance risk. The PAD explicitly noted that the project operates under the Bank's policy on dealings with de facto governments, and identified political and institutional fragmentation as a high risk, with the government apparatus relocated to Port Sudan and significant civil servant displacement <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that political and governance instability is rated High, with competing authority structures undermining coherent implementation across fragmented territories <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. The mass displacement crisis — 14 million forcibly displaced — creates volatile and unpredictable beneficiary populations that challenge the project's biometric registration and 12-month transfer design. The PAD acknowledged that widespread displacement may lead to social tensions between IDPs and host communities, and that flexible geographic targeting would be needed to adapt to evolving conflict dynamics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that environment and social safeguard risks are rated High, with displacement dynamics, famine conditions, and disease outbreaks compounding implementation challenges <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. Component 2, which links cash transfers to health services for 30,000 pregnant and lactating women, depends on "relatively stable areas with functioning primary healthcare facilities" — a baseline assumption that is increasingly untenable given that 37% of health facilities are non-functional and disease outbreaks continue to spread <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-ac984c78-9d8f-4ccb-865f-da4754c9f18c.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – PAD</a>. The severe humanitarian funding gap (5.5% of the 2026 response plan funded as of February 2026) threatens the broader ecosystem of complementary services that cash transfers depend upon to be effective, and the ISR confirms that institutional capacity constraints driven by underfunding are a realized risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>. Fiduciary risk is also rated High in the ISR, reflecting the combined effect of state fragmentation, access obstruction, and suppression of civil society oversight mechanisms <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P505963-29e6468c-9705-464a-a172-383e7b1bf9e4.pdf" target="_blank">P505963 – ISR</a>.

## Sudan Integrated Services Delivery Project (P181490)

This project, targeting 560,000 IDP and host community individuals across Kassala, Northern, and River Nile states through UNICEF and WFP, was designed with the explicit recognition that its target areas were selected for their relative security — currently neutral, not strongholds of either warring party — but the PAD acknowledged that approximately 30% of Sudan's counties are directly affected by conflict and that conflict escalation into target areas remained a credible risk requiring contingency planning <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that political and governance instability is rated High, with Sudan's de facto state fragmentation — the RSF's parallel administration in Darfur and the SAF-aligned government in Khartoum — creating competing authorities and legitimacy vacuums that undermine project coordination even in nominally stable states <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The mass displacement crisis is central to this project's design and its risks. The PAD identified 560,000 IDP and host community beneficiaries, noting that over 5.5 million IDPs were present in target states as of December 2023, with 64% in urban centers, and acknowledged the risk of increased IDP mobility to project areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that IDP and host community social cohesion is a realized implementation challenge, with tensions between displaced and host populations over resource access and service delivery emerging as a key risk <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. The deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties threatens the project's reliance on UN humanitarian supply routes and the UN security umbrella. The PAD acknowledged risks from complex local procurement under conflict and restricted supply of goods if conflict escalates, with mitigation relying on UN security frameworks <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that institutional capacity constraints — driven in part by access obstruction — are rated High <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. Confirmed famine spreading across Darfur and Kordofan creates a forward-looking risk for this project: while target states are not currently listed as famine zones, the projected spread to 20 additional areas through 2026 could overwhelm the project's food security component (agricultural inputs and community consumer cooperatives), requiring a pivot to emergency humanitarian response beyond project scope <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that salary arrears for frontline workers — teachers and health workers — are a realized risk directly undermining service delivery capacity, with unpaid health workers contributing to the broader health system collapse (37% of facilities non-functional) and reducing the project's ability to sustain services for displaced populations <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>. Systematic sexual violence and impunity create risks for the project's GBV response component and community engagement mechanisms. The PAD included GBV case management, medical care, and psychosocial support, with grievance redress mechanisms established through both implementing agencies <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. However, the pervasive violence and impunity documented by the UN Fact-Finding Mission create an environment where beneficiaries — particularly women and girls — may fear reporting or engaging with project services. The project's community-led governance model through Community Development Committees, which requires minimum quotas for women (40%) and IDPs (30%), is also vulnerable to arbitrary detention and political repression: the PAD identified security risks to stakeholders such as resistance committees targeted by warring parties, but did not fully address the risk that CDC members could face detention or retaliation <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-87697e07-7f5d-4c42-94a5-42bab3e22853.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – PAD</a>. The ISR confirms that institutional capacity and stakeholder engagement risks are both rated High <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P181490-adc6779e-512b-426c-b6fd-f151a173ccc4.pdf" target="_blank">P181490 – ISR</a>.

## Sudan Learning Continuity and School Grants Project (P504621)

This education project — providing school grants and learning continuity support to primary school-age children across conflict-affected states — is operating in one of the most severely disrupted educational environments in the world, and the evidence from implementation documents confirms that country-level risks are translating directly into project-level constraints. The active multi-front war is the primary driver of school closures in target states. The Aide Memoire confirms that conflict-related factors are preventing schools from reopening, directly blocking grant distribution and learning material delivery, and that the project's learning continuity component is a direct adaptation to conflict-driven educational disruption <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The ISR confirms that school closures due to active conflict are a realized risk, with the project unable to distribute grants to schools that remain closed in Kordofan and Darfur <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The mass displacement crisis — 14 million forcibly displaced, including 7.3 million IDPs — has fragmented the school-age population and disrupted educational infrastructure. The Aide Memoire confirms that displacement dynamics are directly contributing to limited school access and implementation constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>, and the ISR confirms that displacement-driven beneficiary fragmentation is a realized implementation challenge <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties is creating systemic barriers to grant distribution and learning material delivery. The Aide Memoire confirms that access obstruction is directly constraining project implementation, with restrictions on aid movement in Kordofan and Darfur preventing field teams from reaching schools and beneficiaries <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The ISR confirms that access obstruction is contributing to the project's high fiduciary risk rating and low fund utilization — only 7.3% of funds disbursed as of the reporting period, with procurement at 98.3% underspend ($281k of $16.8m signed contracts executed as of April 2025) <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. Sudan's de facto state fragmentation is undermining the institutional coherence required for school grant implementation. With the RSF's parallel administration controlling Darfur and parts of Kordofan, and the SAF-aligned government in Khartoum, the project faces competing jurisdictions, unclear counterparts, and fragmented accountability chains. The ISR confirms that institutional capacity constraints tied to state fragmentation are rated High, with the Project Implementation Unit facing difficulties coordinating across fragmented territorial control <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The Aide Memoire confirms that the Security Management Framework — a prerequisite for safe implementation — had not yet been cleared as of the reporting period, indicating that unresolved security threats tied to the ongoing conflict are preventing the project from establishing the operational protocols needed to work safely in conflict-affected areas <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. Systematic attacks on civilians and impunity are creating an environment where schools cannot safely reopen and where beneficiaries and project staff face direct physical risk. The Aide Memoire connects the pending Security Management Framework to the broader pattern of war crimes and civilian attacks documented by the UN Fact-Finding Mission <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The ISR confirms that stakeholder engagement is rated High risk, with political repression, civil society suppression, and arbitrary detention creating a climate of fear that inhibits community participation in school selection and learning hub coordination <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-3ce90e04-f708-4d62-bf6e-33745ef8f079.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – ISR</a>. The severe humanitarian funding gap is also materializing as a constraint: the Aide Memoire confirms that budgetary constraints delayed the recruitment of a Third-Party Monitor, creating a verification vacuum precisely when independent oversight is most critical in a context of widespread access obstruction and accountability failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>. The collapsed health system and disease outbreak risks intersect with the project through school closures during cholera, measles, and dengue outbreaks, and through the project's socio-emotional wellness component, which depends on safe school environments that overcrowded displacement sites and disrupted WASH services undermine <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P504621-94870ad3-8b0d-4a33-9db8-35e61adca00f.pdf" target="_blank">P504621 – Aide Memoire</a>.

## Sudan Energy and Digital Infrastructure Project (P507788)

This project — focused on energy and digital infrastructure development — faces a governance and institutional environment that has been fundamentally destabilized by Sudan's ongoing conflict, and the ISR confirms that country-level risks are materializing across all major implementation risk categories. Sudan's de facto state fragmentation is the most structurally significant risk for this project. With the RSF's parallel "Government of Peace and Unity" controlling most of Darfur and parts of Kordofan, and the SAF-aligned government based in Khartoum, there is no unified national authority capable of establishing coherent energy or digital sector policies, approving project activities, or enforcing agreements. The ISR confirms that political and governance instability is rated High, with sector policy instability — the inability to align project activities with coherent national policy frameworks — also rated High <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. Energy and digital infrastructure projects require long-term regulatory certainty and institutional counterparts with legitimate authority; both are absent in Sudan's current fragmented governance landscape. The stalled peace diplomacy and fragmented mediation architecture compound this directly. With multiple parallel peace initiatives (the US-led Quad, the AU Quintet, bilateral Egyptian efforts) having failed to produce a ceasefire as of March 2026, and with the UN Panel of Experts supporting the Sudan Sanctions Committee not yet constituted due to Council member disagreements, there is no credible near-term pathway to the governance stabilization that infrastructure investment requires. The ISR confirms that sector policy instability is a realized risk, with the absence of a unified governance framework preventing coherent policy-making in the energy and digital sectors <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The deliberate obstruction of humanitarian access by both warring parties extends to obstruction of institutional functioning more broadly, preventing government institutions from formulating, communicating, or enforcing sector policies. The ISR confirms that stakeholder engagement is rated High risk, with communities reluctant to engage due to fear of retaliation and government actors potentially weaponizing project activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. Fiduciary risk is also rated High in the ISR, reflecting the combined effect of state fragmentation (competing claims over financial systems and payment mechanisms), access obstruction (preventing monitoring and verification of fund utilization), and the suppression of civil society oversight mechanisms through arbitrary detention and political repression <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P507788-9c68afde-977a-43e9-ae73-f4cf0370aaf9.pdf" target="_blank">P507788 – ISR</a>. The arbitrary detention of political activists, suppression of civil society, and contraction of civilian political space — documented across Gezira state and nationally — directly constrain the institutional partnerships and community-level governance structures that infrastructure projects depend upon.
