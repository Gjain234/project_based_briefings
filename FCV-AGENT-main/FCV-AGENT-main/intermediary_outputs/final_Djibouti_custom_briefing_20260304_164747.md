## Djibouti FCV Portfolio Briefing

**Governance and Institutional Capacity** risks represent the most pervasive and cross-cutting challenge across the Djibouti portfolio. At the country level, the October 2025 constitutional revision removing presidential age limits (DJ-001) has deepened political stagnation, while the near-total collapse of independent civil society and media freedom (DJ-004) has eliminated the external accountability mechanisms that normally discipline institutional performance. Presidential succession uncertainty (DJ-008) adds a further layer of unpredictability. These dynamics were anticipated in project design: the PAD for the Public Finance Management project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-28d37d68-d0ab-4355-acca-fed683f4b387.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – PAD</a> rated political and governance risk as "High" and flagged that vested interests could delay reforms, while the TVET Skills project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a> identified "substantial" political risk but relied on a government-led steering committee under the Economic Development Council without independent oversight safeguards. In practice, these risks have fully materialized. The PFM project's governance decrees, confirmed by the Council of Ministers in February 2025, remained unsigned and unpublished in the Official Gazette months later, blocking project effectiveness and affecting all donor-funded PFM initiatives in the country <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. The TVET project's steering committee remained inactive as of early 2025, with the mission seeking alternative governance arrangements <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. The Digital Fiduciary Network project faced a compounding governance failure: the Digital Code (Book Two on telecommunications regulation) remained unadopted despite repeated mission recommendations, leaving the regulatory authority ARMD without its primary operational tool and stalling multiple project activities <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-a6aeb6a6-887c-41f9-8eb7-fb31c9e7d652.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. Political and governance risk ratings have either escalated—rising from Moderate to Substantial for the health project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>—or remained persistently elevated at Substantial or High across the corridor <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-6fc17b17-9f69-4b50-938f-fac14f07b6b3.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – ISR</a>, statistics <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-4b12f487-05cf-4472-bbf3-012c7cb41ce1.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>, social cohesion <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P506969-c23fc0d1-df16-470e-9ddf-fdd831536a8b.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – ISR</a>, and PFM <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a> projects, confirming that the country's governance trajectory is actively constraining portfolio delivery.

**Service Delivery and Access** risks are acutely realized across health, education, TVET, and infrastructure projects, with refugee and displaced populations consistently among the most underserved beneficiary groups. The country risk of refugee and asylum-seeker hosting amid resource constraints (DJ-009)—with 32,000 displaced persons and 85% residing in remote refugee villages—was flagged as a design risk across multiple PADs, including the health project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b088da7dd6&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P178033 – PAD</a> and education project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b087ef2534&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P166059 – PAD</a>, though mitigation strategies relied primarily on dialogue with UNHCR without addressing structural service delivery gaps. These gaps have proven severe in implementation. The health project reported refugee immunization coverage at only 50% against an 80% target, with vitamin A supplementation declining from 10% to 5%, and disaggregated refugee data remaining unavailable across multiple indicators <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178033-d78154cd-d774-432b-a705-9636c8a8a3b0.pdf" target="_blank">P178033 – ISR</a>. The TVET project recorded zero refugee enrollment and zero completion data, zero persons with disabilities enrolled, and zero refugees engaged in work-based learning against targets of 220, 160, and 180 respectively <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. The education project showed declining refugee primary enrollment—from 596 in 2021–22 to 384 in 2024–25—with refugee schools still not integrated into the national EMIS system <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P166059-1c484167-6c9c-4c60-bb6c-a5a8d9a85a29.pdf" target="_blank">P166059 – ISR</a>. The urban development project reported zero refugee-led CSO sub-projects implemented, zero property access certificates delivered to refugees, and zero recommendations from the 2017 National Refugee Law operationalized <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. Infrastructure access compounds these failures: poor rural road conditions caused logistical delays in in-kind transfer distribution across social protection projects [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire], and the food security project suspended main activities following the June 2025 drought declaration, with only 11 of 25 targeted water infrastructure facilities rehabilitated and the remaining 26 discontinued <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-40bca9d9-77f1-4080-b0cc-d5ba5676b10e.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – ISR</a>. The corridor project documented critical safety failures—unsecured trenches, absent HSE agents, 150 daily laborers without compliant contracts or social security registration—alongside inaccurate road accident data and persistent non-compliance in government quarterly reporting <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174300-1e1324d5-1aa7-4175-a5a6-411c6205fe3f.pdf" target="_blank">P174300 – Aide Memoire</a>.

**Economic Vulnerability** risks are structurally embedded in portfolio design and have constrained implementation outcomes across skills, livelihoods, and infrastructure projects. Djibouti's youth unemployment exceeding 75% (DJ-002), combined with high inequality, endemic corruption, and limited private sector absorptive capacity, was identified as a substantial macroeconomic risk in the TVET project PAD <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>, which acknowledged that no labor market demand assessment or private sector capacity analysis was documented to validate employment targets for 23,775 trained graduates. The agri-food project PAD <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-9320408e-8d34-46ff-a14e-0221bdaeab77.pdf" target="_blank">P178836 – PAD</a> similarly identified macroeconomic risk as substantial without specifying youth employment pathways. In implementation, these structural constraints have proven determinative. The TVET project recorded zero bilateral labor agreements signed against a target of six, zero short-term migrant workers deployed against a target of 250, and the online gig work component—targeting 500 individuals—was deleted during restructuring due to "significant delays" and coordination failures <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. Refugee employment outcomes remained at 5% of target <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-ac50c771-2198-4714-8f62-c0065e446f6c.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – ISR</a>. Social protection projects reported persistent difficulty identifying viable income-generating activities in remote localities due to weak market access and limited economic diversification [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire], with rural savings and credit groups facing governance and financial management capacity constraints <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178992-7c9d64c3-b649-4740-bf35-769304d92e5f.pdf" target="_blank">P508560 – Aide Memoire</a>. The food security project's disbursement rate stood at only 15.2% one year before closure, with the mid-term review postponed due to insufficient results <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>. The statistics project faced staffing constraints slowing macroeconomic modeling and Medium-Term Debt Strategy development <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P171777-4b12f487-05cf-4472-bbf3-012c7cb41ce1.pdf" target="_blank">P171777 – ISR</a>, while the PFM project's institutional capacity gaps were assessed as affecting all donor-funded governance initiatives in the country <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P180696-38a76556-6182-48de-9bfe-ad27e73ddd21.pdf" target="_blank">P180696 – ISR</a>. Community infrastructure sustainability emerged as a cross-cutting concern, with water systems and boreholes facing maintenance funding gaps in multiple projects [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire].

**Social Cohesion and Protection** risks are concentrated around refugee-host community integration, ethnic exclusion, and the collapse of grievance and safeguarding mechanisms across the portfolio. Ethnic tensions between the Issa Somali majority and Afar minority (DJ-003) were identified as a susceptibility risk in multiple PADs—including the TVET project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=IBArchive-38b35d89-2c5c-4636-ab7e-0ba662b95a5f.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – PAD</a>, social cohesion project <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=BOSIB-87c444de-4797-4bf9-b654-4932a7fb0112.pdf" target="_blank">P506969 – PAD</a>, and urban development project <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b086ac6df2&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – PAD</a>—but none documented explicit ethnic representation safeguards in steering committees or beneficiary selection criteria. The escalating Djibouti-Somaliland diplomatic crisis (DJ-006) adds a regional dimension, with potential to destabilize Somali refugee populations concentrated in Ali Sabieh and Obock, areas where multiple projects operate. In implementation, social cohesion outcomes have stalled: the urban development project's assessment of refugee socioeconomic inclusion and social cohesion in target areas remained incomplete as of May 2025, beneficiary perception surveys had not been conducted, and host-refugee social cohesion perception data was absent <a href="http://wbdocsservices.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&RENDITION=Y&TYPE=T&I4_DOCID=090224b0870d5571&stream=Yes" target="_blank">P162901 – ISR</a>. Youth training programs across social protection projects lacked pre-defined eligibility criteria and transparent selection procedures, creating risks of unequal access that missions flagged as requiring proactive management [P508560 | Aide Memoire; P180999 | Aide Memoire]. Grievance redress mechanisms were found to be structurally weak or non-operational across multiple projects: the food security project's complaint management mechanism for sexual exploitation and abuse was only 53% implemented <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178988-93a93266-7e5c-4a13-9659-51561c0a19bc.pdf" target="_blank">P178988 – Aide Memoire</a>; the GBV prevention project lacked accounting software, had delayed financial reporting, and stalled NGO contract negotiations for community mobilization <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P178332-4908093d-8bfd-4f36-a29c-d9575d229601.pdf" target="_blank">P178332 – Aide Memoire</a>; and the digital project's GRM was not operationalized, with complaint channels absent from ministry websites and work sites <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P174461-a6aeb6a6-887c-41f9-8eb7-fb31c9e7d652.pdf" target="_blank">P174461 – Aide Memoire</a>. The TVET project's GBV action plan remained untransmitted despite training in June 2024, and the internal audit function was compromised by a structural conflict of interest—the auditor reporting to the coordinator whose processes they were mandated to review <a href="https://spfilesapi.worldbank.org/services?I4_SERVICE=FILE_URLS2&SITE_KEY=DRPSINT&I4_DOCID=P175483-22796ae4-2f59-4fa6-af78-41747fb3e346.pdf" target="_blank">P175483 – Aide Memoire</a>. Across the portfolio, the absence of independent civil society (DJ-004) renders these project-level accountability mechanisms the last line of recourse for vulnerable beneficiaries, making their non-operationalization a critical protection gap in an already constrained civic environment.
